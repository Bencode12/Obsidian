## Topic Summaries

### Differential Geometry

Studies smooth manifolds and curvature. Language of general relativity.

**Key ideas**: Manifolds, tangent spaces, Riemannian metrics, geodesics.

**Prerequisites**: Multivariable calculus, linear algebra, topology.

### Lie Groups & Lie Algebras

Continuous symmetry. Bridges algebra and geometry.

**Key ideas**: Matrix groups, exponential map, representations, root systems.

**Prerequisites**: Group theory, differential geometry, linear algebra.

### Ergodic Theory

Statistical behavior of dynamical systems.

**Key ideas**: Measure-preserving maps, ergodic theorem, entropy, mixing.

**Prerequisites**: Measure theory, probability.

### Operator Algebras

Algebras of operators on Hilbert spaces. Quantum mechanics foundation.

**Key ideas**: C*-algebras, von Neumann algebras, states, K-theory.

**Prerequisites**: Functional analysis, measure theory.

### Partial Differential Equations

Equations involving partial derivatives. Models physical phenomena.

**Key ideas**: Classification, heat/wave/Laplace equations, weak solutions, Sobolev spaces.

**Prerequisites**: Real analysis, functional analysis.

### Algebraic Geometry

Studies solutions to polynomial equations using algebra.

**Key ideas**: Varieties, schemes, sheaves, cohomology, divisors.

**Prerequisites**: Abstract algebra, commutative algebra, topology.

### Category Theory

"Mathematics of mathematics." Abstract framework for structures.

**Key ideas**: Categories, functors, natural transformations, adjunctions, limits.

**Prerequisites**: Any advanced mathematics (provides unifying language).

### Homological Algebra

Algebraic machinery for topology and geometry.

**Key ideas**: Chain complexes, derived functors, Ext, Tor, spectral sequences.

**Prerequisites**: Abstract algebra, some topology.

### Spectral Theory

Generalizes eigenvalues to infinite dimensions.

**Key ideas**: Spectrum, self-adjoint operators, functional calculus.

**Prerequisites**: Functional analysis, operator theory.

### Stochastic Calculus

Calculus for random processes. Foundation of mathematical finance.

**Key ideas**: Brownian motion, Itô integral, Itô's lemma, SDEs.

**Prerequisites**: Probability theory, real analysis.

### Mathematical Logic & Set Theory

Foundations of mathematics.

**Key ideas**: Completeness, incompleteness, model theory, forcing, large cardinals.

**Prerequisites**: Proof-based mathematics, set theory basics.

## Choosing Your Path

**For Physics:**
- Differential Geometry → Lie Groups → PDEs → Operator Algebras

**For Pure Mathematics:**
- Category Theory → Homological Algebra → Algebraic Geometry

**For Analysis:**
- Operator Algebras → Spectral Theory → Ergodic Theory

**For Foundations:**
- Mathematical Logic & Set Theory

**For Applications:**
- Stochastic Calculus (finance)
- PDEs (engineering, physics)
- Differential Geometry (graphics, robotics)

## Final Advice

These topics represent frontiers of mathematical knowledge. Don't rush—build strong foundations first. Each topic could be a lifetime of study. Focus on understanding core ideas before diving into technical details.

**Remember**: Mathematics is not a spectator sport. Work through proofs, solve problems, and reconstruct arguments yourself.
