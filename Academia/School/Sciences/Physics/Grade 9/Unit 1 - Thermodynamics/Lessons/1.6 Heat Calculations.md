# 1.6 Heat Calculations

## 🔬 Specific Heat Capacity

### Definition

**Specific Heat Capacity (c)** is the energy needed to raise 1 kg of a substance by 1°C (or 1 K).

### Units
- J/(kg·°C) or J/(kg·K)

### Specific Heat Values

| Substance | c (J/kg·°C) |
|-----------|-------------|
| Water | 4200 |
| Ice | 2100 |
| Aluminum | 900 |
| Copper | 385 |
| Iron | 450 |
| Air | 1000 |

**Key Point:** Water has a very high specific heat capacity!

---

## 🧮 Heat Energy Equation

### Basic Formula

```
Q = mcΔT
```

Where:
- **Q** = Heat energy (Joules, J)
- **m** = Mass (kilograms, kg)
- **c** = Specific heat capacity (J/kg·°C)
- **ΔT** = Temperature change (°C or K)

### Temperature Change

```
ΔT = T_final - T_initial
```

## 📊 Worked Examples

### Example 1: Heating Water

**Problem:** Calculate the energy needed to heat 2 kg of water from 20°C to 80°C.

**Solution:**
```
m = 2 kg
c = 4200 J/kg·°C
ΔT = 80 - 20 = 60°C

Q = mcΔT
Q = 2 × 4200 × 60
Q = 504,000 J = 504 kJ
```

**Answer:** 504 kJ of energy needed

### Example 2: Finding Final Temperature

**Problem:** 500 g of copper at 20°C absorbs 10,000 J. What is the final temperature?

**Solution:**
```
m = 0.5 kg
c = 385 J/kg·°C
Q = 10,000 J
T_initial = 20°C

Q = mcΔT
10,000 = 0.5 × 385 × ΔT
10,000 = 192.5 × ΔT
ΔT = 10,000 / 192.5 = 51.9°C

T_final = T_initial + ΔT
T_final = 20 + 51.9 = 71.9°C
```

**Answer:** Final temperature = 71.9°C

### Example 3: Finding Specific Heat

**Problem:** 2 kg of unknown metal absorbs 88,000 J and temperature rises by 100°C. Find c.

**Solution:**
```
Q = mcΔT
88,000 = 2 × c × 100
88,000 = 200c
c = 88,000 / 200
c = 440 J/kg·°C
```

**Answer:** c = 440 J/kg·°C (likely iron)

## ⚖️ Heat Exchange Problems

### Principle: Heat Lost = Heat Gained

When two objects at different temperatures are in contact:

```
Heat lost by hot object = Heat gained by cold object
m₁c₁ΔT₁ = m₂c₂ΔT₂
```

### Example: Mixing Water

**Problem:** Mix 1 kg of water at 80°C with 2 kg of water at 20°C. Find final temperature.

**Solution:**
```
Let T = final temperature

Heat lost by hot water = Heat gained by cold water
m₁c(T₁ - T) = m₂c(T - T₂)
1 × 4200 × (80 - T) = 2 × 4200 × (T - 20)
80 - T = 2(T - 20)
80 - T = 2T - 40
120 = 3T
T = 40°C
```

**Answer:** Final temperature = 40°C

## ✏️ Practice Problems

### Problem 1
Calculate energy to heat 3 kg of aluminum from 25°C to 125°C. (c = 900 J/kg·°C)

**Answer:** 270,000 J or 270 kJ

### Problem 2
A 0.5 kg iron block cools from 200°C to 50°C. How much energy is released? (c = 450 J/kg·°C)

**Answer:** 33,750 J

### Problem 3
2 kg of water at 90°C is mixed with 3 kg of water at 10°C. Find final temperature.

**Answer:** 42°C

### Problem 4
**Explain** why water is used in central heating systems instead of other liquids.

**Answer:** Water has a high specific heat capacity, so it can store and transfer more energy per kg than most liquids.
