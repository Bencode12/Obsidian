## Setup — Labelling a Right Triangle

In a right triangle, pick one of the acute angles and call it $\alpha$. The three sides are named relative to $\alpha$:

|Side|Name|Position|
|---|---|---|
|The longest side|**Hypotenuse**|Always opposite the right angle|
|The side facing $\alpha$|**Opposite**|Directly across from $\alpha$|
|The side next to $\alpha$|**Adjacent**|Between $\alpha$ and the right angle|

> [!warning] Adjacent and opposite swap when you switch angle If you relabel the triangle from the other acute angle, the opposite and adjacent sides switch. The hypotenuse never changes.

---

## The Three Ratios

$$\sin\alpha = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \qquad \cos\alpha = \frac{\text{adjacent}}{\text{hypotenuse}} \qquad \tan\alpha = \frac{\text{opposite}}{\text{adjacent}}$$

> [!tip] SOH-CAH-TOA **S**in = **O**pposite / **H**ypotenuse **C**os = **A**djacent / **H**ypotenuse **T**an = **O**pposite / **A**djacent

---

## Relationship Between the Ratios

$$\tan\alpha = \frac{\sin\alpha}{\cos\alpha}$$

This follows directly from the definitions — divide opposite/hypotenuse by adjacent/hypotenuse and the hypotenuse cancels.

The three ratios are not independent — knowing any one angle determines all three. See [[Trig_Exact_Values]] for the standard values and [[Trig_Identities]] for deeper relationships.

---

## The Two Acute Angles Are Complementary

In a right triangle the two acute angles sum to 90°. If they are $\alpha$ and $\beta = 90° - \alpha$:

$$\sin\alpha = \cos(90° - \alpha) \qquad \cos\alpha = \sin(90° - \alpha)$$

What is the opposite for $\alpha$ is the adjacent for $\beta$, and vice versa.

> [!tip] Practical consequence $\sin 30° = \cos 60°$, $\sin 70° = \cos 20°$, and so on. If you ever see $\sin\alpha = \cos\beta$, it means $\alpha + \beta = 90°$.

---

## Range of Values for Acute Angles

For $0° < \alpha < 90°$:

- $\sin\alpha$ increases from 0 to 1
- $\cos\alpha$ decreases from 1 to 0
- $\tan\alpha$ increases from 0 to $\infty$

At 45°: $\sin 45° = \cos 45°$ — this is the one angle where sine and cosine are equal.

## Worked Examples

**Example 1.** Right triangle with hypotenuse 13, one leg 5. Find $\sin\alpha$, $\cos\alpha$, $\tan\alpha$ for the angle opposite the leg of length 5.

Other leg: $\sqrt{13^2 - 5^2} = \sqrt{144} = 12$

$$\sin\alpha = \frac{5}{13} \qquad \cos\alpha = \frac{12}{13} \qquad \tan\alpha = \frac{5}{12}$$

---

**Example 2.** $\sin\alpha = \frac{3}{5}$. Find $\cos\alpha$ and $\tan\alpha$.

Use Pythagoras: opposite = 3, hypotenuse = 5, adjacent = $\sqrt{25 - 9} = 4$.

$$\cos\alpha = \frac{4}{5} \qquad \tan\alpha = \frac{3}{4}$$

---

**Example 3.** $\tan\alpha = 2$. Find $\sin\alpha$ and $\cos\alpha$.

Opposite = 2, adjacent = 1, hypotenuse = $\sqrt{5}$.

$$\sin\alpha = \frac{2}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{2\sqrt{5}}{5} \qquad \cos\alpha = \frac{1}{\sqrt{5}} = \frac{\sqrt{5}}{5}$$

## Practice Problems

1. A right triangle has legs 8 and 15. Find all six values: $\sin$, $\cos$, $\tan$ for both acute angles.
2. $\cos\alpha = \frac{5}{13}$. Find $\sin\alpha$ and $\tan\alpha$.
3. $\tan\alpha = \frac{7}{24}$. Find $\sin\alpha$ and $\cos\alpha$.
4. Show that if $\sin\alpha = \frac{a}{c}$ then $\cos\alpha = \frac{\sqrt{c^2 - a^2}}{c}$.
5. $\sin\alpha = 0.6$. Without a calculator, find $\cos\alpha$ and $\tan\alpha$.
6. A right triangle has hypotenuse 10 and one acute angle 37°. Express all three sides in terms of $\sin 37°$ and $\cos 37°$.
