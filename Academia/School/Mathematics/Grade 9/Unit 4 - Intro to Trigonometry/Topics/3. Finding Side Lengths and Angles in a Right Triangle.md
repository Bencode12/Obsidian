## The Two Types of Problem

|Problem type|Given|Find|
|---|---|---|
|**Finding a side**|One side + one acute angle|Another side|
|**Finding an angle**|Two sides|An acute angle|

---

## Finding a Side — Method

1. Identify which angle you are working from
2. Label the known side as opposite, adjacent or hypotenuse relative to that angle
3. Label the unknown side the same way
4. Choose the ratio that connects the two labelled sides
5. Solve the equation

$$\text{unknown side} = \text{known side} \times \text{trig ratio}$$

or

$$\text{unknown side} = \frac{\text{known side}}{\text{trig ratio}}$$

> [!tip] Which ratio to use
> 
> |Known|Unknown|Use|
> |---|---|---|
> |Hypotenuse|Opposite|$\sin$|
> |Hypotenuse|Adjacent|$\cos$|
> |Adjacent|Opposite|$\tan$|
> |Opposite|Hypotenuse|$\sin$ (rearranged)|
> |Adjacent|Hypotenuse|$\cos$ (rearranged)|
> |Opposite|Adjacent|$\tan$ (rearranged)|

---

## Finding an Angle — Method

1. Label the two known sides relative to the unknown angle
2. Write the ratio
3. Apply the inverse trig function

$$\alpha = \sin^{-1}\left(\frac{\text{opp}}{\text{hyp}}\right) \qquad \alpha = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{\text{adj}}{\text{hyp}}\right) \qquad \alpha = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\text{opp}}{\text{adj}}\right)$$

> [!tip] $\sin^{-1}$ is not $\frac{1}{\sin}$ The notation $\sin^{-1}(x)$ means "the angle whose sine is $x$" — also written $\arcsin(x)$. It is the inverse function, not the reciprocal. On a calculator it is usually the 2nd function of the sin button.

---

## Special Cases — Exact Answers

If the trig ratio equals one of the values from [[Trig_Exact_Values]], you can give an exact angle without a calculator:

|Ratio|Angle|
|---|---|
|$\sin\alpha = \frac{1}{2}$|$\alpha = 30°$|
|$\cos\alpha = \frac{1}{2}$|$\alpha = 60°$|
|$\tan\alpha = 1$|$\alpha = 45°$|
|$\sin\alpha = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$|$\alpha = 45°$|
|$\cos\alpha = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$|$\alpha = 30°$|
|$\tan\alpha = \sqrt{3}$|$\alpha = 60°$|

---

## Pythagorean Theorem as a Check

After finding a side, always verify with Pythagoras:

$$a^2 + b^2 = c^2$$

If your answer does not satisfy this, there is an error in the calculation.

## Worked Examples

**Example 1 — Finding a side (hypotenuse known)**

Right triangle, hypotenuse = 10, angle $\alpha = 38°$. Find the opposite side.

$$\text{opp} = \text{hyp} \cdot \sin\alpha = 10 \cdot \sin 38° \approx 10 \cdot 0.6157 = 6.16$$

---

**Example 2 — Finding a side (leg known)**

Right triangle, adjacent leg = 7, angle $\alpha = 52°$. Find the opposite side.

$$\text{opp} = \text{adj} \cdot \tan\alpha = 7 \cdot \tan 52° \approx 7 \cdot 1.2799 = 8.96$$

---

**Example 3 — Finding the hypotenuse**

Right triangle, opposite side = 5, angle $\alpha = 30°$.

$$\text{hyp} = \frac{\text{opp}}{\sin\alpha} = \frac{5}{\sin 30°} = \frac{5}{0.5} = 10$$

---

**Example 4 — Finding an angle**

Right triangle, opposite = 4, adjacent = 9. Find $\alpha$.

$$\tan\alpha = \frac{4}{9} \implies \alpha = \tan^{-1}!\left(\frac{4}{9}\right) \approx 23.96°$$

---

**Example 5 — Exact answer**

Right triangle, legs 5 and $5\sqrt{3}$. Find the acute angles.

$$\tan\alpha = \frac{5}{5\sqrt{3}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} \implies \alpha = 30°$$

The other angle is $90° - 30° = 60°$.

---

## Full Triangle — Solving Completely

To fully solve a right triangle (find all missing sides and angles):

1. Find the third side using Pythagoras if two sides are known
2. Find one acute angle using an inverse trig function
3. Find the other acute angle by subtracting from 90°

The methods here feed directly into [[Trig_Broader_Applications]] (non-right triangle problems) and [[Trig_Real_Life]] (word problems).

2. Right triangle, one leg 9, adjacent angle 55°. Find the hypotenuse and the other leg.
3. Right triangle, legs 6 and 11. Find both acute angles.
4. Right triangle, hypotenuse 20, one leg $10\sqrt{3}$. Find the angles exactly (no calculator needed — use [[Trig_Exact_Values]]).
5. Right triangle, hypotenuse 13, one angle 67°. Find the opposite and adjacent sides.
6. Right triangle, one leg 8, the other leg $8\sqrt{3}$. Find the hypotenuse and both angles exactly.
7. $\cos\alpha = 0.28$ and the hypotenuse is 25. Find all sides and the other acute angle.
