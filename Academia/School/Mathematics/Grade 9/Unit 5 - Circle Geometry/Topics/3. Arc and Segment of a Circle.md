A **sector** (ispjova) is the "pizza slice" — bounded by two radii and an arc. A **segment** (nuopjova) is the region between a chord and its arc.

---

## Arc

An arc is a portion of the circle's circumference. Its length depends on the **central angle** $\alpha$ and the **radius** $r$.

$$\boxed{\ell = \frac{\alpha}{360°} \cdot 2\pi r}$$

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$\ell$|Arc length|
|$\alpha$|Central angle in degrees|
|$r$|Radius|

> [!tip] If the angle is given in radians: $\ell = \alpha \cdot r$ — much simpler. At this level degrees are standard, but know radians exist.

---

## Sector (Ispjova)

The sector is the full slice including the two radii.

$$\boxed{A_{\text{sector}} = \frac{\alpha}{360°} \cdot \pi r^2}$$

Think of it as a fraction of the full circle area $\pi r^2$.

**Perimeter of a sector** = arc length + two radii: $$P_{\text{sector}} = \ell + 2r = \frac{\alpha}{360°} \cdot 2\pi r + 2r$$

---

## Segment (Nuopjova)

The segment is the region between a chord and its arc — the sector **minus** the triangle formed by the two radii and the chord.

$$\boxed{A_{\text{segment}} = A_{\text{sector}} - A_{\triangle}}$$

The triangle has two sides equal to $r$ and an included angle of $\alpha$: $$A_{\triangle} = \frac{1}{2} r^2 \sin\alpha$$

So: $$A_{\text{segment}} = \frac{\alpha}{360°} \cdot \pi r^2 - \frac{1}{2} r^2 \sin\alpha$$

---

## Worked Examples

**Example 1.** Find the arc length for $r = 10$ cm, $\alpha = 72°$. $$\ell = \frac{72}{360} \cdot 2\pi \cdot 10 = \frac{1}{5} \cdot 20\pi = 4\pi \approx 12.57 \text{ cm}$$

**Example 2.** Find the sector area for $r = 6$ cm, $\alpha = 120°$. $$A = \frac{120}{360} \cdot \pi \cdot 36 = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 36\pi = 12\pi \approx 37.70 \text{ cm}^2$$

**Example 3.** Find the segment area for $r = 8$ cm, $\alpha = 90°$. $$A_{\text{sector}} = \frac{90}{360} \cdot \pi \cdot 64 = 16\pi$$ $$A_{\triangle} = \frac{1}{2} \cdot 64 \cdot \sin 90° = 32$$ $$A_{\text{segment}} = 16\pi - 32 \approx 50.27 - 32 = 18.27 \text{ cm}^2$$

---

## Summary Table

|Shape|Area|Perimeter|
|---|---|---|
|Full circle|$\pi r^2$|$2\pi r$|
|Sector|$\dfrac{\alpha}{360} \pi r^2$|$\dfrac{\alpha}{360} \cdot 2\pi r + 2r$|
|Segment|$\dfrac{\alpha}{360} \pi r^2 - \dfrac{1}{2}r^2\sin\alpha$|$\dfrac{\alpha}{360} \cdot 2\pi r + 2r\sin\dfrac{\alpha}{2}$|

> [!warning] Common mistake The segment perimeter uses the **chord length**, not $2r$. The chord length for a central angle $\alpha$ and radius $r$ is $2r\sin\frac{\alpha}{2}$.
