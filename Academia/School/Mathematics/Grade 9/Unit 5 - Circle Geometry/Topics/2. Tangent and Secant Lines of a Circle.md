A **tangent** is a line that touches a circle at exactly one point — the **point of tangency**. A **secant** is a line that crosses a circle at two points.

---

## Tangent Line Properties

> [!info] Tangent–Radius Theorem A tangent line is **perpendicular** to the radius drawn to the point of tangency. $$OA \perp t \quad \text{where } O \text{ is the centre, } A \text{ is the point of tangency}$$

This right angle is one of the most used facts in [[Proof_Problems]] — it creates a right triangle you can apply Pythagoras to.

---

> [!info] Equal Tangent Segments Two tangents drawn from the same external point to a circle are **equal in length**. $$PA = PB \quad \text{where } P \text{ is the external point}$$

---

## Secant Line Properties

A secant from an external point P crosses the circle at two points A and B.

**Power of a Point — two secants from the same external point:** $$PA \cdot PB = PC \cdot PD$$

**Power of a Point — one secant and one tangent from the same external point:** $$PT^2 = PA \cdot PB$$ where $T$ is the point of tangency, $A$ and $B$ are the two secant intersection points.

---

## Angles Formed by Tangents and Secants

|Situation|Angle formula|
|---|---|
|Tangent and chord at point of tangency|$\angle = \dfrac{1}{2} \cdot \text{arc}$|
|Two secants from external point|$\angle = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{far arc} - \text{near arc})$|
|Secant and tangent from external point|$\angle = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{far arc} - \text{near arc})$|
|Two tangents from external point|$\angle = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{major arc} - \text{minor arc})$|

## Worked Examples

**Example 1.** A tangent from point P touches the circle at A. The radius to A is 6 cm. The distance from P to the centre O is 10 cm. Find the length of the tangent PA.

Since $OA \perp PA$, triangle OAP is right-angled at A: $$PA = \sqrt{OP^2 - OA^2} = \sqrt{100 - 36} = \sqrt{64} = 8 \text{ cm}$$

**Example 2.** From external point P, two tangents touch the circle at A and B. $PA = 5$ cm. Find $PB$. $$PB = PA = 5 \text{ cm} \quad \text{(equal tangent segments)}$$

**Example 3.** A secant from P passes through A and B on the circle. $PA = 4$, $PB = 9$. A tangent from the same P touches at T. Find $PT$. $$PT^2 = PA \cdot PB = 4 \cdot 9 = 36 \Rightarrow PT = 6$$

---

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning]
> 
> - The tangent–radius right angle is **at the point of tangency**, not at the centre
> - Equal tangent segments apply only when both tangents come from the **same external point**
> - In the Power of a Point formula, use the full lengths from P to each intersection, not just the chord lengths

## Practice

1. A radius is 5 cm. The distance from an external point to the centre is 13 cm. Find the tangent length.
2. Two tangents from P are each 8 cm. Find the distance between the two points of tangency if the radius is 6 cm.
3. From P, a secant hits the circle at points 3 cm and 12 cm from P. A tangent from P touches at T. Find PT.
4. A tangent and a chord meet at the point of tangency. The chord subtends an arc of 140°. Find the angle between tangent and chord.
