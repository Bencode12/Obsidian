Clock problems are applied circle geometry. The clock face is a circle, the hands are radii, and the angle between them is a **central angle**. See [[Central_and_Inscribed_Angles]] for the general theory ‚Äî here we apply it to a specific, structured situation.

---

## Clock Setup

|Hand|Full rotation|Speed|
|---|---|---|
|**Hour hand**|360¬∞ in 12 hours|0.5¬∞ per minute|
|**Minute hand**|360¬∞ in 60 minutes|6¬∞ per minute|
|**Second hand**|360¬∞ in 60 seconds|6¬∞ per second|

> [!info] Key rates $$\text{Hour hand: } \frac{360¬∞}{12 \times 60} = 0.5¬∞ \text{ per minute}$$ $$\text{Minute hand: } \frac{360¬∞}{60} = 6¬∞ \text{ per minute}$$

The minute hand moves **12√ó faster** than the hour hand.

---

## Finding the Angle Between Hands

### Method

At time $h$ hours and $m$ minutes:

$$\theta_{\text{hour}} = 30h + 0.5m \quad \text{(degrees from 12)}$$ $$\theta_{\text{minute}} = 6m \quad \text{(degrees from 12)}$$ $$\angle = \theta_{\text{hour}} - \theta_{\text{minute}}$$

If the result is greater than 180¬∞, subtract from 360¬∞ to get the smaller angle.

---

## Worked Examples

**Example 1.** Find the angle between the hands at 3:40.

$$\theta_{\text{hour}} = 30(3) + 0.5(40) = 90 + 20 = 110¬∞$$ $$\theta_{\text{minute}} = 6(40) = 240¬∞$$ $$\angle = |110 - 240| = 130¬∞$$

The angle between the hands is **130¬∞**.

---

**Example 2.** Find the angle at 7:15.

$$\theta_{\text{hour}} = 30(7) + 0.5(15) = 210 + 7.5 = 217.5¬∞$$ $$\theta_{\text{minute}} = 6(15) = 90¬∞$$ $$\angle = |217.5 - 90| = 127.5¬∞$$

---

**Example 3.** At what time between 4:00 and 5:00 are the hands exactly together (angle = 0¬∞)?

$$30(4) + 0.5m = 6m$$ $$120 = 5.5m$$ $$m = \frac{120}{5.5} = \frac{240}{11} \approx 21.8 \text{ minutes}$$

The hands coincide at **4:21.8** (approximately 4:21 and 49 seconds).

---

**Example 4.** At what time between 2:00 and 3:00 are the hands at 90¬∞ to each other?

$$|30(2) + 0.5m - 6m| = 90$$ $$|60 - 5.5m| = 90$$

Case 1: $60 - 5.5m = 90 \Rightarrow m = -\frac{30}{5.5}$ ‚Äî negative, discard. Case 2: $60 - 5.5m = -90 \Rightarrow 5.5m = 150 \Rightarrow m = \frac{300}{11} \approx 27.3$

The hands are at 90¬∞ at approximately **2:27:16**.

## Arc Length on a Clock

If you need the arc length traced by a hand in a given time, use the arc length formula from [[Arc_and_Segment]]:

$$\ell = \frac{\alpha}{360¬∞} \cdot 2\pi r$$

where $\alpha$ is the angle swept and $r$ is the length of the hand.

**Example.** The minute hand is 8 cm long. How far does its tip travel between 12:00 and 12:20?

$$\alpha = 6¬∞ \times 20 = 120¬∞$$ $$\ell = \frac{120}{360} \cdot 2\pi(8) = \frac{1}{3} \cdot 16\pi = \frac{16\pi}{3} \approx 16.76 \text{ cm}$$

---

## How Many Times Do the Hands Overlap in 12 Hours?

The minute hand gains $5.5¬∞$ per minute on the hour hand. Starting aligned at 12:00, they next align after: $$t = \frac{360¬∞}{5.5¬∞/\text{min}} = \frac{720}{11} \approx 65.45 \text{ minutes}$$

In 12 hours = 720 minutes: $\dfrac{720}{720/11} = 11$ times.

The hands overlap **11 times** in 12 hours (not 12 ‚Äî they start and end at 12:00, which counts once).
