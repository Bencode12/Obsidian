A **central angle** has its vertex at the centre of the circle. An **inscribed angle** has its vertex on the circle itself, with both sides as chords.

---

## Key Theorem

> [!info] Inscribed Angle Theorem An inscribed angle is always **half** the central angle that subtends the same arc. $$\angle \text{inscribed} = \frac{1}{2} \angle \text{central}$$

This means if the central angle is 80°, the inscribed angle over the same arc is 40°.

---

## Consequences of the Theorem

**1. Angles subtended by the same arc are equal** If two inscribed angles subtend the same arc, they are equal — regardless of where on the circle the vertex sits.

**2. Angle in a semicircle is 90°** If the arc is a semicircle (central angle = 180°), then any inscribed angle over it equals 90°. This is Thales' theorem. $$\text{If } AB \text{ is a diameter} \Rightarrow \angle ACB = 90°$$

**3. Opposite angles of a cyclic quadrilateral sum to 180°** A quadrilateral inscribed in a circle has opposite angles that are supplementary. $$\alpha + \gamma = 180° \qquad \beta + \delta = 180°$$

---

## Formulas

|Situation|Formula|
|---|---|
|Central angle|$\angle AOB = \alpha$|
|Inscribed angle over same arc|$\angle ACB = \dfrac{\alpha}{2}$|
|Angle in semicircle|$\angle ACB = 90°$|
|Cyclic quadrilateral|$\alpha + \gamma = 180°$|

---

## Worked Examples

**Example 1.** A central angle is 124°. Find the inscribed angle over the same arc. $$\angle \text{inscribed} = \frac{124°}{2} = 62°$$

**Example 2.** An inscribed angle is 35°. Find the arc it subtends. $$\text{Arc} = 2 \times 35° = 70°$$

**Example 3.** Points A, B, C, D lie on a circle. $\angle DAB = 110°$. Find $\angle BCD$. $$\angle BCD = 180° - 110° = 70° \quad \text{(opposite angles in cyclic quadrilateral)}$$

---

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning]
> 
> - The inscribed angle theorem only applies when both angles subtend the **same arc** — check this first
> - The vertex of the inscribed angle must be **on** the circle, not inside or outside it
> - Do not confuse the inscribed angle with the central angle — they are not equal
