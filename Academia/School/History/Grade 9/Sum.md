## Overview

This note summarizes key concepts and criteria for Grade 9 History, focusing on topics such as the creation of nation-states, nationalism, unification movements, and imperialism.

---

## Criteria A: Knowledge and Understanding

### Key Concepts

-   **Sukilimas (Uprising)**: Understanding the causes, events, and consequences of significant uprisings.
-   **Unification of Germany and Italy**: Analyzing the processes, key figures, and impacts of these 19th-century unification movements.
-   **Terminology of Imperialism**: Defining and distinguishing various terms related to colonial expansion.

> [!example] Imperialism Terminology
> |Term|Definition|
> |---|---|
> |**Metropolis**|The colonizing power/mother country.|
> |**Colony**|A territory directly governed by a foreign power.|
> |**Protectorate**|A territory with its own government but under the control of an outside power.|
> |**Dominion**|A self-governing territory within the British Empire (e.g., Canada, Australia).|

-   **Creation of the Nation-State**: Exploring the historical development and characteristics of the modern nation-state.
-   **Nationalism**: Defining nationalism and its role as a driving force in historical events, including unification and conflicts.

---

## Criteria D: Application of Historical Skills

### Key Focus Areas

-   **Connection to Nation-State**: Establishing direct links between historical events and the development or characteristics of the nation-state.
-   **Spring of Nations (Revolutions of 1848)**: Identifying and describing the key features and commonalities of these widespread European revolutions.
-   **Causality of Imperialism**: Analyzing the causes and effects of imperialism on both colonizers and colonized regions.

> [!example] Causes and Effects of Imperialism
> |Causes|Effects|
> |---|---|
> |Economic (raw materials, markets)|Exploitation of resources|
> |Political (power, prestige)|Border conflicts, loss of sovereignty|
> |Social (social Darwinism, "civilizing mission")|Cultural disruption, resistance movements|
> |Technological (industrialization, weaponry)|Increased global interdependence (unequal)|
