## **Lesson 9.1.1 — Vector Spaces & Subspaces**

Linear algebra begins with the **abstract concept of a vector space**, which generalizes vectors beyond geometry to functions, sequences, matrices, and more.

---

## **9.1.1.1 Definition of a Vector Space**

A **vector space** (V) over a field (F) (usually (\mathbb{R}) or (\mathbb{C})) is a set with two operations:

1. **Vector addition**: (+\colon V \times V \to V)
    
2. **Scalar multiplication**: (\cdot \colon F \times V \to V)
    

satisfying the following **axioms**:

1. **Associativity of addition:** ((u+v)+w = u+(v+w))
    
2. **Commutativity of addition:** (u+v = v+u)
    
3. **Existence of additive identity:** (\exists 0 \in V) such that (v+0 = v)
    
4. **Existence of additive inverse:** (\forall v \in V, \exists -v \in V: v + (-v) = 0)
    
5. **Compatibility of scalar multiplication:** (a(bv) = (ab)v)
    
6. **Identity element of scalar multiplication:** (1v = v)
    
7. **Distributive over vector addition:** (a(u+v) = au + av)
    
8. **Distributive over scalar addition:** ((a+b)v = av + bv)
    

> Essentially, a vector space is any set where you can **add vectors and scale them by scalars** while obeying these rules.

---

## **9.1.1.2 Examples of Vector Spaces**

1. **Euclidean space** (\mathbb{R}^n)
    
    - Vectors: ( (x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n) )
        
    - Scalars: real numbers
        
2. **Polynomial space** (P_n)
    
    - Vectors: all polynomials of degree ≤ n
        
    - Scalars: real numbers
        
3. **Function space** (C([a,b]))
    
    - Vectors: continuous functions on interval ([a,b])
        
    - Scalars: real numbers
        
    - Addition: (f+g) pointwise
        
    - Scalar multiplication: ((\alpha f)(x) = \alpha f(x))
        
4. **Matrix space** (M_{m \times n}(\mathbb{R}))
    
    - Vectors: all (m \times n) matrices
        
    - Scalars: real numbers
        
    - Addition: matrix addition
        
    - Scalar multiplication: multiply every entry by scalar
        

---

## **9.1.1.3 Subspaces**

A **subspace** (W \subseteq V) is a subset that is itself a vector space under the same operations.

**Conditions to check:**

1. (0 \in W) (contains the zero vector)
    
2. Closed under addition: (u,v \in W \implies u+v \in W)
    
3. Closed under scalar multiplication: (v \in W, a \in F \implies av \in W)
    

**Example:**

- (V = \mathbb{R}^3), (W = {(x,y,0) \mid x,y \in \mathbb{R}}) is a subspace (the xy-plane in 3D)

C. Identify subspaces of (\mathbb{R}^3) formed by lines through the origin.

D. Show that the set of all (2 \times 2) upper triangular matrices is a subspace of (M_{2 \times 2}(\mathbb{R})).
