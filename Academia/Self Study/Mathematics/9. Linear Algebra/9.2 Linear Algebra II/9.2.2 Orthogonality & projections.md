## **Lesson 9.2.2 — Orthogonality & Projections**

This lesson extends **inner product spaces** into practical tools for constructing bases, decomposing vectors, and simplifying linear transformations.

---

## **9.2.2.1 Orthogonal Sets**

A set ({v_1, \dots, v_n}) in an inner product space (V) is **orthogonal** if:

[  
\langle v_i, v_j \rangle = 0 \quad \text{for } i \neq j  
]

- If all vectors are also **unit vectors** ((|v_i| = 1)), the set is **orthonormal**.
    
- Orthogonal sets are **automatically linearly independent**.
    

---

## **9.2.2.2 Gram-Schmidt Process**

The **Gram-Schmidt process** converts any linearly independent set ({u_1, \dots, u_n}) into an **orthonormal set** ({v_1, \dots, v_n}).

### **Procedure**

1. Set: (v_1 = \frac{u_1}{|u_1|})
    
2. For (k = 2 \dots n):
    

[  
w_k = u_k - \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \text{proj}_{v_j}(u_k) = u_k - \sum_{j=1}^{k-1} \langle u_k, v_j \rangle v_j  
]

[  
v_k = \frac{w_k}{|w_k|}  
]

- Each (v_k) is orthonormal to previous vectors
    
- Span of ({v_1, \dots, v_k}) = span of ({u_1, \dots, u_k})
    

**Example (2D)**

[  
u_1 = (1,1), \quad u_2 = (1,0)  
]

1. (v_1 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(1,1))
    
2. (w_2 = (1,0) - \langle (1,0), v_1\rangle v_1 = (1,0) - \frac{1}{2}(1,1) = (\frac{1}{2}, -\frac{1}{2}))
    
3. (v_2 = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}(1,-1))
    

---

## **9.2.2.3 Orthogonal Projection**

For a subspace (W \subseteq V) with orthonormal basis ({v_1, \dots, v_k}), the **projection of (x) onto (W)** is:

[  
\text{proj}_W(x) = \sum_{i=1}^{k} \langle x, v_i \rangle v_i  
]

- Minimizes distance:  
    (|x - \text{proj}_W(x)| = \min_{w \in W} |x - w|)
    
- Key in **least squares** and **Fourier series**
    

---

### **9.2.2.4 Properties of Projections**

1. (\text{proj}_W(x) \in W)
    
2. (x - \text{proj}_W(x) \perp W)
    
3. Linearity: (\text{proj}_W(\alpha x + \beta y) = \alpha \text{proj}_W(x) + \beta \text{proj}_W(y))
