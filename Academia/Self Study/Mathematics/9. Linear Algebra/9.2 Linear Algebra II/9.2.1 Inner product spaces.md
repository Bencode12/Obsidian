## **Lesson 9.2.1 — Inner Product Spaces (Rigorous)**

An **inner product space** is a vector space equipped with a structure that allows us to measure **angles, lengths, and orthogonality**.

---

### **Definition**

Let (V) be a vector space over (\mathbb{R}) or (\mathbb{C}). An **inner product** is a function:

[  
\langle \cdot, \cdot \rangle: V \times V \to F  
]

satisfying the following properties for all (u,v,w \in V) and scalars (\alpha):

1. **Conjugate Symmetry**  
    [  
    \langle u, v \rangle = \overline{\langle v, u \rangle}  
    ]
    

- For real spaces, reduces to (\langle u,v\rangle = \langle v,u\rangle)
    

2. **Linearity in the first argument**  
    [  
    \langle \alpha u + w, v \rangle = \alpha \langle u, v \rangle + \langle w, v \rangle  
    ]
    
3. **Positive-Definiteness**  
    [  
    \langle v, v \rangle \ge 0, \quad \langle v, v \rangle = 0 \iff v = 0  
    ]
    

> These axioms generalize the familiar **dot product** in (\mathbb{R}^n).

---

### **Derived Concepts**

1. **Norm (Length)**  
    [  
    |v| = \sqrt{\langle v, v \rangle}  
    ]
    
2. **Distance**  
    [  
    d(u, v) = |u - v|  
    ]
    
3. **Orthogonality**  
    Vectors (u) and (v) are **orthogonal** if (\langle u, v \rangle = 0)
    
4. **Orthonormal Sets**  
    A set ({v_1, \dots, v_n}) is **orthonormal** if:  
    [  
    \langle v_i, v_j \rangle = \delta_{ij} \quad (\text{Kronecker delta})  
    ]
    

- Orthonormal vectors have unit length and are mutually perpendicular.
    

---

### **Key Theorems**

1. **Cauchy–Schwarz Inequality**  
    [  
    |\langle u, v \rangle| \le |u| |v|  
    ]  
    Equality iff (u) and (v) are linearly dependent.
    
2. **Triangle Inequality**  
    [  
    |u + v| \le |u| + |v|  
    ]
    
3. **Pythagorean Theorem**  
    If (u \perp v), then  
    [  
    |u + v|^2 = |u|^2 + |v|^2  
    ]
    

---

### **Projection**

Projection of (x) onto (u) (nonzero):

[  
\text{proj}_u(x) = \frac{\langle x, u \rangle}{\langle u, u \rangle} u  
]

- Used in **least squares** and **Fourier decomposition**
    

---

### **Examples of Inner Product Spaces**

1. **Euclidean space (\mathbb{R}^n)**  
    (\langle x, y \rangle = x_1 y_1 + \dots + x_n y_n)
    
2. **Complex space (\mathbb{C}^n)**  
    (\langle x, y \rangle = \sum_{i=1}^n x_i \overline{y_i})
    
3. **Function space (C([a,b]))**  
    (\langle f, g \rangle = \int_a^b f(x) \overline{g(x)} dx)
    
4. **Polynomial space (P_n)**  
    (\langle p, q \rangle = \sum_{i=0}^n p(i) q(i)) (or via integral)
