## Consistency

### Definition

An estimator $\hat{\theta}_n$ is **consistent** for $\theta$ if:

$$\hat{\theta}_n \xrightarrow{P} \theta \quad \text{as } n \to \infty$$

Equivalently: $\forall \epsilon > 0$, $\lim_{n \to \infty} P(|\hat{\theta}_n - \theta| > \epsilon) = 0$

The estimator converges in probability to the true parameter!

### Sufficient Conditions

If $\hat{\theta}_n$ is asymptotically unbiased and $\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}_n) \to 0$:

$$\text{Bias}(\hat{\theta}_n) \to 0 \quad \text{and} \quad \text{Var}(\hat{\theta}_n) \to 0 \implies \text{Consistency}$$

### Examples

|Estimator|Consistent?|Reason|
|---|---|---|
|Sample mean $\bar{X}$|Yes|WLLN|
|Sample variance $S^2$|Yes|Unbiased + variance → 0|
|Sample median|Yes|Under regularity conditions|
|First observation $X_1$|No|Doesn't improve with $n$|

## Efficiency

### Cramér-Rao Lower Bound (CRLB)

For an unbiased estimator $\hat{\theta}$:

$$\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}) \geq \frac{1}{I_n(\theta)}$$

where $I_n(\theta)$ is the **Fisher information**.

### Fisher Information

For i.i.d. data with likelihood $L(\theta)$:

$$I_n(\theta) = -E\left[\frac{\partial^2}{\partial\theta^2}\log L(\theta)\right] = nI_1(\theta)$$

Measures how much information the data contains about $\theta$!

### Efficient Estimators

An estimator is **efficient** if it achieves the CRLB:

$$\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}) = \frac{1}{I_n(\theta)}$$

This is the minimum possible variance for unbiased estimators!

## Relative Efficiency

### Definition

For two unbiased estimators $\hat{\theta}_1$ and $\hat{\theta}_2$:

$$\text{Efficiency} = \frac{\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}_2)}{\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}_1)}$$

- Efficiency > 1: $\hat{\theta}_1$ is more efficient
- Efficiency < 1: $\hat{\theta}_2$ is more efficient

### Asymptotic Relative Efficiency (ARE)

Compare estimators as $n \to \infty$:

$$\text{ARE}(\hat{\theta}_1, \hat{\theta}_2) = \lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}_2)}{\text{Var}(\hat{\theta}_1)}$$

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Normal Mean

For $X_i \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$ with known $\sigma^2$:

- Fisher information: $I_n(\mu) = \frac{n}{\sigma^2}$
- CRLB: $\text{Var}(\hat{\mu}) \geq \frac{\sigma^2}{n}$
- Sample mean: $\text{Var}(\bar{X}) = \frac{\sigma^2}{n}$ ✅

**Sample mean is efficient!**

### Example 2: Sample Median vs Mean

For normal distribution:
- $\text{Var}(\bar{X}) = \frac{\sigma^2}{n}$
- $\text{Var}(\text{median}) \approx \frac{\pi\sigma^2}{2n}$

$$\text{ARE}(\text{median}, \text{mean}) = \frac{2}{\pi} \approx 0.637$$

Mean is about 57% more efficient than median for normal data!

But for heavy-tailed distributions, median can be better!

```desmos-graph
y = normaldist(0, 1)
y = cauchydist(0, 1)
x = [-5, 5]
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Cauchy distribution has heavier tails—sample mean is unstable, making median preferable despite lower efficiency for normal data.

## Sufficiency

### Definition

A statistic $T(X)$ is **sufficient** for $\theta$ if the conditional distribution of $X$ given $T(X)$ does not depend on $\theta$.

Intuitively: $T(X)$ contains all information about $\theta$ in the data!

### Factorization Theorem

$T(X)$ is sufficient iff the likelihood factors as:

$$L(\theta; x) = g(T(x), \theta) \cdot h(x)$$

### Examples

|Distribution|Sufficient Statistic|
|---|---|
|$N(\mu, \sigma^2)$, $\sigma^2$ known|$\sum X_i$|
|$N(\mu, \sigma^2)$, both unknown|$(\sum X_i, \sum X_i^2)$|
|Binomial$(n, p)$|$\sum X_i$|
|Poisson$(\lambda)$|$\sum X_i$|

## Complete Statistics

### Definition

A statistic $T$ is **complete** if:

$$E_\theta[g(T)] = 0 \text{ for all } \theta \implies P_\theta(g(T) = 0) = 1$$

No non-trivial function of $T$ has expectation zero!

### Lehmann-Scheffé Theorem

If $T$ is complete and sufficient, and $\hat{\theta}$ is an unbiased estimator based on $T$, then $\hat{\theta}$ is the **unique minimum variance unbiased estimator (UMVUE)**!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Consistency with Unbiasedness
> An estimator can be biased but consistent (bias vanishes as $n \to \infty$). Unbiasedness is a finite-sample property!

> [!warning] Assuming MLE is Always Efficient
> MLE is asymptotically efficient under regularity conditions, but may not achieve CRLB for finite samples.

> [!warning] Ignoring Model Assumptions
> CRLB and efficiency depend on the assumed distribution. Wrong model → wrong conclusions!

## Practice Problems

Test consistency:

- Show that sample mean is consistent for population mean
- Prove that sample variance $S^2$ is consistent for $\sigma^2$
- Is the maximum of a sample consistent for the upper bound of Uniform[0,$\theta$]?

Compute efficiency:

- Find Fisher information for Bernoulli($p$) parameter
- Calculate CRLB for estimating mean of exponential distribution
- Compare efficiency of sample mean vs trimmed mean

Apply sufficiency:

- Use factorization theorem to find sufficient statistic for Poisson($\lambda$)
- Show that $(\bar{X}, S^2)$ is sufficient for normal parameters
- Find minimal sufficient statistic for uniform distribution

Conceptual:

- Why is consistency more important than unbiasedness?
- Explain the trade-off between robustness and efficiency
- What makes a statistic "sufficient"?

- Show that exponential family distributions have complete sufficient statistics
