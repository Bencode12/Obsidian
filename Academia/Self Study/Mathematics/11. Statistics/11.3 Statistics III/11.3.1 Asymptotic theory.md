## Definition

**Asymptotic theory** studies the behavior of statistical estimators and test statistics as the sample size $n \to \infty$.

This provides theoretical justification for many statistical methods used in practice!

---

## Convergence Concepts

### Convergence in Probability

A sequence of random variables $X_n$ **converges in probability** to $X$ if:

$$\forall \epsilon > 0, \lim_{n \to \infty} P(|X_n - X| > \epsilon) = 0$$

Notation: $X_n \xrightarrow{P} X$

### Almost Sure Convergence

$X_n$ **converges almost surely** to $X$ if:

$$P\left(\lim_{n \to \infty} X_n = X\right) = 1$$

Notation: $X_n \xrightarrow{a.s.} X$

Almost sure $\implies$ in probability (but not conversely!)

### Convergence in Distribution

$X_n$ **converges in distribution** to $X$ if:

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} F_{X_n}(x) = F_X(x)$$

at all continuity points of $F_X$.

Notation: $X_n \xrightarrow{d} X$

This is the weakest form of convergence!

```desmos-graph
y = normaldist(0, 1/sqrt(n))
n = 1
n = 5
n = 10
n = 50
x = [-4, 4]
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> As $n$ increases, the distribution becomes more concentrated around the mean—illustrating convergence in probability.

## Weak Law of Large Numbers (WLLN)

### Statement

If $X_1, X_2, ...$ are i.i.d. with $E[X_i] = \mu$ and $\text{Var}(X_i) = \sigma^2 < \infty$, then:

$$\bar{X}_n \xrightarrow{P} \mu$$

The sample mean converges in probability to the population mean!

### Proof Sketch

Use Chebyshev's inequality:
$$P(|\bar{X}_n - \mu| \geq \epsilon) \leq \frac{\text{Var}(\bar{X}_n)}{\epsilon^2} = \frac{\sigma^2}{n\epsilon^2} \to 0$$

---

## Strong Law of Large Numbers (SLLN)

### Statement

Under the same conditions:

$$\bar{X}_n \xrightarrow{a.s.} \mu$$

Stronger conclusion: convergence happens with probability 1!

### Implications

- WLLN: For large $n$, $\bar{X}_n$ is "probably close" to $\mu$
- SLLN: With probability 1, $\bar{X}_n$ eventually stays close to $\mu$

## Central Limit Theorem (CLT)

### Statement

If $X_1, X_2, ...$ are i.i.d. with mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$:

$$\frac{\bar{X}_n - \mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \xrightarrow{d} N(0,1)$$

Equivalently:
$$\sqrt{n}(\bar{X}_n - \mu) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, \sigma^2)$$

### Why It Matters

- Explains why normal distributions appear everywhere
- Justifies confidence intervals and hypothesis tests
- Foundation of statistical inference

```desmos-graph
y = normaldist(0, 1)
y = normaldist(0, 1/sqrt(5))
y = normaldist(0, 1/sqrt(20))
x = [-4, 4]
```

## Delta Method

### Statement

If $\sqrt{n}(X_n - \theta) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, \sigma^2)$ and $g$ is differentiable at $\theta$:

$$\sqrt{n}(g(X_n) - g(\theta)) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, [g'(\theta)]^2\sigma^2)$$

### Application

Find asymptotic distribution of transformed estimators!

**Example**: If $\bar{X}_n$ estimates $\mu$, what about $\bar{X}_n^2$?

Let $g(x) = x^2$, then $g'(x) = 2x$:
$$\sqrt{n}(\bar{X}_n^2 - \mu^2) \xrightarrow{d} N(0, 4\mu^2\sigma^2)$$

## Slutsky's Theorem

### Statement

If $X_n \xrightarrow{d} X$ and $Y_n \xrightarrow{P} c$ (constant), then:

- $X_n + Y_n \xrightarrow{d} X + c$
- $X_n Y_n \xrightarrow{d} cX$
- $X_n / Y_n \xrightarrow{d} X/c$ (if $c \neq 0$)

Allows combining different types of convergence!

---

## Asymptotic Normality of MLE

### Key Result

Under regularity conditions, the MLE $\hat{\theta}_{MLE}$ satisfies:

$$\sqrt{n}(\hat{\theta}_{MLE} - \theta) \xrightarrow{d} N\left(0, \frac{1}{I(\theta)}\right)$$

where $I(\theta)$ is the Fisher information.

This justifies using normal approximations for MLE-based inference!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Convergence Types
> Convergence in distribution ≠ convergence in probability! CLT gives distributional convergence, not that $\bar{X}_n \to \mu$.

> [!warning] Assuming Finite Sample Results
> Asymptotic results are limits! They may not hold well for small $n$.

> [!warning] Ignoring Regularity Conditions
> CLT requires finite variance. Heavy-tailed distributions (Cauchy) don't satisfy CLT!
