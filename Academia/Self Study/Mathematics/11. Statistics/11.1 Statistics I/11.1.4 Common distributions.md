## Common Discrete Distributions

### Bernoulli Distribution

A single trial with two outcomes (success/failure).

**PMF**: $P(X = x) = p^x(1-p)^{1-x}$ for $x \in \{0, 1\}$

- Mean: $E[X] = p$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(X) = p(1-p)$

### Binomial Distribution

Number of successes in $n$ independent Bernoulli trials.

**PMF**: $P(X = k) = \binom{n}{k}p^k(1-p)^{n-k}$

- Mean: $E[X] = np$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(X) = np(1-p)$

```desmos-graph
y = binomialdist(10, 0.3, x)
y = binomialdist(10, 0.5, x)
y = binomialdist(10, 0.7, x)
x = [0, 10]
```

### Poisson Distribution

Models rare events occurring at constant rate $\lambda$.

**PMF**: $P(X = k) = \frac{e^{-\lambda}\lambda^k}{k!}$

- Mean: $E[X] = \lambda$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(X) = \lambda$

**Key property**: Sum of independent Poissons is Poisson!

## Common Continuous Distributions

### Normal (Gaussian) Distribution

The most important distribution in statistics!

**PDF**: $f(x) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi\sigma^2}}e^{-\frac{(x-\mu)^2}{2\sigma^2}}$

Notation: $X \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)$

- Mean: $E[X] = \mu$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(X) = \sigma^2$

**Standard normal**: $Z \sim N(0, 1)$

Any normal can be standardized: $Z = \frac{X-\mu}{\sigma}$

```desmos-graph
y = normaldist(0, 1)
y = normaldist(0, 0.5)
y = normaldist(2, 1)
x = [-4, 6]
```

### Exponential Distribution

Models time between events in a Poisson process.

**PDF**: $f(x) = \lambda e^{-\lambda x}$ for $x \geq 0$

- Mean: $E[X] = \frac{1}{\lambda}$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(X) = \frac{1}{\lambda^2}$

**Memoryless property**: $P(X > s+t | X > s) = P(X > t)$

### Gamma Distribution

Generalizes exponential distribution.

**PDF**: $f(x) = \frac{\beta^\alpha}{\Gamma(\alpha)}x^{\alpha-1}e^{-\beta x}$ for $x > 0$

Parameters: shape $\alpha$, rate $\beta$

- Mean: $E[X] = \frac{\alpha}{\beta}$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(X) = \frac{\alpha}{\beta^2}$

Special cases:
- $\alpha = 1$: Exponential($\beta$)
- $\alpha = n/2, \beta = 1/2$: Chi-squared($n$)

## Sampling Distributions

### Chi-Squared Distribution

If $Z_1, ..., Z_n \sim N(0,1)$ independently:

$$\chi^2_n = \sum_{i=1}^n Z_i^2 \sim \chi^2(n)$$

- Mean: $E[\chi^2_n] = n$
- Variance: $\text{Var}(\chi^2_n) = 2n$

Used in variance estimation and goodness-of-fit tests!

### Student's t-Distribution

If $Z \sim N(0,1)$ and $V \sim \chi^2_n$ independently:

$$T = \frac{Z}{\sqrt{V/n}} \sim t_n$$

- Symmetric like normal but heavier tails
- As $n \to \infty$, $t_n \to N(0,1)$
- Used when $\sigma$ is unknown

```desmos-graph
y = normaldist(0, 1)
y = studenttdist(3)
y = studenttdist(10)
x = [-4, 4]
```

### F-Distribution

If $U \sim \chi^2_m$ and $V \sim \chi^2_n$ independently:

$$F = \frac{U/m}{V/n} \sim F_{m,n}$$

Used in ANOVA and comparing variances!

## Key Relationships

|Distribution|Relationship|
|---|---|
|Binomial → Normal|For large $n$, $B(n,p) \approx N(np, np(1-p))$|
|Binomial → Poisson|For large $n$, small $p$, $B(n,p) \approx \text{Poisson}(np)$|
|Sum of Normals|Normal: $N(\mu_1,\sigma_1^2) + N(\mu_2,\sigma_2^2) = N(\mu_1+\mu_2, \sigma_1^2+\sigma_2^2)$|
|Chi-squared → Normal|For large $n$, $\chi^2_n \approx N(n, 2n)$|

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Parameters
> Don't mix up rate ($\lambda$) and scale ($1/\lambda$) in exponential distribution! Check your textbook's convention.

> [!warning] Using Normal Approximation Incorrectly
> Binomial → Normal requires $np \geq 5$ AND $n(1-p) \geq 5$. Use continuity correction!

> [!warning] Forgetting Degrees of Freedom
> t-distribution and chi-squared depend on degrees of freedom. Wrong df → wrong inference!
