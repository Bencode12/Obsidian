## **Lesson 11.1.3 — Confidence Intervals**

A **confidence interval (CI)** gives a **range of plausible values** for an unknown parameter instead of a single estimate. It quantifies **uncertainty** in estimation.

---

## **11.1.3.1 Core Idea**

Instead of estimating (\theta) with a point estimate (\hat{\theta}), we construct an interval:

[  
[\text{Lower bound}, \text{Upper bound}]  
]

such that:

[  
P(\theta \in \text{interval}) = 1 - \alpha  
]

- (1 - \alpha) = **confidence level** (e.g., 95%)
    
- (\alpha) = significance level (e.g., 0.05)
    

---

## **11.1.3.2 Interpretation (Important)**

A **95% confidence interval** means:

> If we repeated the experiment many times, **95% of constructed intervals would contain the true parameter**.

❗ Not: “There is 95% probability the parameter is inside this specific interval” (parameter is fixed, not random).

---

## **11.1.3.3 General Form**

Most confidence intervals follow:

[  
\text{Estimator} \pm (\text{critical value}) \times (\text{standard error})  
]

---

## **11.1.3.4 Confidence Interval for Mean (Normal, σ Known)**

Let (X_1, \dots, X_n \sim N(\mu, \sigma^2)), with known (\sigma).

[  
\bar{X} \sim N\Big(\mu, \frac{\sigma^2}{n}\Big)  
]

Standardize:

[  
Z = \frac{\bar{X} - \mu}{\sigma/\sqrt{n}} \sim N(0,1)  
]

Confidence interval:

[  
\bar{X} \pm z_{\alpha/2} \cdot \frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}  
]

- (z_{\alpha/2}): critical value from standard normal
    

---

## **11.1.3.5 Confidence Interval for Mean (σ Unknown)**

Use **t-distribution**:

[  
\bar{X} \pm t_{\alpha/2,, n-1} \cdot \frac{S}{\sqrt{n}}  
]

- (t_{\alpha/2, n-1}): critical value with (n-1) degrees of freedom
    
- Used when population variance is unknown (most real cases)
    

---

## **11.1.3.6 Confidence Interval for Proportion**

Let (X \sim \text{Bin}(n,p)), estimator:

[  
\hat{p} = \frac{X}{n}  
]

Approximate (large (n)):

[  
\hat{p} \pm z_{\alpha/2} \sqrt{\frac{\hat{p}(1-\hat{p})}{n}}  
]

---

## **11.1.3.7 Effect of Sample Size**

- Larger (n) → smaller standard error → **narrower interval**
    
- Smaller (n) → wider interval
    

[  
\text{Width} \propto \frac{1}{\sqrt{n}}  
]

---

## **11.1.3.8 Effect of Confidence Level**

- Higher confidence (99%) → wider interval
    
- Lower confidence (90%) → narrower interval
    

Trade-off:

- More confidence = less precision
    
- Less confidence = more precision
    

---

## **11.1.3.9 Examples**

### Example 1 — Known σ

- (\bar{X} = 100), (\sigma = 10), (n = 25), 95% CI
    
- (z_{0.025} \approx 1.96)
    

[  
100 \pm 1.96 \cdot \frac{10}{5} = 100 \pm 3.92  
]

CI: ([96.08, 103.92])

---

### Example 2 — Unknown σ

- Same data but use (t)-distribution
    
- (t_{0.025,24} \approx 2.064)
    

[  
100 \pm 2.064 \cdot \frac{S}{5}  
]

(Slightly wider interval)
