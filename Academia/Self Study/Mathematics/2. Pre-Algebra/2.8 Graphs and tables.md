## Tables

A table organises data in **rows and columns** for easy reading.

|Day|Sales (units)|
|---|---|
|Mon|10|
|Tue|15|
|Wed|12|

- **Rows** → individual items or observations
- **Columns** → variables or measurements

## Types of Graphs

|Graph type|Best used for|
|---|---|
|**Bar graph**|Comparing quantities across categories|
|**Line graph**|Showing trends over time|
|**Pie chart**|Showing parts of a whole (proportions)|

## The Coordinate Plane

|Axis|Direction|
|---|---|
|$x$-axis|Horizontal|
|$y$-axis|Vertical|

Every point is written as an ordered pair $(x,\ y)$.

$$( 3,\ 5 ) \quad \longrightarrow \quad \text{move } 3 \text{ right, then } 5 \text{ up}$$

---

## Plotting a Point — Steps

1. Read the $x$-coordinate → move right (positive) or left (negative)
2. Read the $y$-coordinate → move up (positive) or down (negative)
3. Mark the point

---

## Reading Graphs

1. **Identify the axes** — what does each axis measure? What are the units?
2. **Check the scale** — what does each grid division represent?
3. **Read values** accurately at each point
4. **Interpret the trend**:

|Pattern|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Values increasing left to right|The quantity is **growing**|
|Values decreasing left to right|The quantity is **falling**|
|Values flat|The quantity is **constant**|

> [!tip] Always check the scale before reading a value A graph with divisions of 5 looks the same as one with divisions of 100 — the scale label is the only difference.
