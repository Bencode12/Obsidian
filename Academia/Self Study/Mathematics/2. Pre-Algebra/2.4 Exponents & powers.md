## What is an Exponent?

An exponent shows how many times a **base** is multiplied by itself.

$$a^n$$

|Part|Name|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|$a$|Base|The number being multiplied|
|$n$|Exponent / Power|How many times to multiply|

$$3^4 = 3 \times 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 81$$

$2^5$ is read as **"2 to the power of 5"** → $2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 \times 2 = 32$

---

## Laws of Exponents

|Law|Rule|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Zero power**|$a^0 = 1 \quad (a \neq 0)$|$5^0 = 1$|
|**Power of 1**|$a^1 = a$|$7^1 = 7$|
|**Multiply — same base**|$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$|$2^2 \times 2^3 = 2^5 = 32$|
|**Divide — same base**|$a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}$|$5^4 \div 5^2 = 5^2 = 25$|
|**Power of a power**|$(a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}$|$(3^2)^3 = 3^6 = 729$|
|**Negative exponent**|$a^{-n} = \dfrac{1}{a^n}$|$2^{-3} = \dfrac{1}{8}$|

> [!tip] Negative exponent means reciprocal $a^{-n}$ does not make the number negative — it flips it: $a^{-n} = \dfrac{1}{a^n}$.
