### **2.4.1 What is an exponent?**

An **exponent** shows how many times a number (the **base**) is multiplied by itself.

Form:

ana^nan

- $a$ → base
- $n$ → exponent / power

Example:

- 34=3×3×3×3=813^4 = 3 × 3 × 3 × 3 = 8134=3×3×3×3=81

### **2.4.2 Reading exponents**

- 252^525 → “2 to the power of 5” or “2 raised to 5”
- Means: 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 = 32

### **2.4.3 Special rules**

1. **Any number to the power 0**:

a0=1(a≠0)a^0 = 1 \quad (a \neq 0)a0=1(a=0)

Example: 5⁰ = 1

2. **Any number to the power 1**:

a1=aa^1 = aa1=a

3. **Multiplying same base**:

am×an=am+na^m × a^n = a^{m+n} am×an=am+n

Example: 2² × 2³ = 2⁵ = 32

4. **Dividing same base**:

am÷an=am−na^m ÷ a^n = a^{m-n} am÷an=am−n

Example: 5⁴ ÷ 5² = 5² = 25

5. **Power of a power**:

(am)n=am×n(a^m)^n = a^{m×n} (am)n=am×n

Example: (3²)³ = 3⁶ = 729

6. **Negative exponent**:

a−n=1ana^{-n} = $\frac{1}{a^n}$ a−n=an1

Example: 2⁻³ = 1 / 2³ = 1/8
