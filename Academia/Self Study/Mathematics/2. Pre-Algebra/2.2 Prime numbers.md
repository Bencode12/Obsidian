### **2.2.1 What is a prime number?**

A **prime number** is a number greater than 1 that has **exactly two factors**: 1 and itself.

Examples:

- 2, 3, 5, 7, 11, 13, 17

**Note:** 1 is **not prime** because it has only one factor.

### **2.2.2 What is a composite number?**

A **composite number** has **more than two factors**.

Examples:

- 4 (factors: 1, 2, 4)
- 6 (factors: 1, 2, 3, 6)
- 12 (factors: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12)

### **2.2.3 Why 2 is special**

- 2 is the **only even prime number**.
- All other even numbers are composite (divisible by 2).

### **2.2.4 Prime factorization**

Every number can be broken into a product of prime numbers.

Example:

- 30 = 2 × 3 × 5

This is called the **unique prime factorization**.
