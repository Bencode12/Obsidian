## Primes and Composite Numbers

Related: [[Factors_and_Multiples]] · [[Division]]

---

## Prime Numbers

A prime number is a number **greater than 1** with exactly **two factors**: $1$ and itself.

$$2,\ 3,\ 5,\ 7,\ 11,\ 13,\ 17,\ 19,\ 23,\ \ldots$$

> [!warning] 1 is not prime $1$ has only one factor (itself), so it does not meet the definition. Prime numbers must have **exactly two** distinct factors.

---

## Composite Numbers

A composite number has **more than two factors**.

|Number|Factors|
|---|---|
|$4$|$1, 2, 4$|
|$6$|$1, 2, 3, 6$|
|$12$|$1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12$|

---

## Prime vs Composite — Summary

|Type|Factors|Examples|
|---|---|---|
|**Prime**|Exactly $2$|$2, 3, 5, 7, 11$|
|**Composite**|More than $2$|$4, 6, 8, 9, 12$|
|**Neither**|Exactly $1$|$1$|

---

## Why 2 is Special

$2$ is the **only even prime number**. Every other even number is divisible by $2$, giving it at least three factors — making it composite.

---

## Prime Factorisation

Every integer greater than $1$ can be broken into a **unique** product of prime numbers — this is the **Fundamental Theorem of Arithmetic**.

$$30 = 2 \times 3 \times 5$$

$$72 = 2^3 \times 3^2$$

Factor tree method:

$$30 \to 2 \times 15 \to 2 \times 3 \times 5$$
