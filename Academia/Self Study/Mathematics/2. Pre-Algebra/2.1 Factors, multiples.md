### **2.1.1 What is a factor?**

A **factor** of a number is an integer that divides the number **exactly**, leaving no remainder.

Example:

- Factors of 12: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12
- Why? Because 12 ÷ 1 = 12, 12 ÷ 2 = 6, 12 ÷ 3 = 4, etc.

### **2.1.2 What is a multiple?**

A **multiple** of a number is the result of multiplying that number by an integer.

Example:

- Multiples of 4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, …
- Multiples go on infinitely.

### **2.1.3 Relationship between factors and multiples**

- Factors are numbers that **divide** the given number.
- Multiples are numbers that the given number **can divide**.

Example:

- 3 is a factor of 12
- 12 is a multiple of 3

### **2.1.4 Prime factorization**

Breaking a number into prime factors.

Example:

- 12 = 2 × 2 × 3 = 22×32^2 × 322×3
