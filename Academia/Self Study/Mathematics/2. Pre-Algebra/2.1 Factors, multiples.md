## Factors and Multiples

Related: [[Division]] · [[Multiplication]] · [[Primes_and_Divisibility]]

---

## Factors

A factor of a number is an integer that **divides it exactly** — no remainder.

$$\text{Factors of } 12: \quad 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12$$

$$12 \div 1 = 12 \quad 12 \div 2 = 6 \quad 12 \div 3 = 4 \quad 12 \div 4 = 3 \quad 12 \div 6 = 2 \quad 12 \div 12 = 1$$

---

## Multiples

A multiple of a number is the result of multiplying it by any integer. Multiples go on **infinitely**.

$$\text{Multiples of } 4: \quad 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, \ldots$$

---

## Relationship Between Factors and Multiples

They are two sides of the same relationship:

| Statement | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| $3$ is a **factor** of $12$ | $12 \div 3 = 4$ exactly |
| $12$ is a **multiple** of $3$ | $3 \times 4 = 12$ |

> [!tip]
> Factors **divide into** the number. Multiples are what the number **divides into**.

---

## Prime Factorisation

Breaking a number into a product of **prime factors** — primes that multiply together to give the original number.

$$12 = 2 \times 2 \times 3 = 2^2 \times 3$$

A factor tree is the standard method:

$$12 \to 2 \times 6 \to 2 \times 2 \times 3$$
