### **2.3.1 What is a ratio?**

A **ratio** compares two quantities, showing how many times one value contains another.

Form:

Ratio=a:borab\text{Ratio} = a:b \quad \text{or} \quad \frac{a}{b}Ratio=a:borba​

Example:

- In a basket, 8 apples and 4 oranges → ratio of apples to oranges = 8:4 = 2:1

### **2.3.2 Simplifying ratios**

Divide both parts by their **greatest common factor (GCF)**.

Example:

- 12:18 → divide both by 6 → 2:3

### **2.3.3 What is a proportion?**

A **proportion** states that two ratios are equal.

Example:

- 23=46\frac{2}{3} = \frac{4}{6}32​=64​ → 2:3 is **proportional** to 4:6

### **2.3.4 Solving proportion problems**

If ab=cd\frac{a}{b} = \frac{c}{d}ba​=dc​, then **cross multiply**:

a×d=b×ca × d = b × ca×d=b×c

Example:

- If 25=x15\frac{2}{5} = \frac{x}{15}52​=15x​
