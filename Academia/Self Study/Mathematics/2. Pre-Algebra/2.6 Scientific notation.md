## Scientific Notation

Related: [[Exponents]] · [[Decimals]] · [[Multiplication]]

---

## What is Scientific Notation?

Scientific notation expresses very large or very small numbers in the form:

$$N \times 10^n$$

|Part|Name|Condition|
|---|---|---|
|$N$|Coefficient|Must satisfy $1 \leq N < 10$|
|$10^n$|Power of ten|$n$ is an integer|

$$5{,}000 = 5 \times 10^3 \qquad 0.0042 = 4.2 \times 10^{-3}$$

---

## Converting to Scientific Notation

1. Move the decimal point until the number is between $1$ and $10$
2. Count how many places the decimal moved — that is the exponent

|Direction moved|Exponent|
|---|---|
|Left|Positive|
|Right|Negative|

$$12{,}300 \to 1.23 \times 10^4$$ $$0.00056 \to 5.6 \times 10^{-4}$$

> [!tip] Memory trick **Large numbers → positive exponent** (decimal moved left). **Small numbers → negative exponent** (decimal moved right).

---

## Converting Back to Standard Form

Multiply the coefficient by $10^n$ — or equivalently, shift the decimal point.

$$3.2 \times 10^5 = 320{,}000$$ $$7.5 \times 10^{-3} = 0.0075$$

---

## Operations in Scientific Notation

### Multiplication

Multiply the coefficients and **add** the exponents.

$$(2 \times 10^3) \times (3 \times 10^4) = 6 \times 10^7$$

### Division

Divide the coefficients and **subtract** the exponents.

$$\frac{6 \times 10^5}{2 \times 10^2} = 3 \times 10^3$$

> [!warning] Check the coefficient after operating If the result has a coefficient outside $[1, 10)$, adjust it. E.g. $15 \times 10^4 = 1.5 \times 10^5$

## Practice Problems

1. Write $7{,}400{,}000$ in scientific notation.
2. Write $0.000031$ in scientific notation.
3. Convert $6.02 \times 10^{23}$ to standard form.
4. Calculate $(4 \times 10^3) \times (2 \times 10^5)$.
5. Calculate $\dfrac{9 \times 10^8}{3 \times 10^3}$.
6. Add $3.0 \times 10^4 + 2.0 \times 10^4$.
