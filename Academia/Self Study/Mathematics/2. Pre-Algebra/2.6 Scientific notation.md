### **2.6.1 What is scientific notation?**

Scientific notation expresses **very large or very small numbers** in the form:

N×10nN × 10^nN×10n

- NNN → a number between 1 and 10 (the **coefficient**)
- 10n10^n10n → a power of ten (the **exponent**)

Example:

- 5,000 = 5×1035 × 10^35×103
- 0.0042 = 4.2×10−34.2 × 10^{-3}4.2×10−3

### **2.6.2 Converting to scientific notation**

1. Move the decimal to create a number between 1 and 10
2. Count how many places the decimal moved → exponent
    - Right → negative exponent
    - Left → positive exponent

Example:

- 12,300 → 1.23 × 10⁴
- 0.00056 → 5.6 × 10⁻⁴

### **2.6.3 Converting back to standard form**

Multiply the coefficient by 10 raised to the exponent.

Example:

- 3.2 × 10⁵ = 320,000
- 7.5 × 10⁻³ = 0.0075
