### **2.5.1 What is a square root?**

A **square root** of a number is a value that, when multiplied by itself, gives the original number.

Symbol: \sqrt{}

Example:

- 25=5\sqrt{25} = 525=5 because 5 × 5 = 25
- 49=7\sqrt{49} = 749=7 because 7 × 7 = 49

### **2.5.2 What is a cube root?**

A **cube root** of a number is a value that, when multiplied by itself three times, gives the original number.

Symbol: 3\sqrt[3]{}3

Example:

- 273=3\sqrt[3]{27} = 3327=3 because 3 × 3 × 3 = 27

### **2.5.3 Properties of roots**

1. a×b=a×b\sqrt{a × b} = \sqrt{a} × \sqrt{b}a×b=a×b
2. ab=ab\sqrt{$\frac{a}{b}$} = \frac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}ba=ba
3. (a)2=a(\sqrt{a})^2 = a(a)2=a

### **2.5.4 Relation to exponents**

- a=a1/2\sqrt{a} = a^{1/2}a=a1/2
- a3=a1/3\sqrt[3]{a} = a^{1/3}3a=a
