## Square Roots

A square root of a number is a value that, when **multiplied by itself**, gives the original number. Symbol: $\sqrt{\phantom{0}}$

$$\sqrt{25} = 5 \quad \text{because} \quad 5 \times 5 = 25$$ $$\sqrt{49} = 7 \quad \text{because} \quad 7 \times 7 = 49$$

---

## Cube Roots

A cube root of a number is a value that, when **multiplied by itself three times**, gives the original number. Symbol: $\sqrt[3]{\phantom{0}}$

$$\sqrt[3]{27} = 3 \quad \text{because} \quad 3 \times 3 \times 3 = 27$$

---

## Relation to Exponents

Roots are fractional exponents — see [[Exponents]].

|Root|Exponent form|
|---|---|
|$\sqrt{a}$|$a^{1/2}$|
|$\sqrt[3]{a}$|$a^{1/3}$|
|$\sqrt[n]{a}$|$a^{1/n}$|

---

## Properties of Roots

|Property|Rule|
|---|---|
|Product|$\sqrt{a \times b} = \sqrt{a} \times \sqrt{b}$|
|Quotient|$\sqrt{\dfrac{a}{b}} = \dfrac{\sqrt{a}}{\sqrt{b}}$|
|Squaring|$\left(\sqrt{a}\right)^2 = a$|

> [!tip] Simplifying roots using the product rule $\sqrt{72} = \sqrt{36 \times 2} = \sqrt{36} \times \sqrt{2} = 6\sqrt{2}$ Look for the largest perfect square factor, extract it, leave the rest under the root.
