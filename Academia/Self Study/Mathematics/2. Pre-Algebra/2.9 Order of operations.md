## Definition

**Order of operations** is the sequence in which mathematical operations should be performed to ensure consistent results.

The standard order is remembered by **PEMDAS** or **BODMAS**:

|Acronym|Stands for|
|---|---|
|**PEMDAS**|Parentheses, Exponents, Multiplication/Division, Addition/Subtraction|
|**BODMAS**|Brackets, Orders (powers), Division/Multiplication, Addition/Subtraction|

> [!warning] Common Misconception
> 
> Multiplication and division have **equal priority** — perform them left to right. Same for addition and subtraction. Don't do all multiplication before division!

---

## The PEMDAS Rule

### Step-by-Step Process

1. **P**arentheses (or brackets) - simplify inside first
2. **E**xponents (or orders/powers) - evaluate powers and roots
3. **M**ultiplication and **D**ivision - left to right
4. **A**ddition and **S**ubtraction - left to right

```graph
y = x^2 + 2*x + 1
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> 
> The graph shows $y = x^2 + 2x + 1$. To evaluate this at $x = 3$:
> 
> 1. Exponent: $3^2 = 9$
> 2. Multiplication: $2 \times 3 = 6$
> 3. Addition: $9 + 6 + 1 = 16$

## Examples

### Example 1: Basic Application

Evaluate: $3 + 4 \times 2$

**Wrong way** (left to right): $3 + 4 = 7$, then $7 \times 2 = 14$ ❌

**Correct way** (PEMDAS): $4 \times 2 = 8$, then $3 + 8 = 11$ ✅

### Example 2: With Parentheses

Evaluate: $(3 + 4) \times 2$

1. Parentheses: $3 + 4 = 7$
2. Multiplication: $7 \times 2 = 14$

Notice how parentheses change the result!

### Example 3: Complex Expression

Evaluate: $2^3 + 6 \div 2 \times 3 - 1$

1. Exponents: $2^3 = 8$
   - Expression becomes: $8 + 6 \div 2 \times 3 - 1$
2. Division (left to right): $6 \div 2 = 3$
   - Expression becomes: $8 + 3 \times 3 - 1$
3. Multiplication: $3 \times 3 = 9$
   - Expression becomes: $8 + 9 - 1$
4. Addition: $8 + 9 = 17$
5. Subtraction: $17 - 1 = 16$

Answer: **16**

### Example 4: Nested Parentheses

Evaluate: $2 \times [(3 + 1) \times 2 - 4]$

1. Innermost parentheses: $3 + 1 = 4$
   - Expression becomes: $2 \times [4 \times 2 - 4]$
2. Multiplication inside brackets: $4 \times 2 = 8$
   - Expression becomes: $2 \times [8 - 4]$
3. Subtraction inside brackets: $8 - 4 = 4$
   - Expression becomes: $2 \times 4$
4. Final multiplication: $2 \times 4 = 8$

Answer: **8**

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Mistake 1: Ignoring Left-to-Right Rule
> 
> For $12 \div 3 \times 2$:
> - Wrong: $12 \div (3 \times 2) = 12 \div 6 = 2$ ❌
> - Correct: $(12 \div 3) \times 2 = 4 \times 2 = 8$ ✅

> [!warning] Mistake 2: Doing Addition Before Multiplication
> 
> For $2 + 3 \times 4$:
> - Wrong: $(2 + 3) \times 4 = 5 \times 4 = 20$ ❌
> - Correct: $2 + (3 \times 4) = 2 + 12 = 14$ ✅

> [!warning] Mistake 3: Confusing Negative Signs with Subtraction
> 
> For $-3^2$:
> - This means $-(3^2) = -9$, not $(-3)^2 = 9$
> - Exponents apply before the negative sign unless parentheses indicate otherwise
