## Optimization of Functions of Several Variables

### Critical Points

For $f(x,y)$, find $(x_0, y_0)$ such that:

$$f_x(x_0, y_0) = 0, \quad f_y(x_0, y_0) = 0$$

- Solve **simultaneous equations** to find critical points
- These are candidates for local maxima, minima, or saddle points

### Second Derivative Test

Let:

$$D = f_{xx}f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2$$

|Condition|Conclusion|
|---|---|
|$D > 0$, $f_{xx} > 0$|**Local minimum**|
|$D > 0$, $f_{xx} < 0$|**Local maximum**|
|$D < 0$|**Saddle point**|
|$D = 0$|Test inconclusive|

> [!example] Example
> 
> For $f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2 - 2x - 4y$:
> 1. Find critical points: $f_x = 2x - 2 = 0 \Rightarrow x = 1$, $f_y = 2y - 4 = 0 \Rightarrow y = 2$
> 2. Critical point: $(1, 2)$
> 3. Second derivatives: $f_{xx} = 2$, $f_{yy} = 2$, $f_{xy} = 0$
> 4. $D = (2)(2) - 0^2 = 4 > 0$ and $f_{xx} = 2 > 0$
> 5. Conclusion: **Local minimum** at $(1, 2)$

## Constrained Optimization (Lagrange Multipliers)

For constraint $g(x,y) = 0$, optimize $f(x,y)$:

$$\nabla f = \lambda \nabla g$$

This gives the system:
$$\begin{cases} f_x = \lambda g_x \\ f_y = \lambda g_y \\ g(x,y) = 0 \end{cases}$$

### Method

1. Set up Lagrangian: $\mathcal{L}(x,y,\lambda) = f(x,y) - \lambda g(x,y)$
2. Solve system with constraint $g(x,y) = 0$
3. Evaluate $f$ at all solutions to find max/min

> [!tip] Geometric Interpretation
> 
> At the optimum, the gradient of $f$ is parallel to the gradient of $g$. This means the level curve of $f$ is tangent to the constraint curve.

### Example

Maximize $f(x,y) = xy$ subject to $x^2 + y^2 = 1$:

1. $\nabla f = \langle y, x \rangle$, $\nabla g = \langle 2x, 2y \rangle$
2. System: $y = 2\lambda x$, $x = 2\lambda y$, $x^2 + y^2 = 1$
3. Solutions: $(\pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \pm \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})$
4. Maximum value: $f = \frac{1}{2}$ at $(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}})$

## Surface Area

Surface $z = f(x,y)$ over region $R$:

$$\text{Surface area} = \iint_R \sqrt{1 + (f_x)^2 + (f_y)^2} \, dA$$

- Generalizes **arc length in 2D** to 3D surfaces
- Derivation comes from approximating surface with tangent planes

### Example

Find surface area of $z = x^2 + y^2$ over unit disk $x^2 + y^2 \leq 1$:

1. Partials: $f_x = 2x$, $f_y = 2y$
2. Integrand: $\sqrt{1 + 4x^2 + 4y^2}$
3. Convert to polar: $\int_0^{2\pi} \int_0^1 \sqrt{1 + 4r^2} \, r \, dr \, d\theta$
4. Evaluate to get surface area

## Center of Mass and Moment of Inertia

### Center of Mass

For a solid with density $\rho(x,y,z)$:

$$\bar{x} = \frac{\iiint_V x \rho \, dV}{\iiint_V \rho \, dV}, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{\iiint_V y \rho \, dV}{\iiint_V \rho \, dV}, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{\iiint_V z \rho \, dV}{\iiint_V \rho \, dV}$$

- Denominator is total mass $M = \iiint_V \rho \, dV$
- For uniform density ($\rho = \text{constant}$), this is the **centroid**

### Moment of Inertia

About $z$-axis:

$$I_z = \iiint_V (x^2 + y^2) \rho \, dV$$

Similarly:
- About $x$-axis: $I_x = \iiint_V (y^2 + z^2) \rho \, dV$
- About $y$-axis: $I_y = \iiint_V (x^2 + z^2) \rho \, dV$

> [!example] Physical Significance
> 
> Moment of inertia measures resistance to rotational acceleration. Larger $I$ means harder to spin the object.

### Applications

- **Mechanics**: Rotational dynamics, angular momentum
- **Robotics**: Manipulator arm dynamics
- **Structural engineering**: Beam bending, torsion
- **Computer graphics**: Physics simulations

## Practice Problems

1. Find and classify all critical points of $f(x,y) = x^3 - 3xy + y^3$.
2. Use Lagrange multipliers to maximize $f(x,y) = x^2y$ subject to $x^2 + y^2 = 4$.
3. Calculate the surface area of the paraboloid $z = x^2 + y^2$ for $0 \leq z \leq 4$.
4. Find the center of mass of a solid hemisphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \leq R^2$, $z \geq 0$ with uniform density.
5. Compute the moment of inertia about the $z$-axis for a cylinder $x^2 + y^2 \leq R^2$, $0 \leq z \leq H$ with density $\rho = kz$.
6. Minimize $f(x,y,z) = x^2 + y^2 + z^2$ subject to $x + y + z = 1$ using Lagrange multipliers.
7. Show that for a function with a local minimum, we must have $D > 0$ and $f_{xx} > 0$.
8. Find the point on the plane $x + 2y + 3z = 6$ closest to the origin.
9. Calculate the surface area of the sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = R^2$ using the surface integral formula.
10. A wire has shape of the curve $y = x^2$ from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$ with density $\rho(x,y) = x$. Find its center of mass.
