## **7.2.3.1 Statement of Divergence Theorem**

Let $V$ be a solid region in R3\mathbb{R}^3R3 with **closed surface** $S$ oriented **outward**, and let F⃗=⟨P,Q,R⟩\vec{F} = \langle P, Q, R \rangleF=⟨P,Q,R⟩ be a continuously differentiable vector field on an open set containing $V$. Then:

∬SF⃗⋅n⃗ dS=∭V(∇⋅F⃗) dV\iint_S \vec{F} \cdot \vec{n}\, dS = \iiint_V (\nabla \cdot \vec{F}) \, dV∬SF⋅ndS=∭V(∇⋅F)dV

- LHS: **flux through surface** $S$
- RHS: **triple integral of divergence** inside $V$

---

## **7.2.3.2 Interpretation**

- Total **outflow through the boundary** equals the sum of **sources minus sinks inside the volume**
- Useful for **fluid flow, electromagnetism, and mass conservation**

---

## **7.2.3.3 Examples**

1. F⃗=⟨x,y,z⟩\vec{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangleF=⟨x,y,z⟩, $S$ = surface of unit cube 0≤x,y,z≤10 \le x,y,z \le 10≤x,y,z≤1

∇⋅F⃗=1+1+1=3\nabla \cdot \vec{F} = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3∇⋅F=1+1+1=3 ∭V(∇⋅F⃗) dV=3×1=3\iiint_V (\nabla \cdot \vec{F}) \, dV = 3 \times 1 = 3∭V(∇⋅F)dV=3×1=3

- Same as **flux through cube faces** using surface integrals

---

## **7.2.3.4 Conditions**

1. Surface must be **closed and piecewise smooth**
2. Vector field must have **continuous partial derivatives**
3. Surface orientation must be **outward**
