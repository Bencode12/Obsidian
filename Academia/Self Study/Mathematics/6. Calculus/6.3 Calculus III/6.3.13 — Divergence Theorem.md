## Statement of Divergence Theorem

Let $V$ be a solid region in $\mathbb{R}^3$ with **closed surface** $S$ oriented **outward**, and let $\mathbf{F} = \langle P, Q, R \rangle$ be a continuously differentiable vector field on an open set containing $V$. Then:

$$\iint_S \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} \, dS = \iiint_V (\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F}) \, dV$$

### Key Components

- **LHS**: Flux through closed surface $S$ (surface integral)
- **RHS**: Triple integral of divergence inside volume $V$
- Also called **Gauss's Theorem** or **Ostrogradsky's Theorem**

---

## Interpretation

- Total **outflow through the boundary** equals the sum of **sources minus sinks inside the volume**
- If divergence is positive throughout $V$, there's net outflow
- If divergence is negative throughout $V$, there's net inflow
- Zero divergence means what flows in must flow out (incompressible flow)

> [!tip] Physical Meaning
> 
> Think of $V$ as a container: the Divergence Theorem says the total fluid flowing out through the walls equals the total amount being created inside by sources.

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Unit Cube

For $\mathbf{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$ and $V$ = unit cube $0 \leq x,y,z \leq 1$:

1. Compute divergence: $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3$
2. Triple integral: $\iiint_V 3 \, dV = 3 \times \text{Volume} = 3 \times 1 = 3$
3. This equals the flux through all six faces of the cube

### Example 2: Sphere

Find flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$ through sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = R^2$:

**Using Divergence Theorem:**
1. $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 3$
2. $\iiint_V 3 \, dV = 3 \times \frac{4}{3}\pi R^3 = 4\pi R^3$

**Direct calculation would require parameterizing the sphere - much harder!**

## Applications

### Fluid Dynamics

- **Conservation of mass**: For incompressible fluid ($\nabla \cdot \mathbf{v} = 0$), flux through any closed surface is zero
- **Flow rate**: Calculate total fluid leaving a region

### Electromagnetism

- **Gauss's Law**: $\oint_S \mathbf{E} \cdot d\mathbf{S} = \frac{Q_{\text{enc}}}{\epsilon_0}$ relates electric flux to enclosed charge
- Derived from $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{E} = \frac{\rho}{\epsilon_0}$ using Divergence Theorem

### Heat Transfer

- Relates heat flow across boundaries to heat generation within a volume

---

## Conditions for Validity

1. Surface $S$ must be **closed and piecewise smooth**
2. Vector field must have **continuous partial derivatives** on region containing $V$
3. Surface orientation must be **outward** (normal pointing away from interior)
4. Region $V$ should be **simple** (can be decomposed into simple regions if needed)

> [!warning] Common Pitfalls
> 
> - Using inward normal instead of outward (gives negative of correct answer)
> - Applying to surfaces that aren't closed (missing part of boundary)
> - Forgetting that field must be defined everywhere inside $V$ (singularities cause problems)

---

## Relationship to Other Theorems

The Divergence Theorem completes the family of fundamental theorems:

|Theorem|Connects|Dimensions|
|---|---|---|
|Fundamental Theorem of Calculus|Integral over interval ↔ Boundary values|1D → 0D|
|Green's Theorem|Double integral ↔ Line integral|2D → 1D|
|Stokes' Theorem|Surface integral ↔ Line integral|3D surface → 1D|
|Divergence Theorem|Triple integral ↔ Surface integral|3D volume → 2D|

All relate integrals over a region to integrals over its boundary.

## Practice Problems

1. Use the Divergence Theorem to find the flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle x^2, y^2, z^2 \rangle$ through the surface of the cube $0 \leq x,y,z \leq 2$.
2. Calculate the outward flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle xy, yz, zx \rangle$ through the sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 9$.
3. Verify the Divergence Theorem for $\mathbf{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$ over the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 \leq 1$, $0 \leq z \leq 2$.
4. Find the flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle e^x \sin y, e^x \cos y, z \rangle$ through the boundary of the box $[0,1] \times [0,1] \times [0,1]$.
5. Use the Divergence Theorem to show that $\iint_S \mathbf{n} \, dS = \mathbf{0}$ for any closed surface.
6. Calculate the flux of $\mathbf{F} = \frac{\langle x, y, z \rangle}{(x^2+y^2+z^2)^{3/2}}$ through the sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = R^2$. (Careful: field has singularity at origin!)
7. Prove that for constant vector field $\mathbf{F} = \langle a, b, c \rangle$, the flux through any closed surface is zero.
8. Use the Divergence Theorem to find the volume of a region given only its boundary surface integral.
9. Show that if $\nabla \cdot \mathbf{F} = 0$ everywhere, then the flux through any two closed surfaces enclosing the same region is equal.
10. Calculate the flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle x^3, y^3, z^3 \rangle$ through the surface of the ellipsoid $\frac{x^2}{4} + \frac{y^2}{9} + \frac{z^2}{16} = 1$.
