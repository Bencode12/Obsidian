## Unit Tangent Vector $\vec{T}$

$$\vec{T}(t) = \frac{\vec{r}'(t)}{|\vec{r}'(t)|}$$

Points in the **direction of motion**. Depends only on the shape of the curve, not the speed.

---

## Curvature $\kappa$

Curvature measures how sharply the curve bends:

$$\kappa = \left|\frac{d\vec{T}}{ds}\right| = \frac{|\vec{r}'(t) \times \vec{r}''(t)|}{|\vec{r}'(t)|^3}$$

|Curvature|Meaning|
|---|---|
|Large $\kappa$|Sharp turn|
|Small $\kappa$|Gentle curve|
|$\kappa = 0$|Straight line|

---

## Principal Normal Vector $\vec{N}$

Points **toward the centre of curvature** always perpendicular to $\vec{T}$:

$$\vec{N}(t) = \frac{d\vec{T}/dt}{|d\vec{T}/dt|}$$

---

## Binormal Vector $\vec{B}$

Perpendicular to both $\vec{T}$ and $\vec{N}$:

$$\vec{B} = \vec{T} \times \vec{N}$$

Defines the **osculating plane** and indicates how the curve twists in space.

---

## Frenet–Serret Frame

At each point the three vectors $(\vec{T}, \vec{N}, \vec{B})$ form an **orthonormal moving frame** a coordinate system that travels along the curve.

---

## Worked Example Helix

$$\vec{r}(t) = \langle \cos t,\ \sin t,\ t \rangle$$

$$\vec{r}'(t) = \langle -\sin t,\ \cos t,\ 1 \rangle \qquad \vec{r}''(t) = \langle -\cos t,\ -\sin t,\ 0 \rangle$$

$$|\vec{r}'(t)| = \sqrt{2} \qquad \kappa = \frac{1}{2} \quad \text{(constant uniform bending)}$$

---

## Tangential and Normal Acceleration

$$\vec{a} = v'\vec{T} + v^2\kappa,\vec{N}$$

|Component|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$v'\vec{T}$|Change in **speed**|
|$v^2\kappa,\vec{N}$|Change in **direction**|

## Practice Problems

1. Find $\vec{T}(t)$ for $\vec{r}(t) = \langle 3\cos t,\ 3\sin t,\ 4t \rangle$.
2. Find the curvature of $\vec{r}(t) = \langle t,\ t^2,\ 0 \rangle$ at $t = 0$.
3. For the helix in the example, find $\vec{N}(t)$.
4. A curve has $v = 2$ and $\kappa = 3$ at a point. Find the magnitude of the normal acceleration.
