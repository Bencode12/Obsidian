## **7.2.2.1 Statement of Stokes’ Theorem**

Let $S$ be an oriented smooth surface with **boundary curve $C$** (positively oriented with respect to the surface normal n⃗\vec{n}n), and let F⃗\vec{F}F be a vector field with continuous partial derivatives on an open region containing $S$.

∮CF⃗⋅dr⃗=∬S(∇×F⃗)⋅n⃗ dS\oint_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \iint_S (\nabla \times \vec{F}) \cdot \vec{n} \, dS∮CF⋅dr=∬S(∇×F)⋅ndS

- LHS: line integral around boundary $C$
- RHS: flux of **curl** through the surface $S$

---

## **7.2.2.2 Interpretation**

- Line integral along the edge = sum of **tiny rotations (curl) across the surface**
- Reduces complicated 3D line integrals to **surface integrals**, or vice versa

---

## **7.2.2.3 Examples**

1. F⃗=⟨−y,x,0⟩\vec{F} = \langle -y, x, 0 \rangleF=⟨−y,x,0⟩, $S$ = unit disk in xy-plane, boundary $C$ = unit circle

∇×F⃗=⟨0,0,2⟩\nabla \times \vec{F} = \langle 0, 0, 2 \rangle∇×F=⟨0,0,2⟩ ∬S(∇×F⃗)⋅n⃗ dS=2∬S1 dA=2π\iint_S (\nabla \times \vec{F}) \cdot \vec{n} \, dS = 2 \iint_S 1\, dA = 2 \pi∬S(∇×F)⋅ndS=2∬S1dA=2π

- Matches line integral along $C$

---

## **7.2.2.4 Orientation**

- **Positive orientation:** curve must follow **right-hand rule** with respect to the chosen surface normal
- Flip normal → negative sign

---

## **7.2.2.5 Conditions**

1. Surface must be **smooth** or piecewise smooth
2. Vector field must have **continuous partial derivatives**
3. Curve $C$ is **boundary of the surface**
