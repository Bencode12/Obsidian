## Gradient

For a scalar function $f(x,y,z)$:

$$\nabla f = \left\langle \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}, \frac{\partial f}{\partial z} \right\rangle$$

|Property|Description|
|---|---|
|**Direction**|Steepest increase|
|**Magnitude**|Rate of steepest ascent|
|**Perpendicular to**|Level surfaces|

> [!example] For $f(x,y,z) = x^2 + y^2 + z^2$:
> $$\nabla f = \langle 2x, 2y, 2z \rangle$$
>
> At point $(1,2,3)$: $\nabla f = \langle 2, 4, 6 \rangle$
> - Direction of fastest increase: $\langle 2, 4, 6 \rangle$
> - Rate of increase: $|\nabla f| = \sqrt{4+16+36} = \sqrt{56}$

## Divergence

For a vector field $\vec{F} = \langle P, Q, R \rangle$:

$$\nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \frac{\partial P}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial Q}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial R}{\partial z}$$

> [!info] Physical Meaning Divergence measures how much a vector field "spreads out" from a point:
> - **Positive divergence**: source (field flows outward)
> - **Negative divergence**: sink (field flows inward)
> - **Zero divergence**: incompressible flow
>
> **Example:** For $\vec{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$:
> $$\nabla \cdot \vec{F} = \frac{\partial x}{\partial x} + \frac{\partial y}{\partial y} + \frac{\partial z}{\partial z} = 1 + 1 + 1 = 3$$
> Positive → this is a source!

## Curl

For a vector field $\vec{F} = \langle P, Q, R \rangle$:

$$\nabla \times \vec{F} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ P & Q & R \end{vmatrix} = \langle R_y - Q_z, P_z - R_x, Q_x - P_y \rangle$$

> [!warning] Curl Measures Rotation The curl tells you how much a vector field rotates around a point:
> - **Nonzero curl**: field has rotational/twisting motion
> - **Zero curl**: field is irrotational (conservative)
>
> **Example:** For $\vec{F} = \langle -y, x, 0 \rangle$ (rotation around z-axis):
> $$\nabla \times \vec{F} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \\ \frac{\partial}{\partial x} & \frac{\partial}{\partial y} & \frac{\partial}{\partial z} \\ -y & x & 0 \end{vmatrix} = \langle 0, 0, 2 \rangle$$
>
> The curl points in the $z$ direction with magnitude 2 confirming counterclockwise rotation!

## Properties

|Operation|Result|Name|
|---|---|---|
|$\nabla f$|Vector|Gradient|
|$\nabla \cdot (\nabla f)$|Scalar|Laplacian: $\Delta f$|
|$\nabla \times (\nabla f)$|Zero vector|Always $\mathbf{0}$|
