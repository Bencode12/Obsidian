## **7.1.3.1 Unit Tangent Vector T⃗\vec{T}T**

Given a smooth space curve r⃗(t)\vec{r}(t)r(t):

\vec{T}(t) = \frac{\vec{r}\,'(t)}{|\vec{r}\,'(t)|}

- Points in the direction of motion
- Depends only on the **shape** of the curve, not the speed

---

## **7.1.3.2 Curvature κ\kappaκ**

**Curvature** measures how sharply a curve bends.

κ=∣dT⃗ds∣\kappa = \left|\frac{d\vec{T}}{ds}\right|κ=dsdT

Equivalent practical formula:

\kappa = \frac{|\vec{r}\,'(t) \times \vec{r}\,''(t)|}{|\vec{r}\,'(t)|^3}

- Large curvature → sharp turn
- Small curvature → flatter curve
- Straight line → κ=0\kappa = 0κ=0

---

## **7.1.3.3 Principal Normal Vector N⃗\vec{N}N**

Points **toward the center of curvature**.

N⃗=dT⃗/dt∣dT⃗/dt∣\vec{N} = \frac{d\vec{T}/dt}{|d\vec{T}/dt|}N=∣dT/dt∣dT/dt

- Always perpendicular to T⃗\vec{T}T

---

## **7.1.3.4 Binormal Vector B⃗\vec{B}B**

Perpendicular to both T⃗\vec{T}T and N⃗\vec{N}N:

B⃗=T⃗×N⃗\vec{B} = \vec{T} \times \vec{N}B=T×N

- Defines the **osculating plane**
- Indicates how the curve twists in space

---

## **7.1.3.5 Frenet–Serret Frame**

At each point on a curve, the three vectors:

(T⃗,N⃗,B⃗)(\vec{T}, \vec{N}, \vec{B})(T,N,B)

form an **orthonormal coordinate system** that moves along the curve.

## **7.1.3.7 Tangential and Normal Acceleration**

Velocity:

v⃗=vT⃗\vec{v} = v\vec{T}v=vT

Acceleration:

a⃗=v′T⃗+v2κN⃗\vec{a} = v'\vec{T} + v^2\kappa \vec{N}a=v′T+v2κN

- v′T⃗v'\vec{T}v′T → change in speed
