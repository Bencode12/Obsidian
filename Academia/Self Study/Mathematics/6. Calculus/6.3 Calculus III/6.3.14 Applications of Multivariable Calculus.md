## Optimization of Functions of Several Variables

### Critical Points

For $f(x,y)$, find $(x_0,y_0)$ such that:

$$f_x(x_0,y_0) = 0, \quad f_y(x_0,y_0) = 0$$

- Solve **simultaneous equations** to find critical points

### Second Derivative Test

Let:

$$D = f_{xx}f_{yy} - (f_{xy})^2$$

|Condition|Conclusion|
|---|---|
|$D > 0$, $f_{xx} > 0$|Local minimum|
|$D > 0$, $f_{xx} < 0$|Local maximum|
|$D < 0$|Saddle point|
|$D = 0$|Test inconclusive|

> [!tip] Classification Strategy
> 1. Find all critical points by solving $f_x = 0$ and $f_y = 0$
> 2. Compute $D$ at each point
> 3. Classify using the table above

## Lagrange Multipliers

To optimize $f(x,y)$ subject to constraint $g(x,y) = k$:

$$\nabla f = \lambda \nabla g$$

This gives the system:
- $f_x = \lambda g_x$
- $f_y = \lambda g_y$
- $g(x,y) = k$

> [!info] Geometric Meaning
> At the optimum, the gradient of $f$ is parallel to the gradient of $g$. This means the level curves of $f$ and $g$ are tangent.

## Center of Mass

For a lamina with density $\rho(x,y)$:

$$M = \iint_R \rho\,dA \quad \text{(total mass)}$$

$$\bar{x} = \frac{1}{M}\iint_R x\rho\,dA, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{1}{M}\iint_R y\rho\,dA$$

---

## Moment of Inertia

Resistance to rotational motion:

$$I_x = \iint_R y^2\rho\,dA, \quad I_y = \iint_R x^2\rho\,dA$$

$$I_0 = I_x + I_y = \iint_R (x^2 + y^2)\rho\,dA$$

> [!warning] Common Applications
> - **Engineering**: Structural analysis, beam design
> - **Physics**: Rotational dynamics, angular momentum
> - **Computer Graphics**: Object rotation, collision detection

## Practice Problems

1. Find and classify all critical points of $f(x,y) = x^3 + y^3 - 3xy$.
2. Use Lagrange multipliers to find the maximum of $f(x,y) = xy$ subject to $x^2 + y^2 = 1$.
3. Find the center of mass of a triangular lamina with vertices $(0,0)$, $(1,0)$, $(0,1)$ and constant density.
4. Calculate the moment of inertia of a uniform disk of radius $R$ about its center.
5. Optimize $f(x,y,z) = xyz$ subject to $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1$ using Lagrange multipliers.
