## Double Integrals

For $f(x,y)$ over a region $R$ in the xy-plane:

$$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA$$

- Represents **volume under the surface** $z = f(x,y)$ over $R$
- $dA = dx\,dy$ or $dy\,dx$ depending on order of integration

### Iterated Integrals

$$\iint_R f(x,y) \, dA = \int_a^b \int_{g_1(x)}^{g_2(x)} f(x,y) \, dy \, dx$$

- Integrate **inner variable first**, then outer
- Change order if necessary to simplify

> [!example] Example
> 
> Evaluate $\iint_R xy \, dA$ where $R: 0 \leq x \leq 1, 0 \leq y \leq x$:
> $$\int_0^1 \int_0^x xy \, dy \, dx = \int_0^1 x\left[\frac{y^2}{2}\right]_0^x dx = \int_0^1 \frac{x^3}{2} dx = \frac{1}{8}$$

## Triple Integrals

For $f(x,y,z)$ over a 3D region $V$:

$$\iiint_V f(x,y,z) \, dV$$

where $dV = dx\,dy\,dz$ (or other orders).

### Interpretations

- $\iiint_V 1 \, dV$ gives **volume** of $V$
- $\iiint_V \rho(x,y,z) \, dV$ gives **mass** with density $\rho$
- Represents accumulation over 3D regions

## Change of Variables

### Polar Coordinates (2D)

$$x = r\cos\theta, \quad y = r\sin\theta, \quad dA = r\,dr\,d\theta$$

Useful for circular regions.

### Cylindrical Coordinates (3D)

$$x = r\cos\theta, \quad y = r\sin\theta, \quad z = z, \quad dV = r\,dr\,d\theta\,dz$$

Useful for cylinders, cones, and regions with rotational symmetry about $z$-axis.

### Spherical Coordinates (3D)

$$x = \rho\sin\phi\cos\theta, \quad y = \rho\sin\phi\sin\theta, \quad z = \rho\cos\phi$$
$$dV = \rho^2\sin\phi \, d\rho\,d\phi\,d\theta$$

Useful for spheres and regions with spherical symmetry.

> [!tip] Jacobian Factor
> 
> The extra factors ($r$ in polar/cylindrical, $\rho^2\sin\phi$ in spherical) come from the **Jacobian determinant** of the coordinate transformation.

## Applications

### Volume of a Solid

$$\text{Volume} = \iiint_V 1 \, dV$$

### Mass with Variable Density

$$M = \iiint_V \rho(x,y,z) \, dV$$

### Center of Mass

$$\bar{x} = \frac{1}{M}\iiint_V x\rho \, dV, \quad \bar{y} = \frac{1}{M}\iiint_V y\rho \, dV, \quad \bar{z} = \frac{1}{M}\iiint_V z\rho \, dV$$

### Moment of Inertia

About $z$-axis:
$$I_z = \iiint_V (x^2 + y^2)\rho \, dV$$

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\iint_R (x + y) \, dA$ where $R$ is the rectangle $[0,2] \times [0,3]$.
2. Set up (but don't evaluate) a double integral for the volume under $z = x^2 + y^2$ over the unit disk.
3. Convert $\int_0^1 \int_0^{\sqrt{1-x^2}} xy \, dy \, dx$ to polar coordinates and evaluate.
4. Find the volume of the solid bounded by $z = 4 - x^2 - y^2$ and the xy-plane using polar coordinates.
5. Set up a triple integral in cylindrical coordinates for the volume of a cylinder $x^2 + y^2 \leq 4$, $0 \leq z \leq 5$.
6. Convert $\iiint_V (x^2 + y^2 + z^2) \, dV$ to spherical coordinates where $V$ is the unit sphere.
7. Find the mass of a sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \leq R^2$ with density $\rho = kz$.
8. Calculate the center of mass of a hemisphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 \leq R^2$, $z \geq 0$ with uniform density.
9. Evaluate $\iiint_V xyz \, dV$ where $V$ is the box $[0,1] \times [0,2] \times [0,3]$.
10. Use a change to polar coordinates to evaluate $\iint_R e^{-(x^2+y^2)} \, dA$ where $R$ is the entire xy-plane.
