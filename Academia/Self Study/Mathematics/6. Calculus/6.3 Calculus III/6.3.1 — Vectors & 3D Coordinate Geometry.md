## Vectors in 2D and 3D

$$\vec{v} = \langle x, y \rangle \quad \text{(2D)} \qquad \vec{v} = \langle x, y, z \rangle \quad \text{(3D)}$$

The **position vector** from the origin to $P(x, y, z)$ is $\vec{r} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$.

---

## Vector Operations

$$\langle a,b,c \rangle + \langle d,e,f \rangle = \langle a+d,\ b+e,\ c+f \rangle$$

$$k\langle a,b,c \rangle = \langle ka,\ kb,\ kc \rangle$$

**Magnitude:**

$$|\vec{v}| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2 + z^2} \qquad \hat{v} = \frac{\vec{v}}{|\vec{v}|}$$

---

## Dot Product

$$\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = a_x b_x + a_y b_y + a_z b_z = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\cos\theta$$

> [!tip] $\vec{a} \cdot \vec{b} = 0 \implies$ the vectors are **perpendicular**.

---

## Cross Product (3D only)

$$\vec{a} \times \vec{b} = \begin{vmatrix} \mathbf{i} & \mathbf{j} & \mathbf{k} \ a_1 & a_2 & a_3 \ b_1 & b_2 & b_3 \end{vmatrix}$$

$$|\vec{a} \times \vec{b}| = |\vec{a}||\vec{b}|\sin\theta$$

The result is a vector **perpendicular to both** $\vec{a}$ and $\vec{b}$. Used for areas and normals.

---

## Lines in 3D

Line through point $\vec{r}_0$ with direction $\vec{v}$:

$$\vec{r}(t) = \vec{r}_0 + t\vec{v}$$

**Parametric form:**

$$x = x_0 + at \qquad y = y_0 + bt \qquad z = z_0 + ct$$

---

## Planes in 3D

Plane with normal $\vec{n} = \langle A, B, C \rangle$ through point $(x_0, y_0, z_0)$:

$$A(x - x_0) + B(y - y_0) + C(z - z_0) = 0$$

**Standard form:** $Ax + By + Cz + D = 0$

3. Find $\vec{a} \times \vec{b}$ for $\vec{a} = \langle 1, 0, 0 \rangle$, $\vec{b} = \langle 0, 1, 0 \rangle$.
4. Write the equation of the plane through $(1, 2, 3)$ with normal $\langle 1, -1, 2 \rangle$.
5. Write the parametric equations of the line through $(0,1,2)$ with direction $\langle 3, -1, 1 \rangle$.
