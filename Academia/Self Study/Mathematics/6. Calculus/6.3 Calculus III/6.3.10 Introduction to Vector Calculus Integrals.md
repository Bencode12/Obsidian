## Line Integrals (Scalar Fields)

For a scalar field $f(x,y,z)$ along a curve $C$ parameterized by $\vec{r}(t)$, $a \leq t \leq b$:

$$\int_C f\, ds = \int_a^b f(\vec{r}(t)) \, |\vec{r}\,'(t)| \, dt$$

- Measures **accumulated quantity along a path** (mass, temperature, etc.)
- $ds =$ differential arc length

**Example:**
$\int_C x\, ds$ along a line from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$

---

## Line Integrals (Vector Fields / Work)

For a vector field $\vec{F} = \langle P,Q,R \rangle$ along a curve $C$:

$$\int_C \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{r} = \int_a^b \vec{F}(\vec{r}(t)) \cdot \vec{r}\,'(t) \, dt$$

- Represents **work done by a force field along a path**

**Example:**
$\vec{F} = \langle y, -x, 0\rangle$, $C$ = unit circle → calculates circulation

## Surface Integrals (Scalar Fields)

For a surface $S$ parameterized by $\vec{r}(u,v)$:

$$\iint_S f(x,y,z) \, dS = \iint_D f(\vec{r}(u,v)) \, |\vec{r}_u \times \vec{r}_v| \, du\, dv$$

- $dS =$ differential area element of the surface
- Measures **accumulated quantity over a surface** (mass, charge, heat)

---

## Surface Integrals (Vector Fields / Flux)

Flux through a surface $S$ with normal $\vec{n}$:

$$\iint_S \vec{F} \cdot d\vec{S} = \iint_S \vec{F} \cdot \vec{n} \, dS$$

- Measures **flow of a vector field through a surface**
- Important in electromagnetism and fluid dynamics

---

## Orientation and Normal Vectors

- Surface must have a **chosen orientation** (upward/downward)
- Normal vector can be computed via:

$$\vec{n} = \frac{\vec{r}_u \times \vec{r}_v}{|\vec{r}_u \times \vec{r}_v|}$$

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\int_C (x + y)\, ds$ where $C$ is the line segment from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$.
2. Find the work done by $\vec{F} = \langle y, x \rangle$ along the parabola $y = x^2$ from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$.
3. Compute the flux of $\vec{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$ through the unit sphere.
4. Set up a surface integral for the area of the paraboloid $z = x^2 + y^2$ over the unit disk.
