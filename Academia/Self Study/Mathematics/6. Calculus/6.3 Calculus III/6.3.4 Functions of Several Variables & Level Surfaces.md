## Definition

A function of **two variables** maps a pair $(x, y)$ to a single output:

$$f(x, y): \mathbb{R}^2 \to \mathbb{R} \qquad z = f(x, y)$$

Think of $z$ as the **height** above the $xy$-plane.

---

## Domain and Range

|Concept|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Domain**|All $(x, y)$ for which $f$ is defined|
|**Range**|All possible $z$-values|

> [!example] $f(x, y) = \sqrt{1 - x^2 - y^2}$ Domain: $x^2 + y^2 \leq 1$ (unit disk) Range: $0 \leq z \leq 1$

---

## Common Surfaces

|Equation|Surface|
|---|---|
|$z = ax + by + c$|Plane|
|$z = x^2 + y^2$|Paraboloid (bowl)|
|$z = x^2 - y^2$|Saddle surface|

---

## Level Curves and Surfaces

|Concept|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Level curve**|$f(x, y) = c$ a curve in the $xy$-plane|
|**Level surface**|$f(x, y, z) = c$ a surface in 3D|

> [!example] $f(x, y) = x^2 + y^2$ → level curves are **circles** $x^2 + y^2 = c$.

---

## Partial Derivatives (Intro)

Differentiate with respect to one variable while treating the others as constants:

$$f_x = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} \qquad f_y = \frac{\partial f}{\partial y}$$

> [!example] $f(x, y) = x^2y + 3y^2$ $$f_x = 2xy \qquad f_y = x^2 + 6y$$

These are the building blocks for gradients and directional derivatives.

## Practice Problems

1. Find the domain and range of $f(x, y) = \ln(x + y)$.
2. Sketch (or describe) the level curves of $f(x, y) = x^2 + 4y^2$ for $c = 1, 4, 9$.
3. Find $f_x$ and $f_y$ for $f(x, y) = x^3y - e^{xy}$.
4. Identify the surface $z = 4 - x^2 - y^2$ and describe its level curves.
