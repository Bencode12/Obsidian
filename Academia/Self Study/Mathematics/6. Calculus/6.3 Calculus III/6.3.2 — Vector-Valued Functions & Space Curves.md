## What is a Vector-Valued Function?

$$\vec{r}(t) = \langle x(t),\ y(t),\ z(t) \rangle$$

Think of $\vec{r}(t)$ as the **position** of a particle at time $t$. The curve traced is a **space curve**.

> [!example] $\vec{r}(t) = \langle \cos t,\ \sin t,\ t \rangle$ — a **helix**.

---

## Limits and Continuity

Limits and continuity apply **component-wise**:

$$\lim_{t\to a} \vec{r}(t) = \left\langle \lim_{t\to a} x(t),\ \lim_{t\to a} y(t),\ \lim_{t\to a} z(t) \right\rangle$$

---

## Derivatives

$$\vec{r}'(t) = \langle x'(t),\ y'(t),\ z'(t) \rangle \quad \text{(velocity)}$$

$$\vec{r}''(t) \quad \text{(acceleration)}$$

## Arc Length

$$L = \int_a^b |\vec{r}'(t)|,dt$$

> [!example] $\vec{r}(t) = \langle \cos t,\ \sin t,\ t \rangle$ $$|\vec{r}'(t)| = \sqrt{\sin^2 t + \cos^2 t + 1} = \sqrt{2}$$ Arc length from $0$ to $2\pi$: $L = 2\pi\sqrt{2}$

---

## Motion in Space

If acceleration $\vec{a}(t)$ is known, integrate twice to recover position:

$$\vec{v}(t) = \int \vec{a}(t),dt + \vec{v}_0 \qquad \vec{r}(t) = \int \vec{v}(t),dt + \vec{r}_0$$

## Practice Problems

1. Find $\vec{r}'(t)$ for $\vec{r}(t) = \langle t^2,\ \sin t,\ e^t \rangle$.
2. Find the unit tangent $\vec{T}(t)$ for $\vec{r}(t) = \langle 3t,\ 4t,\ 0 \rangle$.
3. Find the arc length of $\vec{r}(t) = \langle t,\ t^2,\ t^3 \rangle$ from $t = 0$ to $t = 1$.
4. A particle has acceleration $\vec{a}(t) = \langle 0, 0, -g \rangle$, $\vec{v}(0) = \langle 1, 0, 0 \rangle$, $\vec{r}(0) = \mathbf{0}$. Find $\vec{r}(t)$.
