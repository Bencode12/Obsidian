## Line Integrals (Scalar Fields)

For a scalar field $f(x,y,z)$ along a curve $C$ parameterized by $\mathbf{r}(t)$, $a \leq t \leq b$:

$$\int_C f \, ds = \int_a^b f(\mathbf{r}(t)) \, |\mathbf{r}'(t)| \, dt$$

- Measures **accumulated quantity along a path** (mass, temperature, etc.)
- $ds = |\mathbf{r}'(t)| \, dt$ is the differential arc length

### Example

Evaluate $\int_C x \, ds$ along the line from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$:

1. Parameterize: $\mathbf{r}(t) = \langle t, t \rangle$, $0 \leq t \leq 1$
2. $\mathbf{r}'(t) = \langle 1, 1 \rangle$, so $|\mathbf{r}'(t)| = \sqrt{2}$
3. Integral: $\int_0^1 t\sqrt{2} \, dt = \sqrt{2}\left[\frac{t^2}{2}\right]_0^1 = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{2}$

## Line Integrals (Vector Fields / Work)

For a vector field $\mathbf{F} = \langle P,Q,R \rangle$ along a curve $C$:

$$\int_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r} = \int_a^b \mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) \cdot \mathbf{r}'(t) \, dt$$

### Physical Interpretation

- Represents **work done by a force field along a path**
- Also called **circulation** when $C$ is closed

### Example

For $\mathbf{F} = \langle y, -x, 0 \rangle$ along the unit circle:

1. Parameterize: $\mathbf{r}(t) = \langle \cos t, \sin t, 0 \rangle$, $0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$
2. $\mathbf{r}'(t) = \langle -\sin t, \cos t, 0 \rangle$
3. $\mathbf{F}(\mathbf{r}(t)) = \langle \sin t, -\cos t, 0 \rangle$
4. Dot product: $\mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{r}' = -\sin^2 t - \cos^2 t = -1$
5. Integral: $\int_0^{2\pi} -1 \, dt = -2\pi$

## Surface Integrals (Scalar Fields)

For a surface $S$ parameterized by $\mathbf{r}(u,v)$:

$$\iint_S f(x,y,z) \, dS = \iint_D f(\mathbf{r}(u,v)) \, |\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v| \, du \, dv$$

- $dS = |\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v| \, du \, dv$ is the differential area element
- Measures **accumulated quantity over a surface** (mass, charge, heat)

---

## Surface Integrals (Vector Fields / Flux)

Flux through a surface $S$ with normal $\mathbf{n}$:

$$\iint_S \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{S} = \iint_S \mathbf{F} \cdot \mathbf{n} \, dS$$

### Physical Interpretation

- Measures **flow of a vector field through a surface**
- Positive flux → net flow in direction of normal
- Negative flux → net flow opposite to normal

> [!example] Applications
> 
> - **Electromagnetism**: Electric flux, magnetic flux
> - **Fluid dynamics**: Flow rate through a surface
> - **Heat transfer**: Heat flux across boundaries

## Orientation and Normal Vectors

### Choosing Orientation

- Surface must have a **chosen orientation** (upward/downward, inward/outward)
- For closed surfaces: typically outward normal
- For open surfaces: depends on context

### Computing Normal Vector

For parameterized surface $\mathbf{r}(u,v)$:

$$\mathbf{n} = \frac{\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v}{|\mathbf{r}_u \times \mathbf{r}_v|}$$

For surface $z = f(x,y)$:

$$\mathbf{n} = \frac{\langle -f_x, -f_y, 1 \rangle}{\sqrt{1 + f_x^2 + f_y^2}}$$

> [!warning] Orientation Matters
> 
> Reversing the orientation changes the sign of the flux integral. Always verify the correct orientation for your problem.

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\int_C (x + y) \, ds$ where $C$ is the line segment from $(0,0)$ to $(3,4)$.
2. Compute the work done by $\mathbf{F} = \langle x^2, y^2 \rangle$ along the parabola $y = x^2$ from $(0,0)$ to $(1,1)$.
3. Find the flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle x, y, z \rangle$ through the unit sphere with outward orientation.
4. Parameterize the surface $z = x^2 + y^2$ for $x^2 + y^2 \leq 1$ and compute $dS$.
5. Evaluate $\int_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ for $\mathbf{F} = \langle -y, x \rangle$ along the unit circle counterclockwise.
6. Calculate the surface area of the hemisphere $z = \sqrt{1 - x^2 - y^2}$ using a surface integral.
7. Find the mass of a surface $z = xy$ over $[0,1] \times [0,1]$ with density $\rho = x + y$.
8. Show that for a conservative field $\mathbf{F} = \nabla f$, the line integral depends only on endpoints.
9. Compute the flux of $\mathbf{F} = \langle 0, 0, z \rangle$ through the disk $x^2 + y^2 \leq 4$ in the plane $z = 3$.
10. Verify that reversing the orientation of a curve changes the sign of the line integral.
