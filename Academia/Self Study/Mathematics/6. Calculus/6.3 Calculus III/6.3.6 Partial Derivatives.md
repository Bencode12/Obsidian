## Definition

For a function $f(x,y)$, the **partial derivative with respect to x** is:

$$f_x(x,y) = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x+h, y) - f(x,y)}{h}$$

|Concept|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Partial w.r.t. $x$**|Treat $y$ as constant|
|**Partial w.r.t. $y$**|Treat $x$ as constant|

> [!tip] How to Compute Partial Derivatives To find $f_x$, differentiate normally but treat ALL $y$ terms as constants (like numbers). For $f_y$, treat all $x$ terms as constants.

## Higher-Order Partial Derivatives

|Notation|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$f_{xx}$|$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}$|
|$f_{yy}$|$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y^2}$|
|$f_{xy}$|$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x \partial y}$|
|$f_{yx}$|$\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial y \partial x}$|

> [!info] Clairaut's Theorem If $f_{xy}$ and $f_{yx}$ are continuous, then:
> $$f_{xy} = f_{yx}$$
>
> This means the order of differentiation doesn't matter for "nice" functions!

> [!example] For $f(x,y) = x^2y + xy^3$:
> - $f_x = 2xy + y^3$, then $f_{xy} = 2x + 3y^2$
> - $f_y = x^2 + 3xy^2$, then $f_{yx} = 2x + 3y^2$ ✓

## Gradient Vector

The **gradient** points in the direction of **maximum increase**:

$$\nabla f(x,y) = \langle f_x, f_y \rangle$$

|Property|Description|
|---|---|
|**Direction**|Steepest ascent|
|**Magnitude**|Rate of steepest increase|
|**Perpendicular to**|Level curves|

> [!warning] Gradient Points Uphill The gradient vector $\nabla f$ always points in the direction of **steepest increase**.
>
> - Moving in the direction of $\nabla f$: function increases fastest
> - Moving opposite to $\nabla f$: function decreases fastest
> - Moving perpendicular to $\nabla f$: function doesn't change (along level curve)

## Tangent Plane

For a surface $z = f(x,y)$ at $(x_0, y_0)$:

$$z - f(x_0,y_0) = f_x(x_0,y_0)(x - x_0) + f_y(x_0,y_0)(y - y_0)$$

- Linear approximation of the surface near the point

---

## Directional Derivative

Derivative of $f$ in the direction of unit vector $\vec{u} = \langle a, b \rangle$:

$$D_{\vec{u}} f(x,y) = f_x a + f_y b = \nabla f \cdot \vec{u}$$

- Measures **rate of change along a specific direction**

## Practice Problems

1. Find $f_x$ and $f_y$ for $f(x,y) = x^3y^2 + e^{xy}$.
2. Compute all second-order partial derivatives for $f(x,y) = x^2y + \sin(xy)$.
3. Find the gradient of $f(x,y,z) = x^2 + y^2 + z^2$ at $(1,2,3)$.
4. Find the directional derivative of $f(x,y) = x^2 + y^2$ at $(1,1)$ in the direction $\vec{u} = \langle \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}, \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \rangle$.
5. Find the equation of the tangent plane to $z = x^2 + y^2$ at $(1,2,5)$.
