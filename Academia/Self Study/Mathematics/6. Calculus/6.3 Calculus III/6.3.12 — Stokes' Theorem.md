## Statement of Stokes' Theorem

Let $S$ be an oriented smooth surface with **boundary curve $C$** (positively oriented with respect to the surface normal $\mathbf{n}$), and let $\mathbf{F}$ be a vector field with continuous partial derivatives on an open region containing $S$. Then:

$$\oint_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r} = \iint_S (\nabla \times \mathbf{F}) \cdot \mathbf{n} \, dS$$

### Key Components

- **LHS**: Line integral (circulation) around boundary curve $C$
- **RHS**: Surface integral of curl over surface $S$
- **Orientation**: Right-hand rule - if thumb points along $\mathbf{n}$, fingers curl in direction of $C$

---

## Interpretation

Stokes' Theorem generalizes Green's Theorem to 3D:

- Relates **circulation around a closed loop** to **flux of curl through any surface** bounded by that loop
- The surface integral depends only on the boundary, not the specific surface chosen
- Measures total "rotation" or "swirl" of the vector field through the surface

> [!tip] Power of Stokes' Theorem
> 
> You can choose **any convenient surface** with the given boundary. This flexibility often simplifies calculations dramatically.

## Examples

### Example 1: Hemisphere

Evaluate $\oint_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ where $\mathbf{F} = \langle z, x, y \rangle$ and $C$ is the boundary of the upper hemisphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1$, $z \geq 0$:

**Method 1: Direct line integral**
- Parameterize circle: $\mathbf{r}(t) = \langle \cos t, \sin t, 0 \rangle$, $0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$
- Compute directly (tedious)

**Method 2: Stokes' Theorem**
1. Compute curl: $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \langle 1, 1, 1 \rangle$
2. Choose simpler surface: disk $x^2 + y^2 \leq 1$ in $z = 0$ plane
3. Normal: $\mathbf{n} = \langle 0, 0, 1 \rangle$
4. $(\nabla \times \mathbf{F}) \cdot \mathbf{n} = 1$
5. Integral: $\iint_D 1 \, dA = \pi$

### Example 2: Verification

Verify Stokes' Theorem for $\mathbf{F} = \langle -y, x, 0 \rangle$ over the paraboloid $z = 1 - x^2 - y^2$, $z \geq 0$:

1. Boundary: circle $x^2 + y^2 = 1$ in $z = 0$ plane
2. Line integral: $\oint_C (-y \, dx + x \, dy) = 2\pi$
3. Curl: $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \langle 0, 0, 2 \rangle$
4. Surface integral with upward normal also gives $2\pi$ ✓

## Relationship to Other Theorems

|Theorem|Dimension|Relates|
|---|---|---|
|Fundamental Theorem of Calculus|1D|Integral over interval to values at endpoints|
|Green's Theorem|2D|Line integral to double integral|
|Stokes' Theorem|3D|Line integral to surface integral|
|Divergence Theorem|3D|Surface integral to triple integral|

> [!example] Unified View
> 
> All these theorems relate integrals over a region to integrals over its boundary. They are special cases of the **generalized Stokes' theorem** from differential geometry.

---

## Conditions for Validity

1. Surface $S$ must be **orientable** and piecewise-smooth
2. Boundary curve $C$ must be **closed, simple, and piecewise-smooth**
3. Vector field $\mathbf{F}$ must have **continuous partial derivatives** on region containing $S$
4. Orientation of $C$ must be **consistent** with orientation of $S$ (right-hand rule)

## Practice Problems

1. Use Stokes' Theorem to evaluate $\oint_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ where $\mathbf{F} = \langle yz, xz, xy \rangle$ and $C$ is the boundary of the part of the paraboloid $z = 4 - x^2 - y^2$ above the xy-plane.
2. Verify Stokes' Theorem for $\mathbf{F} = \langle z, x, y \rangle$ over the triangle with vertices $(1,0,0)$, $(0,1,0)$, $(0,0,1)$.
3. Calculate the circulation of $\mathbf{F} = \langle e^x, e^y, e^z \rangle$ around the boundary of the square $0 \leq x,y \leq 1$ in the plane $z = 0$.
4. Show that if $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$ everywhere, then $\oint_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r} = 0$ for any closed curve.
5. Use Stokes' Theorem to find $\oint_C (y \, dx + z \, dy + x \, dz)$ where $C$ is the intersection of the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 = 1$ and the plane $x + y + z = 3$.
6. Evaluate the surface integral $\iint_S (\nabla \times \mathbf{F}) \cdot \mathbf{n} \, dS$ for $\mathbf{F} = \langle x^2, y^2, z^2 \rangle$ over the upper hemisphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 4$, $z \geq 0$.
7. Prove that the flux of $\nabla \times \mathbf{F}$ through any two surfaces with the same boundary is equal.
8. Use Stokes' Theorem to show that $\oint_C \mathbf{F} \cdot d\mathbf{r}$ is independent of the path if $\nabla \times \mathbf{F} = \mathbf{0}$.
9. Calculate the work done by $\mathbf{F} = \langle yz, zx, xy \rangle$ around the ellipse formed by intersecting the cylinder $x^2 + y^2 = 4$ with the plane $z = x + y$.
10. Verify Stokes' Theorem for the vector field $\mathbf{F} = \langle x^2y, y^2z, z^2x \rangle$ over the portion of the sphere $x^2 + y^2 + z^2 = 1$ in the first octant.
