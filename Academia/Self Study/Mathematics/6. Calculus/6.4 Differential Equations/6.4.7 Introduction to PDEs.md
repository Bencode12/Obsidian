## Definition

**Partial Differential Equation (PDE)**: Equation involving unknown function of multiple variables and its partial derivatives.

General form for $u(x,y)$:
$$F(x, y, u, u_x, u_y, u_{xx}, u_{xy}, u_{yy}, \ldots) = 0$$

**Order**: Highest derivative appearing.

---

## Classification of Second-Order Linear PDEs

### Standard Form

$$Au_{xx} + Bu_{xy} + Cu_{yy} + Du_x + Eu_y + Fu = G$$

where coefficients may depend on $x, y$.

### Discriminant

$$\Delta = B^2 - 4AC$$

|Type|Discriminant|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Elliptic**|$\Delta < 0$|Laplace's equation|
|**Parabolic**|$\Delta = 0$|Heat equation|
|**Hyperbolic**|$\Delta > 0$|Wave equation|

---

## Heat Equation (Parabolic)

### Standard Form

$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = k\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}$$

Models heat diffusion in one dimension.

### Physical Meaning

- $u(x,t)$: Temperature at position $x$, time $t$
- $k$: Thermal diffusivity
- Rate of temperature change proportional to curvature

### Initial-Boundary Value Problem

$$\begin{aligned} u_t &= ku_{xx}, \quad 0 < x < L, \, t > 0 \\ u(0,t) &= u(L,t) = 0 \quad \text{(boundary)} \\ u(x,0) &= f(x) \quad \text{(initial)} \end{aligned}$$

### Solution by Separation of Variables

Assume $u(x,t) = X(x)T(t)$:

$$\frac{T'}{kT} = \frac{X''}{X} = -\lambda$$

Solutions:
$$X_n(x) = \sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right), \quad T_n(t) = e^{-k(n\pi/L)^2t}$$

Full solution:
$$u(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty b_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)e^{-k(n\pi/L)^2t}$$

Coefficients from Fourier sine series of $f(x)$.

```desmos-graph
y = sin(pi*x)*e^(-0.5*t)
t = 0
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Heat distribution smooths out over time, approaching equilibrium.

## Wave Equation (Hyperbolic)

### Standard Form

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2}$$

Models vibrations of string, sound waves, etc.

### Physical Meaning

- $u(x,t)$: Displacement at position $x$, time $t$
- $c$: Wave speed
- Acceleration proportional to curvature

### D'Alembert's Solution

For infinite domain with initial conditions:
$$u(x,0) = f(x), \quad u_t(x,0) = g(x)$$

Solution:
$$u(x,t) = \frac{1}{2}[f(x-ct) + f(x+ct)] + \frac{1}{2c}\int_{x-ct}^{x+ct}g(s)\,ds$$

Two traveling waves moving in opposite directions!

### Vibrating String (Finite Domain)

Boundary value problem:
$$\begin{aligned} u_{tt} &= c^2u_{xx}, \quad 0 < x < L \\ u(0,t) &= u(L,t) = 0 \\ u(x,0) &= f(x), \quad u_t(x,0) = g(x) \end{aligned}$$

Solution:
$$u(x,t) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \left[A_n\cos\left(\frac{n\pi ct}{L}\right) + B_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi ct}{L}\right)\right]\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)$$

Standing waves with frequencies $\omega_n = \frac{n\pi c}{L}$.

## Laplace's Equation (Elliptic)

### Standard Form

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} + \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial y^2} = 0$$

or $\nabla^2 u = 0$ (Laplacian).

### Applications

- Steady-state heat distribution
- Electrostatic potential
- Incompressible fluid flow
- Gravitational potential

### Properties

**Harmonic functions** (solutions to Laplace's equation):
- Satisfy mean value property
- No local maxima or minima (maximum principle)
- Infinitely differentiable

### Dirichlet Problem

Find $u$ such that:
$$\nabla^2 u = 0 \text{ in } D, \quad u = f \text{ on } \partial D$$

Unique solution exists for reasonable domains.

### Example: Rectangle

Solve $\nabla^2 u = 0$ on $0 < x < a$, $0 < y < b$ with:
$$u(0,y) = u(a,y) = u(x,0) = 0, \quad u(x,b) = f(x)$$

Solution:
$$u(x,y) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty A_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{a}\right)\sinh\left(\frac{n\pi y}{a}\right)$$

where $A_n$ from Fourier sine series of $f(x)$.

## Method of Separation of Variables

### General Procedure

1. Assume product solution: $u(x,t) = X(x)T(t)$ or $u(x,y) = X(x)Y(y)$
2. Substitute into PDE
3. Separate variables (each side depends on only one variable)
4. Set equal to separation constant $-\lambda$
5. Solve resulting ODEs
6. Apply boundary conditions to find eigenvalues
7. Superpose all solutions

### When It Works

- Linear PDE
- Homogeneous boundary conditions
- Simple geometry (rectangle, circle, sphere)

## Fourier Series Solutions

### Connection to PDEs

Many PDE solutions involve Fourier series because:
- Eigenfunctions of $\frac{d^2}{dx^2}$ are sines and cosines
- Boundary conditions select specific frequencies
- Initial conditions determine coefficients

### Common Expansions

**Fourier sine series** (for $u(0) = u(L) = 0$):
$$f(x) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty b_n\sin\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)$$

**Fourier cosine series** (for $u'(0) = u'(L) = 0$):
$$f(x) = \frac{a_0}{2} + \sum_{n=1}^\infty a_n\cos\left(\frac{n\pi x}{L}\right)$$

## Higher Dimensions

### 2D Heat Equation

$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} = k\nabla^2 u = k(u_{xx} + u_{yy})$$

Separate: $u(x,y,t) = X(x)Y(y)T(t)$

### 3D Wave Equation

$$\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial t^2} = c^2\nabla^2 u = c^2(u_{xx} + u_{yy} + u_{zz})$$

Models sound waves, electromagnetic waves.

### Polar Coordinates

Laplace's equation in polar coordinates:
$$\nabla^2 u = \frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial r^2} + \frac{1}{r}\frac{\partial u}{\partial r} + \frac{1}{r^2}\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial \theta^2} = 0$$

Solutions involve Bessel functions for circular domains.

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Misclassifying PDE Type
> Check discriminant carefully. Type can change if coefficients are variable.

> [!warning] Forgetting Boundary Conditions
> PDEs require both initial AND boundary conditions for unique solution.

> [!warning] Incorrect Separation Constant Sign
> Sign of $-\lambda$ determined by boundary conditions. Wrong sign gives no nontrivial solutions.

## Practice Problems

1. Classify the PDE $u_{xx} + 4u_{xy} + 4u_{yy} = 0$ as elliptic, parabolic, or hyperbolic.
2. Verify that $u(x,t) = e^{-kt}\sin(x)$ satisfies the heat equation $u_t = ku_{xx}$.
3. Find d'Alembert's solution for the wave equation with $u(x,0) = \sin(x)$ and $u_t(x,0) = 0$.
4. Solve the Dirichlet problem for Laplace's equation on the unit square with $u(x,0) = \sin(\pi x)$ and zero on other boundaries.
5. Use separation of variables to solve $u_t = u_{xx}$ with $u(0,t) = u(\pi,t) = 0$ and $u(x,0) = \sin(2x) + 3\sin(5x)$.
6. Explain why Laplace's equation has no time dependence.
7. Derive the general solution to the 1D wave equation using characteristic coordinates.
8. Compare the physical behavior of solutions to heat vs wave equations.
