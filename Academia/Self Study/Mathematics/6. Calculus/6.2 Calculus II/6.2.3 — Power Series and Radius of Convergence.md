### **6.2.3.1 What is a Power Series?**

A **power series centered at $a$** has the form:

∑n=0∞cn(x−a)n\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n (x - a)^nn=0∑∞cn(x−a)n

- $a$ → **center** of the series
- cnc_ncn → coefficients
- Special case a=0a = 0a=0: **Maclaurin series**

---

### **6.2.3.2 Convergence of a Power Series**

For a power series, there are **only three possibilities**:

1. Converges **only at** x=ax = ax=a
2. Converges for **all real $x$**
3. Converges for:

∣x−a∣<R|x - a| < R∣x−a∣<R

where $R$ is the **radius of convergence**

---

### **6.2.3.3 Radius and Interval of Convergence**

- **Radius of convergence:** $R$
- **Interval of convergence:** (a−R,  a+R)(a - R,\; a + R)(a−R,a+R)
- Endpoints must be checked **separately**

L<1⇒∣x−a∣<RL < 1 \Rightarrow |x - a| < RL<1⇒∣x−a∣<R
