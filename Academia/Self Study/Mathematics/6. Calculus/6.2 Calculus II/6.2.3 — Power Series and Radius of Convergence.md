## What is a Power Series?

A power series centered at $a$:

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} c_n(x - a)^n$$

|Term|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$a$|Centre of the series|
|$c_n$|Coefficients|
|$x$|Variable|

When $a = 0$ this is a **Maclaurin series** — see [[6.2.4_Taylor_and_Maclaurin_Series]].

---

## Convergence — Three Possibilities

1. Converges **only at** $x = a$
2. Converges for **all** $x \in \mathbb{R}$
3. Converges for $|x - a| < R$ — where $R$ is the **radius of convergence**

The set of all $x$ where it converges is the **interval of convergence** $(a - R,\ a + R)$.

> [!warning] Always check endpoints separately The ratio test determines $R$ but gives no information about convergence at $x = a \pm R$.

---

## Finding $R$ — Ratio Test

Apply the ratio test to $\sum c_n(x-a)^n$:

$$L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left|\frac{c_{n+1}}{c_n}\right| |x - a| < 1 \implies R = \lim_{n\to\infty} \left|\frac{c_n}{c_{n+1}}\right|$$

---

## Worked Examples

**Example 1** — $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!}$

$$L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{|x|}{n+1} = 0 \quad \text{for all } x \implies R = \infty$$

**Example 2** — $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n$

$$L = |x| < 1 \implies R = 1, \quad \text{interval } (-1, 1)$$

**Example 3** — $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(x-2)^n}{n}$

Ratio test gives $|x-2| < 1 \implies (1, 3)$.

Endpoint test:

- $x = 1$: alternating harmonic series → **converges**
- $x = 3$: harmonic series → **diverges**

Interval of convergence: $[1, 3)$

---

## Operations on Power Series

Within the radius of convergence, power series can be **differentiated and integrated term-by-term**.

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n = \frac{1}{1-x} \implies \frac{d}{dx}\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} nx^{n-1} = \frac{1}{(1-x)^2}$$
