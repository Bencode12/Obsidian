## Taylor Series — General Form

A **Taylor series** represents $f(x)$ as a power series centered at $a$:

$$f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(a)}{n!}(x - a)^n$$

where $f^{(n)}(a)$ is the $n$-th derivative of $f$ evaluated at $x = a$.

---

## Maclaurin Series

A Taylor series centered at $a = 0$:

$$f(x) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(0)}{n!} x^n$$

---

## Common Maclaurin Series

|Function|Series|Interval|
|---|---|---|
|$e^x$|$\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{x^n}{n!}$|$(-\infty, \infty)$|
|$\sin x$|$\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}$|$(-\infty, \infty)$|
|$\cos x$|$\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^n \frac{x^{2n}}{(2n)!}$|$(-\infty, \infty)$|
|$\dfrac{1}{1-x}$|$\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} x^n$|$(-1, 1)$|
|$\ln(1+x)$|$\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} (-1)^{n+1} \frac{x^n}{n}$|$(-1, 1]$|

---

## Deriving a Taylor Series

> [!example] Find the Taylor series of $e^x$ centered at $a = 1$.
> 
> All derivatives of $e^x$ are $e^x$, so $f^{(n)}(1) = e$. $$e^x = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{e}{n!}(x - 1)^n$$

---

## Approximating Functions

Using the first few terms as a polynomial approximation:

$$\sin x \approx x - \frac{x^3}{6} + \frac{x^5}{120}$$

> [!example] $\sin(0.1) \approx 0.1 - \dfrac{0.001}{6} + \dfrac{0.00001}{120} \approx 0.09983$

---

## Error Bound (Remainder)

After $n$ terms, the error is bounded by:

$$|R_n(x)| \leq \frac{M|x - a|^{n+1}}{(n+1)!}$$

where $M = \max |f^{(n+1)}|$ on the interval.
