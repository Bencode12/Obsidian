## What are Parametric Equations?

Instead of $y = f(x)$, both $x$ and $y$ are expressed as functions of a **parameter** $t$:

$$x = x(t) \qquad y = y(t)$$

As $t$ varies, the point $(x(t), y(t))$ traces a curve.

> [!example] $x = \cos t,\ y = \sin t$ traces the **unit circle**.

---

## Eliminating the Parameter

> [!example] $x = t^2,\ y = t^3$ $t = \sqrt{x} \implies y = x^{3/2}$

---

## First Derivative

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{dy/dt}{dx/dt}$$

> [!example] $x = t^2 + 1,\ y = t^3$ $$\frac{dx}{dt} = 2t \qquad \frac{dy}{dt} = 3t^2 \implies \frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{3t^2}{2t} = \frac{3t}{2}$$

---

## Second Derivative

$$\frac{d^2y}{dx^2} = \frac{\dfrac{d}{dt}!\left(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\right)}{\dfrac{dx}{dt}}$$

Used for concavity analysis.

---

## Tangent Line at $t = t_0$

|Quantity|Formula|
|---|---|
|Point|$(x(t_0),\ y(t_0))$|
|Slope|$\left.\dfrac{dy}{dx}\right|
|Equation|$y - y(t_0) = \left(\dfrac{dy}{dx}\right)_{t_0}(x - x(t_0))$|

---

## Area Under a Parametric Curve

$$A = \int_a^b y(t),\frac{dx}{dt},dt$$

---

## Arc Length

$$L = \int_a^b \sqrt{\left(\frac{dx}{dt}\right)^2 + \left(\frac{dy}{dt}\right)^2},dt$$

## Practice Problems

1. Find $\dfrac{dy}{dx}$ for $x = e^t,\ y = t^2$.
2. Find the equation of the tangent at $t = 1$ for $x = t^2,\ y = t^3$.
3. Find the arc length of $x = 3t,\ y = 4t$ from $t = 0$ to $t = 1$.
4. Eliminate the parameter: $x = 2\cos t,\ y = 3\sin t$.
