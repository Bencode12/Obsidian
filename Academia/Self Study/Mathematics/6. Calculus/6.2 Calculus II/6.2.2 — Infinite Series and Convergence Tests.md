## What is an Infinite Series?

An infinite series is the sum of the terms of an infinite sequence:

$$\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \cdots$$

The **partial sum** $S_n = a_1 + a_2 + \cdots + a_n$.

|Type|Condition|
|---|---|
|**Convergent**|$\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} S_n = S$ (finite)|
|**Divergent**|Limit does not exist|

---

## Geometric Series

$$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n = \frac{a}{1-r} \quad \text{if } |r| < 1 \qquad \text{diverges if } |r| \geq 1$$

> [!example] $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^n = \frac{1}{1 - 1/3} = \frac{3}{2}$

## Convergence Tests

|Test|Condition for convergence|Notes|
|---|---|---|
|**$n$-th term test**|$\lim a_n = 0$ required|If $\lim a_n \neq 0$ → diverges. If $= 0$ → inconclusive|
|**Comparison test**|$a_n \leq b_n$ and $\sum b_n$ converges|Or $a_n \geq b_n$ and $\sum b_n$ diverges|
|**Ratio test**|$L = \lim\left\|\dfrac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right\| < 1$|$L > 1$ diverges; $L = 1$ inconclusive|
|**Alternating series (Leibniz)**|$a_n$ decreases monotonically to $0$|For series $\sum(-1)^n a_n$|

## Worked Examples

**Geometric:** $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty}\left(\frac{1}{3}\right)^n = \frac{3}{2}$ ✓

**Harmonic** $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{1}{n}$ — diverges (classic result).

**Ratio test** on $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty}\frac{2^n}{n!}$:

$$L = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{2^{n+1}/(n+1)!}{2^n/n!} = \lim_{n\to\infty} \frac{2}{n+1} = 0 < 1 \implies \text{converges}$$

## Practice Problems

1. Determine whether $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{n}{n+1}$ converges.
2. Find the sum of $\displaystyle\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \left(\frac{2}{5}\right)^n$.
3. Test $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{3^n}{n!}$ using the ratio test.
4. Test $\displaystyle\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n}{n}$ using the alternating series test.
