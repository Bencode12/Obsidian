### **6.2.2.1 What is an infinite series?**

An **infinite series** is the sum of the terms of an infinite sequence:

∑n=1∞an=a1+a2+a3+…\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n = a_1 + a_2 + a_3 + \dotsn=1∑∞an=a1+a2+a3+…

- Unlike a finite series, it **may or may not converge** to a finite number.
- **Partial sum**: Sn=a1+a2+…+anS_n = a_1 + a_2 + … + a_nSn=a1+a2+…+an

---

### **6.2.2.2 Convergence and Divergence**

- **Convergent series:** lim⁡n→∞Sn=S$\lim_{n \to \infty} S_n$ = Slimn→∞Sn=S exists
- **Divergent series:** lim⁡n→∞Sn$\lim_{n \to \infty} S_nlimn→∞Sn$ does not exist

**Example:**  
∑n=1∞12n=1/2+1/4+1/8+…=1\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} $\frac{1}{2^n}$ = 1/2 + 1/4 + 1/8 + … = 1∑n=1∞2n1=1/2+1/4+1/8+…=1 (convergent)  
∑n=1∞1=1+1+1+…\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} 1 = 1 + 1 + 1 + …∑n=1∞1=1+1+1+… (divergent)

### **6.2.2.3 Geometric Series**

- General form: a+ar+ar2+ar3+…=∑n=0∞arna + ar + ar^2 + ar^3 + … = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^na+ar+ar2+ar3+…=∑n=0∞arn
- Converges if ∣r∣<1|r| < 1∣r∣<1

∑n=0∞arn=a1−r\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} ar^n = $\frac{a}{1 - r}$n=0∑∞arn=1−ra

- Diverges if ∣r∣≥1|r| \ge 1∣r∣≥1

### **6.2.2.4 Convergence Tests**

**1. nth-Term Test**

- If lim⁡n→∞an≠0$\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n$ \neq 0limn→∞an=0, the series **diverges**
- If lim⁡n→∞an=0$\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n$ = 0limn→∞an=0, test is inconclusive

**2. Comparison Test**

- Compare with a known convergent/divergent series

**3. Ratio Test**

- Let L=lim⁡n→∞∣an+1an∣L = $\lim_{n\to\infty} \left|\frac{a_{n+1}}{a_n}\right|L=limn→∞anan+1$
    - L<1⇒L < 1 \RightarrowL<1⇒ convergent
    - L>1⇒L > 1 \RightarrowL>1⇒ divergent
    - L=1⇒L = 1 \RightarrowL=1⇒ inconclusive

**4. Alternating Series Test (Leibniz Test)**

- Series of the form ∑(−1)nan\sum (-1)^n a_n∑(−1)nan
- Convergent if ana_nan decreases monotonically to 0
