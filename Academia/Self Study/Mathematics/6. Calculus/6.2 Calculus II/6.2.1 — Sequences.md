## What is a Sequence?

A sequence is an **ordered list of numbers**:

$$a_1, a_2, a_3, \ldots, a_n, \ldots$$

- $a_n$ → the **$n$-th term**
- Sequences can be **finite** (have a last term) or **infinite** (continue forever)

---

## Types of Sequences

### Arithmetic Sequence (AP)

The difference between consecutive terms is **constant**:

$$d = a_n - a_{n-1}$$

**General term:**

$$a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$$

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$a_1$|First term|
|$d$|Common difference|
|$n$|Term number|

> [!example] Sequence: $3, 7, 11, 15, \ldots$ $d = 4$, so $a_n = 3 + (n-1) \cdot 4 = 4n - 1$

---

### Geometric Sequence (GP)

The ratio between consecutive terms is **constant**:

$$r = \frac{a_n}{a_{n-1}}$$

**General term:**

$$a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}$$

|Symbol|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$a_1$|First term|
|$r$|Common ratio|
|$n$|Term number|

> [!example] Sequence: $2, 6, 18, 54, \ldots$ $r = 3$, so $a_n = 2 \cdot 3^{n-1}$

---

### Special Sequences

**Fibonacci sequence:** $0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, \ldots$

$$a_n = a_{n-1} + a_{n-2}$$

Each term is the **sum of the two preceding terms**. This is a **recurrence relation** — the sequence is defined in terms of itself.

---

## Convergence of Sequences

A sequence **converges** if its terms approach a finite limit $L$ as $n \to \infty$:

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} a_n = L$$

A sequence **diverges** if the limit does not exist or approaches $\pm\infty$.

|Type|Condition|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Convergent**|$\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n = L$ (finite)|$\dfrac{1}{n} \to 0$|
|**Divergent**|limit does not exist or $\to \infty$|$n^2 \to \infty$|

> [!example] $\displaystyle\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} = 0$ → **convergent**
> 
> $\displaystyle\lim_{n \to \infty} (-1)^n$ → does not exist → **divergent**

> [!tip] Quick test for GPs A geometric sequence converges if and only if $|r| < 1$. If $|r| \geq 1$ (and $r \neq 0$), it diverges.
