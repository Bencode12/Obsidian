## What is a Sequence?

An ordered list of numbers $a_1, a_2, a_3, \ldots, a_n, \ldots$ where $a_n$ is the **$n$-th term**. Sequences can be finite or infinite.

---

## Types of Sequences

### Arithmetic Sequence (AP)

$$d = a_n - a_{n-1} \qquad a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$$

> [!example] $3, 7, 11, 15, \ldots$ — $d = 4$, so $a_n = 4n - 1$

### Geometric Sequence (GP)

$$r = \frac{a_n}{a_{n-1}} \qquad a_n = a_1 \cdot r^{n-1}$$

> [!example] $2, 6, 18, 54, \ldots$ — $r = 3$, so $a_n = 2 \cdot 3^{n-1}$

### Fibonacci Sequence

$$a_n = a_{n-1} + a_{n-2} \qquad 0, 1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, \ldots$$

A **recurrence relation** — each term defined by the two before it.

---

## Convergence

|Type|Condition|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Convergent**|$\displaystyle\lim_{n\to\infty} a_n = L$ (finite)|$\dfrac{1}{n} \to 0$|
|**Divergent**|Limit does not exist or $\to \infty$|$n^2 \to \infty$|

$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} = 0 \quad \text{(convergent)} \qquad \lim_{n \to \infty} (-1)^n \quad \text{does not exist (divergent)}$$

> [!tip] GP convergence test A geometric sequence converges if and only if $|r| < 1$.
