## Function Approximation

Taylor polynomials approximate functions **near the centre $a$**.

> [!example] Approximate $\sqrt{1+x}$ for small $x$: $$\sqrt{1+x} = (1+x)^{1/2} \approx 1 + \frac{x}{2} - \frac{x^2}{8} + \frac{x^3}{16}$$ Used heavily in physics and engineering for small-signal approximations.

---

## Evaluating Limits

Taylor series resolve indeterminate forms without L'Hôpital's rule.

> [!example] $$\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{\sin x - x}{x^3}$$ Substitute $\sin x = x - \dfrac{x^3}{6} + \cdots$: $$= \lim_{x\to 0} \frac{-\frac{x^3}{6}}{x^3} = -\frac{1}{6}$$

---

## Approximating Integrals

Some integrals have no closed form — expand the integrand as a series and integrate term-by-term.

> [!example] $$\int_0^{0.2} e^{-x^2},dx$$ Use $e^{-x^2} \approx 1 - x^2 + \dfrac{x^4}{2} - \cdots$ then integrate each term.

---

## Solving Differential Equations (Preview)

Some ODEs are solved by assuming $y = \sum a_n x^n$ and matching coefficients. For example $y'' + y = 0$ yields $\sin x$ and $\cos x$ as natural series solutions.

## Error Estimation

The remainder after $n$ terms satisfies:

$$|R_n| \leq \frac{|x|^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} \cdot M$$

where $M$ bounds $|f^{(n+1)}|$ on the interval. Increase $n$ until the bound falls below the required tolerance.

> [!example] To approximate $e^{0.1}$ within $10^{-5}$: test increasing $n$ until $\dfrac{(0.1)^{n+1}}{(n+1)!} < 10^{-5}$.

## Practice Problems

1. Use Taylor series to find $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x^2}$.
2. Approximate $\displaystyle\int_0^{0.1} \sin(x^2),dx$ using the first two nonzero terms.
3. How many terms of the $e^x$ series are needed to approximate $e^{0.5}$ within $10^{-4}$?
4. Use the series for $\ln(1+x)$ to evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{x\to 0} \frac{\ln(1+x)}{x}$.
