## The Definite Integral

A definite integral computes the **net area** under a curve between $x = a$ and $x = b$:

$$\int_a^b f(x),dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

where $F(x)$ is any antiderivative of $f(x)$. The result is a **number**, not a function.

---

## Geometric Interpretation

|Region|Contribution|
|---|---|
|Above $x$-axis|Positive|
|Below $x$-axis|Negative|
|Net area|Sum of both (may cancel)|

> [!tip] Total area vs net area To find **total** area when the curve dips below the $x$-axis, split the integral at each zero and take absolute values of each part.

---

## Properties

|Property|Rule|
|---|---|
|Reversed limits|$\displaystyle\int_a^b f = -\int_b^a f$|
|Same limits|$\displaystyle\int_a^a f = 0$|
|Sum|$\displaystyle\int_a^b [f + g] = \int_a^b f + \int_a^b g$|
|Constant multiple|$\displaystyle\int_a^b kf = k\int_a^b f$|
|Split interval|$\displaystyle\int_a^b f = \int_a^c f + \int_c^b f$|

---

## Area under a Curve — Method

1. Identify the interval $[a, b]$
2. Compute $\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x),dx = F(b) - F(a)$
3. If asked for total area, split at zeros and sum absolute values

> [!example] Area under $f(x) = x^2$ from $0$ to $2$: $$\int_0^2 x^2,dx = \left[\frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^2 = \frac{8}{3}$$

---

## Area Between Two Curves

$$\text{Area} = \int_a^b \bigl[f(x) - g(x)\bigr],dx$$

where $f(x) \geq g(x)$ on $[a, b]$.

> [!example] Area between $y = x$ and $y = x^2$ from $0$ to $1$: $$\int_0^1 (x - x^2),dx = \left[\frac{x^2}{2} - \frac{x^3}{3}\right]_0^1 = \frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{3} = \frac{1}{6}$$

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_1^3 (2x + 1),dx$.
2. Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_0^{\pi} \sin x,dx$.
3. Find the area under $f(x) = x^3$ from $x = 0$ to $x = 2$.
4. Find the area between $y = x^2$ and $y = x$ from $x = 0$ to $x = 1$.
5. Evaluate $\displaystyle\int_{-1}^{1} x^3,dx$ and explain the result geometrically.
