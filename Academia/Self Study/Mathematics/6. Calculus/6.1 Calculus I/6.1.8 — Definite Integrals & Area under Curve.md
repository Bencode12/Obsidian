### **6.1.8.1 Definite Integral**

A **definite integral** computes the **net area under a curve** between two points x=ax = ax=a and x=bx = bx=b:

‚ąęabf(x)dx=F(b)‚ąíF(a)\int_a^b f(x) dx = F(b) - F(a)‚ąęabf(x)dx=F(b)‚ąíF(a)

- $F(x)$ is any **antiderivative** of $f(x)$ (F‚Ä≤(x)=f(x)F'(x) = f(x)F‚Ä≤(x)=f(x))
- Gives a **numerical value**, unlike indefinite integral

### **6.1.8.2 Geometric Interpretation**

- Area **above x-axis** ‚Üí positive
- Area **below x-axis** ‚Üí negative
- Net area = **sum of positive and negative areas**

Example:

‚ąę02xdx=[x22]02=2\int_0^2 x dx = \left[$\frac{x^2}{2}$\right]_0^2 = 2‚ąę02xdx=[2x2]02=2
