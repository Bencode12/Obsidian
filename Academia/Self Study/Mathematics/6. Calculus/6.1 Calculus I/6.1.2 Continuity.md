## What is Continuity?

A function is **continuous at a point** if you can draw its graph through that point **without lifting the pen**.

Formally, $f(x)$ is continuous at $x = a$ if **all three** conditions hold:

1. $f(a)$ is defined
2. $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ exists
3. $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)$

If **any one** fails, the function is **discontinuous** at $x = a$.

---

## Types of Discontinuity

### Removable (hole)

The limit exists, but $f(a)$ is missing or has the wrong value.

$$f(x) = \begin{cases} \dfrac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} & x \neq 1 \ 5 & x = 1 \end{cases}$$

$$\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = 2 \neq f(1) = 5 \quad \longrightarrow \quad \text{removable}$$

### Jump

Left and right limits exist but are **not equal**.

$$f(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & x < 0 \ 3 & x \geq 0 \end{cases}$$

### Infinite

The function blows up to $\pm\infty$.

$$f(x) = \frac{1}{x} \quad \text{at } x = 0$$

---

## Continuity on an Interval

|Interval|Condition|
|---|---|
|Open $(a, b)$|Continuous at every interior point|
|Closed $[a, b]$|Continuous on $(a, b)$ + right-continuous at $a$ + left-continuous at $b$|

---

## Functions Continuous Everywhere They Are Defined

- Polynomials
- Rational functions (except where denominator $= 0$)
- Exponential and logarithmic functions
- Trigonometric functions

---

## Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT)

If $f$ is continuous on $[a, b]$ and $k$ is any value between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, then there exists some $c \in (a, b)$ such that $f(c) = k$.

> [!tip] IVT guarantees existence — it does not find $c$ It is used to prove that a root or solution must exist in an interval.

## Practice Problems

1. Is $f(x) = \dfrac{x^2 - 4}{x - 2}$ continuous at $x = 2$? Classify the discontinuity.
2. Find all points of discontinuity of $f(x) = \dfrac{x + 3}{x^2 - x - 6}$.
3. Use the IVT to show that $f(x) = x^3 - x - 1$ has a root in $[1, 2]$.
4. A function has $f(1) = 3$ and $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 1} f(x) = 3$ but $f(1)$ is defined as $5$. Is it continuous at $x = 1$?
