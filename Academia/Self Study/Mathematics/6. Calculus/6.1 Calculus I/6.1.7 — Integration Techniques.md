## Substitution (u-substitution)

Use when the integral contains a function **and its derivative**.

**Steps:**

1. Choose $u = g(x)$ (the inner function)
2. Compute $du = g'(x),dx$
3. Rewrite the integral in terms of $u$
4. Integrate with respect to $u$
5. Substitute $x$ back

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int 2x\cos(x^2),dx$
> 
> Let $u = x^2 \implies du = 2x,dx$ $$= \int \cos(u),du = \sin(u) + C = \sin(x^2) + C$$

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int \frac{dx}{x\ln x}$
> 
> Let $u = \ln x \implies du = \dfrac{dx}{x}$ $$= \int \frac{du}{u} = \ln|u| + C = \ln|\ln x| + C$$

## Integration by Parts

Use when the integral is a **product of two functions**.

$$\int u,dv = uv - \int v,du$$

> [!tip] LIATE — choose $u$ in this order **L**ogarithms, **I**nverse trig, **A**lgebraic, **T**rig, **E**xponential. The function earlier in the list is usually the better choice for $u$.

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int xe^x,dx$
> 
> $u = x \implies du = dx$ · $dv = e^x,dx \implies v = e^x$ $$= xe^x - \int e^x,dx = xe^x - e^x + C = e^x(x-1) + C$$

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int \ln x,dx$
> 
> $u = \ln x \implies du = \frac{dx}{x}$ · $dv = dx \implies v = x$ $$= x\ln x - \int x \cdot \frac{1}{x},dx = x\ln x - x + C$$
