### **6.1.5.1 Slope of Tangent and Normal**

**Tangent Line:**

- A line that touches the curve **at a point** without crossing it (locally).
- Slope at x=ax = ax=a:

mtangent=f′(a)m_\text{tangent} = f'(a)mtangent​=f′(a)

- Equation of tangent:

y−f(a)=f′(a)(x−a)y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)y−f(a)=f′(a)(x−a)

**Normal Line:**

- Perpendicular to tangent at same point
- Slope:

mnormal=−1f′(a)m_\text{normal} = -\frac{1}{f'(a)}mnormal​=−f′(a)1​

- Equation:

y−f(a)=−1f′(a)(x−a)y - f(a) = -\frac{1}{f'(a)}(x - a)y−f(a)=−f′(a)1​(x−a)

**Example:**  
y=x2y = x^2y=x2 at x=1x = 1x=1

- f′(x)=2xf'(x) = 2xf′(x)=2x → slope of tangent = 2
- Tangent equation: y−1=2(x−1)⇒y=2x−1y - 1 = 2(x - 1) \Rightarrow y = 2x - 1y−1=2(x−1)⇒y=2x−1
- Normal slope: −1/2-1/2−1/2 → equation: y−1=−12(x−1)y - 1 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - 1)y−1=−21​(x−1)

### **6.1.5.3 Maxima and Minima**

**Critical points:**

- Points where f′(x)=0f'(x) = 0f′(x)=0 or f′(x)f'(x)f′(x) is undefined.

**Second derivative test:**

- f′′(x)>0f''(x) > 0 f′′(x)>0 → **local minimum**
- f′′(x)<0f''(x) < 0 f′′(x)<0 → **local maximum**
- f′′(x)=0f''(x) = 0 f′′(x)=0 → inconclusive

**Example:**  
f(x)=x3−3x2+2f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 2f(x)=x3−3x2+2

1. f′(x)=3x2−6x=3x(x−2)f'(x) = 3x^2 - 6x = 3x(x-2)f′(x)=3x2−6x=3x(x−2) → critical points: x=0,2
2. f′′(x)=6x−6f''(x) = 6x - 6f′′(x)=6x−6

- x=0: f′′(0)=−6<0f''(0) = -6 <0f′′(0)=−6<0 → local max
- x=2: f′′(2)=6>0f''(2) = 6 >0f′′(2)=6>0 → local min
