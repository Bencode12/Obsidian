## Tangent and Normal Lines

|Line|Slope|Equation|
|---|---|---|
|**Tangent** at $x = a$|$m_t = f'(a)$|$y - f(a) = f'(a)(x - a)$|
|**Normal** at $x = a$|$m_n = -\dfrac{1}{f'(a)}$|$y - f(a) = -\dfrac{1}{f'(a)}(x - a)$|

> [!tip] Tangent and normal are perpendicular — their slopes multiply to $-1$.

> [!example] $y = x^2$ at $x = 1$: $f'(x) = 2x \implies f'(1) = 2$ Tangent: $y - 1 = 2(x - 1) \implies y = 2x - 1$ Normal: $y - 1 = -\frac{1}{2}(x - 1)$

## Increasing and Decreasing Functions

|Condition|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$f'(x) > 0$ on an interval|$f$ is **increasing**|
|$f'(x) < 0$ on an interval|$f$ is **decreasing**|
|$f'(x) = 0$|Possible turning point|

## Critical Points and Maxima / Minima

**Critical points** occur where $f'(x) = 0$ or $f'(x)$ is undefined.

### Second Derivative Test

|$f''(a)$|Conclusion|
|---|---|
|$> 0$|Local **minimum** at $x = a$|
|$< 0$|Local **maximum** at $x = a$|
|$= 0$|Inconclusive — use first derivative test|

> [!example] $f(x) = x^3 - 3x^2 + 2$ $f'(x) = 3x^2 - 6x = 3x(x-2) = 0 \implies x = 0,\ 2$ $f''(x) = 6x - 6$ $f''(0) = -6 < 0$ → local **max** at $x = 0$ $f''(2) = 6 > 0$ → local **min** at $x = 2$

---

## Points of Inflection

A point of inflection is where the curve **changes concavity**.

**Condition:** $f''(x) = 0$ **and** the sign of $f''(x)$ changes across that point.
