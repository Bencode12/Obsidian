### **6.1.1 Limits**

### **What is a limit?**

A **limit** describes the value that a function **approaches** as the input approaches a certain number.

Notation:

$$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$$

This means: as $x$ gets closer to $a$, $f(x)$ gets closer to $L$.

---

### **Intuitive understanding**

- The limit is about **behavior near a point**, not necessarily at the point.
- A function may have a limit even if:
    - $f(a)$ is undefined
    - $f(a)$ ≠ limit

Example:

$$f(x) = \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1}$$

At $x = 1$, function is undefined.  
But:

$$\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} = \lim_{x \to 1} (x + 1) = 2$$

---

### **One-sided limits**

- **Left-hand limit**:

$$\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x)$$

- **Right-hand limit**:

$$\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x)$$

The limit exists **only if both are equal**.

---

### **Basic limit laws**

If limits exist:

1. $\lim (f(x) + g(x)) = \lim f(x) + \lim g(x)$
2. $\lim (cf(x)) = c \lim f(x)$
3. $\lim (f(x)g(x)) = (\lim f(x))(\lim g(x))$
4. $\lim \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \frac{\lim f(x)}{\lim g(x)}$, $g(x) \neq 0$

---

### **Standard limits**

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1$$

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0$$

$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0$$

---

### **Limits involving infinity**

- Horizontal asymptotes

$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x^n} = 0 \quad (n > 0)$$

Example:

$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2 + 1}{5x^2 - x} = \frac{3}{5}$$

---

### **Continuity (preview)**

A function is continuous at $x = a$ if:

1. $f(a)$ is defined
2. $\lim_{x \to a} f(x)$ exists
3. $\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)$
