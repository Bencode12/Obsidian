## What is a Limit?

A limit describes the value a function **approaches** as the input approaches a certain number.

$$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$$

As $x$ gets closer to $a$, $f(x)$ gets closer to $L$ — even if $f(a)$ is undefined or $f(a) \neq L$.

> [!example] $$\lim_{x \to 1} \frac{x^2 - 1}{x - 1} = \lim_{x \to 1}(x + 1) = 2$$ The function is undefined at $x = 1$, but the limit still exists.

---

## One-Sided Limits

|Notation|Name|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|$\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a^-} f(x)$|Left-hand limit|$x$ approaches $a$ from below|
|$\displaystyle\lim_{x \to a^+} f(x)$|Right-hand limit|$x$ approaches $a$ from above|

The two-sided limit exists **only if both one-sided limits are equal**.

---

## Limit Laws

If $\lim f(x)$ and $\lim g(x)$ both exist:

|Law|Rule|
|---|---|
|Sum|$\lim[f(x) + g(x)] = \lim f(x) + \lim g(x)$|
|Constant multiple|$\lim[cf(x)] = c\lim f(x)$|
|Product|$\lim[f(x)g(x)] = \lim f(x) \cdot \lim g(x)$|
|Quotient|$\lim\dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)} = \dfrac{\lim f(x)}{\lim g(x)} \quad (g(x) \neq 0)$|

## Standard Limits

$$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin x}{x} = 1 \qquad \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{1 - \cos x}{x} = 0 \qquad \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x} = 0$$

---

## Limits at Infinity

$$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{1}{x^n} = 0 \quad (n > 0)$$

> [!tip] Rational functions at infinity — divide by highest power $$\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{3x^2 + 1}{5x^2 - x} = \frac{3}{5}$$ Divide numerator and denominator by $x^2$; lower-order terms vanish.

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 3} (x^2 - 2x + 1)$.
2. Evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{\sin 3x}{x}$. _(Hint: use the standard limit.)_
3. Evaluate $\displaystyle\lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{2x^3 + x}{4x^3 - 1}$.
4. For $f(x) = \begin{cases} x + 1 & x < 2 \ 3x - 1 & x \geq 2 \end{cases}$, find $\lim_{x \to 2^-}$ and $\lim_{x \to 2^+}$. Does the limit exist?
