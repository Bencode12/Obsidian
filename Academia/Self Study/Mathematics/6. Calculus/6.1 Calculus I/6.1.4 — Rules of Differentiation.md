## Product Rule

When $y = f(x)g(x)$:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)$$

> [!example] $y = x^2 \sin x$ $$y' = 2x\sin x + x^2\cos x$$

---

## Quotient Rule

When $y = \dfrac{f(x)}{g(x)}$:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{f'(x)g(x) - f(x)g'(x)}{[g(x)]^2}$$

> [!example] $y = \dfrac{x^2}{x+1}$ $$y' = \frac{2x(x+1) - x^2}{(x+1)^2} = \frac{x^2 + 2x}{(x+1)^2}$$

---

## Chain Rule

When $y = f(g(x))$ — a function nested inside another:

$$\frac{dy}{dx} = f'(g(x)) \cdot g'(x)$$

> [!example] $y = (3x + 2)^5$ Outer: $u^5$, inner: $u = 3x + 2$ $$y' = 5(3x+2)^4 \cdot 3 = 15(3x+2)^4$$

---

## Combined Examples

**Product rule:**

$$y = xe^x \implies y' = e^x + xe^x = e^x(1 + x)$$

**Quotient rule:**

$$y = \frac{\sin x}{x} \implies y' = \frac{x\cos x - \sin x}{x^2}$$

**Chain rule:**

$$y = \sin(x^2) \implies y' = \cos(x^2) \cdot 2x$$

---

## Logarithmic Differentiation

Used when the function involves a variable in both base and exponent.

> [!example] $y = x^x$ Take $\ln$ of both sides: $\ln y = x\ln x$ Differentiate: $\dfrac{1}{y}y' = \ln x + 1$ Solve: $y' = x^x(\ln x + 1)$

## Practice Problems

1. Differentiate $y = x^3 \cos x$ using the product rule.
2. Differentiate $y = \dfrac{e^x}{x^2 + 1}$ using the quotient rule.
3. Differentiate $y = (x^2 + 3)^7$ using the chain rule.
4. Differentiate $y = \ln(\sin x)$.
5. Differentiate $y = e^{\cos x}$.
6. Find $y'$ for $y = x^{\sin x}$ using logarithmic differentiation.
