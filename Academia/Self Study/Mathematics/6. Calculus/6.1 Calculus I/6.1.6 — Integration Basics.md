### **6.1.6.1 What is Integration?**

**Integration** is the **reverse process of differentiation**.

- If FÔÇ▓(x)=f(x)F'(x) = f(x)FÔÇ▓(x)=f(x), then $F(x)$ is an **antiderivative** of $f(x)$.
- Notation:

Ôłźf(x)ÔÇëdx=F(x)+C\int f(x) \, dx = F(x) + CÔłźf(x)dx=F(x)+C

- $C$ is the **constant of integration**.

**Geometric interpretation:**

- The integral of a function can represent **area under the curve**.
