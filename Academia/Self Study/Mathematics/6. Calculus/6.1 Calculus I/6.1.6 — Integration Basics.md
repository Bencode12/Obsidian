## What is Integration?

Integration is the **reverse process of differentiation**. If $F'(x) = f(x)$, then $F(x)$ is an **antiderivative** of $f(x)$.

$$\int f(x), dx = F(x) + C$$

$C$ is the **constant of integration** — it accounts for all possible antiderivatives. Geometrically, the integral represents the **area under the curve**.

---

## Basic Integration Rules

|Function|Integral|
|---|---|
|$x^n \quad (n \neq -1)$|$\dfrac{x^{n+1}}{n+1} + C$|
|$\dfrac{1}{x}$|$\ln|
|$e^x$|$e^x + C$|
|$a^x$|$\dfrac{a^x}{\ln a} + C$|
|$\sin x$|$-\cos x + C$|
|$\cos x$|$\sin x + C$|
|$\sec^2 x$|$\tan x + C$|
|$\csc^2 x$|$-\cot x + C$|
|$\sec x\tan x$|$\sec x + C$|
|$\csc x\cot x$|$-\csc x + C$|

## Properties of Integrals

$$\int [f(x) \pm g(x)],dx = \int f(x),dx \pm \int g(x),dx$$

$$\int kf(x),dx = k\int f(x),dx$$

---

## Indefinite vs Definite Integral

|Type|Notation|Result|
|---|---|---|
|**Indefinite**|$\displaystyle\int f(x),dx$|Family of functions $F(x) + C$|
|**Definite**|$\displaystyle\int_a^b f(x),dx$|A number — area from $a$ to $b$|

$$\int_a^b f(x),dx = F(b) - F(a)$$
