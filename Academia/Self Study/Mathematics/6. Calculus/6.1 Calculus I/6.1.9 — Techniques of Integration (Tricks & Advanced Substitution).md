## Trig Substitution

Use when the integrand contains $\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$, $\sqrt{a^2 + x^2}$, or $\sqrt{x^2 - a^2}$.

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int \frac{dx}{\sqrt{4 - x^2}}$
> 
> Let $x = 2\sin\theta \implies dx = 2\cos\theta,d\theta$ $$= \int \frac{2\cos\theta,d\theta}{\sqrt{4 - 4\sin^2\theta}} = \int d\theta = \theta + C = \arcsin!\left(\frac{x}{2}\right) + C$$

## Advanced Substitution

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int x\sqrt{x^2 + 1},dx$
> 
> Let $u = x^2 + 1 \implies du = 2x,dx$ $$= \frac{1}{2}\int \sqrt{u},du = \frac{1}{3}(x^2 + 1)^{3/2} + C$$

## Repeated Integration by Parts

When one application of integration by parts produces another integral that also requires it. Use the **tabular method** for efficiency.

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int x^2 e^x,dx$
> 
> |Differentiate|Integrate|
> |---|---|
> |$x^2$|$e^x$|
> |$2x$|$e^x$|
> |$2$|$e^x$|
> |$0$|$e^x$|
> 
> Result: $x^2 e^x - 2xe^x + 2e^x + C = e^x(x^2 - 2x + 2) + C$

## Trigonometric Integrals

Use **power-reducing formulas** for powers of $\sin$ and $\cos$:

$$\sin^2 x = \frac{1 - \cos 2x}{2} \qquad \cos^2 x = \frac{1 + \cos 2x}{2}$$

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int \sin^2 x,dx = \int \frac{1 - \cos 2x}{2},dx = \frac{x}{2} - \frac{\sin 2x}{4} + C$

---

## Partial Fraction Decomposition

For rational functions $\displaystyle\int \frac{P(x)}{Q(x)},dx$ — split into simpler fractions first.

> [!example] $\displaystyle\int \frac{2x + 3}{x^2 + x},dx = \int \frac{2x+3}{x(x+1)},dx$
> 
> Decompose: $\dfrac{3}{x} - \dfrac{1}{x+1}$ $$= 3\ln|x| - \ln|x+1| + C$$

## Summary of Techniques

|Situation|Method|
|---|---|
|Inner function and its derivative present|$u$-substitution|
|Contains $\sqrt{a^2 - x^2}$ etc.|Trig substitution|
|Product of two functions|Integration by parts|
|Power of $\sin$ or $\cos$|Power-reducing formula|
|Rational function|Partial fractions|
