## Beyond Brownian Motion

Brownian motion has continuous paths. Many real phenomena exhibit jumps:
- Stock price crashes
- Insurance claims
- Earthquake occurrences
- Neuron firing

Need processes with discontinuous paths!

---

## Poisson Process

### Definition

**Poisson process** $N_t$ with intensity $\lambda > 0$:

1. $N_0 = 0$
2. Independent increments
3. $N_t - N_s \sim \text{Poisson}(\lambda(t-s))$ for $t > s$
4. Right-continuous with left limits (càdlàg)

### Properties

- **Mean**: $\mathbb{E}[N_t] = \lambda t$
- **Variance**: $\text{Var}(N_t) = \lambda t$
- **Jump size**: Always 1
- **Inter-arrival times**: Exponential with rate $\lambda$

```desmos-graph
// Approximation of Poisson process
y = floor(sum(random() < 0.1, k, 1, floor(10*x)))
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Poisson process increases by jumps of size 1 at random times.

### Martingale Property

$$M_t = N_t - \lambda t$$

is a martingale (compensated Poisson process).

## Compound Poisson Process

### Definition

$$X_t = \sum_{i=1}^{N_t} Y_i$$

where:
- $N_t$: Poisson process with rate $\lambda$
- $Y_i$: i.i.d. jump sizes (independent of $N_t$)

Jumps occur at Poisson times with random magnitudes.

### Properties

- **Mean**: $\mathbb{E}[X_t] = \lambda t \mathbb{E}[Y_1]$
- **Variance**: $\text{Var}(X_t) = \lambda t \mathbb{E}[Y_1^2]$

### Example: Insurance Risk

- Jumps: Claim amounts
- Intensity: Claim frequency
- Model total claims over time

## Lévy Processes

### Definition

**Lévy process** $X_t$ is stochastic process with:

1. $X_0 = 0$
2. Independent increments
3. Stationary increments: $X_t - X_s \stackrel{d}{=} X_{t-s}$
4. Stochastically continuous

Generalizes both Brownian motion and Poisson processes!

### Examples

- Brownian motion (continuous, Gaussian)
- Poisson process (pure jump)
- Compound Poisson process
- Gamma process
- Variance gamma process

## Lévy-Itô Decomposition

### Theorem

Any Lévy process can be decomposed as:

$$X_t = bt + \sigma W_t + \int_{|z|<1} z\tilde{N}(t, dz) + \int_{|z|\geq 1} zN(t, dz)$$

where:
- $bt$: Drift
- $\sigma W_t$: Brownian component
- Small jumps ($|z|<1$): Compensated sum
- Large jumps ($|z|\geq 1$): Compound Poisson

### Lévy Measure

Measure $\nu(dz)$ characterizes jump structure:
$$\nu(A) = \mathbb{E}[\text{number of jumps in } A \text{ per unit time}]$$

Must satisfy: $\int_{\mathbb{R}} (1 \wedge z^2)\,\nu(dz) < \infty$

## Jump-Diffusion Models

### Merton Model

Stock price with jumps:
$$\frac{dS_t}{S_{t-}} = \mu\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t + (J-1)\,dN_t$$

where:
- $N_t$: Poisson process with intensity $\lambda$
- $J$: Random jump size (lognormal)
- $S_{t-}$: Left limit (value before jump)

### Solution

$$S_t = S_0\exp\left[\left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t\right]\prod_{i=1}^{N_t} J_i$$

Combines geometric Brownian motion with multiplicative jumps.

## Itô's Formula for Jump Processes

### Semimartingale

Process $X_t$ is **semimartingale** if:
$$X_t = X_0 + M_t + A_t$$

where $M_t$ is local martingale, $A_t$ is finite variation.

### Itô's Formula (with Jumps)

For $f \in C^2$ and semimartingale $X$:

$$\begin{aligned} f(X_t) &= f(X_0) + \int_0^t f'(X_{s-})\,dX_s + \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t f''(X_{s-})\,d[X]^c_s \\ &+ \sum_{0<s\leq t}[f(X_s) - f(X_{s-}) - f'(X_{s-})\Delta X_s] \end{aligned}$$

where:
- $[X]^c$: Continuous part of quadratic variation
- $\Delta X_s = X_s - X_{s-}$: Jump at time $s$

Extra sum accounts for jumps!

## Applications

### Finance: Modeling Crashes

Standard GBM cannot capture sudden price drops. Jump-diffusion models:
- Better fit to option prices
- Captures volatility smile
- Models market crashes

### Insurance: Risk Theory

Total claim process:
$$C_t = \sum_{i=1}^{N_t} Y_i$$

Ruin probability: $\psi(u) = \mathbb{P}(\inf_{t \geq 0}(u + ct - C_t) < 0)$

### Physics: anomalous Diffusion

Particles with occasional long jumps (Lévy flights):
$$\mathbb{P}(|X_t| > x) \sim t x^{-\alpha}, \quad \alpha \in (0, 2)$$

Heavier tails than Brownian motion.

## Characteristic Functions

### Lévy-Khintchine Formula

Characteristic function of Lévy process:
$$\mathbb{E}[e^{iuX_t}] = e^{t\psi(u)}$$

where:
$$\psi(u) = ibu - \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2u^2 + \int_{\mathbb{R}}(e^{iuz} - 1 - iuz\mathbb{1}_{|z|<1})\,\nu(dz)$$

Triplet $(b, \sigma^2, \nu)$ completely characterizes Lévy process.

### Examples

- **Brownian motion**: $\psi(u) = -\frac{1}{2}\sigma^2u^2$
- **Poisson**: $\psi(u) = \lambda(e^{iu} - 1)$
- **Compound Poisson**: $\psi(u) = \lambda(\phi_Y(u) - 1)$ where $\phi_Y$ is characteristic function of jump size

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Ignoring Jump Component
> For processes with jumps, cannot use standard Itô formula. Must include jump term.

> [!warning] Confusing $S_t$ and $S_{t-}$
> Use left limit $S_{t-}$ in integrands. Value before jump, not after.

> [!warning] Assuming Finite Activity
> Some Lévy processes have infinite jump activity (infinite small jumps). Need careful treatment.
