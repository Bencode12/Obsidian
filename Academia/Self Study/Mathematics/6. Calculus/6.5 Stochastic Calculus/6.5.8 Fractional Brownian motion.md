## Motivation

Standard Itô calculus assumes semimartingale integrators. Fractional Brownian motion is NOT a semimartingale for $H \neq 1/2$.

Need new integration theory!

---

## Fractional Brownian Motion

### Definition

**Fractional Brownian motion (fBm)** $B_t^H$ with Hurst parameter $H \in (0,1)$:

1. $B_0^H = 0$
2. Gaussian process
3. Mean zero: $\mathbb{E}[B_t^H] = 0$
4. Covariance:
   $$\mathbb{E}[B_t^H B_s^H] = \frac{1}{2}(t^{2H} + s^{2H} - |t-s|^{2H})$$

### Special Cases

- **$H = 1/2$**: Standard Brownian motion
- **$H > 1/2$**: Persistent (positive correlation)
- **$H < 1/2$**: Antipersistent (negative correlation)

```desmos-graph
// Approximation of fBm with H=0.7 (persistent)
y = sum(sin(2^n*x + n)*2^(-n*0.7), n, 1, 10)
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> fBm with $H > 1/2$ shows smoother, more persistent paths than standard BM.

### Properties

**Self-similarity**: For $c > 0$:
$$\{B_{ct}^H\}_{t \geq 0} \stackrel{d}{=} \{c^H B_t^H\}_{t \geq 0}$$

**Stationary increments**: $B_{t+h}^H - B_h^H \stackrel{d}{=} B_t^H$

**Long-range dependence** (for $H > 1/2$):
$$\text{Corr}(B_{t+1}^H - B_t^H, B_1^H) \sim H(2H-1)t^{2H-2} \text{ as } t \to \infty$$

Decays slowly (not summable)!

## Why Standard Itô Fails

### Not a Semimartingale

For $H \neq 1/2$, fBm is NOT a semimartingale.

Cannot decompose as local martingale + finite variation process.

### Consequences

- No classical Itô integral $\int X\,dB^H$
- Itô's formula doesn't apply directly
- Need alternative approaches

---

## Approaches to Integration

### 1. Pathwise Integration (Young Integral)

For $H > 1/2$, fBm has Hölder continuous paths with exponent $< H$.

If $X$ has Hölder exponent $> 1-H$, define pathwise:
$$\int_0^t X_s\,dB_s^H = \lim_{|\Pi| \to 0} \sum X_{t_i}(B_{t_{i+1}}^H - B_{t_i}^H)$$

**Advantage**: Deterministic, no probability needed.

**Limitation**: Only works for smooth enough integrands.

### 2. Skorokhod Integral

Extend Malliavin calculus to fBm.

Define divergence operator $\delta$ (Skorokhod integral):
$$\int_0^t u_s\,\delta B_s^H$$

Works for adapted and non-adapted processes.

**Advantage**: Rigorous probabilistic framework.

**Limitation**: Technically complex, less intuitive.

### 3. Wick Product Approach

Use Wick product $\diamond$ instead of ordinary product:
$$\int_0^t X_s\,dB_s^H = \lim \sum X_{t_i} \diamond (B_{t_{i+1}}^H - B_{t_i}^H)$$

Related to white noise analysis.

## Fractional Itô Formula

### For $H > 1/2$ (Pathwise)

If $f \in C^2$ and $X_t = B_t^H$:
$$f(B_t^H) = f(0) + \int_0^t f'(B_s^H)\,dB_s^H$$

No correction term! (Unlike standard Itô where $(dW)^2 = dt$)

Pathwise integral behaves like Riemann-Stieltjes.

### For Skorokhod Integral

$$f(B_t^H) = f(0) + \int_0^t f'(B_s^H)\,\delta B_s^H + H\int_0^t f''(B_s^H)s^{2H-1}\,ds$$

Extra term resembles Itô correction but with different structure.

## Stochastic Differential Equations

### Fractional SDE

$$dX_t = b(X_t)\,dt + \sigma(X_t)\,dB_t^H$$

Interpretation depends on integration approach.

### Pathwise Solution ($H > 1/2$)

Treat as ordinary differential equation driven by rough path:
$$X_t = X_0 + \int_0^t b(X_s)\,ds + \int_0^t \sigma(X_s)\,dB_s^H$$

Second integral defined pathwise (Young integral).

### Existence and Uniqueness

For $H > 1/2$ and Lipschitz coefficients, unique solution exists pathwise.

For $H < 1/2$, need rough path theory (advanced!).

## Applications

### Finance: Long Memory in Volatility

Empirical evidence shows volatility has long-range dependence.

Model log-volatility as fBm with $H \approx 0.7$:
$$d\log(\sigma_t) = \mu\,dt + \xi\,dB_t^H$$

Better fit to realized volatility data than standard models.

### Hydrology: River Flows

Hurst phenomenon: River discharge exhibits long-range dependence.

Original application where Hurst discovered fBm (1951).

### Telecommunications: Network Traffic

Internet traffic shows self-similarity and long-range dependence.

fBm models packet arrivals better than Poisson processes.

### Climate Science: Temperature Records

Global temperature anomalies exhibit persistence.

fBm captures long-term correlations in climate data.

## Estimation of Hurst Parameter

### Rescaled Range (R/S) Statistic

For time series $X_1, \ldots, X_n$:

1. Compute cumulative deviations: $Y_k = \sum_{i=1}^k (X_i - \bar{X})$
2. Range: $R(n) = \max Y_k - \min Y_k$
3. Standard deviation: $S(n)$
4. Rescaled range: $R(n)/S(n) \sim c n^H$

Estimate $H$ from log-log regression.

### Other Methods

- **Variance-time plot**: Variance of aggregated series
- **Periodogram method**: Spectral density estimation
- **Wavelet analysis**: Multiscale decomposition
- **Maximum likelihood**: Parametric estimation

## Challenges and Open Problems

### Mathematical Challenges

- No general Itô formula for $H < 1/2$
- Rough path theory needed (Lyons, 1998)
- Numerical methods less developed

### Practical Issues

- Parameter estimation difficult with limited data
- Model validation challenging
- Computational cost higher than standard models

### Research Directions

- Multidimensional fBm
- Stochastic control with fBm
- Optimal stopping problems
- Statistical inference for fractional SDEs

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Treating fBm Like Standard BM
> fBm is NOT a semimartingale for $H \neq 1/2$. Cannot use standard Itô calculus.

> [!warning] Ignoring Integration Method
> Different integration approaches (pathwise, Skorokhod, Wick) give different results!

> [!warning] Assuming Markov Property
> fBm is NOT Markov for $H \neq 1/2$. Future depends on entire history.
