## Definition

**Stochastic Differential Equation (SDE)**: Differential equation involving stochastic process.

General form:
$$dX_t = \mu(t, X_t)\,dt + \sigma(t, X_t)\,dW_t$$

where:
- $\mu(t,x)$: Drift coefficient
- $\sigma(t,x)$: Diffusion/volatility coefficient
- $W_t$: Brownian motion

### Integral Form

$$X_t = X_0 + \int_0^t \mu(s, X_s)\,ds + \int_0^t \sigma(s, X_s)\,dW_s$$

## Linear SDEs

### General Linear SDE

$$dX_t = (a(t)X_t + b(t))\,dt + (c(t)X_t + d(t))\,dW_t$$

Special cases have explicit solutions.

### Homogeneous Linear SDE

$$dX_t = aX_t\,dt + cX_t\,dW_t$$

Solution via Itô's formula with $f(x) = \ln x$:

$$X_t = X_0\exp\left[\left(a - \frac{c^2}{2}\right)t + cW_t\right]$$

Geometric Brownian motion!

### Example: Geometric Brownian Motion

SDE: $dS_t = \mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t$

Solution:
$$S_t = S_0\exp\left[\left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t\right]$$

Used in Black-Scholes model for stock prices.

```desmos-graph
// Sample paths of GBM (approximation)
```

## Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process

### SDE

$$dX_t = -\theta X_t\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t$$

where $\theta > 0$ (mean reversion rate).

### Solution Method

Use integrating factor $e^{\theta t}$:

$d(e^{\theta t}X_t) = e^{\theta t}dX_t + \theta e^{\theta t}X_t\,dt$

$= e^{\theta t}(-\theta X_t\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t) + \theta e^{\theta t}X_t\,dt$

$= \sigma e^{\theta t}\,dW_t$

Integrate:
$$e^{\theta t}X_t = X_0 + \sigma\int_0^t e^{\theta s}\,dW_s$$

Therefore:
$$X_t = X_0e^{-\theta t} + \sigma\int_0^t e^{-\theta(t-s)}\,dW_s$$

### Properties

- **Mean**: $\mathbb{E}[X_t] = X_0e^{-\theta t} \to 0$ as $t \to \infty$
- **Variance**: $\text{Var}(X_t) = \frac{\sigma^2}{2\theta}(1 - e^{-2\theta t}) \to \frac{\sigma^2}{2\theta}$
- Mean-reverting to zero
- Stationary distribution: $N\left(0, \frac{\sigma^2}{2\theta}\right)$

### Applications

- Interest rate models (Vasicek model)
- Velocity of particle in fluid
- Mean-reverting financial assets

## Existence and Uniqueness

### Theorem (Itô)

SDE $dX_t = \mu(t,X_t)\,dt + \sigma(t,X_t)\,dW_t$ with $X_0 = x_0$ has unique strong solution if:

1. **Lipschitz condition**: 
   $$|\mu(t,x) - \mu(t,y)| + |\sigma(t,x) - \sigma(t,y)| \leq K|x-y|$$

2. **Linear growth**:
   $$|\mu(t,x)|^2 + |\sigma(t,x)|^2 \leq K^2(1 + |x|^2)$$

### Strong vs Weak Solutions

**Strong solution**: Adapted to filtration of given Brownian motion.

**Weak solution**: May require constructing probability space and Brownian motion.

## Numerical Methods for SDEs

### Euler-Maruyama Method

Discretize time: $t_n = nh$, $n = 0, 1, \ldots, N$.

Approximation:
$$X_{n+1} = X_n + \mu(t_n, X_n)h + \sigma(t_n, X_n)\Delta W_n$$

where $\Delta W_n = W_{t_{n+1}} - W_{t_n} \sim N(0, h)$.

### Milstein Method

Higher order correction:
$$X_{n+1} = X_n + \mu h + \sigma\Delta W_n + \frac{1}{2}\sigma\frac{\partial\sigma}{\partial x}[(\Delta W_n)^2 - h]$$

More accurate than Euler-Maruyama.

### Convergence

- **Euler-Maruyama**: Strong order 1/2, weak order 1
- **Milstein**: Strong order 1, weak order 1

## Systems of SDEs

### Multidimensional SDE

$$d\mathbf{X}_t = \boldsymbol{\mu}(t, \mathbf{X}_t)\,dt + \boldsymbol{\sigma}(t, \mathbf{X}_t)\,d\mathbf{W}_t$$

where $\mathbf{W}_t = (W_t^1, \ldots, W_t^m)$ is vector of independent Brownian motions.

### Example: Correlated Assets

Two correlated stocks:
$$\begin{aligned} dS_t^1 &= \mu_1 S_t^1\,dt + \sigma_1 S_t^1\,dW_t^1 \\ dS_t^2 &= \mu_2 S_t^2\,dt + \sigma_2 S_t^2\,dW_t^2 \end{aligned}$$

with $\text{Corr}(dW_t^1, dW_t^2) = \rho$.

Write $dW_t^2 = \rho\,dW_t^1 + \sqrt{1-\rho^2}\,dW_t^\perp$ where $W^\perp$ independent of $W^1$.

## Applications

### Finance: Black-Scholes Model

Stock price follows GBM:
$$dS_t = rS_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t$$

Risk-neutral pricing leads to Black-Scholes PDE.

### Physics: Langevin Equation

Particle velocity:
$$dv_t = -\gamma v_t\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t$$

Ornstein-Uhlenbeck process for velocity.

### Biology: Population Dynamics

Stochastic logistic growth:
$$dN_t = rN_t\left(1 - \frac{N_t}{K}\right)dt + \sigma N_t\,dW_t$$

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Treating SDE Like ODE
> Cannot separate variables or use standard ODE methods. Must use Itô calculus.

> [!warning] Forgetting Itô Correction
> When transforming SDE, must apply Itô's formula, not classical chain rule.

> [!warning] Confusing Strong and Weak Solutions
> Strong solution adapted to given Brownian motion. Weak solution may need new probability space.
