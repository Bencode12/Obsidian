## Motivation

Classical chain rule fails for stochastic processes. Need new formula accounting for quadratic variation.

### Classical vs Stochastic

For smooth $f$ and $X_t$:
- **Classical**: $df(X_t) = f'(X_t)dX_t$
- **Stochastic**: Extra second-order term needed!

---

## Itô's Formula (One Dimension)

### Theorem

Let $W_t$ be Brownian motion and $f \in C^2(\mathbb{R})$. Then:

$$df(W_t) = f'(W_t)\,dW_t + \frac{1}{2}f''(W_t)\,dt$$

Or in integral form:
$$f(W_t) = f(W_0) + \int_0^t f'(W_s)\,dW_s + \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t f''(W_s)\,ds$$

### Key Insight

The $\frac{1}{2}f''(W_t)dt$ term comes from $(dW_t)^2 = dt$.

Taylor expansion to second order is necessary!

---

## Derivation Sketch

### Taylor Expansion

$$f(W_{t+\Delta t}) - f(W_t) = f'(W_t)\Delta W_t + \frac{1}{2}f''(W_t)(\Delta W_t)^2 + \cdots$$

Sum over partition:
$$f(W_t) - f(W_0) = \sum f'(W_{t_i})\Delta W_i + \frac{1}{2}\sum f''(W_{t_i})(\Delta W_i)^2 + \cdots$$

As mesh $\to 0$:
- First sum $\to \int f'(W_s)\,dW_s$ (Itô integral)
- Second sum $\to \int f''(W_s)\,ds$ (since $(\Delta W_i)^2 \approx \Delta t_i$)

Higher order terms vanish.

## Examples

### Example 1: $f(x) = x^2$

$f'(x) = 2x$, $f''(x) = 2$:

$$d(W_t^2) = 2W_t\,dW_t + \frac{1}{2}(2)\,dt = 2W_t\,dW_t + dt$$

Therefore:
$$W_t^2 = W_0^2 + \int_0^t 2W_s\,dW_s + \int_0^t ds = 2\int_0^t W_s\,dW_s + t$$

Rearranging:
$$\int_0^t W_s\,dW_s = \frac{1}{2}W_t^2 - \frac{t}{2}$$

This explains the "extra term" from earlier!

### Example 2: $f(x) = e^x$

$f'(x) = e^x$, $f''(x) = e^x$:

$$d(e^{W_t}) = e^{W_t}\,dW_t + \frac{1}{2}e^{W_t}\,dt = e^{W_t}\left(dW_t + \frac{1}{2}dt\right)$$

Geometric Brownian motion without drift!

### Example 3: $f(x) = \sin(x)$

$f'(x) = \cos(x)$, $f''(x) = -\sin(x)$:

$$d(\sin W_t) = \cos(W_t)\,dW_t - \frac{1}{2}\sin(W_t)\,dt$$

## Itô's Formula (Time-Dependent)

### General Form

For $f(t, x) \in C^{1,2}([0,\infty) \times \mathbb{R})$:

$$df(t, W_t) = \frac{\partial f}{\partial t}\,dt + \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\,dW_t + \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}\,dt$$

Combine $dt$ terms:
$$df(t, W_t) = \left(\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} + \frac{1}{2}\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2}\right)dt + \frac{\partial f}{\partial x}\,dW_t$$

### Example: $f(t,x) = tx$

$\frac{\partial f}{\partial t} = x$, $\frac{\partial f}{\partial x} = t$, $\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x^2} = 0$:

$$d(tW_t) = W_t\,dt + t\,dW_t$$

No correction term since $f$ linear in $x$.

## Itô's Formula (General Itô Process)

### Itô Process

Process of form:
$$dX_t = \mu_t\,dt + \sigma_t\,dW_t$$

where $\mu_t$ (drift) and $\sigma_t$ (volatility) are adapted processes.

### Formula

For $f \in C^2(\mathbb{R})$:
$$df(X_t) = f'(X_t)\,dX_t + \frac{1}{2}f''(X_t)(dX_t)^2$$

Compute $(dX_t)^2$:
$$(dX_t)^2 = (\mu_t\,dt + \sigma_t\,dW_t)^2 = \sigma_t^2\,dt$$

Since $(dt)^2 = 0$, $dt \cdot dW_t = 0$, $(dW_t)^2 = dt$.

Therefore:
$$df(X_t) = \left[f'(X_t)\mu_t + \frac{1}{2}f''(X_t)\sigma_t^2\right]dt + f'(X_t)\sigma_t\,dW_t$$

## Applications

### Geometric Brownian Motion

SDE: $dS_t = \mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t$

Solution via Itô's formula with $f(x) = \ln(x)$:

$d(\ln S_t) = \frac{1}{S_t}dS_t - \frac{1}{2S_t^2}(dS_t)^2$

$= \frac{1}{S_t}(\mu S_t\,dt + \sigma S_t\,dW_t) - \frac{1}{2S_t^2}(\sigma^2 S_t^2\,dt)$

$= \left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)dt + \sigma\,dW_t$

Integrate:
$$\ln S_t = \ln S_0 + \left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t$$

Therefore:
$$S_t = S_0\exp\left[\left(\mu - \frac{\sigma^2}{2}\right)t + \sigma W_t\right]$$

Foundation of Black-Scholes model!

### Ornstein-Uhlenbeck Process

SDE: $dX_t = -\theta X_t\,dt + \sigma\,dW_t$

Mean-reverting process. Solution involves integrating factor.

## Integration by Parts

### Formula

For Itô processes $X_t, Y_t$:

$$d(X_tY_t) = X_t\,dY_t + Y_t\,dX_t + dX_t\,dY_t$$

Extra term $dX_t\,dY_t$ compared to classical calculus!

### Example

Let $X_t = W_t$, $Y_t = t$:

$d(W_t \cdot t) = W_t\,dt + t\,dW_t + 0 = W_t\,dt + t\,dW_t$

Matches product rule from earlier example.

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Forgetting the Second-Order Term
> Must include $\frac{1}{2}f''(X_t)\sigma_t^2 dt$ term. This is NOT optional!

> [!warning] Using Classical Chain Rule
> $df(W_t) \neq f'(W_t)dW_t$. Always check if correction term needed.

> [!warning] Miscomputing $(dX_t)^2$
> Remember: $(dt)^2 = 0$, $dt \cdot dW_t = 0$, $(dW_t)^2 = dt$.
