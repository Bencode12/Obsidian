## Lévy Processes

### Definition

A **Lévy process** $\{X_t\}_{t \geq 0}$ satisfies:

1. $X_0 = 0$ (a.s.)
2. Independent increments
3. Stationary increments: $X_{t+s} - X_s \stackrel{d}{=} X_t$
4. Stochastically continuous: $X_s \xrightarrow{P} X_t$ as $s \to t$

Generalizes both Brownian motion and Poisson processes!

### Lévy-Khintchine Formula

Characteristic function has form:
$$E[e^{iuX_t}] = \exp\left(t\left[i\gamma u - \frac{\sigma^2 u^2}{2} + \int_{\mathbb{R}\setminus\{0\}}(e^{iux} - 1 - iux1_{|x|<1})\nu(dx)\right]\right)$$

**Lévy triplet**: $(\gamma, \sigma^2, \nu)$
- $\gamma$: drift
- $\sigma^2$: Gaussian component
- $\nu$: Lévy measure (jump intensity)

### Examples

- **Brownian motion**: $(\mu, \sigma^2, 0)$
- **Poisson process**: $(0, 0, \lambda\delta_1)$
- **Compound Poisson**: Finite activity jumps
- **Stable processes**: Heavy-tailed jumps

## Semimartingales

### Definition

Process $X$ is **semimartingale** if:

$$X_t = X_0 + M_t + A_t$$

where:
- $M$: local martingale
- $A$: finite variation process

Largest class for which stochastic integration is defined!

### Importance

- All Lévy processes are semimartingales
- Solutions to SDEs are semimartingales
- Foundation of modern stochastic calculus

### Doob-Meyer Decomposition

For submartingale $X$:
$$X_t = M_t + A_t$$

unique decomposition into martingale and predictable increasing process.

## Stochastic Integration

### Ito Integral

For adapted process $H$ and semimartingale $X$:

$$\int_0^t H_s\,dX_s$$

Defined as limit of Riemann sums using left endpoints.

### Key Properties

- **Linearity**: $\int (aH + bG)\,dX = a\int H\,dX + b\int G\,dX$
- **Martingale property**: If $X$ martingale, integral is martingale
- **Ito isometry**: $E[(\int H\,dB)^2] = E[\int H^2\,dt]$ for Brownian motion

### Stratonovich Integral

Alternative definition using midpoints:
$$\int_0^t H_s \circ dX_s$$

Satisfies classical chain rule but not martingale property.

Used in physics and geometry!

## Ito's Formula

### Statement

For $C^2$ function $f$ and semimartingale $X$:

$$f(X_t) = f(X_0) + \int_0^t f'(X_s)\,dX_s + \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t f''(X_s)\,d[X]_s$$

where $[X]$ is quadratic variation.

### For Brownian Motion

$$df(B_t) = f'(B_t)\,dB_t + \frac{1}{2}f''(B_t)\,dt$$

Extra term $\frac{1}{2}f''$ compared to ordinary calculus!

### Multivariate Version

For $f: \mathbb{R}^n \to \mathbb{R}$ and vector semimartingale $\mathbf{X}$:

$$df(\mathbf{X}_t) = \sum_i \frac{\partial f}{\partial x_i}\,dX_t^i + \frac{1}{2}\sum_{i,j}\frac{\partial^2 f}{\partial x_i \partial x_j}\,d[X^i, X^j]_t$$

## Girsanov's Theorem

### Change of Measure

If $Q \ll P$ with Radon-Nikodym derivative $Z_T = \frac{dQ}{dP}$, then under certain conditions, process that's Brownian motion under $P$ becomes Brownian motion with drift under $Q$.

### Statement

Let $B$ be Brownian motion under $P$. Define:
$$Z_t = \exp\left(-\int_0^t \theta_s\,dB_s - \frac{1}{2}\int_0^t \theta_s^2\,ds\right)$$

If $Z$ is martingale, then under $Q$ with $\frac{dQ}{dP} = Z_T$:
$$\tilde{B}_t = B_t + \int_0^t \theta_s\,ds$$

is Brownian motion!

### Application: Risk-Neutral Pricing

Change from physical measure to risk-neutral measure to price derivatives.

Foundation of mathematical finance!

## Quadratic Variation

### Definition

For process $X$, quadratic variation:
$$[X]_t = \lim_{|\Pi| \to 0} \sum_{i}(X_{t_{i+1}} - X_{t_i})^2$$

where limit over partitions $\Pi$ of $[0,t]$.

### Properties

- **Brownian motion**: $[B]_t = t$
- **Finite variation processes**: $[A]_t = 0$
- **Semimartingale**: $[X]_t = [M]_t$ (only martingale part contributes)

### Covariation

For two semimartingales:
$$[X,Y]_t = \frac{1}{4}([X+Y]_t - [X-Y]_t)$$

Appears in multivariate Ito formula!

## Feynman-Kac Formula

### Connection Between SDEs and PDEs

For SDE:
$$dX_t = \mu(X_t)\,dt + \sigma(X_t)\,dB_t$$

and function:
$$u(t,x) = E[f(X_T) | X_t = x]$$

Then $u$ satisfies PDE:
$$\frac{\partial u}{\partial t} + \mu\frac{\partial u}{\partial x} + \frac{1}{2}\sigma^2\frac{\partial^2 u}{\partial x^2} = 0$$

with terminal condition $u(T,x) = f(x)$.

### Application: Black-Scholes Equation

Option price satisfies heat equation after change of variables.

Allows solving PDE using Monte Carlo simulation!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Using Ordinary Chain Rule for Ito Calculus
> Must use Ito's formula with second-order term. Classical calculus fails!

> [!warning] Confusing Ito and Stratonovich Integrals
> They differ by correction term. Choose based on application context.

> [!warning] Ignoring Technical Conditions for Girsanov
> Need Novikov condition or similar to ensure valid change of measure.
