## **Lesson 10.3.3 — Weak Convergence (Convergence in Distribution)**

Weak convergence, also called **convergence in distribution**, is a central concept in probability theory, particularly in **limit theorems** such as the **Central Limit Theorem**. It describes how the **distribution of a sequence of random variables approaches a limiting distribution**.

---

### **1. Definition**

A sequence of random variables (X_n) **converges in distribution** to a random variable (X), denoted:

[  
X_n \xrightarrow{d} X  
]

if the cumulative distribution functions (CDFs) satisfy:

[  
F_{X_n}(x) = P(X_n \le x) \to F_X(x) = P(X \le x)  
]

for all points (x) where (F_X) is continuous.

- Focuses on the **distribution** rather than individual outcomes.
    
- Unlike almost sure convergence, the convergence may not hold **for specific ω**.
    

---

### **2. Key Properties**

1. **Relationship to other convergences:**
    

- Almost sure convergence or convergence in probability (\implies) convergence in distribution.
    
- Converse is **not true** in general.
    

2. **Continuous mapping theorem:**
    

- If (X_n \xrightarrow{d} X) and (g) is a **continuous function**, then:  
    [  
    g(X_n) \xrightarrow{d} g(X)  
    ]
    

3. **Slutsky’s theorem:**
    

- If (X_n \xrightarrow{d} X) and (Y_n \xrightarrow{P} c) (constant), then:  
    [  
    X_n + Y_n \xrightarrow{d} X + c, \quad X_n Y_n \xrightarrow{d} c X  
    ]
    

---

### **3. Examples**

1. **Central Limit Theorem (CLT):**
    

- Let (X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n) be i.i.d. with mean (\mu), variance (\sigma^2)
    
- Standardized sum:  
    [  
    Z_n = \frac{\sum_{i=1}^n X_i - n \mu}{\sigma \sqrt{n}}  
    ]
    
- CLT: (Z_n \xrightarrow{d} N(0,1))
    

2. **Discrete example:**
    

- (X_n) takes value (n) with probability (1/n), 0 otherwise.
    
- Then (P(X_n \le 0) = 1 - 1/n \to 1)
    
- So (X_n \xrightarrow{d} 0)
    

3. **Convergence in distribution but not a.s.:**
    

- Let (X_n) = 1 if n-th decimal of π is 7, else 0
    
- Each (X_n \xrightarrow{d} 0) but not converging almost surely
    

---

### **4. Multivariate Weak Convergence**

- For vector random variables (\mathbf{X}_n = (X_{n1}, \dots, X_{nk})):  
    [  
    \mathbf{X}_n \xrightarrow{d} \mathbf{X}  
    ]
    
- Convergence is in terms of **joint CDFs**:  
    [  
    F_{\mathbf{X}_n}(\mathbf{x}) \to F_{\mathbf{X}}(\mathbf{x})  
    ]
    
- Foundation for **multivariate CLT**, multivariate statistics, and asymptotic theory
    

---

### **5. Applications**

1. **Central Limit Theorem** – sums of i.i.d. variables
    
2. **Asymptotic statistics** – distribution of estimators as sample size → ∞
    
3. **Bootstrap methods** – approximate distribution of a statistic
    
4. **Queueing theory and stochastic processes** – limiting distributions
