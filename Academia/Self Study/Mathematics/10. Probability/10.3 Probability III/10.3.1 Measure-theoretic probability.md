## **Lesson 10.3.1 — Measure-Theoretic Probability**

Measure-theoretic probability is the **mathematical foundation of modern probability theory**. It rigorously defines random variables, probability spaces, and expectations using **measure theory**, which is essential for advanced probability, stochastic processes, and theoretical statistics.

---

### **1. Probability Space**

A probability space is a triple ((\Omega, \mathcal{F}, P)):

1. **Sample space (\Omega)** — all possible outcomes
    
2. **σ-algebra (\mathcal{F})** — collection of “measurable” events (subsets of (\Omega)) satisfying:
    
    - (\emptyset \in \mathcal{F})
        
    - If (A \in \mathcal{F}), then (A^c \in \mathcal{F})
        
    - Countable unions of sets in (\mathcal{F}) are also in (\mathcal{F})
        
3. **Probability measure (P: \mathcal{F} \to [0,1])** satisfying:
    
    - (P(\Omega) = 1)
        
    - Countable additivity: For disjoint events (A_i),  
        [  
        P\Big(\bigcup_{i=1}^{\infty} A_i\Big) = \sum_{i=1}^{\infty} P(A_i)  
        ]
        

> This formalizes probability rigorously, handling infinite outcomes like continuous distributions.

---

### **2. Random Variables as Measurable Functions**

A **random variable (X)** is a measurable function:

[  
X: (\Omega, \mathcal{F}) \to (\mathbb{R}, \mathcal{B})  
]

- (\mathcal{B}) = Borel σ-algebra on (\mathbb{R})
    
- Measurability ensures ({ \omega : X(\omega) \le x } \in \mathcal{F})
    
- Enables defining **probabilities for events like (X \le x)**
    

---

### **3. Expectation via Lebesgue Integration**

Expectation is defined as a **Lebesgue integral**:

[  
E[X] = \int_\Omega X(\omega) , dP(\omega)  
]

- Generalizes discrete sums and continuous integrals
    
- Works for **complex and infinite sample spaces**
    
- Properties:
    

1. Linearity: (E[aX + bY] = a E[X] + b E[Y])
    
2. Monotone convergence: For (X_n \uparrow X), (E[X_n] \to E[X])
    
3. Dominated convergence: For (|X_n| \le Y) and (E[Y] < \infty), (E[X_n] \to E[X])
    

---

### **4. Conditional Expectation (Measure-Theoretic)**

For σ-algebra (\mathcal{G} \subseteq \mathcal{F}), the **conditional expectation** (E[X \mid \mathcal{G}]) is a **(\mathcal{G})-measurable random variable** such that:

[  
\int_G E[X \mid \mathcal{G}] , dP = \int_G X , dP, \quad \forall G \in \mathcal{G}  
]

- Generalizes (E[X \mid Y]) to arbitrary σ-algebras
    
- Basis for **martingales, stochastic integration, and filtrations**
    

---

### **5. Convergence in Measure Theory**

1. **Almost Sure Convergence:**  
    (\ P(\lim_{n\to\infty} X_n = X) = 1)
    
2. **Convergence in Probability:**  
    (\ P(|X_n - X| > \epsilon) \to 0)
    
3. **Convergence in Distribution:**  
    (\ F_{X_n}(x) \to F_X(x) ) at continuity points of (F_X)
    
4. **Convergence in (L^p):**  
    (\ E[|X_n - X|^p] \to 0)
    

> Measure-theoretic framework unifies all convergence types.

---

### **6. Examples**

1. **Discrete Random Variable:** (X) on finite sample space (\Omega = {1,2,3}), (P(X=1)=0.2, P(X=2)=0.5, P(X=3)=0.3)
    

- Expectation: (E[X] = 1_0.2 + 2_0.5 + 3*0.3 = 2.1)
    

2. **Continuous Random Variable:** (X \sim U[0,1])
    

- Expectation: (E[X] = \int_0^1 x dx = 0.5) (Lebesgue integral)
    

3. **Conditional Expectation:**
    

- (X \sim \text{Uniform}[0,1]), (\mathcal{G}) generated by indicator (1_{X>0.5})
    
- Then (E[X \mid \mathcal{G}]) = (0.25) if (X \le 0.5), (0.75) if (X>0.5)
