## Bayes' Theorem for Random Variables

### Continuous Case

For continuous random variables $X$ and $Y$:

$$f_{X|Y}(x|y) = \frac{f_{Y|X}(y|x)f_X(x)}{f_Y(y)}$$

where:
- $f_X(x)$: prior density
- $f_{X|Y}(x|y)$: posterior density
- $f_{Y|X}(y|x)$: likelihood
- $f_Y(y)$: marginal likelihood (evidence)

### Marginal Likelihood

$$f_Y(y) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty f_{Y|X}(y|x)f_X(x)\,dx$$

Normalizes the posterior to integrate to 1.

## Conjugate Priors

### Definition

A prior is **conjugate** to a likelihood if the posterior is in the same family as the prior.

Makes Bayesian computation tractable!

### Common Conjugate Pairs

|Likelihood|Prior|Posterior|
|---|---|---|
|Bernoulli/Binomial|Beta($\alpha, \beta$)|Beta($\alpha + k, \beta + n - k$)|
|Poisson|Gamma($\alpha, \beta$)|Gamma($\alpha + \sum x_i, \beta + n$)|
|Normal (known $\sigma^2$)|Normal($\mu_0, \tau_0^2$)|Normal($\mu_n, \tau_n^2$)|
|Exponential|Gamma($\alpha, \beta$)|Gamma($\alpha + n, \beta + \sum x_i$)|

### Example: Beta-Binomial

**Prior**: $\theta \sim \text{Beta}(\alpha, \beta)$

**Likelihood**: $X|\theta \sim B(n, \theta)$

**Posterior**: $\theta|X=k \sim \text{Beta}(\alpha + k, \beta + n - k)$

Interpretation: Prior "pseudo-counts" $\alpha, \beta$ updated with data $k, n-k$.

```desmos-graph
y = x^(2-1)*(1-x)^(5-1)
y = x^(5-1)*(1-x)^(5-1)
y = x^(8-1)*(1-x)^(5-1)
x = [0, 1]
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Beta distributions with different parameters. Prior Beta(2,5) updates to Beta(8,5) after observing 6 successes in 10 trials.

## Bayesian Estimation

### Posterior Mean Estimator

Under squared error loss, optimal estimator is posterior mean:

$$\hat{\theta}_B = E[\theta|X]$$

Minimizes expected posterior squared error.

### MAP Estimator

**Maximum A Posteriori** (MAP):

$$\hat{\theta}_{MAP} = \arg\max_\theta f_{\theta|X}(\theta|x)$$

Mode of posterior distribution.

Under 0-1 loss, MAP is optimal.

### Comparison with MLE

- **MLE**: $\hat{\theta}_{MLE} = \arg\max_\theta L(\theta;x)$
- **MAP**: $\hat{\theta}_{MAP} = \arg\max_\theta L(\theta;x)\pi(\theta)$

MAP includes prior; MLE doesn't. As $n \to \infty$, they converge!

## Credible Intervals

### Definition

A **$100(1-\alpha)\%$ credible interval** $(a, b)$ satisfies:

$$P(a \leq \theta \leq b | X) = 1 - \alpha$$

Direct probability statement about parameter!

### Highest Posterior Density (HPD) Interval

Shortest interval containing $1-\alpha$ posterior probability.

For unimodal posteriors: region where posterior density exceeds some threshold.

### vs. Confidence Intervals

|Property|Credible Interval|Confidence Interval|
|---|---|---|
|Interpretation|$P(\theta \in CI | \text{data}) = 0.95$|Long-run coverage frequency|
|Philosophy|Bayesian|Frequentist|
|Depends on prior|Yes|No|

## Bayesian Hypothesis Testing

### Bayes Factors

Compare two models/hypotheses $H_0$ and $H_1$:

$$BF_{01} = \frac{P(X|H_0)}{P(X|H_1)} = \frac{\int P(X|\theta_0,H_0)\pi(\theta_0|H_0)d\theta_0}{\int P(X|\theta_1,H_1)\pi(\theta_1|H_1)d\theta_1}$$

- $BF_{01} > 1$: Evidence favors $H_0$
- $BF_{01} < 1$: Evidence favors $H_1$

### Interpretation Scale (Kass & Raftery)

|$BF_{01}$|Evidence|
|---|---|
|1-3|Not worth mentioning|
|3-20|Positive|
|20-150|Strong|
|>150|Very strong|

### Posterior Model Probabilities

With prior model probabilities $P(H_0)$ and $P(H_1)$:

$$P(H_0|X) = \frac{BF_{01}P(H_0)}{BF_{01}P(H_0) + P(H_1)}$$

## Hierarchical Bayes

### Structure

Multiple levels of priors:

**Level 1**: Data model
$$X|\theta \sim f(x|\theta)$$

**Level 2**: Prior
$$\theta|\phi \sim g(\theta|\phi)$$

**Level 3**: Hyperprior
$$\phi \sim h(\phi)$$

### Benefits

- Share information across groups
- Automatic regularization
- Natural uncertainty propagation

### Example: Random Effects

$$Y_{ij}|\mu_i, \sigma^2 \sim N(\mu_i, \sigma^2)$$
$$\mu_i|\mu, \tau^2 \sim N(\mu, \tau^2)$$
$$\mu \sim N(0, 100), \quad \tau^2 \sim \text{Inverse-Gamma}(1, 1)$$

Groups borrow strength through common hyperparameters!

## Computational Methods

### Why We Need Them

Most Bayesian models don't have closed-form posteriors.

Need numerical methods to:
- Sample from posterior
- Compute posterior expectations
- Approximate integrals

### Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC)

Generate dependent samples from posterior distribution.

**Common algorithms**:
- Metropolis-Hastings
- Gibbs sampling
- Hamiltonian Monte Carlo (HMC)

After burn-in, samples approximate posterior.

### Variational Inference

Approximate posterior with simpler distribution.

Optimize to minimize KL divergence:
$$q^* = \arg\min_q KL(q(\theta)||p(\theta|X))$$

Faster than MCMC but approximate!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Using Improper Priors Carelessly
> Improper priors can lead to improper posteriors! Always verify posterior integrates to 1.

> [!warning] Ignoring Prior Sensitivity
> Check how results change with different priors. Robust conclusions shouldn't depend heavily on prior choice.

> [!warning] Confusing Credible and Confidence Intervals
> They have fundamentally different interpretations. Don't mix Bayesian and frequentist concepts!
