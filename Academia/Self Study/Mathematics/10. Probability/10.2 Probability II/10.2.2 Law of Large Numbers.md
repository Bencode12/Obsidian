## **Lesson 10.2.2 — Law of Large Numbers (LLN)**

The **Law of Large Numbers** formalizes the idea that **averages of random variables converge to expected values** as the number of trials grows. It is a cornerstone of probability theory and statistics.

---

### **1. Setup**

Let (X_1, X_2, \dots, X_n) be **i.i.d. (independent and identically distributed) random variables** with:

[  
E[X_i] = \mu, \quad \text{Var}(X_i) = \sigma^2 < \infty  
]

Define the **sample mean**:

[  
\bar{X}_n = \frac{1}{n} \sum_{i=1}^n X_i  
]

LLN studies the behavior of (\bar{X}_n) as (n \to \infty).

---

### **2. Weak Law of Large Numbers (WLLN)**

[  
\bar{X}_n \xrightarrow{P} \mu \quad \text{as } n \to \infty  
]

- Meaning: For any (\epsilon > 0),
    

[  
\lim_{n \to \infty} P(|\bar{X}_n - \mu| \ge \epsilon) = 0  
]

- Intuition: The sample mean **converges in probability** to the true mean.
    

---

### **3. Strong Law of Large Numbers (SLLN)**

[  
\bar{X}_n \xrightarrow{\text{a.s.}} \mu \quad \text{as } n \to \infty  
]

- Meaning: With probability 1,  
    (\bar{X}_n) **converges almost surely** to (\mu) as (n \to \infty).
    
- Stronger than WLLN; it guarantees convergence for **almost every sequence of outcomes**.
    

---

### **4. Examples**

1. **Coin toss**: Let (X_i = 1) if heads, 0 if tails, (p = 0.5)
    

- Sample mean: fraction of heads in (n) tosses
    
- LLN: As (n \to \infty), fraction of heads (\to 0.5)
    

2. **Dice rolls**: Let (X_i) = outcome of i-th die roll, (E[X_i] = 3.5)
    

- LLN: Average of dice rolls converges to 3.5 as number of rolls increases
    

---

### **5. Implications**

- Provides **theoretical justification for empirical averages** in experiments
    
- Basis for **statistics**: sample mean approximates population mean
    
- Justifies **Monte Carlo simulations**: repeated trials approximate expected value
    

---

### **6. Exercises**

1. Toss a fair coin 1000 times. Simulate the running mean and check if it approaches 0.5.
    
2. Let (X_i \sim U(0,1)). Compute (\bar{X}_n) for (n = 10, 100, 1000) and verify convergence to (0.5).
    
3. Show using Chebyshev’s inequality that (P(|\bar{X}_n - \mu| \ge \epsilon) \le \frac{\sigma^2}{n \epsilon^2}) (WLLN proof).
