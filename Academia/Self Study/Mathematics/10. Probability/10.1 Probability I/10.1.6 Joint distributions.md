## Joint Distributions

### Joint Probability Mass Function (Discrete)

For discrete random variables $X$ and $Y$:

$$p_{X,Y}(x,y) = P(X = x, Y = y)$$

Properties:
- $p_{X,Y}(x,y) \geq 0$ for all $(x,y)$
- $\sum_x\sum_y p_{X,Y}(x,y) = 1$

### Joint Probability Density Function (Continuous)

For continuous random variables $X$ and $Y$:

$$P((X,Y) \in A) = \iint_A f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dx\,dy$$

Properties:
- $f_{X,Y}(x,y) \geq 0$ for all $(x,y)$
- $\int_{-\infty}^\infty\int_{-\infty}^\infty f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dx\,dy = 1$

```

> Bivariate normal density forms a bell-shaped surface in 3D. Height represents joint probability density.

## Marginal Distributions

### From Joint PMF

$$p_X(x) = \sum_y p_{X,Y}(x,y)$$

$$p_Y(y) = \sum_x p_{X,Y}(x,y)$$

Sum over the other variable to get marginal!

### From Joint PDF

$$f_X(x) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dy$$

$$f_Y(y) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty f_{X,Y}(x,y)\,dx$$

Integrate out the other variable.

## Conditional Distributions

### Conditional PMF

$$P(X = x | Y = y) = \frac{p_{X,Y}(x,y)}{p_Y(y)}$$

provided $p_Y(y) > 0$.

### Conditional PDF

$$f_{X|Y}(x|y) = \frac{f_{X,Y}(x,y)}{f_Y(y)}$$

provided $f_Y(y) > 0$.

Interpretation: Distribution of $X$ given that we know $Y = y$.

## Independence

### Definition

$X$ and $Y$ are **independent** if:

$$P(X \in A, Y \in B) = P(X \in A)P(Y \in B)$$

for all sets $A$ and $B$.

### Equivalent Conditions

**For discrete**: $p_{X,Y}(x,y) = p_X(x)p_Y(y)$ for all $(x,y)$

**For continuous**: $f_{X,Y}(x,y) = f_X(x)f_Y(y)$ for all $(x,y)$

If independent, knowing $Y$ tells us nothing about $X$!

## Covariance and Correlation

### Covariance

$$\text{Cov}(X,Y) = E[(X - \mu_X)(Y - \mu_Y)] = E[XY] - E[X]E[Y]$$

Measures linear association:
- Positive: $X$ and $Y$ tend to increase together
- Negative: When one increases, other decreases
- Zero: No linear relationship (but may have nonlinear!)

### Correlation Coefficient

$$\rho_{XY} = \frac{\text{Cov}(X,Y)}{\sigma_X\sigma_Y}$$

Standardized measure:
- Range: $[-1, 1]$
- $\rho = 1$: Perfect positive linear relationship
- $\rho = -1$: Perfect negative linear relationship
- $\rho = 0$: No linear correlation

```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Top two: perfect correlation ($\rho = \pm 1$). Bottom: zero correlation but strong nonlinear relationship!

## Properties

### Variance of Sum

$$\text{Var}(X + Y) = \text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y) + 2\text{Cov}(X,Y)$$

If independent: $\text{Var}(X + Y) = \text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y)$

### Covariance Properties

- $\text{Cov}(X,X) = \text{Var}(X)$
- $\text{Cov}(aX + b, cY + d) = ac\,\text{Cov}(X,Y)$
- $\text{Cov}(X,Y) = \text{Cov}(Y,X)$

### Correlation Properties

- Invariant to location and scale changes
- $\rho_{aX+b, cY+d} = \text{sign}(ac)\rho_{X,Y}$

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Independence and Uncorrelated
> Independence $\implies$ uncorrelated, but NOT conversely! Zero correlation doesn't mean independent.

> [!warning] Forgetting to Normalize Conditional Distributions
> Always divide by marginal! Conditional must integrate/sum to 1.

> [!warning] Assuming Linear Relationship
> Correlation only measures LINEAR association. Use scatterplots to check for nonlinear patterns.
