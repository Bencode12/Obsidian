## Moment Generating Functions

### Definition

The **moment generating function (MGF)** of random variable $X$:

$$M_X(t) = E[e^{tX}]$$

For discrete: $M_X(t) = \sum_x e^{tx}p_X(x)$

For continuous: $M_X(t) = \int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{tx}f_X(x)\,dx$

Exists in neighborhood of $t = 0$.

---

## Why MGFs are Useful

### Generating Moments

If $M_X(t)$ exists, then:

$$E[X^n] = M_X^{(n)}(0) = \left.\frac{d^n}{dt^n}M_X(t)\right|_{t=0}$$

Take derivatives at zero to get moments!

**Example**: 
- $M_X'(0) = E[X]$ (mean)
- $M_X''(0) = E[X^2]$ (second moment)

### Uniqueness Theorem

If two random variables have the same MGF (in a neighborhood of 0), they have the same distribution!

Powerful tool for identifying distributions.

---

## MGFs of Common Distributions

|Distribution|MGF|
|---|---|
|Bernoulli($p$)|$1 - p + pe^t$|
|Binomial($n,p$)|$(1 - p + pe^t)^n$|
|Poisson($\lambda$)|$\exp(\lambda(e^t - 1))$|
|Normal($\mu,\sigma^2$)|$\exp(\mu t + \sigma^2 t^2/2)$|
|Exponential($\lambda$)|$\frac{\lambda}{\lambda - t}$ for $t < \lambda$|
|Gamma($\alpha,\beta$)|$(1 - t/\beta)^{-\alpha}$ for $t < \beta$|

## Properties of MGFs

### Linear Transformations

If $Y = aX + b$:

$$M_Y(t) = e^{bt}M_X(at)$$

### Sum of Independent Variables

If $X$ and $Y$ are independent:

$$M_{X+Y}(t) = M_X(t)M_Y(t)$$

This makes finding distributions of sums easy!

### Example: Sum of Normals

If $X \sim N(\mu_1, \sigma_1^2)$ and $Y \sim N(\mu_2, \sigma_2^2)$ independent:

$$M_{X+Y}(t) = \exp\left((\mu_1+\mu_2)t + \frac{(\sigma_1^2+\sigma_2^2)t^2}{2}\right)$$

Therefore: $X + Y \sim N(\mu_1+\mu_2, \sigma_1^2+\sigma_2^2)$

## Characteristic Functions

### Definition

The **characteristic function (CF)** always exists:

$$\phi_X(t) = E[e^{itX}]$$

where $i = \sqrt{-1}$ is the imaginary unit.

### Advantages over MGF

- Always exists (MGF may not)
- Works for all distributions
- Unique correspondence with distribution

### Relationship to MGF

If MGF exists: $\phi_X(t) = M_X(it)$

---

## Properties of Characteristic Functions

### Basic Properties

- $\phi_X(0) = 1$
- $|\phi_X(t)| \leq 1$ for all $t$
- $\phi_X(-t) = \overline{\phi_X(t)}$ (complex conjugate)

### Inversion Formula

Can recover PDF from CF:

$$f_X(x) = \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_{-\infty}^\infty e^{-itx}\phi_X(t)\,dt$$

### Continuity Theorem

$X_n \xrightarrow{d} X$ if and only if $\phi_{X_n}(t) \to \phi_X(t)$ for all $t$.

Foundation for proving Central Limit Theorem!

---

## Cumulant Generating Function

### Definition

$$K_X(t) = \log M_X(t)$$

### Cumulants

Expand as power series:
$$K_X(t) = \sum_{n=1}^\infty \kappa_n\frac{t^n}{n!}$$

Coefficients $\kappa_n$ are **cumulants**:
- $\kappa_1 = E[X]$ (mean)
- $\kappa_2 = \text{Var}(X)$ (variance)
- $\kappa_3$: related to skewness
- $\kappa_4$: related to kurtosis

### Additivity

For independent $X$ and $Y$:
$$K_{X+Y}(t) = K_X(t) + K_Y(t)$$

Cumulants add for independent variables!

---

## Applications

### Proving Limit Theorems

Use characteristic functions to prove:
- Weak Law of Large Numbers
- Central Limit Theorem
- Other convergence results

### Finding Distributions of Sums

Instead of convolution, multiply MGFs/CFs!

**Example**: Sum of independent Poissons is Poisson.

### Method of Moments

Match sample moments to theoretical moments via MGF derivatives.

---

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Assuming MGF Always Exists
> MGF doesn't exist for heavy-tailed distributions (e.g., Cauchy). Use characteristic functions instead!

> [!warning] Forgetting Domain Restrictions
> MGF may only exist for certain values of $t$. Check convergence!

> [!warning] Confusing MGF with Laplace Transform
> MGF is bilateral Laplace transform evaluated at $-t$, not just any Laplace transform.
