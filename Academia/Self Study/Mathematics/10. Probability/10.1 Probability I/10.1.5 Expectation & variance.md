## **Lesson 10.1.5 — Expectation & Variance**

Expectation and variance are **fundamental numerical characteristics** of a random variable. They describe the **center** and **spread** of a distribution.

---

### **1. Expectation (Mean)**

The **expected value** (E[X]) represents the **average or “long-run” value** of a random variable (X).

- **Discrete random variable** (X) with PMF (p_X(x_i)):
    

[  
E[X] = \sum_i x_i p_X(x_i)  
]

- **Continuous random variable** (X) with PDF (f_X(x)):
    

[  
E[X] = \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} x f_X(x) dx  
]

**Properties of Expectation:**

1. **Linearity:**  
    [  
    E[aX + bY + c] = a E[X] + b E[Y] + c  
    ]
    
2. **Expectation of constant:**  
    [  
    E[c] = c  
    ]
    
3. **Monotonicity:** If (X \le Y), then (E[X] \le E[Y])
    

---

### **2. Variance**

The **variance** measures how much a random variable **deviates from its mean**:

[  
\text{Var}(X) = E[(X - E[X])^2] = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2  
]

- Standard deviation: (\sigma_X = \sqrt{\text{Var}(X)})
    

**Properties of Variance:**

1. **Scaling:** (\text{Var}(aX) = a^2 \text{Var}(X))
    
2. **Shift:** (\text{Var}(X+b) = \text{Var}(X))
    
3. **Sum of independent variables:** If (X) and (Y) are independent,  
    (\text{Var}(X+Y) = \text{Var}(X) + \text{Var}(Y))
    

---

### **3. Moments**

- **n-th moment:** (E[X^n])
    
- **Central moment:** (E[(X - E[X])^n])
    
- **Skewness:** (\gamma_1 = \frac{E[(X-\mu)^3]}{\sigma^3})
    
- **Kurtosis:** (\gamma_2 = \frac{E[(X-\mu)^4]}{\sigma^4})
    

> Moments describe the **shape** of the distribution beyond mean and variance.

---

### **4. Examples**

1. **Discrete example:** (X) = number of heads in 2 coin tosses  
    [  
    \Omega = {HH, HT, TH, TT}, \quad X = 0,1,2  
    ]
    

|X|0|1|2|
|---|---|---|---|
|P(X)|1/4|1/2|1/4|

- (E[X] = 0*(1/4) + 1*(1/2) + 2*(1/4) = 1)
    
- (\text{Var}(X) = E[X^2] - (E[X])^2 = (0+1/2+4/4) - 1^2 = 0.5)
    

2. **Continuous example:** (X \sim U(0,1))
    

- PDF: (f_X(x) = 1, 0 \le x \le 1)
    
- (E[X] = \int_0^1 x dx = 0.5)
    
- (\text{Var}(X) = \int_0^1 x^2 dx - 0.5^2 = 1/3 - 1/4 = 1/12)
