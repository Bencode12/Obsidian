## Order of Operations

Related: [[1.1 Addition]] · [[1.3 Multiplication]] · [[1.4 Division]]

---

## Why Order Matters

Performing operations in the wrong order gives the wrong answer.

$$2 + 3 \times 4$$

|Approach|Working|Result|Correct?|
|---|---|---|---|
|Add first|$(2 + 3) \times 4 = 5 \times 4$|$20$|❌|
|Multiply first|$3 \times 4 = 12 \to 2 + 12$|$14$|✅|

---

## The Rule — PEMDAS / BODMAS

|Priority|PEMDAS|BODMAS|What it means|
|---|---|---|---|
|1st|**P** — Parentheses|**B** — Brackets|Evaluate everything inside brackets first|
|2nd|**E** — Exponents|**O** — Orders|Powers and roots|
|3rd|**M / D** — Multiplication and Division|**D / M**|Left to right — equal priority|
|4th|**A / S** — Addition and Subtraction|**A / S**|Left to right — equal priority|

> [!warning] Multiplication and division are equal priority When both appear in a row, work **left to right** — do not always multiply before dividing. Same rule applies to addition and subtraction.

## Worked Examples

$$5 + (2 \times 3) = 5 + 6 = 11$$

$$(4 + 6) \div 2 = 10 \div 2 = 5$$

$$2^3 + 4 = 8 + 4 = 12$$
