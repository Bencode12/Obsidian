### **1.8.1 Why order matters**

In math, performing operations in the wrong order gives the wrong answer.

Example:

- 2 + 3 × 4  
    If you add first: (2 + 3) × 4 = 5 × 4 = 20 ❌  
    Correct: multiply first: 3 × 4 = 12 → 2 + 12 = 14 ✅

### **1.8.2 The rule: PEMDAS / BODMAS**

- **P / B** → Parentheses / Brackets
- **E / O** → Exponents / Orders (powers, roots)
- **MD** → Multiplication and Division (left to right)
- **AS** → Addition and Subtraction (left to right)

Remember: **Operations in parentheses first, then powers, then multiply/divide, then add/subtract.**

### **1.8.3 Examples**

1. 5 + (2 × 3) = 5 + 6 = 11
2. (4 + 6) ÷ 2 = 10 ÷ 2 = 5
3. 2³ + 4 = 8 + 4 = 12
