### **1.9.1 Understanding word problems**

Word problems describe real-life situations using numbers and operations. To solve them:

1. **Read carefully** – identify the quantities and what is asked.
2. **Translate words into math** – use +, −, ×, ÷, fractions, etc.
3. **Solve step by step**
4. **Check your answer** – does it make sense in context?

### **1.9.2 Common keywords**

- **Addition**: total, sum, combined, together, in all
- **Subtraction**: difference, left, remaining, how many more/less
- **Multiplication**: times, product, each, groups of
- **Division**: per, out of, shared equally, ratio

### **1.9.3 Example**

**Problem:** Sarah has 12 apples. She buys 8 more. How many apples does she have in total?

- Operation: 12 + 8
- Answer: 20 apples

**Problem:** 15 candies are shared equally among 5 children. How many does each get?

- Operation: 15 ÷ 5
- Answer: 3 candies per child

### **1.9.4 Tips for reasoning**

- Draw a diagram if needed
- Break the problem into smaller steps
- Always relate the answer back to the question
