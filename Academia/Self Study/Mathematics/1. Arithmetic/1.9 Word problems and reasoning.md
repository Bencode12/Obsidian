## How to Solve a Word Problem

1. **Read carefully** — identify the quantities and what is being asked
2. **Translate** — convert words into mathematical operations
3. **Solve** — work through step by step
4. **Check** — does the answer make sense in context?

---

## Common Keywords

|Operation|Keywords|
|---|---|
|**Addition** $+$|total, sum, combined, together, in all|
|**Subtraction** $-$|difference, left, remaining, how many more/less|
|**Multiplication** $\times$|times, product, each, groups of|
|**Division** $\div$|per, out of, shared equally, ratio|

---

## Worked Examples

**Problem 1**

_Sarah has 12 apples. She buys 8 more. How many does she have in total?_

$$12 + 8 = 20 \text{ apples}$$

---

**Problem 2**

_15 candies are shared equally among 5 children. How many does each child get?_

$$15 \div 5 = 3 \text{ candies per child}$$

---

## Tips for Reasoning

> [!tip]
> 
> - Draw a diagram if the problem involves measurements, distances or groups
> - Break multi-step problems into smaller parts — solve one operation at a time
> - Always re-read the question at the end and check the answer has the right **units** and makes **common sense**
