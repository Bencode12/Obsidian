## What is a Fraction?

A fraction represents **part of a whole**:

$$\frac{\text{numerator}}{\text{denominator}}$$

|Part|Meaning|
|---|---|
|**Numerator**|How many parts you have|
|**Denominator**|Total number of equal parts|

$$\frac{3}{4} \quad \longrightarrow \quad \text{3 parts out of 4 equal parts}$$

---

## Types of Fractions

|Type|Condition|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Proper fraction**|Numerator $<$ denominator|$\dfrac{2}{5}$|
|**Improper fraction**|Numerator $\geq$ denominator|$\dfrac{7}{4}$|

---

## Mixed Numbers

An improper fraction can be written as a **mixed number** — a whole number plus a proper fraction.

$$\frac{7}{4} = 1\frac{3}{4}$$

To convert: divide the numerator by the denominator. The quotient is the whole number, the remainder is the new numerator.

$$7 \div 4 = 1 \text{ remainder } 3 \quad \longrightarrow \quad 1\frac{3}{4}$$

---

## Equivalent Fractions

Different fractions can represent the **same value**.

$$\frac{1}{2} = \frac{2}{4} = \frac{3}{6}$$

Multiply or divide **both** numerator and denominator by the same number — the value does not change.

$$\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{3}{3} = \frac{3}{6}$$

---

## Simplifying Fractions

Reduce a fraction to **lowest terms** by dividing both numerator and denominator by their greatest common factor (GCF).

$$\frac{6}{8} \div \frac{2}{2} = \frac{3}{4}$$

> [!tip] A fraction is fully simplified when numerator and denominator share no common factor other than 1.
