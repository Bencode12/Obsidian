### **1.5.1 What is a fraction?**

A fraction represents **part of a whole**.

Form:

$$\frac{\text{numerator}}{\text{denominator}}$$

- Numerator → how many parts you have
- Denominator → total equal parts

Example:

- $\frac{3}{4}$ means 3 parts out of 4 equal parts.

### **1.5.2 Proper and improper fractions**

- **Proper fraction**: numerator < denominator  
    Example: $\frac{2}{5}$
- **Improper fraction**: numerator ≥ denominator  
    Example: $\frac{7}{4}$

### **1.5.3 Mixed numbers**

An improper fraction can be written as a mixed number.

Example:

$$\frac{7}{4} = 1 \frac{3}{4}$$

### **1.5.4 Equivalent fractions**

Different fractions can represent the same value.

Example:

$$\frac{1}{2} = $\frac{2}{4}$ = \frac{3}{6}$$

Multiply or divide numerator and denominator by the same number.

### **1.5.5 Simplifying fractions**

Reduce fractions to lowest terms.

Example:

$$\frac{6}{8} = \frac{3}{4}$$
