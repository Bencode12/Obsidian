## What is Multiplication?

Multiplication means **repeated addition**. Symbols: $\times$ or $\cdot$

$$4 \times 3 = 4 + 4 + 4 = 12$$

---

## Multiplication on a Number Line

Multiplying means making **equal jumps**.

$$3 \times 5 \quad \longrightarrow \quad \text{jump } 3 \text{ units, } 5 \text{ times, land on } 15$$

---

## Properties of Multiplication

### Commutative Property — order does not matter

$$a \times b = b \times a$$

|Example|Result|
|---|---|
|$3 \times 4$|$12$|
|$4 \times 3$|$12$|

### Associative Property — grouping does not matter

$$(a \times b) \times c = a \times (b \times c)$$

|Example|Result|
|---|---|
|$(2 \times 3) \times 4$|$24$|
|$2 \times (3 \times 4)$|$24$|

### Identity Property — multiplying by 1

One is the **multiplicative identity** — it does not change the number.

$$a \times 1 = a$$

### Zero Property — multiplying by 0

Any number multiplied by zero equals zero.

$$a \times 0 = 0$$

> [!tip] Commutative + Associative together Just like in [[Addition]], you can reorder and regroup any multiplication to make calculation easier — e.g. pair numbers that multiply to a round number first.
