### **1.3.1 What is multiplication?**

Multiplication means **repeated addition**.

Symbol: **×** or **·**

Example:

- 4 × 3 means adding 4 three times:  
    4 + 4 + 4 = 12

### **1.3.2 Multiplication on a number line**

Multiplying moves in **equal jumps**.

Example:

- 3 × 5  
    Jump 3 units, 5 times → land on 15

### **1.3.3 Order does not matter (Commutative property)**

- 3 × 4 = 12
- 4 × 3 = 12

So:  
a × b = b × a

### **1.3.4 Grouping does not matter (Associative property)**

- (2 × 3) × 4 = 24
- 2 × (3 × 4) = 24

### **1.3.5 Multiplying by 1**

One is the **identity** for multiplication.

- 7 × 1 = 7

### **1.3.6 Multiplying by 0**

Any number multiplied by zero equals zero.

- 9 × 0 = 0
