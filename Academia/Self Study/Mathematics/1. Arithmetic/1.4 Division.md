## Division

Related: [[Multiplication]] · [[Subtraction]] · [[Number_Basics]]

---

## What is Division?

Division means **splitting into equal parts** or **repeated subtraction**. Symbols: $\div$ or $/$

$$12 \div 3 = 4$$

Twelve items split equally into three groups gives four per group.

---

## Division as Repeated Subtraction

$$12 \div 3 \quad \longrightarrow \quad 12 - 3 - 3 - 3 - 3 = 0$$

It took $4$ subtractions to reach zero — the answer is $4$.

---

## Relationship to Multiplication

Division **undoes** multiplication — they are inverse operations.

$$4 \times 5 = 20 \qquad \Longleftrightarrow \qquad 20 \div 5 = 4$$

> [!tip] Use multiplication to check division If $a \div b = c$, then $c \times b = a$. Verify any division result by multiplying back.

---

## Properties of Division

### Not Commutative — order matters

$$a \div b \neq b \div a$$

$$8 \div 4 = 2 \qquad \text{but} \qquad 4 \div 8 = 0.5$$

### Dividing by 1 — no change

$$a \div 1 = a$$

$$9 \div 1 = 9$$

### Dividing zero — always zero

$$0 \div a = 0 \quad (a \neq 0)$$

$$0 \div 5 = 0$$

### Division by zero — undefined

> [!warning] Division by zero is not allowed $5 \div 0$ has no answer — there is no number that satisfies the operation. This is one of the few hard rules in arithmetic: **never divide by zero**.
