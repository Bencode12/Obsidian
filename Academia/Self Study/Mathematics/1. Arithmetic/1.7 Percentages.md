## What is a Percentage?

A percentage represents **a part of 100**. Symbol: $%$

$$25% = \frac{25}{100} = 0.25$$

---

## Converting Between Fractions, Decimals and Percentages

|From|To|Method|Example|
|---|---|---|---|
|Fraction|Percentage|Multiply by $100$|$\dfrac{3}{4} = 0.75 = 75%$|
|Decimal|Percentage|Multiply by $100$|$0.6 = 60%$|
|Percentage|Decimal|Divide by $100$|$45% = 0.45$|
|Percentage|Fraction|Write over $100$, simplify|$20% = \dfrac{20}{100} = \dfrac{1}{5}$|

---

## Finding a Percentage of a Quantity

$$X% \text{ of } N = \frac{X}{100} \times N$$

> [!example] $20%$ of $50 = \dfrac{20}{100} \times 50 = 0.2 \times 50 = 10$

---

## Percentage Increase and Decrease

|Operation|Formula|Example (starting at $200$, $10%$)|
|---|---|---|
|**Increase**|$\text{original} \times (1 + r)$|$200 \times 1.1 = 220$|
|**Decrease**|$\text{original} \times (1 - r)$|$200 \times 0.9 = 180$|

where $r$ is the percentage written as a decimal.

> [!tip] Multiplier shortcut Add or subtract the percentage from $1$ to get the **multiplier** — this lets you apply the change in a single step instead of two.
