### **1.7.1 What is a percentage?**

A percentage represents a **part of 100**.

Symbol: **%**

Example:

- 25% = 25 out of 100 = 25100=0.25\frac{25}{100} = 0.2510025​=0.25

### **1.7.2 Converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages**

- Fraction → Percentage: Multiply by 100  
    Example: 34=0.75=75%\frac{3}{4} = 0.75 = 75\%43​=0.75=75%
- Decimal → Percentage: Multiply by 100  
    Example: 0.6 = 60%
- Percentage → Decimal: Divide by 100  
    Example: 45% = 0.45
- Percentage → Fraction: Write as fraction over 100 and simplify  
    Example: 20% = 20100=15\frac{20}{100} = \frac{1}{5}10020​=51​

### **1.7.3 Finding percentages of a quantity**

To find X% of a number, multiply:

$X% of number=X100×numberX\% \text{ of number} = \frac{X}{100} \times \text{number}X% of number=100X​×number$

Example:

- 20% of 50 = 0.2×50=100.2 × 50 = 100.2×50=10

### **1.7.4 Increase and decrease by a percentage**

- Increase: new value = original × (1 + % as decimal)
- Decrease: new value = original × (1 − % as decimal)

Example:

- Increase 200 by 10% → 200 × 1.1 = 220
- Decrease 200 by 10% → 200 × 0.9 = 180
