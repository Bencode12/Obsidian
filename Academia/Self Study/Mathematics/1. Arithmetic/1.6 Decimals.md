## What is a Decimal?

A decimal represents **part of a whole** using a dot (decimal point).

|Decimal|Fraction|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|$0.5$|$\dfrac{5}{10}$|5 parts out of 10|
|$0.25$|$\dfrac{25}{100}$|25 parts out of 100|

---

## Place Value in Decimals

Positions to the **right** of the decimal point are fractions of powers of 10:

|Position|Value|Example digit in $3.476$|
|---|---|---|
|Ones|$1$|$3$|
|Tenths|$\frac{1}{10}$|$4$|
|Hundredths|$\frac{1}{100}$|$7$|
|Thousandths|$\frac{1}{1000}$|$6$|

$$3.476 = 3 + \frac{4}{10} + \frac{7}{100} + \frac{6}{1000}$$

---

## Converting Fractions to Decimals

Divide the numerator by the denominator.

$$\frac{3}{4} = 3 \div 4 = 0.75$$

---

## Converting Decimals to Fractions

Write the decimal as a fraction over the appropriate power of 10, then simplify.

$$0.6 = \frac{6}{10} = \frac{3}{5}$$

$$0.125 = \frac{125}{1000} = \frac{1}{8}$$

> [!tip] Which power of 10? Count the decimal places — 1 place → over $10$, 2 places → over $100$, 3 places → over $1000$. Then simplify using GCF as in [[Fractions]].

---

## Comparing Decimals

Compare **place by place from left to right** — more digits do not always mean a larger value.

|Comparison|Result|
|---|---|
|$0.75$ vs $0.6$|$0.75 > 0.6$|
|$0.605$ vs $0.65$|$0.605 < 0.65$|

> [!warning] Common mistake $0.605$ has more digits than $0.65$ but is smaller — always compare position by position, not by total digit count.
