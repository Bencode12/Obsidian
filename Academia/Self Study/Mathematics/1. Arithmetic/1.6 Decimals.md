### **1.6.1 What is a decimal?**

A decimal represents a **part of a whole** using a **dot (decimal point)**.

Example:

- 0.5 means 5 parts out of 10 → same as 510$\frac{5}{10}$105
- 0.25 means 25 parts out of 100 → same as 25100$\frac{25}{100}$10025

### **1.6.2 Place value in decimals**

Positions **right of the decimal** are fractions of 10:

- 0.1 → tenths (1/10)
- 0.01 → hundredths (1/100)
- 0.001 → thousandths (1/1000)

Example: 3.476

- 3 → ones
- 4 → tenths (4/10)
- 7 → hundredths (7/100)
- 6 → thousandths (6/1000)

### **1.6.3 Converting fractions to decimals**

Divide numerator by denominator.

Example:

- $\frac{3}{4} = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75$

### **1.6.4 Converting decimals to fractions**

Write in terms of powers of 10 and simplify.

Example:

- 0.6 = 610=35$\frac{6}{10}$ = $\frac{3}{5}$106=53
- 0.125 = 1251000=18$\frac{125}{1000}$ = $\frac{1}{8}$1000125=81

### **1.6.5 Comparing decimals**

- Compare place by place from left to right.
- More digits do not always mean larger value; check carefully.

Example:

- 0.75 > 0.6
- 0.605 < 0.65
