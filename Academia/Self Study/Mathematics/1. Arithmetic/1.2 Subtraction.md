### **1.2.1 What is subtraction?**

Subtraction means **taking away** or finding the **difference** between two quantities.

Symbol: **−**

Example:

- 7 − 3 = 4  
    From seven objects, remove three, leaving four.

### **1.2.2 Subtraction on a number line**

Start at the first number and move **left**.

Example:

- 9 − 5  
    Start at 9 → move 5 steps left → land on 4

### **1.2.3 Subtraction is not commutative**

Order matters.

- 7 − 3 ≠ 3 − 7

So:  
a − b ≠ b − a

### **1.2.4 Subtracting zero**

Zero does nothing.

- 6 − 0 = 6

### **1.2.5 Subtracting a number from itself**

Any number minus itself equals zero.

- 8 − 8 = 0

### **1.2.6 Relationship to addition**

Subtraction “undoes” addition.

- 5 + 2 = 7
- 7 − 2 = 5
