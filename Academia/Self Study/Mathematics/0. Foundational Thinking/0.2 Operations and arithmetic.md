## Counting

Counting is the process of assigning numbers to objects **one-by-one** to measure quantity.

$$1 \to \text{first object} \quad 2 \to \text{second object} \quad 3 \to \text{third object} \quad \ldots$$

The **last number spoken** tells you the total quantity.

---

## Cardinality

Cardinality means the final counting number **equals the number of objects** — regardless of their size, shape, or the order they were counted in.

> [!tip] Cardinality Principle The last number you say when counting tells you the total quantity — not just which position an object is in.

---

## Order Does Not Change Quantity

|Counting direction|Result|
|---|---|
|Left to right|6 objects|
|Right to left|6 objects|

> [!warning] Common Mistake Children often think that counting faster or slower changes the total — it doesn't. Only the one-to-one matching matters.

---

## One-to-One Correspondence

> [!info] Why This Matters
> 
> One-to-one correspondence is the foundation for understanding equality, comparison, and later, functions in algebra.

| Rule | Description | Example |
| --- | --- | --- |
| ✅ **Correct** | One object → one number | Count each apple once |
| ❌ **Incorrect** | Skipping a number | 1, 3, 5, 7 (missed 2, 4, 6) |
| ❌ **Incorrect** | Counting the same object twice | 1, 2, 2, 3 (double-counted) |

---

## Counting Different Types of Objects

Quantity **ignores type** — the number only measures _how many_, not _what kind_.

$$2 \text{ books} + 3 \text{ pens} = 5 \text{ objects}$$
