### **0.3.1 The base-10 number system**

We use a **base-10 (decimal)** system.  
This means every position is a power of 10.

From right to left:

- Ones → 10⁰
- Tens → 10¹
- Hundreds → 10²
- Thousands → 10³
- And so on

### **0.3.2 Why place matters**

The same digit can represent very different values depending on its position.

Example:

- **3** in 3 = three ones
- **3** in 30 = three tens
- **3** in 300 = three hundreds

The digit is the same; the _place_ changes the value.

### **0.3.3 Breaking a number apart**

Example: **4,582**

This means:

- 4 × 1,000
- 5 × 100
- 8 × 10
- 2 × 1

So:  
4,582 = 4,000 + 500 + 80 + 2

### **0.3.4 Zero as a placeholder**

Zero does not always mean “nothing.”  
Sometimes it holds a place.

Example:

- 105 = one hundred, **zero tens**, five ones
- Without the zero, 15 would mean something completely different.

### **0.3.5 Expanded form**

Expanded form writes numbers as a sum of their place values.

Example:

- 7,403 = 7,000 + 400 + 3
