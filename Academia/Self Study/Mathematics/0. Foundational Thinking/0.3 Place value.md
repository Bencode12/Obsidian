## The Base-10 Number System

We use a **base-10 (decimal)** system — every position is a power of 10.

|Position|Power of 10|Value|
|---|---|---|
|Ones|$10^0$|$1$|
|Tens|$10^1$|$10$|
|Hundreds|$10^2$|$100$|
|Thousands|$10^3$|$1{,}000$|
|Ten-thousands|$10^4$|$10{,}000$|

---

## Why Place Matters

The **same digit** can represent very different values depending on its position.

|Number|What the 3 means|
|---|---|
|$3$|$3 \times 1$ = three ones|
|$30$|$3 \times 10$ = three tens|
|$300$|$3 \times 100$ = three hundreds|

The digit is the same — the place changes the value.

## Breaking a Number Apart

$$4{,}582 = (4 \times 1{,}000) + (5 \times 100) + (8 \times 10) + (2 \times 1)$$ $$= 4{,}000 + 500 + 80 + 2$$

---

## Zero as a Placeholder

Zero does not always mean nothing — sometimes it **holds a place**.

$$105 = (1 \times 100) + (0 \times 10) + (5 \times 1)$$

Without the zero, $15$ would mean something completely different — the hundreds digit would collapse into the tens position.

> [!tip] Zero is structural — it tells you a position is empty, not that the number is smaller.

---

## Expanded Form

Expanded form writes a number as a **sum of its place values**.

$$7{,}403 = 7{,}000 + 400 + 0 + 3$$

$$7{,}403 = (7 \times 10^3) + (4 \times 10^2) + (0 \times 10^1) + (3 \times 10^0)$$
