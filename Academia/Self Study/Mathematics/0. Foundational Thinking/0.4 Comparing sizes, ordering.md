### **0.4.1 Comparing numbers**

Comparing means deciding which number is:

- Greater than ( > )
- Less than ( < )
- Equal to ( = )

Examples:

- 7 > 5
- 3 < 9
- 4 = 4

### **0.4.2 Comparing by place value**

To compare two numbers:

1. Compare the **number of digits**
    - More digits → larger number
2. If digits are equal, compare **from left to right**

Example:

- 4,215 vs 3,982  
    → Thousands: 4 > 3  
    → 4,215 is larger

### **0.4.3 Using symbols**

Symbols always point to the **smaller** number.

Example:

- 6 > 2
- 2 < 6

Think: the open side “wants” the bigger number.

### **0.4.4 Ordering numbers**

Ordering means placing numbers in:

- **Ascending order** (smallest → largest)
- **Descending order** (largest → smallest)

Example:  
Numbers: 8, 1, 5

- Ascending: 1, 5, 8
- Descending: 8, 5, 1

### **0.4.5 Number line**

A number line shows order visually.

- Numbers increase as you move **right**
- Numbers decrease as you move **left**

Example:  
0 — 1 — 2 — 3 — 4
