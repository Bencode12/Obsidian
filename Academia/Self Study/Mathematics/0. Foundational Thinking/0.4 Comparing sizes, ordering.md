## Comparing Numbers

Comparing means deciding which number is greater, lesser, or equal.

|Symbol|Meaning|Example|
|---|---|---|
|$>$|Greater than|$7 > 5$|
|$<$|Less than|$3 < 9$|
|$=$|Equal to|$4 = 4$|

> [!tip] How to remember the symbols The symbol always **points to the smaller number**. The open side faces the bigger number — think of it as a mouth that wants to eat the larger value.

---

## Comparing by Place Value

To compare two numbers:

**Step 1 — Count the digits.** More digits means a larger number.

**Step 2 — If the digit count is equal**, compare from **left to right**, position by position, stopping at the first difference.

> [!example] $4{,}215$ vs $3{,}982$ Both have 4 digits → compare thousands: $4 > 3$ → $4{,}215$ is larger.

## Ordering Numbers

|Order|Direction|Example (8, 1, 5)|
|---|---|---|
|**Ascending**|Smallest → largest|$1, 5, 8$|
|**Descending**|Largest → smallest|$8, 5, 1$|

## The Number Line

A number line shows order visually. Numbers **increase moving right** and **decrease moving left**.

$$0 \longrightarrow 1 \longrightarrow 2 \longrightarrow 3 \longrightarrow 4 \longrightarrow \cdots$$

Any number to the right of another is **greater**. Any number to the left is **lesser**.
