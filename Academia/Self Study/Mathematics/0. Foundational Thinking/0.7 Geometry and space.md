## Definition

**Symmetry** means that an object looks the same after certain transformations like reflection, rotation, or translation.

Symmetry is one of the most beautiful and fundamental patterns in mathematics and nature. It appears in art, architecture, biology, physics, and everywhere we look!

> [!example] Why Symmetry Matters
> Symmetry helps us:  
> - Understand structure and balance  
> - Simplify complex problems  
> - Create aesthetically pleasing designs  
> - Discover conservation laws in physics

---

## Types of Symmetry

### Reflection Symmetry (Mirror Symmetry)

An object has reflection symmetry if it can be divided into two mirror-image halves.

The dividing line is called the **line of symmetry** or **axis of symmetry**.

**Examples:**
- Butterfly wings
- Human face (approximately)
- Letters: A, H, M, T, V, W, X, Y

```graph
y = x
y = -x
x = 0
```

> [!example] Testing for Reflection Symmetry
> Fold the object along the line. If both halves match perfectly, it has reflection symmetry!

### Rotational Symmetry

An object has rotational symmetry if it looks the same after rotating by a certain angle (less than 360°).

The **order** of rotational symmetry is how many times it matches during a full 360° rotation.

**Examples:**

|Object|Order|Angle of Rotation|
|---|---|---|
|Equilateral triangle|3|120°|
|Square|4|90°|
|Regular pentagon|5|72°|
|Circle|Infinite|Any angle|

### Translational Symmetry

An object has translational symmetry if it looks the same after shifting (translating) by a certain distance.

**Examples:**
- Brick wall pattern
- Wallpaper designs
- Frieze patterns

### Point Symmetry (Rotational Symmetry of Order 2)

An object has point symmetry if it looks the same after rotating 180° around a central point.

**Examples:**
- Letter S
- Letter Z
- Playing card symbols (some)

## Symmetry in Nature

Nature loves symmetry! Here's why:

### Bilateral Symmetry

Most animals have bilateral symmetry (one line of symmetry down the middle).

**Examples:**
- Humans
- Butterflies
- Fish
- Dogs

**Why?** Efficient movement forward through space.

### Radial Symmetry

Some organisms have radial symmetry (multiple lines through center).

**Examples:**
- Starfish (5-fold)
- Flowers (often 5 or 6-fold)
- Jellyfish
- Snowflakes (6-fold)

**Why?** Equal access to environment from all directions.

```graph
y = sqrt(3)*x
y = -sqrt(3)*x
y = 0
```

> [!example] Snowflake Symmetry
> Snowflakes have 6-fold rotational symmetry because water molecules form hexagonal crystal structures when freezing!

## Symmetry in Mathematics

### Geometric Shapes

|Shape|Lines of Symmetry|Rotational Order|
|---|---|---|
|Scalene triangle|0|1 (none)|
|Isosceles triangle|1|1|
|Equilateral triangle|3|3|
|Rectangle|2|2|
|Square|4|4|
|Regular pentagon|5|5|
|Regular hexagon|6|6|
|Circle|Infinite|Infinite|

### Functions and Graphs

**Even Functions**: Symmetric about y-axis

$$f(-x) = f(x)$$

Example: $f(x) = x^2$

```graph
y = x^2
```

**Odd Functions**: Symmetric about origin (180° rotation)

$$f(-x) = -f(x)$$

Example: $f(x) = x^3$

```graph
y = x^3
```

## Mathematical Properties of Symmetry

### Symmetry Operations Form Groups

The set of all symmetries of an object forms a **symmetry group**.

**Example**: Square's symmetry group has 8 elements:
- 4 rotations (0°, 90°, 180°, 270°)
- 4 reflections (horizontal, vertical, two diagonals)

This is called the **dihedral group** $D_4$.

### Noether's Theorem

In physics, every symmetry corresponds to a conservation law!

|Symmetry|Conservation Law|
|---|---|
|Time translation|Energy|
|Space translation|Momentum|
|Rotation|Angular momentum|

This is one of the deepest connections in all of science!

## Asymmetry

Not everything is symmetric—and that's important too!

### Examples of Asymmetry

- Human heart (slightly left of center)
- Spiral galaxies
- Most handwriting
- Abstract art

### Why Asymmetry Exists

- Function over form (heart placement optimizes blood flow)
- Growth patterns (spirals in shells)
- Randomness and creativity
- Breaking symmetry can create interest

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Symmetry Types
> Reflection ≠ Rotation ≠ Translation  
> Make sure you identify which type(s) of symmetry an object has!

> [!warning] Assuming All Regular Shapes Have Same Symmetry
> A regular n-gon has n lines of symmetry and order-n rotational symmetry.  
> Don't assume a pentagon has 4-fold symmetry like a square!

> [!warning] Missing Multiple Symmetries
> Many objects have BOTH reflection and rotational symmetry. Check for all types!
