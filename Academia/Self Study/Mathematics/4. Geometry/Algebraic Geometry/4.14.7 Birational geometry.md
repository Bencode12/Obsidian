## Birational Maps

### Definition

Rational map $f: X \dashrightarrow Y$ is **birational** if exists inverse rational map $g: Y \dashrightarrow X$ such that:

$$g \circ f = \text{id}_X, \quad f \circ g = \text{id}_Y$$

(on dense open sets).

Varieties $X$ and $Y$ are **birationally equivalent**.

### Birational Invariants

Properties preserved under birational equivalence:
- Function field $K(X) \cong K(Y)$
- Dimension
- Plurigenera $P_m = h^0(X, mK_X)$
- Kodaira dimension

NOT preserved: Cohomology, Chern classes, etc.

## Blow-ups

### Definition

**Blow-up** of variety $X$ along subvariety $Z$:

$$\text{Bl}_Z(X) = \{(x, [v]) \in X \times \mathbb{P}(N_{Z/X}) : x \in Z \implies v \text{ represents tangent direction}\}$$

Replaces $Z$ with projectivized normal bundle.

### Example: Point in Surface

Blow-up of point $p \in S$ (surface):

$$\text{Bl}_p(S) \to S$$

Exceptional divisor $E \cong \mathbb{P}^1$ replaces point.

Self-intersection: $E^2 = -1$.

### Universal Property

Blow-up is "smallest" modification making ideal sheaf invertible.

## Resolution of Singularities

### Statement (Hironaka)

For variety $X$ over characteristic 0 field:

Exists smooth variety $\tilde{X}$ and proper birational morphism:

$$\pi: \tilde{X} \to X$$

that is isomorphism over smooth locus of $X$.

**Resolution of singularities**!

### Method

Iteratively blow up singular loci.

Eventually obtain smooth variety.

### Example: Node

Curve $y^2 = x^3 + x^2$ has node at origin.

Blow-up resolves to smooth curve.

## Minimal Models

### Motivation

Among all smooth varieties birational to $X$, find "simplest" one.

**Minimal model**: Variety with nef canonical bundle.

$K_X$ is **nef** if $K_X \cdot C \geq 0$ for all curves $C$.

### Surfaces

For surfaces, minimal model obtained by contracting $(-1)$-curves.

**Castelnuovo's criterion**: Curve $C \cong \mathbb{P}^1$ with $C^2 = -1$ can be contracted.

### Higher Dimensions

More complicated! May need flips and flops.

Subject of **Minimal Model Program (MMP)**.

## Kodaira Dimension

### Definition

**Kodaira dimension** of variety $X$:

$$\kappa(X) = \begin{cases} -\infty & \text{if } P_m = 0 \text{ for all } m > 0 \\ \max\{\dim(\phi_{mK_X}(X)) : m > 0\} & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

where $\phi_{mK_X}: X \dashrightarrow \mathbb{P}(H^0(X, mK_X)^*)$ is pluricanonical map.

Birational invariant!

### Classification

|$\kappa(X)$|Type|Examples|
|---|---|---|
|$-\infty$|Uniruled|$\mathbb{P}^n$, Fano varieties|
|$0$|Calabi-Yau type|K3 surfaces, abelian varieties|
|$1 \leq \kappa < n$|Intermediate|Elliptic surfaces|
|$n$|General type|Most hypersurfaces|

---

## Enriques-Kodaira Classification

### Surfaces

Complete classification of complex surfaces by Kodaira dimension:

**$\kappa = -\infty$**: Rational or ruled surfaces

**$\kappa = 0$**: 
- K3 surfaces ($h^{1,0}=0$, $K_X$ trivial)
- Enriques surfaces ($K_X$ torsion)
- Abelian surfaces
- Hyperelliptic surfaces

**$\kappa = 1$**: Elliptic surfaces

**$\kappa = 2$**: Surfaces of general type

### Higher Dimensions

No complete classification, but MMP provides framework.

## Rational Varieties

### Definition

Variety $X$ is **rational** if birational to $\mathbb{P}^n$.

Equivalently: Function field $K(X) \cong k(x_1, \ldots, x_n)$.

### Examples

- $\mathbb{P}^n$: Rational
- Smooth quadric in $\mathbb{P}^n$: Rational (projection from point)
- Cubic surface in $\mathbb{P}^3$: Rational (27 lines!)
- General cubic threefold: NOT rational (Clemens-Griffiths)

### Unirational vs Rational

**Unirational**: Dominant rational map $\mathbb{P}^n \dashrightarrow X$.

Rational ⟹ Unirational, but converse false in dimension $\geq 3$!

## Cremona Group

### Definition

**Cremona group** $\text{Cr}_n(k)$: Group of birational automorphisms of $\mathbb{P}^n$.

For $n=1$: $\text{Cr}_1(k) = \text{PGL}_2(k)$ (Möbius transformations).

For $n=2$: Generated by quadratic transformations (Noether-Castelnuovo).

For $n \geq 3$: Very complicated, not finitely generated!

### Standard Quadratic Transformation

In $\mathbb{P}^2$:
$$[x:y:z] \mapsto [yz:xz:xy]$$

Blows up three points, contracts three lines.

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Birational and Isomorphic
> Birational allows undefined points. Much weaker than isomorphism!

> [!warning] Assuming All Varieties Have Minimal Models
> In higher dimensions, may need to allow mild singularities.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Kodaira Dimension Can Be Negative
> Many important varieties (Fano, rational) have $\kappa = -\infty$.

- Compute self-intersection of exceptional divisor

- Show that two given varieties are birational
- Find birational map between quadric and projective space
- Determine if cubic surface is rational

- Find Kodaira dimension of hypersurface of degree $d$ in $\mathbb{P}^n$

- Verify that Kodaira dimension is birational invariant
