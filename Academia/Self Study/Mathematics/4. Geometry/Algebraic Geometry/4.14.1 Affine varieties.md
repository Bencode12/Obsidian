## Affine Space

### Definition

**Affine $n$-space** over field $k$:
$$\mathbb{A}^n_k = k^n = \{(a_1, \ldots, a_n) : a_i \in k\}$$

Points are $n$-tuples of field elements.

### Polynomial Ring

Coordinate ring of $\mathbb{A}^n$:
$$k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$$

Polynomials in $n$ variables with coefficients in $k$.

---

## Algebraic Sets

### Zero Locus

For subset $S \subseteq k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$, define:

$$V(S) = \{p \in \mathbb{A}^n : f(p) = 0 \text{ for all } f \in S\}$$

Set of common zeros of polynomials in $S$.

### Affine Variety

**Affine algebraic set**: Any set of form $V(S)$ for some $S$.

**Affine variety**: Irreducible affine algebraic set.

Irreducible: Cannot write as union of two proper closed subsets.

---

## Examples

### Plane Curves in $\mathbb{A}^2$

- Circle: $V(x^2 + y^2 - 1)$
- Parabola: $V(y - x^2)$
- Hyperbola: $V(xy - 1)$

### Surfaces in $\mathbb{A}^3$

- Sphere: $V(x^2 + y^2 + z^2 - 1)$
- Cone: $V(x^2 + y^2 - z^2)$
- Cylinder: $V(x^2 + y^2 - 1)$

### Twisted Cubic

In $\mathbb{A}^3$:
$$C = V(y - x^2, z - x^3) = \{(t, t^2, t^3) : t \in k\}$$

Parametric curve!

## Ideals and Varieties

### Ideal of Set

For $X \subseteq \mathbb{A}^n$:
$$I(X) = \{f \in k[x_1, \ldots, x_n] : f(p) = 0 \text{ for all } p \in X\}$$

Ideal of all polynomials vanishing on $X$.

### Correspondence

|Algebraic Object|Geometric Object|
|---|---|
|Ideal $I \subseteq k[x_1,\ldots,x_n]$|Variety $V(I)$|
|Variety $X \subseteq \mathbb{A}^n$|Ideal $I(X)$|

Not quite one-to-one (need radical ideals).

## Hilbert's Nullstellensatz

### Weak Form

If $k$ is algebraically closed and $I \subsetneq k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$ is proper ideal, then:

$$V(I) \neq \emptyset$$

Proper ideals have zeros!

### Strong Form

For any ideal $I$:
$$I(V(I)) = \sqrt{I}$$

where $\sqrt{I} = \{f : f^m \in I \text{ for some } m\}$ is radical.

### Corollary

One-to-one correspondence between:
- Radical ideals in $k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]$
- Affine algebraic sets in $\mathbb{A}^n$

Foundation of algebraic geometry!

## Coordinate Ring

### Definition

For affine variety $X = V(I)$:

$$k[X] = k[x_1, \ldots, x_n]/I(X)$$

Ring of polynomial functions on $X$.

### Properties

- Elements are equivalence classes $[f]$ where $f \sim g$ if $f-g$ vanishes on $X$
- Integral domain iff $X$ is irreducible (variety)
- Dimension of $X$ = Krull dimension of $k[X]$

### Example

For parabola $X = V(y - x^2) \subseteq \mathbb{A}^2$:
$$k[X] = k[x,y]/(y-x^2) \cong k[x]$$

Isomorphic to polynomial ring in one variable!

## Morphisms

### Regular Maps

Map $\phi: X \to Y$ between affine varieties is **regular** (morphism) if:

$$\phi = (\phi_1, \ldots, \phi_m)$$

where each $\phi_i \in k[X]$ (polynomial function).

### Pullback

Morphism $\phi: X \to Y$ induces ring homomorphism:
$$\phi^*: k[Y] \to k[X]$$
$$\phi^*(f) = f \circ \phi$$

Contravariant functor!

### Isomorphism

$\phi: X \to Y$ is **isomorphism** if exists inverse morphism $\psi: Y \to X$.

Equivalent to $\phi^*$ being ring isomorphism.

## Dimension Theory

### Krull Dimension

**Dimension** of variety $X$:
$$\dim(X) = \sup\{n : \exists \text{ chain } Z_0 \subsetneq Z_1 \subsetneq \cdots \subsetneq Z_n\}$$

where $Z_i$ are irreducible closed subsets.

### Examples

- Point: $\dim = 0$
- Curve: $\dim = 1$
- Surface: $\dim = 2$
- $\mathbb{A}^n$: $\dim = n$

### Hypersurfaces

Variety defined by single equation $V(f)$ has dimension $n-1$ in $\mathbb{A}^n$.

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Affine and Projective Varieties
> Affine: subset of $\mathbb{A}^n$. Projective: subset of $\mathbb{P}^n$. Different compactifications!

> [!warning] Forgetting Algebraically Closed Field
> Nullstellensatz requires $k$ algebraically closed. Over $\mathbb{R}$, things are more complicated.

> [!warning] Assuming All Algebraic Sets Are Varieties
> Variety must be irreducible. Union of two lines is algebraic set but not variety.
