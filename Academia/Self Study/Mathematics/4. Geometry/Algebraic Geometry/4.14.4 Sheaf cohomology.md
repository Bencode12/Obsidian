## Sheaves

### Presheaf

**Presheaf** $\mathcal{F}$ on topological space $X$:
- For each open $U \subseteq X$: abelian group (or ring) $\mathcal{F}(U)$
- For $V \subseteq U$: restriction map $\text{res}_{U,V}: \mathcal{F}(U) \to \mathcal{F}(V)$
- Compatible: $\text{res}_{V,W} \circ \text{res}_{U,V} = \text{res}_{U,W}$

### Sheaf Axioms

Presheaf is **sheaf** if:

1. **Locality**: If $s,t \in \mathcal{F}(U)$ and $s|_{U_i} = t|_{U_i}$ for cover $\{U_i\}$, then $s=t$

2. **Gluing**: If $s_i \in \mathcal{F}(U_i)$ agree on overlaps, exists $s \in \mathcal{F}(U)$ with $s|_{U_i} = s_i$

Sections can be checked and constructed locally!

## Examples of Sheaves

### Structure Sheaf

On scheme $X$: $\mathcal{O}_X(U)$ = ring of regular functions on $U$.

### Constant Sheaf

$\underline{A}(U) = A$ (constant abelian group) for connected $U$.

### Sheaf of Differential Forms

On smooth variety: $\Omega^p_X$ = sheaf of $p$-forms.

### Ideal Sheaf

For closed subscheme $Y \subseteq X$: $\mathcal{I}_Y(U)$ = functions vanishing on $Y$.

## Sheaf Cohomology

### Motivation

Global sections functor $\Gamma(X, -)$ is left exact but not right exact.

**Sheaf cohomology** measures failure of exactness.

Derived functors of global sections!

### Definition

$$H^i(X, \mathcal{F}) = R^i\Gamma(X, \mathcal{F})$$

Computed using injective resolutions or Čech cohomology.

---

## Čech Cohomology

### Construction

For open cover $\mathfrak{U} = \{U_i\}$ of $X$:

**Čech complex**:
$$0 \to C^0 \xrightarrow{d^0} C^1 \xrightarrow{d^1} C^2 \to \cdots$$

where $C^p = \prod_{i_0 < \cdots < i_p} \mathcal{F}(U_{i_0} \cap \cdots \cap U_{i_p})$

### Coboundary Map

For $(s_{i_0\ldots i_p}) \in C^p$:
$$(d^p s)_{i_0\ldots i_{p+1}} = \sum_{k=0}^{p+1} (-1)^k s_{i_0\ldots \hat{i_k} \ldots i_{p+1}}|_{U_{i_0\ldots i_{p+1}}}$$

### Cohomology Groups

$$\check{H}^p(\mathfrak{U}, \mathcal{F}) = \frac{\ker(d^p)}{\text{im}(d^{p-1})}$$

For good covers, agrees with sheaf cohomology.

## Basic Properties

### Vanishing

- $H^0(X, \mathcal{F}) = \Gamma(X, \mathcal{F})$ (global sections)
- $H^i(X, \mathcal{F}) = 0$ for $i > \dim(X)$ (Grothendieck vanishing)

### Long Exact Sequence

Short exact sequence of sheaves:
$$0 \to \mathcal{F}' \to \mathcal{F} \to \mathcal{F}'' \to 0$$

Induces long exact sequence:
$$0 \to H^0(\mathcal{F}') \to H^0(\mathcal{F}) \to H^0(\mathcal{F}'') \to H^1(\mathcal{F}') \to \cdots$$

Powerful computational tool!

## Cohomology of Projective Space

### Key Result

For $\mathbb{P}^n$ and line bundle $\mathcal{O}(d)$:

$$H^q(\mathbb{P}^n, \mathcal{O}(d)) = \begin{cases} \text{Sym}^d(k^{n+1}) & q=0, d \geq 0 \\ 0 & 0 < q < n \\ \text{Sym}^{-d-n-1}(k^{n+1})^* & q=n, d \leq -n-1 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

Fundamental calculation!

### Serre's Theorem

For coherent sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ on projective scheme:
- $H^i(X, \mathcal{F})$ is finite-dimensional
- $H^i(X, \mathcal{F}(d)) = 0$ for $i > 0$ and $d \gg 0$

## Applications

### Riemann-Roch Theorem

For curve $C$ of genus $g$ and divisor $D$:

$$h^0(C, \mathcal{O}(D)) - h^1(C, \mathcal{O}(D)) = \deg(D) + 1 - g$$

Relates cohomology to topology!

### Classification of Varieties

Cohomology groups are birational invariants.

Help classify algebraic varieties.

### Deformation Theory

$H^1(X, T_X)$ parametrizes infinitesimal deformations.

$H^2(X, T_X)$ contains obstructions.

### Hodge Theory

For smooth projective variety over $\mathbb{C}$:

$$H^k(X, \mathbb{C}) = \bigoplus_{p+q=k} H^{p,q}(X)$$

where $H^{p,q} \cong H^q(X, \Omega^p_X)$.

Deep connection between algebraic and topology!

## Derived Categories

### Motivation

Want to work with complexes up to quasi-isomorphism.

**Derived category** $D(X)$: Localization of category of complexes.

### Derived Functors

In derived category, derived functors become actual functors.

More conceptual approach to cohomology.

### Modern Perspective

Many results cleaner in derived category language.

Foundation of modern algebraic geometry.

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Presheaf and Sheaf
> Presheaf may not satisfy gluing axiom. Must sheafify!

> [!warning] Assuming Higher Cohomology Always Vanishes
> Only vanishes for affine schemes (Serre's criterion). Projective varieties have rich cohomology.

> [!warning] Forgetting About Twisting
> On projective space, must consider $\mathcal{O}(d)$ for various $d$, not just $\mathcal{O}$.
