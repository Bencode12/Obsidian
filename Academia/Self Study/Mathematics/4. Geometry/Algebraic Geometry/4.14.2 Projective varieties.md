## Projective Space

### Definition

**Projective $n$-space** over field $k$:
$$\mathbb{P}^n_k = (\mathbb{A}^{n+1} \setminus \{0\})/\sim$$

where $(x_0, \ldots, x_n) \sim (\lambda x_0, \ldots, \lambda x_n)$ for $\lambda \in k^\times$.

Points are equivalence classes $[x_0 : x_1 : \cdots : x_n]$ (homogeneous coordinates).

### Homogeneous Polynomials

Polynomial $f$ is **homogeneous of degree $d$** if:
$$f(\lambda x_0, \ldots, \lambda x_n) = \lambda^d f(x_0, \ldots, x_n)$$

Well-defined on projective space!

## Projective Varieties

### Zero Locus

For homogeneous polynomials $f_1, \ldots, f_r$:
$$V(f_1, \ldots, f_r) = \{[x] \in \mathbb{P}^n : f_i(x) = 0 \text{ for all } i\}$$

Well-defined because zeros don't depend on representative.

### Projective Variety

Irreducible projective algebraic set.

Defined by homogeneous ideal in $k[x_0, \ldots, x_n]$.

---

## Examples

### Projective Line $\mathbb{P}^1$

Points: $[x_0 : x_1]$

Can think as $\mathbb{A}^1 \cup \{\infty\}$ where:
- $[x:1]$ corresponds to $x \in \mathbb{A}^1$
- $[1:0]$ is point at infinity

### Conics in $\mathbb{P}^2$

General conic: $V(ax_0^2 + bx_1^2 + cx_2^2 + dx_0x_1 + ex_0x_2 + fx_1x_2)$

All smooth conics are isomorphic to $\mathbb{P}^1$!

### Twisted Cubic in $\mathbb{P}^3$

Image of Veronese embedding:
$$\nu_3: \mathbb{P}^1 \to \mathbb{P}^3, \quad [s:t] \mapsto [s^3 : s^2t : st^2 : t^3]$$

Defined by $2 \times 2$ minors of matrix:
$$\begin{pmatrix} x_0 & x_1 & x_2 \\ x_1 & x_2 & x_3 \end{pmatrix}$$

## Affine Charts

### Standard Cover

$\mathbb{P}^n$ covered by $n+1$ affine charts:
$$U_i = \{[x_0:\cdots:x_n] : x_i \neq 0\} \cong \mathbb{A}^n$$

Map: $[x_0:\cdots:x_n] \mapsto \left(\frac{x_0}{x_i}, \ldots, \widehat{\frac{x_i}{x_i}}, \ldots, \frac{x_n}{x_i}\right)$

### Example: $\mathbb{P}^1$

Two charts:
- $U_0 = \{[x_0:x_1] : x_0 \neq 0\} \cong \mathbb{A}^1$ via $[x_0:x_1] \mapsto x_1/x_0$
- $U_1 = \{[x_0:x_1] : x_1 \neq 0\} \cong \mathbb{A}^1$ via $[x_0:x_1] \mapsto x_0/x_1$

Glue along $\mathbb{A}^1 \setminus \{0\}$ via $z \mapsto 1/z$.

## Homogeneous Coordinate Ring

### Definition

For projective variety $X \subseteq \mathbb{P}^n$ defined by homogeneous ideal $I$:

$$S(X) = k[x_0, \ldots, x_n]/I$$

Graded ring (decomposes by degree).

### Important Difference

Unlike affine case, $S(X)$ is NOT ring of functions on $X$!

Only constant functions are globally defined on projective varieties.

---

## Hilbert Polynomial

### Hilbert Function

For graded ring $S = \bigoplus_{d \geq 0} S_d$:
$$H_S(d) = \dim_k S_d$$

Dimension of degree-$d$ component.

### Hilbert Polynomial

For large $d$, $H_S(d)$ agrees with polynomial $P_X(d)$.

**Degree** of $P_X$ = dimension of $X$.

**Leading coefficient** relates to degree of variety.

### Example

For $\mathbb{P}^n$:
$$H(d) = \binom{n+d}{n}$$

Polynomial of degree $n$.

## Degree of Variety

### Definition

**Degree** of projective variety $X \subseteq \mathbb{P}^n$ of dimension $k$:

Number of intersection points with general linear subspace of codimension $k$.

### Examples

- Line in $\mathbb{P}^2$: degree 1
- Conic in $\mathbb{P}^2$: degree 2
- Twisted cubic in $\mathbb{P}^3$: degree 3
- Plane in $\mathbb{P}^3$: degree 1

### Bezout's Theorem

For curves $C_1, C_2 \subseteq \mathbb{P}^2$ of degrees $d_1, d_2$:

$$|C_1 \cap C_2| = d_1 d_2$$

Counting multiplicities and points at infinity!

## Compactness

### Key Advantage

Projective varieties are **complete** (proper):

- Closed in Zariski topology
- Analog of compactness in algebraic geometry
- Images of morphisms are closed

Contrast: Affine varieties are rarely complete.

### Example

Affine line $\mathbb{A}^1$ not complete.

Projective line $\mathbb{P}^1$ is complete (compactification of $\mathbb{A}^1$).

## Morphisms

### Regular Maps

Map $\phi: X \to Y$ between projective varieties given by homogeneous polynomials of same degree:

$$\phi([x_0:\cdots:x_n]) = [f_0(x):\cdots:f_m(x)]$$

Must be well-defined (not all zero simultaneously).

### Veronese Embedding

$d$-uple Veronese:
$$\nu_d: \mathbb{P}^n \to \mathbb{P}^N, \quad N = \binom{n+d}{d}-1$$

Maps to higher-dimensional projective space using all monomials of degree $d$.

Embeds variety with higher degree but better properties.

---

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Using Non-Homogeneous Polynomials
> Must use homogeneous polynomials for projective varieties. Otherwise not well-defined!

> [!warning] Confusing Affine and Projective Closure
> Projective closure adds points at infinity. Not always straightforward.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Functions Are Constant
> Only constant regular functions on projective varieties. Use rational functions instead.
