## The Coordinate Plane

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|$x$-axis|Horizontal reference line|
|$y$-axis|Vertical reference line|
|**Origin**|$(0, 0)$ — intersection of the axes|

Every point is written as an **ordered pair** $(x, y)$.

$$( 3,\ 4 ) \quad \longrightarrow \quad 3 \text{ right},\ 4 \text{ up}$$

---

## Distance Formula

Distance between $(x_1, y_1)$ and $(x_2, y_2)$:

$$d = \sqrt{(x_2 - x_1)^2 + (y_2 - y_1)^2}$$

> [!tip] This is just Pythagoras The horizontal gap is $(x_2 - x_1)$, the vertical gap is $(y_2 - y_1)$. The distance is the hypotenuse.

---

## Midpoint Formula

Midpoint of the segment from $(x_1, y_1)$ to $(x_2, y_2)$:

$$M = \left( \frac{x_1 + x_2}{2},\ \frac{y_1 + y_2}{2} \right)$$

Average the $x$-coordinates, average the $y$-coordinates.

---

## Slope

Slope $m$ measures the **steepness** of a line:

$$m = \frac{y_2 - y_1}{x_2 - x_1} = \frac{\text{rise}}{\text{run}}$$

|Slope|Meaning|
|---|---|
|$m > 0$|Line rises left to right|
|$m < 0$|Line falls left to right|
|$m = 0$|Horizontal line|
|Undefined|Vertical line ($x_2 - x_1 = 0$)|

---

## Equation of a Line

|Form|Equation|Use|
|---|---|---|
|**Slope-intercept**|$y = mx + c$|When you know slope and $y$-intercept|
|**Point-slope**|$y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$|When you know slope and one point|

> [!tip] Converting between forms Start with point-slope, expand, then rearrange to $y = mx + c$.

## Practice Problems

1. Find the distance between $(1, 2)$ and $(4, 6)$.
2. Find the midpoint of the segment from $(-3, 5)$ to $(7, -1)$.
3. Find the slope of the line through $(2, 3)$ and $(6, 11)$.
4. Write the equation of the line with slope $3$ and $y$-intercept $-4$.
5. Write the equation of the line through $(1, 5)$ with slope $2$.
6. Find the slope of a line parallel to $y = -3x + 7$.
