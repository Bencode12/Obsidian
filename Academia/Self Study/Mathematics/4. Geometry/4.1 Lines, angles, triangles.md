### **4.1.1 Lines**

- **Line**: straight path extending infinitely in both directions
- **Line segment**: part of a line with two endpoints
- **Ray**: starts at one point, extends infinitely in one direction

### **4.1.2 Types of lines**

- **Parallel lines**: never meet
- **Perpendicular lines**: intersect at 90°
- **Intersecting lines**: cross at any angle

### **4.1.3 Angles**

- **Angle**: formed when two lines meet at a point
- **Measured in degrees (°)**

Types of angles:

- Acute: < 90°
- Right: = 90°
- Obtuse: > 90° and < 180°
- Straight: = 180°
- Reflex: > 180°

### **4.1.4 Angle relationships**

- **Complementary**: sum = 90°
- **Supplementary**: sum = 180°
- **Vertically opposite angles**: equal when lines intersect

### **4.1.5 Triangles**

- Triangle: 3 sides, 3 angles
- **Types by sides**:
    - Equilateral: all sides equal
    - Isosceles: 2 sides equal
    - Scalene: all sides different
- **Types by angles**:
    - Acute: all angles < 90°
    - Right: one 90° angle
    - Obtuse: one angle > 90°

### **4.1.6 Properties**

1. Sum of angles in a triangle = 180°
2. Exterior angle = sum of two opposite interior angles
