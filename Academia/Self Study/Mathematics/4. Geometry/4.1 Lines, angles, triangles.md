## Lines

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Line**|Straight path extending infinitely in both directions|
|**Line segment**|Part of a line with two endpoints|
|**Ray**|Starts at one point, extends infinitely in one direction|

### Types of Lines

|Type|Property|
|---|---|
|**Parallel**|Never meet — constant distance apart|
|**Perpendicular**|Intersect at exactly $90°$|
|**Intersecting**|Cross at any angle|

---

## Angles

An angle is formed when two rays meet at a point. Measured in **degrees ($°$)**.

|Type|Measure|
|---|---|
|**Acute**|$0° < \theta < 90°$|
|**Right**|$\theta = 90°$|
|**Obtuse**|$90° < \theta < 180°$|
|**Straight**|$\theta = 180°$|
|**Reflex**|$180° < \theta < 360°$|

### Angle Relationships

|Relationship|Condition|Sum|
|---|---|---|
|**Complementary**|Two angles together form a right angle|$= 90°$|
|**Supplementary**|Two angles together form a straight line|$= 180°$|
|**Vertically opposite**|Formed by two intersecting lines|Equal|

---

## Triangles

A triangle has **3 sides** and **3 angles**.

### By Sides

|Type|Property|
|---|---|
|**Equilateral**|All three sides equal — all angles $60°$|
|**Isosceles**|Two sides equal — base angles equal|
|**Scalene**|All sides different — all angles different|

### By Angles

|Type|Property|
|---|---|
|**Acute**|All angles $< 90°$|
|**Right**|One angle $= 90°$|
|**Obtuse**|One angle $> 90°$|

---

## Triangle Properties

$$\text{Sum of interior angles} = 180°$$

$$\text{Exterior angle} = \text{sum of the two non-adjacent interior angles}$$

> [!tip] Exterior angle shortcut If two interior angles of a triangle are $40°$ and $65°$, the exterior angle at the third vertex is $40° + 65° = 105°$ — no need to find the third interior angle first.

## Practice Problems

1. Two angles are complementary. One is $34°$. Find the other.
2. Find the missing angle in a triangle with angles $72°$ and $48°$.
3. An exterior angle of a triangle is $110°$. One of the non-adjacent interior angles is $63°$. Find the other.
4. Classify a triangle with sides $5, 5, 8$ by sides and angles.
5. Two lines intersect. One angle is $37°$. Find all four angles.
