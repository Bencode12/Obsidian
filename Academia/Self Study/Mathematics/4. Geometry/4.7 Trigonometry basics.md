## The Six Trigonometric Ratios

For a right triangle with angle $\theta$, opposite $O$, adjacent $A$, hypotenuse $H$:

|Function|Ratio|Reciprocal of|
|---|---|---|
|$\sin\theta$|$O/H$|$\csc\theta$|
|$\cos\theta$|$A/H$|$\sec\theta$|
|$\tan\theta$|$O/A$|$\cot\theta$|
|$\csc\theta$|$H/O$|$\sin\theta$|
|$\sec\theta$|$H/A$|$\cos\theta$|
|$\cot\theta$|$A/O$|$\tan\theta$|

## Pythagorean Identities

$$\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$$

$$1 + \tan^2\theta = \sec^2\theta$$

$$1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$$

See [[Trig_Identities]] for derivations and applications.

## Domain and Range

|Function|Domain|Range|
|---|---|---|
|$\sin\theta$, $\cos\theta$|All real numbers|$[-1,\ 1]$|
|$\tan\theta$|$\theta \neq 90° + n \cdot 180°$|All real numbers|
|$\cot\theta$|$\theta \neq n \cdot 180°$|All real numbers|
|$\sec\theta$|$\theta \neq 90° + n \cdot 180°$|$(-\infty, -1] \cup [1, \infty)$|
|$\csc\theta$|$\theta \neq n \cdot 180°$|$(-\infty, -1] \cup [1, \infty)$|

## The Unit Circle

On a unit circle (radius $= 1$), for any angle $\theta$ measured from the positive $x$-axis:

$$x = \cos\theta \qquad y = \sin\theta$$

This extends the definitions beyond right triangles to all angles.
