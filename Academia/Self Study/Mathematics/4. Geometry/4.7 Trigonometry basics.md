### **4.7.1 What is trigonometry?**

Trigonometry studies the relationships between **angles and sides** of **right-angled triangles**.

### **4.7.2 Key ratios (for a right triangle)**

Let a right triangle have:

- **Opposite** side to angle θ → $O$
- **Adjacent** side → $A$
- **Hypotenuse** → $H$

Trigonometric functions:

|Function|Formula|
|---|---|
|Sine (sin)|sin⁡θ=O/H\sin θ = O / Hsinθ=O/H|
|Cosine (cos)|cos⁡θ=A/H\cos θ = A / Hcosθ=A/H|
|Tangent (tan)|tan⁡θ=O/A\tan θ = O / Atanθ=O/A|
|Cotangent (cot)|cot⁡θ=A/O\cot θ = A / Ocotθ=A/O|
|Secant (sec)|sec⁡θ=H/A\sec θ = H / Asecθ=H/A|
|Cosecant (csc)|csc⁡θ=H/O\csc θ = H / Ocscθ=H/O|

### **4.7.3 Important identities**

1. sin⁡2θ+cos⁡2θ=1\sin^2 θ + \cos^2 θ = 1sin2θ+cos2θ=1
2. 1+tan⁡2θ=sec⁡2θ1 + \tan^2 θ = \sec^2 θ1+tan2θ=sec2θ
3. 1+cot⁡2θ=csc⁡2θ1 + \cot^2 θ = \csc^2 θ1+cot2θ=csc2θ
4. tan⁡θ=sin⁡θ/cos⁡θ\tan θ = \sin θ / \cos θtanθ=sinθ/cosθ, cot⁡θ=cos⁡θ/sin⁡θ\cot θ = \cos θ / \sin θcotθ=cosθ/sinθ

### **4.7.5 Key values for sine, cosine, tangent**

For angles 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°:

|θ|0°|30°|45°|60°|90°|
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
|sin θ|0|1/2|√2/2|√3/2|1|
|cos θ|1|√3/2|√2/2|1/2|0|
|tan θ|0|1/√3|1|√3|∞|

Other ratios can be derived from these (cot = 1/tan, sec = 1/cos, csc = 1/sin).

### **4.7.6 Unit circle idea**

- Angles measured from positive x-axis
- x-coordinate = cos θ, y-coordinate = sin θ
