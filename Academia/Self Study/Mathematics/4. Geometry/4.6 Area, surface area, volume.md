## Area of 2D Shapes

|Shape|Formula|
|---|---|
|**Rectangle**|$A = l \times w$|
|**Square**|$A = a^2$|
|**Triangle**|$A = \dfrac{1}{2} b h$|
|**Parallelogram**|$A = b h$|
|**Trapezium**|$A = \dfrac{1}{2}(a + b)h$|
|**Circle**|$A = \pi r^2$|

> [!tip] Triangle vs Parallelogram A triangle is exactly half a parallelogram with the same base and height — that is where the $\frac{1}{2}$ comes from.

## Surface Area of 3D Solids

|Solid|Formula|
|---|---|
|**Cube**|$SA = 6a^2$|
|**Cuboid**|$SA = 2(lw + lh + wh)$|
|**Sphere**|$SA = 4\pi r^2$|
|**Cylinder**|$SA = 2\pi r(r + h)$|
|**Cone**|$SA = \pi r(r + l)$ where $l$ = slant height|

---

## Volume of 3D Solids

|Solid|Formula|
|---|---|
|**Cube**|$V = a^3$|
|**Cuboid**|$V = l \times w \times h$|
|**Sphere**|$V = \dfrac{4}{3}\pi r^3$|
|**Cylinder**|$V = \pi r^2 h$|
|**Cone**|$V = \dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h$|

> [!tip] Cone vs Cylinder A cone holds exactly $\frac{1}{3}$ the volume of a cylinder with the same base and height.

## Practice Problems

1. Find the area of a trapezium with parallel sides $6$ cm and $10$ cm, height $4$ cm.
2. Find the surface area of a cuboid $3 \times 5 \times 8$ cm.
3. Find the volume of a sphere with radius $3$ cm.
4. A cylinder has radius $4$ cm and height $10$ cm. Find its volume and surface area.
5. A cone has base radius $5$ cm and slant height $13$ cm. Find its surface area and volume. _(Hint: find the perpendicular height using Pythagoras first.)_
