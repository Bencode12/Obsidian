## What is a Polygon?

A polygon is a **closed figure** formed by three or more straight line segments.

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Side**|Each straight line segment|
|**Vertex**|The point where two sides meet|

## Regular vs Irregular

|Type|Property|
|---|---|
|**Regular**|All sides equal **and** all angles equal|
|**Irregular**|Sides and/or angles are unequal|

---

## Sum of Interior Angles

$$\text{Sum of interior angles} = (n - 2) \times 180°$$

where $n$ is the number of sides.

|Polygon|$n$|Interior angle sum|
|---|---|---|
|Triangle|3|$180°$|
|Quadrilateral|4|$360°$|
|Pentagon|5|$540°$|
|Hexagon|6|$720°$|

> [!tip] Each interior angle of a regular polygon Divide the total by $n$: $$\text{Each angle} = \frac{(n-2) \times 180°}{n}$$ A regular hexagon: $\dfrac{4 \times 180°}{6} = 120°$

---

## Exterior Angles

$$\text{Sum of exterior angles of any polygon} = 360°$$

This holds for **every** polygon regardless of the number of sides.

For a regular polygon, each exterior angle $= \dfrac{360°}{n}$.
