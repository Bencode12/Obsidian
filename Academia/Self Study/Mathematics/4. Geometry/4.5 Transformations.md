### **4.5.1 What are transformations?**

Transformations are **changes applied to a shape** on a plane without altering its size or angles (except for scaling).

Types of transformations:

### **4.5.2 Translation**

- **Moves** a shape from one position to another without rotating or flipping.
- Each point moves **same distance in the same direction**.

Example: Move 3 units right, 2 units up:  
(x,y)→(x+3,y+2)(x, y) → (x+3, y+2)(x,y)→(x+3,y+2)

### **4.5.3 Reflection**

- **Flips** a shape over a line (mirror line).
- Each point is **equidistant from the mirror line**.

Example: Reflection over X-axis: (x,y)→(x,−y)(x, y) → (x, −y)(x,y)→(x,−y)  
Reflection over Y-axis: (x,y)→(−x,y)(x, y) → (−x, y)(x,y)→(−x,y)

### **4.5.4 Rotation**

- **Turns** a shape around a fixed point (center of rotation).
- Specified by **angle and direction** (clockwise or counterclockwise).

Example: 90° clockwise rotation about origin: (x,y)→(y,−x)(x, y) → (y, −x)(x,y)→(y,−x)

### **4.5.5 Scaling / Dilation**

- **Changes size** of a shape, either enlarging or reducing.
- Multiply coordinates by a **scale factor**.

Example: Scale factor 2: (x,y)→(2x,2y)(x, y) → (2x, 2y)(x,y)→(2x,2y)
