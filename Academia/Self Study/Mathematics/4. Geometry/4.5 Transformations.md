## What is a Transformation?

A transformation is a change applied to a shape on the coordinate plane. Three of the four types **preserve size and shape** — only dilation changes size.

|Transformation|Changes size?|Changes orientation?|
|---|---|---|
|Translation|No|No|
|Reflection|No|Yes (flipped)|
|Rotation|No|Yes (turned)|
|Dilation|Yes|No|

---

## Translation

Moves every point the **same distance in the same direction**. No rotation, no flipping.

$$(x, y) \to (x + a,\ y + b)$$

> [!example] Move 3 right, 2 up: $(x, y) \to (x + 3,\ y + 2)$

---

## Reflection

Flips a shape over a **mirror line**. Each point is equidistant from the line on the opposite side.

|Mirror line|Rule|
|---|---|
|$x$-axis|$(x, y) \to (x, -y)$|
|$y$-axis|$(x, y) \to (-x, y)$|
|Line $y = x$|$(x, y) \to (y, x)$|

---

## Rotation

Turns a shape around a **fixed point** by a given angle and direction.

|Rotation about origin|Rule|
|---|---|
|$90°$ clockwise|$(x, y) \to (y, -x)$|
|$90°$ counter-clockwise|$(x, y) \to (-y, x)$|
|$180°$|$(x, y) \to (-x, -y)$|

> [!tip] $180°$ is the same in both directions Rotating $180°$ clockwise gives the same result as $180°$ counter-clockwise.

---

## Dilation (Scaling)

**Enlarges or reduces** a shape by multiplying coordinates by a **scale factor** $k$.

$$(x, y) \to (kx,\ ky)$$

|Scale factor|Effect|
|---|---|
|$k > 1$|Enlargement|
|$0 < k < 1$|Reduction|
|$k = 1$|No change|

> [!example] Scale factor $2$: $(3, 5) \to (6, 10)$

## Practice Problems

1. Translate $(2, -3)$ by $4$ left and $1$ up.
2. Reflect $(5, 3)$ over the $x$-axis.
3. Reflect $(-2, 4)$ over the $y$-axis.
4. Rotate $(3, 1)$ by $90°$ clockwise about the origin.
5. Rotate $(4, -2)$ by $180°$ about the origin.
6. Dilate $(3, 6)$ with scale factor $\frac{1}{3}$.
