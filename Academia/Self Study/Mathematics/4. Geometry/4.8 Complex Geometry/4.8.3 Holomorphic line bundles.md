## Holomorphic Line Bundles

### Definition

**Holomorphic line bundle**: Rank 1 holomorphic vector bundle.

Transition functions $g_{\alpha\beta}: U_\alpha \cap U_\beta \to \mathbb{C}^*$ are holomorphic.

### Picard Group

**Picard group** $\text{Pic}(M)$: Group of holomorphic line bundles under tensor product.

Isomorphic to $H^1(M, \mathcal{O}^*)$ via Čech cohomology.

### Examples

- **Trivial bundle**: $M \times \mathbb{C}$
- **Tautological bundle** $\mathcal{O}(-1)$ on $\mathbb{CP}^n$
- **Hyperplane bundle** $\mathcal{O}(1)$ on $\mathbb{CP}^n$
- **Canonical bundle** $K_M = \Lambda^n T^{*1,0}M$

## Divisors on Complex Manifolds

### Divisors

**Divisor**: Formal sum $D = \sum n_i D_i$ where $D_i$ are codimension-1 analytic subvarieties.

Group of divisors: $\text{Div}(M)$.

### Principal Divisors

For meromorphic function $f$:
$$(f) = \sum_D \text{ord}_D(f) \cdot D$$

**Principal divisor**: Divisor of meromorphic function.

### Linear Equivalence

$D_1 \sim D_2$ if $D_1 - D_2 = (f)$ for some meromorphic $f$.

**Divisor class group**: $\text{Cl}(M) = \text{Div}(M)/\text{Prin}(M)$.

## Correspondence: Divisors and Line Bundles

### From Divisor to Line Bundle

For divisor $D$, construct line bundle $\mathcal{O}(D)$:

Locally defined by meromorphic functions with prescribed zeros/poles along $D$.

### From Line Bundle to Divisor

For line bundle $L$ with meromorphic section $s$:
$$D = (s) = \text{divisor of zeros and poles of } s$$

Well-defined up to linear equivalence!

### Isomorphism

$$\text{Pic}(M) \cong \text{Cl}(M)$$

for smooth complex manifolds.

## Chern Class of Line Bundle

### First Chern Class

For holomorphic line bundle $L$:
$$c_1(L) \in H^2(M, \mathbb{Z})$$

Topological invariant.

### Curvature Representation

If $L$ has Hermitian metric with curvature form $F$:
$$c_1(L) = \left[\frac{i}{2\pi}F\right] \in H^2(M, \mathbb{R})$$

Chern-Weil theory!

### Example: $\mathbb{CP}^n$

$$c_1(\mathcal{O}(1)) = [\omega_{FS}] \in H^2(\mathbb{CP}^n, \mathbb{Z})$$

Generator of cohomology ring.

## Ample Line Bundles

### Definition

Line bundle $L$ is **ample** if some power $L^{\otimes k}$ is very ample.

**Very ample**: Defines embedding into projective space.

### Kodaira Embedding Theorem

Compact complex manifold is projective algebraic ⟺ admits positive (ample) line bundle.

Bridge between complex and algebraic geometry!

### Nakai-Moishezon Criterion

On surface, $L$ ample ⟺:
- $L^2 > 0$ (self-intersection positive)
- $L \cdot C > 0$ for all curves $C$

## Canonical Bundle

### Definition

**Canonical bundle**: $K_M = \Lambda^n T^{*1,0}M$

Bundle of holomorphic $n$-forms.

Sections: Holomorphic volume forms.

### Plurigenera

**Plurigenera**: $P_m = h^0(M, K_M^{\otimes m})$

Dimension of space of holomorphic $m$-canonical forms.

Birational invariants!

### Classification by Canonical Bundle

|Type|Condition|Example|
|---|---|---|
|Fano|$K_M^{-1}$ ample|$\mathbb{CP}^n$|
|Calabi-Yau|$K_M$ trivial|K3 surface|
|General type|$K_M$ big|Most hypersurfaces|

## Adjunction Formula

### Statement

For smooth hypersurface $D \subset M$:
$$K_D = (K_M \otimes \mathcal{O}(D))|_D$$

Relates canonical bundles of submanifold and ambient space.

### Example: Hypersurface in $\mathbb{CP}^n$

For degree $d$ hypersurface $X_d \subset \mathbb{CP}^n$:
$$K_{X_d} = \mathcal{O}(d-n-1)|_{X_d}$$

- $d < n+1$: Fano ($K^{-1}$ ample)
- $d = n+1$: Calabi-Yau ($K$ trivial)
- $d > n+1$: General type ($K$ ample)

## Riemann-Roch Theorem

### For Curves

On Riemann surface of genus $g$, for divisor $D$:
$$h^0(D) - h^1(D) = \deg(D) + 1 - g$$

Using Serre duality: $h^1(D) = h^0(K-D)$.

### Hirzebruch-Riemann-Roch

For vector bundle $E$ on compact complex manifold:
$$\chi(E) = \int_M \text{ch}(E)\text{Td}(M)$$

where $\text{ch}$ is Chern character, $\text{Td}$ is Todd class.

### Grothendieck-Riemann-Roch

Relative version for morphism $f: X \to Y$:
$$\text{ch}(f_!E)\text{Td}(Y) = f_*(\text{ch}(E)\text{Td}(X))$$

Most general form!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Algebraic and Analytic Divisors
> On projective varieties they coincide. On general complex manifolds, may differ.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Not All Line Bundles Have Sections
> Many line bundles have $h^0 = 0$. Ampleness guarantees sections for high powers.

> [!warning] Assuming Canonical Bundle Is Always Trivial
> Only for special manifolds (tori, K3, etc.). Generally non-trivial.
