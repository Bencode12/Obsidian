## Stein Manifolds

### Definition

Complex manifold $X$ is **Stein** if:
1. Holomorphically convex
2. Holomorphic functions separate points
3. Global holomorphic functions give local coordinates

Analog of affine varieties in algebraic geometry!

### Examples

- $\mathbb{C}^n$: Prototype Stein manifold
- Open subsets of $\mathbb{C}^n$ (domains of holomorphy)
- Smooth affine algebraic varieties

### Properties

- No compact complex subvarieties (except points)
- Homotopy equivalent to CW complex of dimension $\leq n$
- $H^q(X, \mathcal{O}) = 0$ for $q > 0$ (Cartan's Theorem B)

## Domains of Holomorphy

### Definition

Domain $\Omega \subset \mathbb{C}^n$ is **domain of holomorphy** if exists holomorphic function on $\Omega$ that cannot be extended beyond any boundary point.

### Levi Problem

**Levi problem**: Characterize domains of holomorphy.

**Solution**: Domain of holomorphy ⟺ Pseudoconvex.

### Pseudoconvexity

Domain is **pseudoconvex** if admits plurisubharmonic exhaustion function.

Generalizes convexity to complex setting!

---

## Plurisubharmonic Functions

### Definition

Function $\phi: X \to \mathbb{R} \cup \{-\infty\}$ is **plurisubharmonic (psh)** if:
- Upper semicontinuous
- Restriction to every complex line is subharmonic

Equivalently: $i\partial\bar{\partial}\phi \geq 0$ (positive current).

### Examples

- $\phi(z) = |z|^2$: Strictly psh on $\mathbb{C}^n$
- $\phi(z) = \log|z|$: Psh on $\mathbb{C}^n \setminus \{0\}$

## Coherent Sheaves

### Definition

Sheaf $\mathcal{F}$ of $\mathcal{O}_X$-modules is **coherent** if locally finitely presented.

On Stein manifold: Global sections determine sheaf!

### Cartan's Theorems

**Theorem A**: Coherent sheaf on Stein manifold generated by global sections.

**Theorem B**: $H^q(X, \mathcal{F}) = 0$ for $q > 0$ and coherent $\mathcal{F}$.

Powerful vanishing results!

## Analytic Subvarieties

### Definition

**Analytic subvariety** $V \subset X$: Locally defined as zero set of holomorphic functions.

### Regular and Singular Points

- **Regular point**: Variety is manifold near point
- **Singular point**: Not regular

Singular locus has codimension $\geq 1$.

---

## Resolution of Singularities

### Hironaka's Theorem

For complex analytic variety $X$, exists smooth variety $\tilde{X}$ and proper birational morphism:

$$\pi: \tilde{X} \to X$$

that is isomorphism over smooth locus.

### Applications

- Define integration on singular varieties
- Study topology of singularities
- Compute invariants via resolution

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Stein and Projective
> Stein = affine-like (many functions). Projective = compact (few functions). Opposite extremes!

> [!warning] Assuming Pseudoconvex = Convex
> Pseudoconvexity is weaker. Many non-convex domains are pseudoconvex.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Coherence Is Local
> Must check finite presentation locally, not just globally.
