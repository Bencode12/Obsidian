## Classification of Complex Surfaces

Compact complex surfaces classified by Kodaira dimension $\kappa$:

|$\kappa$|Type|Examples|
|---|---|---|
|$-\infty$|Rational/ruled|$\mathbb{CP}^2$, Hirzebruch surfaces|
|$0$|K3, Enriques, tori|K3 surface|
|$1$|Elliptic surfaces|Elliptic fibrations|
|$2$|General type|Hypersurfaces in $\mathbb{CP}^3$|

Enriques-Kodaira classification!

---

## K3 Surfaces

### Definition

Compact complex surface with:
- $K_X$ trivial (canonical bundle)
- $q = h^{1,0} = 0$

Simply connected Calabi-Yau surface!

### Examples

- Smooth quartic in $\mathbb{CP}^3$: $V(x^4 + y^4 + z^4 + w^4)$
- Kummer surface: Resolution of $T/\mathbb{Z}_2$ for torus $T$

### Hodge Diamond

```
      1
    0   0
  1  20  1
    0   0
      1
```

$h^{1,1} = 20$ is Picard number!

### Properties

- All K3 surfaces diffeomorphic
- Moduli space dimension 20
- Admit Ricci-flat Kähler metric (Yau's theorem)

## Elliptic Surfaces

### Definition

Surface with holomorphic map to curve:
$$\pi: X \to C$$

Generic fiber is elliptic curve (genus 1).

### Weierstrass Form

Locally fibers given by:
$$y^2 = x^3 + f(t)x + g(t)$$

where $t$ is coordinate on base curve.

---

## Surfaces of General Type

### Definition

Surface with Kodaira dimension $\kappa = 2$.

Canonical bundle is big (many sections).

### Noether's Formula

$$12\chi(\mathcal{O}_X) = K_X^2 + e(X)$$

where $e(X)$ is topological Euler characteristic.

### Bogomolov-Miyaoka-Yau Inequality

For minimal surface of general type:
$$K_X^2 \leq 3e(X)$$

Equality iff universal cover is unit ball $\mathbb{B}^2 \subset \mathbb{C}^2$.

---

## Minimal Models

### Definition

Surface is **minimal** if contains no $(-1)$-curves.

$(-1)$-curve: Rational curve $C \cong \mathbb{CP}^1$ with $C^2 = -1$.

### Castelnuovo Contraction

Any $(-1)$-curve can be contracted to smooth point.

Reverse of blow-up!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Different Surface Classes
> K3 ≠ Enriques ≠ Abelian. Check invariants carefully!

> [!warning] Assuming All Surfaces Are Algebraic
> Some complex surfaces are not projective algebraic.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Minimal Model May Not Be Unique
> For $\kappa = -\infty$, multiple minimal models possible.

## Practice Problems

Classify surfaces:

- Determine Kodaira dimension of given surface
- Calculate Hodge numbers from topological data
- Identify surface type from invariants

Work with specific examples:

- Verify that quartic surface is K3
- Show that cubic surface is rational
- Calculate invariants of elliptic surface

Conceptual:

- Why is classification by Kodaira dimension natural?
- Explain geometric meaning of $(-1)$-curves
- What makes K3 surfaces special?

- Prove Castelnuovo's rationality criterion
- Classify all minimal rational surfaces
