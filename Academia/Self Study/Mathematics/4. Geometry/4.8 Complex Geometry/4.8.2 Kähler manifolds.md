## Hermitian Metrics

### Definition

**Hermitian metric** on complex manifold $M$: Riemannian metric $g$ compatible with complex structure $J$.

$$g(JX, JY) = g(X,Y) \quad \text{for all } X,Y \in TM$$

Equivalently: $J$ is orthogonal transformation.

### Hermitian Form

Associated **Hermitian form**:
$$h(X,Y) = g(X,Y) - i\omega(X,Y)$$

where $\omega(X,Y) = g(JX,Y)$ is **fundamental 2-form**.

In holomorphic coordinates:
$$h = h_{i\bar{j}} \, dz^i \otimes d\bar{z}^j$$

with $(h_{i\bar{j}})$ positive-definite Hermitian matrix.

## Kähler Manifolds

### Definition

Hermitian manifold is **Kähler** if fundamental form is closed:

$$d\omega = 0$$

Equivalently: $\omega$ is symplectic form!

### Alternative Characterizations

Following are equivalent:
1. $d\omega = 0$
2. $\nabla J = 0$ (complex structure parallel)
3. Holonomy group $\subseteq U(n)$
4. Locally exists Kähler potential $\phi$ with $h_{i\bar{j}} = \frac{\partial^2 \phi}{\partial z^i \partial \bar{z}^j}$

## Examples of Kähler Manifolds

### Complex Euclidean Space

$\mathbb{C}^n$ with standard metric:
$$\omega = \frac{i}{2}\sum_{k=1}^n dz^k \wedge d\bar{z}^k = \sum_{k=1}^n dx^k \wedge dy^k$$

Clearly $d\omega = 0$. Flat Kähler!

### Complex Projective Space

**Fubini-Study metric** on $\mathbb{CP}^n$:

In affine chart, Kähler potential:
$$\phi = \log(1 + |z|^2)$$

$$\omega_{FS} = i\partial\bar{\partial}\log(1 + |z|^2)$$

Compact Kähler manifold with positive curvature.

### Complex Tori

Flat torus $\mathbb{C}^n/\Lambda$ with constant metric.

Kähler with $\omega = \frac{i}{2}\sum h_{i\bar{j}} dz^i \wedge d\bar{z}^j$.

### Algebraic Varieties

Smooth projective algebraic varieties inherit Kähler metric from ambient $\mathbb{CP}^N$.

**Kodaira embedding theorem**: Compact Kähler manifold with integral Kähler class embeds in $\mathbb{CP}^N$.

## Kähler Identities

### Commutation Relations

On Kähler manifold, operators satisfy special relations:

$$[\Lambda, \partial] = i\bar{\partial}^*, \quad [\Lambda, \bar{\partial}] = -i\partial^*$$

where $\Lambda = L^*$ is adjoint of Lefschetz operator $L(\alpha) = \omega \wedge \alpha$.

### Consequences

- Laplacians coincide: $\Delta_d = 2\Delta_\partial = 2\Delta_{\bar{\partial}}$
- Hodge decomposition respects $(p,q)$ types
- Hard Lefschetz theorem holds

Powerful computational tools!

## Hodge Theory on Kähler Manifolds

### Hodge Decomposition

For compact Kähler manifold:

$$H^k(M, \mathbb{C}) = \bigoplus_{p+q=k} H^{p,q}(M)$$

where $H^{p,q}(M) \cong H^{p,q}_{\bar{\partial}}(M)$.

### Symmetries

**Complex conjugation**: $H^{p,q} \cong \overline{H^{q,p}}$

Therefore: $h^{p,q} = h^{q,p}$ where $h^{p,q} = \dim H^{p,q}$.

**Serre duality**: $H^{p,q} \cong H^{n-p,n-q*}$

---

## Hard Lefschetz Theorem

### Statement

For compact Kähler manifold of complex dimension $n$, Lefschetz operator:

$$L^k: H^{n-k}(M) \to H^{n+k}(M), \quad \alpha \mapsto \omega^k \wedge \alpha$$

is isomorphism for $0 \leq k \leq n$.

### Consequences

**Lefschetz decomposition**: Every cohomology class decomposes uniquely into primitive parts.

**Betti number constraints**:
- $b_{2k+1}$ even for all $k$
- $b_{2k} \geq b_{2k-2}$ for $k \leq n$

Strong topological restrictions!

## Hodge Numbers

### Definition

**Hodge numbers**: $h^{p,q} = \dim H^{p,q}(M)$

Arranged in **Hodge diamond**:

```
        h^{0,0}
     h^{1,0} h^{0,1}
  h^{2,0} h^{1,1} h^{0,2}
     ...
```

### Examples

**Riemann surface** (genus $g$):
```
    1
  g   g
    1
```

**K3 surface**:
```
      1
    0   0
  1  20  1
    0   0
      1
```

**$\mathbb{CP}^2$**:
```
      1
    0   0
  1   1   1
    0   0
      1
```

## Curvature of Kähler Manifolds

### Chern Connection

On holomorphic vector bundle with Hermitian metric, unique connection preserving both structures.

For tangent bundle of Kähler manifold: Coincides with Levi-Civita connection!

### Ricci Form

**Ricci form** of Kähler metric:
$$\rho = -i\partial\bar{\partial}\log\det(h_{i\bar{j}})$$

Represents first Chern class: $[\rho] = 2\pi c_1(M)$.

### Scalar Curvature

Trace of Ricci form with respect to $\omega$.

Important in Kähler-Einstein problem.

---

## Kähler-Einstein Metrics

### Definition

Kähler metric is **Kähler-Einstein** if:

$$\text{Ric} = \lambda\omega$$

for constant $\lambda$.

Equivalent to: $\rho = \lambda\omega$.

### Existence

Depends on sign of first Chern class:

- **$c_1 < 0$**: Always exists (Aubin-Yau theorem)
- **$c_1 = 0$**: Exists (Yau's solution to Calabi conjecture)
- **$c_1 > 0$**: Exists iff manifold is K-stable (Yau-Tian-Donaldson conjecture, proved 2012-2015)

Deep connection between differential geometry and algebraic stability!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Assuming All Complex Manifolds Are Kähler
> Many complex manifolds don't admit Kähler metrics (e.g., Hopf surfaces).

> [!warning] Confusing Hermitian and Kähler
> Kähler requires $d\omega = 0$. General Hermitian metrics don't satisfy this.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Kähler Implies Symplectic
> Fundamental form $\omega$ is both compatible with $J$ AND closed.
