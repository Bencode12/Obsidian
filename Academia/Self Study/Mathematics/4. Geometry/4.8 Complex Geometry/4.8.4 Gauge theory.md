## Principal Bundles in Complex Geometry

### Holomorphic Principal Bundles

**Holomorphic principal $G$-bundle**: Principal bundle with complex Lie group $G$ and holomorphic transition functions.

Structure group acts holomorphically on fibers.

### Examples

- **Frame bundle** of holomorphic tangent bundle: Principal $GL(n,\mathbb{C})$
- **Unitary frame bundle** (for Hermitian metric): Principal $U(n)$
- **Circle bundles**: Principal $S^1 = U(1)$ (classified by first Chern class)

## Connections on Holomorphic Bundles

### Chern Connection

On holomorphic vector bundle with Hermitian metric, unique connection satisfying:

1. Compatible with Hermitian metric
2. $(0,1)$-part equals $\bar{\partial}$ operator

Called **Chern connection** or **canonical connection**.

### Connection 1-Form

In local holomorphic frame, connection 1-form:
$$A = h^{-1}\partial h$$

where $h$ is Hermitian metric matrix.

Pure type $(1,0)$!

### Curvature

Curvature 2-form:
$$F = \bar{\partial}A = \bar{\partial}(h^{-1}\partial h)$$

Pure type $(1,1)$: $F \in \Omega^{1,1}(\text{End}(E))$.

## Yang-Mills Theory

### Yang-Mills Functional

For connection $A$ on bundle $E$ over Riemannian manifold:

$$YM(A) = \int_M \|F_A\|^2 \, d\text{vol}$$

Measures "energy" of connection.

### Yang-Mills Equations

Critical points satisfy:
$$D_A^* F_A = 0$$

where $D_A^*$ is adjoint of covariant derivative.

Nonlinear PDE for connection!

## Hermite-Einstein Metrics

### Definition

Hermitian metric $h$ on holomorphic bundle $E$ is **Hermite-Einstein** if:

$$i\Lambda F_h = \lambda \cdot \text{id}_E$$

where $\Lambda$ contracts with Kähler form, $\lambda$ is constant.

Generalizes Einstein condition to bundles!

### Donaldson-Uhlenbeck-Yau Theorem

Holomorphic bundle admits Hermite-Einstein metric ⟺ bundle is polystable.

Deep correspondence between differential geometry and algebraic stability!

## Stability of Bundles

### Slope Stability

For holomorphic bundle $E$ on Kähler manifold:

**Slope**: $\mu(E) = \frac{\deg(E)}{\text{rank}(E)} = \frac{\int_M c_1(E) \wedge \omega^{n-1}}{\text{rank}(E)}$

**Stable**: For all proper subsheaves $F \subset E$:
$$\mu(F) < \mu(E)$$

### Polystability

**Polystable**: Direct sum of stable bundles with same slope.

Condition for existence of Hermite-Einstein metric!

## Moduli Spaces of Bundles

### Motivation

Want parameter space for isomorphism classes of bundles.

**Moduli space** $\mathcal{M}$: Points correspond to bundles.

### Construction via GIT

Moduli of stable bundles constructed as quotient:
$$\mathcal{M} = \mathcal{A}^{ss} // \mathcal{G}$$

where $\mathcal{A}$ is space of connections, $\mathcal{G}$ is gauge group.

Geometric Invariant Theory!

### Properties

For stable bundles on curve of genus $g$:
- $\dim \mathcal{M} = (r^2-1)(g-1)$ for rank $r$
- Smooth if $\gcd(r,d) = 1$
- Compact after adding semistable bundles

## Instantons

### Definition

On 4-manifold, connection is **instanton** if curvature is self-dual or anti-self-dual:

$$F = \pm *F$$

Minimizes Yang-Mills functional in topological class!

### ADHM Construction

All instantons on $S^4$ constructed via linear algebra data (ADHM construction).

Explicit parametrization!

### Donaldson Invariants

Use moduli space of instantons to define smooth 4-manifold invariants.

Revolutionary tool in 4-dimensional topology!

## Higgs Bundles

### Definition

**Higgs bundle**: Pair $(E, \phi)$ where:
- $E$: Holomorphic vector bundle
- $\phi \in H^0(M, \text{End}(E) \otimes K_M)$: Higgs field

Satisfies $\phi \wedge \phi = 0$ (automatically in dimension 1).

### Hitchin Equations

Generalization of Hermite-Einstein:
$$F_h + [\phi, \phi^*_h] = 0, \quad \bar{\partial}_E \phi = 0$$

Non-abelian Hodge theory!

### Moduli Space

**Hitchin moduli space**: Moduli of stable Higgs bundles.

Hyperkähler manifold (three compatible complex structures).

## Non-Abelian Hodge Correspondence

### Statement

For compact Kähler manifold, equivalence of categories:

1. Polystable Higgs bundles with $c_1 = 0$
2. Irreducible flat connections
3. Representations of fundamental group $\pi_1(M) \to GL(r,\mathbb{C})$

Deep bridge between algebraic, differential, and topological perspectives!

### Consequences

- Relates Hodge theory to representation theory
- Provides hyperkähler structure on moduli spaces
- Foundation of geometric Langlands program

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Stability Conditions
> Different notions: Mumford stability, slope stability, Gieseker stability. Context matters!

> [!warning] Assuming All Bundles Admit Special Metrics
> Only (poly)stable bundles admit Hermite-Einstein metrics.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Higgs Field Is Not Arbitrary
> Must be holomorphic section of $\text{End}(E) \otimes K_M$.
