## Mirror Symmetry

### Phenomenon

**Mirror symmetry**: Pairs of Calabi-Yau manifolds $(X, Y)$ with swapped Hodge numbers:

$$h^{p,q}(X) = h^{n-p,q}(Y)$$

where $n = \dim_{\mathbb{C}} X$.

Exchanges symplectic and complex geometry!

### Example: Quintic Threefolds

Quintic in $\mathbb{CP}^4$ and its mirror have:
- $X$: $h^{1,1} = 1$, $h^{2,1} = 101$
- $Y$: $h^{1,1} = 101$, $h^{2,1} = 1$

Hodge numbers exchanged!

## Homological Mirror Symmetry

### Kontsevich's Conjecture

For mirror pair $(X, Y)$, equivalence of categories:

$$D^b\text{Coh}(X) \cong D^\pi\text{Fuk}(Y)$$

where:
- $D^b\text{Coh}(X)$: Derived category of coherent sheaves (algebraic side)
- $D^\pi\text{Fuk}(Y)$: Derived Fukaya category (symplectic side)

Deep categorical formulation!

## Gromov-Witten Invariants

### Motivation

Count holomorphic curves in symplectic manifold.

Virtual counts (may be rational, not integer)!

### Definition

For homology class $\beta \in H_2(X, \mathbb{Z})$, **Gromov-Witten invariant**:

$$\langle \gamma_1, \ldots, \gamma_k \rangle_{g,\beta}^X$$

Counts genus $g$ curves in class $\beta$ intersecting cycles $\gamma_i$.

## Quantum Cohomology

### Definition

**Quantum cohomology** $QH^*(X)$: Deformation of ordinary cohomology ring.

Product includes contributions from holomorphic curves:

$$\alpha * \beta = \alpha \cup \beta + \sum_{\beta \neq 0} (\alpha * \beta)_\beta q^\beta$$

where $q^\beta$ are formal variables.

### Example: $\mathbb{CP}^n$

$$QH^*(\mathbb{CP}^n) = \mathbb{C}[H, q]/(H^{n+1} - q)$$

where $H$ is hyperplane class.

## Floer Homology

### Lagrangian Floer Homology

For Lagrangian submanifolds $L_0, L_1 \subset (M, \omega)$:

**Floer homology** $HF^*(L_0, L_1)$ counts pseudoholomorphic strips between intersection points.

Generalizes Morse homology!

### Applications

- Symplectic topology invariants
- Study of Lagrangian intersections
- Foundation for Fukaya category

## Enumerative Geometry

### Counting Curves

Classical problem: Count curves satisfying geometric conditions.

Modern approach: Use Gromov-Witten invariants.

### Example: Lines on Cubic Surface

Smooth cubic surface in $\mathbb{CP}^3$ contains exactly 27 lines.

Classical result, now understood via moduli spaces!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Different Types of Mirror Symmetry
> Homological, SYZ, and physical mirror symmetry are related but distinct formulations.

> [!warning] Assuming Gromov-Witten Invariants Are Integers
> They're rational numbers (virtual counts), not actual curve counts.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Quantum Cohomology Is Formal
> Variables $q^\beta$ are formal. Convergence issues often ignored.
