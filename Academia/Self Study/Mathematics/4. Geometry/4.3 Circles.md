## Parts of a Circle

|Term|Definition|
|---|---|
|**Circle**|Set of all points equidistant from a centre|
|**Radius** ($r$)|Distance from the centre to any point on the circle|
|**Diameter** ($d$)|Distance across the circle through the centre: $d = 2r$|
|**Chord**|Line segment connecting two points on the circle|
|**Arc**|Part of the circumference|
|**Sector**|Region between two radii and an arc (pie slice)|

---

## Key Formulas

|Quantity|Formula|
|---|---|
|**Diameter**|$d = 2r$|
|**Circumference**|$C = 2\pi r = \pi d$|
|**Area**|$A = \pi r^2$|

> [!tip] Relating circumference and area Both formulas use $r$ — if you know the circumference you can find $r$, then find the area. $C = 2\pi r \implies r = \dfrac{C}{2\pi}$

## Angles in Circles

|Angle type|Vertex|Measure|
|---|---|---|
|**Central angle**|At the centre|Equals the intercepted arc|
|**Inscribed angle**|On the circle|Half the intercepted arc|

See [[Central_and_Inscribed_Angles]] for full theorems and proofs.
