## Ricci and Scalar Curvature

### Ricci Curvature

**Ricci tensor**: Trace of Riemann curvature tensor.

$$\text{Ric}_{ij} = R_{kij}^k = g^{kl}R_{kilj}$$

Symmetric $(0,2)$-tensor: $\text{Ric}_{ij} = \text{Ric}_{ji}$.

Measures average sectional curvature in all directions!

### Scalar Curvature

**Scalar curvature**: Trace of Ricci tensor.

$$R = g^{ij}\text{Ric}_{ij} = R_{ij}^{ij}$$

Single function on manifold.

Further averaging of curvature information.

### Examples

- **Sphere** $S^n$ (radius 1): $\text{Ric} = (n-1)g$, $R = n(n-1)$
- **Euclidean space**: $\text{Ric} = 0$, $R = 0$
- **Hyperbolic space**: $\text{Ric} = -(n-1)g$, $R = -n(n-1)$

## Einstein Manifolds

### Definition

Manifold is **Einstein** if:
$$\text{Ric} = \lambda g$$

for constant $\lambda$.

Proportional Ricci and metric tensors!

### Examples

- Sphere $S^n$: $\lambda = n-1 > 0$
- Euclidean space: $\lambda = 0$
- Hyperbolic space: $\lambda = -(n-1) < 0$
- Complex projective space $\mathbb{CP}^n$: Einstein with $\lambda > 0$

### Importance

Critical points of total scalar curvature functional.

Solutions to vacuum Einstein equations in general relativity!

## Jacobi Fields

### Motivation

Study how geodesics spread apart or converge.

Variation of geodesics!

### Definition

**Jacobi field** along geodesic $\gamma$: Vector field $J$ satisfying:

$$\nabla_{\gamma'} \nabla_{\gamma'} J + R(J, \gamma')\gamma' = 0$$

Second-order linear ODE (Jacobi equation).

### Geometric Meaning

If $\gamma_s$ is family of geodesics, then:
$$J(t) = \frac{\partial \gamma_s}{\partial s}\bigg|_{s=0}$$

is Jacobi field along $\gamma_0$.

Measures infinitesimal separation of nearby geodesics.

## Conjugate Points

### Definition

Points $\gamma(a)$ and $\gamma(b)$ are **conjugate** along geodesic $\gamma$ if exists non-zero Jacobi field $J$ with:
$$J(a) = J(b) = 0$$

Geodesics starting at $\gamma(a)$ focus at $\gamma(b)$!

### Properties

- No conjugate points in Euclidean space
- On sphere: Antipodal points are conjugate
- First conjugate point limits minimizing property of geodesic

### Index Form

For vector field $V$ along $\gamma$:
$$I(V,V) = \int_a^b \left(\|\nabla_{\gamma'} V\|^2 - \langle R(V,\gamma')\gamma', V\rangle\right) dt$$

Jacobi fields minimize index form!

## Comparison Theorems

### Rauch Comparison Theorem

Compare Jacobi fields on manifolds with different curvature bounds.

If $K_M \leq K_N$, then Jacobi fields grow faster on $M$ than $N$.

### Toponogov's Theorem

Compare triangles in manifold with triangles in model space.

If sectional curvature $K \geq \kappa$, then triangles are "thinner" than in space of constant curvature $\kappa$.

### Applications

- Bounds on diameter
- Sphere theorems
- Structure of manifolds with curvature constraints

## Cut Locus

### Definition

**Cut locus** of point $p$: Set of points where geodesics from $p$ cease to be minimizing.

$$\text{Cut}(p) = \{q \in M : \exists \text{ multiple minimizing geodesics from } p \text{ to } q\}$$

### Properties

- Closed set of measure zero
- Complement is diffeomorphic to open ball in $T_pM$ via $\exp_p$
- Distance to cut locus = injectivity radius

### Examples

- Sphere: Cut locus of point is antipodal point
- Cylinder: Cut locus is line opposite to starting point
- Torus: Complicated structure

## Hopf-Rinow Theorem

### Statement

For connected Riemannian manifold $M$, following are equivalent:

1. $M$ is complete as metric space
2. Closed bounded sets are compact
3. Geodesics defined for all time (geodesically complete)
4. Any two points connected by minimizing geodesic

### Consequences

Complete manifolds have nice global properties.

Compact manifolds are always complete!

---

## Myers' Theorem

### Statement

If complete Riemannian manifold has $\text{Ric} \geq (n-1)\kappa > 0$, then:

1. $M$ is compact
2. Diameter $\leq \pi/\sqrt{\kappa}$
3. Fundamental group $\pi_1(M)$ is finite

Positive Ricci curvature forces compactness!

### Synge's Theorem

Even-dimensional, orientable, compact manifold with positive sectional curvature is simply connected.

---

## Splitting Theorem

### Cheeger-Gromoll Splitting

If complete manifold has $\text{Ric} \geq 0$ and contains line (geodesic minimizing between any two points), then:

$$M \cong N \times \mathbb{R}$$

isometric to product.

Nonnegative Ricci curvature with line splits off Euclidean factor!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Different Types of Curvature
> Sectional, Ricci, and scalar curvatures contain different amounts of information.

> [!warning] Assuming Positive Curvature Implies Simple Topology
> Only true in certain dimensions. Exotic spheres exist!

> [!warning] Forgetting Completeness Assumptions
> Many theorems require geodesic completeness. Check hypotheses!
