## Parametric Curves

### Definition

**Parametric curve** in $\mathbb{R}^n$:
$$\gamma: I \to \mathbb{R}^n, \quad t \mapsto (x_1(t), \ldots, x_n(t))$$

where $I \subseteq \mathbb{R}$ is an interval.

Assume $\gamma$ is smooth ($C^\infty$) or at least $C^2$.

### Regular Curves

Curve is **regular** if $\gamma'(t) \neq 0$ for all $t$.

Velocity vector never vanishes!

### Arc Length Parameterization

**Arc length** from $t_0$ to $t$:
$$s(t) = \int_{t_0}^t \|\gamma'(\tau)\| \, d\tau$$

If reparameterized by arc length: $\|\gamma'(s)\| = 1$ (unit speed).

## Frenet-Serret Frame (Space Curves)

### For Unit Speed Curves in $\mathbb{R}^3$

**Tangent vector**:
$$T(s) = \gamma'(s)$$

**Curvature**:
$$\kappa(s) = \|T'(s)\| = \|\gamma''(s)\|$$

Measures how fast curve bends.

**Principal normal** (if $\kappa \neq 0$):
$$N(s) = \frac{T'(s)}{\|T'(s)\|} = \frac{\gamma''(s)}{\kappa(s)}$$

**Binormal**:
$$B(s) = T(s) \times N(s)$$

Completes orthonormal frame!

> Parabola with tangent vectors showing direction of motion.

## Frenet-Serret Formulas

### The Equations

For unit speed curve in $\mathbb{R}^3$:

$$\begin{pmatrix} T' \\ N' \\ B' \end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix} 0 & \kappa & 0 \\ -\kappa & 0 & \tau \\ 0 & -\tau & 0 \end{pmatrix} \begin{pmatrix} T \\ N \\ B \end{pmatrix}$$

Or explicitly:
- $T' = \kappa N$
- $N' = -\kappa T + \tau B$
- $B' = -\tau N$

### Torsion

**Torsion** $\tau(s)$ measures twisting out of osculating plane.

Defined by: $B' = -\tau N$

Formula:
$$\tau = -\langle B', N\rangle = \frac{\det(\gamma', \gamma'', \gamma''')}{\|\gamma' \times \gamma''\|^2}$$

## Curvature and Torsion Geometrically

### Curvature

- $\kappa = 0$: Straight line
- $\kappa = \text{constant}$: Circle (radius $1/\kappa$)
- Large $\kappa$: Sharp bend
- Small $\kappa$: Gentle curve

**Radius of curvature**: $\rho = 1/\kappa$

### Torsion

- $\tau = 0$: Planar curve
- $\tau > 0$: Right-handed helix
- $\tau < 0$: Left-handed helix
- Constant $\kappa, \tau$: Circular helix

### Osculating Objects

- **Osculating plane**: Spanned by $T$ and $N$
- **Osculating circle**: Circle with radius $\rho = 1/\kappa$ in osculating plane
- Best second-order approximation to curve!

## Fundamental Theorem of Curves

### Statement

Given smooth functions $\kappa(s) > 0$ and $\tau(s)$, there exists unique (up to rigid motion) unit speed curve $\gamma: I \to \mathbb{R}^3$ with these curvature and torsion.

Curves determined entirely by $\kappa$ and $\tau$!

### Proof Idea

1. Solve Frenet-Serret equations for $T, N, B$
2. Integrate: $\gamma(s) = \int T(s) \, ds$
3. Uniqueness up to initial position and orientation

## Plane Curves

### Signed Curvature

For plane curve $\gamma(t) = (x(t), y(t))$:

$$\kappa_s = \frac{x'y'' - y'x''}{(x'^2 + y'^2)^{3/2}}$$

Sign indicates direction of turning (left/right).

### Examples

**Circle** of radius $R$:
$$\kappa = \frac{1}{R}, \quad \tau = 0$$

**Helix**:
$$\gamma(t) = (a\cos t, a\sin t, bt)$$
$$\kappa = \frac{a}{a^2 + b^2}, \quad \tau = \frac{b}{a^2 + b^2}$$

**Cycloid**: Path traced by point on rolling circle.

## Global Properties

### Total Curvature

For closed curve:
$$\int_C \kappa \, ds \geq 2\pi$$

Equality iff convex plane curve (Fenchel's theorem).

### Rotation Index

For closed plane curve:
$$\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_C \kappa_s \, ds \in \mathbb{Z}$$

Counts number of times tangent vector rotates.

### Four Vertex Theorem

Simple closed convex plane curve has at least 4 vertices (points where $\kappa' = 0$).

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Curvature with Torsion
> Curvature = bending, torsion = twisting. Independent quantities!

> [!warning] Forgetting Unit Speed Assumption
> Frenet formulas assume arc length parameterization. Adjust for general parameterization.

> [!warning] Assuming All Curves Have Frenet Frame
> Need $\kappa \neq 0$ everywhere. Inflection points cause problems.
