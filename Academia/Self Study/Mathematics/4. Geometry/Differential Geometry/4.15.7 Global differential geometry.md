## Gauss-Bonnet Theorem

### Local Version (Surfaces)

For compact surface $S$ with boundary $\partial S$:

$$\int_S K \, dA + \int_{\partial S} k_g \, ds = 2\pi\chi(S)$$

where:
- $K$: Gaussian curvature
- $k_g$: Geodesic curvature of boundary
- $\chi(S)$: Euler characteristic

### Global Version

For closed (compact, no boundary) surface:

$$\int_S K \, dA = 2\pi\chi(S)$$

Connects geometry (curvature) to topology (Euler characteristic)!

### Examples

- **Sphere**: $\int K = 4\pi$, $\chi = 2$ ✓
- **Torus**: $\int K = 0$, $\chi = 0$ ✓
- **Genus $g$ surface**: $\int K = 2\pi(2-2g)$

```desmos-graph
x = cos(t)
y = sin(t)
t = [0, 2*pi]
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Total curvature of sphere equals $4\pi$, reflecting its topology.

## Higher-Dimensional Gauss-Bonnet

### Chern-Gauss-Bonnet Theorem

For compact oriented even-dimensional Riemannian manifold $M^{2n}$:

$$\int_M \text{Pf}(\Omega) = (2\pi)^n \chi(M)$$

where $\text{Pf}(\Omega)$ is Pfaffian of curvature form.

Generalizes classical Gauss-Bonnet!

### Odd Dimensions

$\chi(M) = 0$ for odd-dimensional compact manifolds.

No direct Gauss-Bonnet formula.

## Index Theorems

### Motivation

Relate analytical indices (from differential operators) to topological invariants.

Bridge between analysis and topology!

### Atiyah-Singer Index Theorem

For elliptic operator $D$ on compact manifold:

$$\text{index}(D) = \dim\ker(D) - \dim\text{coker}(D) = \int_M \text{ch}(E)\hat{A}(M)$$

where:
- $\text{ch}(E)$: Chern character of bundle
- $\hat{A}(M)$: $\hat{A}$-genus (from Pontryagin classes)

Deep result connecting analysis, geometry, topology!

## Hodge Theory

### Hodge Star Operator

On oriented Riemannian $n$-manifold, **Hodge star**:
$$*: \Omega^k(M) \to \Omega^{n-k}(M)$$

Defined by: $\alpha \wedge *\beta = \langle \alpha, \beta\rangle \text{vol}$

### Laplacian on Forms

**Hodge Laplacian**:
$$\Delta = d\delta + \delta d$$

where $\delta = (-1)^{n(k+1)+1} * d *$ is codifferential.

On functions: $\Delta f = -\text{div}(\text{grad } f)$ (negative of usual Laplacian).

### Harmonic Forms

**Harmonic form**: $\omega$ with $\Delta\omega = 0$.

Equivalently: $d\omega = 0$ and $\delta\omega = 0$.

---

## Hodge Decomposition Theorem

### Statement

On compact Riemannian manifold, every $k$-form decomposes uniquely:

$$\omega = d\alpha + \delta\beta + \gamma$$

where:
- $d\alpha$: Exact form
- $\delta\beta$: Coexact form
- $\gamma$: Harmonic form

Orthogonal decomposition with respect to $L^2$ inner product!

### Consequences

**Hodge theorem**: Each de Rham cohomology class has unique harmonic representative.

$$H^k_{dR}(M) \cong \mathcal{H}^k(M) = \{\omega \in \Omega^k : \Delta\omega = 0\}$$

Isomorphism between cohomology and harmonic forms.

## Spectral Geometry

### Laplace-Beltrami Operator

On functions, **Laplace-Beltrami operator**:
$$\Delta f = \frac{1}{\sqrt{|g|}}\frac{\partial}{\partial x^i}\left(\sqrt{|g|}g^{ij}\frac{\partial f}{\partial x^j}\right)$$

Self-adjoint, elliptic operator.

### Spectrum

Eigenvalue problem:
$$\Delta f = \lambda f$$

Spectrum: $0 = \lambda_0 < \lambda_1 \leq \lambda_2 \leq \cdots \to \infty$

Discrete spectrum for compact manifolds!

### Hearing the Shape

**Question**: Can you determine manifold from spectrum?

**Answer**: Not always! Isospectral non-isometric manifolds exist.

But spectrum contains geometric information:
- $\lambda_1$ relates to diameter
- Asymptotics give dimension, volume

## Minimal Submanifolds

### Definition

Submanifold $N \subseteq M$ is **minimal** if mean curvature vector vanishes.

Critical points of volume functional!

### Examples

- Great circles on sphere
- Catenoid in $\mathbb{R}^3$
- Complex submanifolds of Kähler manifolds

### Variational Characterization

Minimal ⟹ Stationary for volume under compactly supported variations.

Second variation involves stability operator.

## Yamabe Problem

### Statement

Given compact Riemannian manifold $(M,g)$, find metric $\tilde{g}$ conformal to $g$ with constant scalar curvature.

$$\tilde{g} = u^{4/(n-2)}g \quad \text{with} \quad R_{\tilde{g}} = \text{constant}$$

### Solution

Solved by Aubin, Schoen, Trudinger, Yamabe.

Always exists solution!

Reduces to nonlinear PDE (Yamabe equation).

## Ricci Flow

### Definition

**Ricci flow**: Evolution equation for metric:

$$\frac{\partial g}{\partial t} = -2\text{Ric}$$

Metric changes in direction of negative Ricci curvature.

### Properties

- Smooths out irregularities in metric
- Preserves certain curvature conditions
- May develop singularities

### Hamilton-Perelman Theory

Used to prove:
- Poincaré conjecture (Perelman, 2003)
- Geometrization conjecture
- Sphere theorems

Revolutionary tool in geometric analysis!

## Comparison Geometry

### Bishop-Gromov Volume Comparison

If $\text{Ric} \geq (n-1)\kappa$, then volume growth bounded by model space of curvature $\kappa$.

Volume ratio $\frac{\text{Vol}(B_r(p))}{\text{Vol}_\kappa(B_r)}$ is non-increasing in $r$.

### Cheeger's Constant

**Cheeger constant**:
$$h(M) = \inf_\Sigma \frac{\text{Area}(\Sigma)}{\min(\text{Vol}(M_1), \text{Vol}(M_2))}$$

where $\Sigma$ separates $M$ into $M_1, M_2$.

Relates to first eigenvalue: $\lambda_1 \geq \frac{h^2}{4}$.

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Different Versions of Gauss-Bonnet
> Classical (surfaces) vs Chern-Gauss-Bonnet (higher dimensions) have different formulations.

> [!warning] Assuming Spectrum Determines Geometry
> Isospectral manifolds can be non-isometric. Spectrum doesn't determine shape uniquely.

> [!warning] Forgetting Compactness Assumptions
> Many theorems (Hodge, spectral theory) require compact manifolds.
