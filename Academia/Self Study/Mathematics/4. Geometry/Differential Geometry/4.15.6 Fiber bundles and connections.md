## Vector Bundles

### Definition

**Vector bundle** of rank $k$ over manifold $M$: Manifold $E$ with projection $\pi: E \to M$ such that:

1. Each fiber $E_p = \pi^{-1}(p)$ is $k$-dimensional vector space
2. Locally trivial: For each $p$, exists neighborhood $U$ with:
   $$\pi^{-1}(U) \cong U \times \mathbb{R}^k$$

### Examples

- **Tangent bundle** $TM$: Rank $n$ bundle over $n$-manifold
- **Cotangent bundle** $T^*M$: Dual bundle
- **Trivial bundle**: $M \times \mathbb{R}^k$
- **Möbius strip**: Non-trivial line bundle over $S^1$

### Sections

**Section**: Map $s: M \to E$ with $\pi \circ s = \text{id}_M$.

Space of sections: $\Gamma(E)$.

For tangent bundle: Vector fields.

## Principal Bundles

### Definition

**Principal $G$-bundle**: Bundle with fiber $G$ (Lie group) and free transitive $G$-action on fibers.

Locally: $\pi^{-1}(U) \cong U \times G$.

### Examples

- **Frame bundle** $FM$: Principal $GL(n)$-bundle
- **Orthonormal frame bundle**: Principal $O(n)$-bundle (for Riemannian metric)
- **Hopf fibration**: $S^3 \to S^2$ with fiber $S^1$

### Importance

Principal bundles encode "symmetry" of geometric structures.

Foundation for gauge theory!

## Connections on Bundles

### Motivation

Need to differentiate sections of bundles (not just functions).

Generalize Levi-Civita connection!

### Connection 1-Form

On principal $G$-bundle, **connection** is $\mathfrak{g}$-valued 1-form $\omega$ satisfying:

1. $\omega(\xi^*) = \xi$ for fundamental vector fields
2. $R_g^*\omega = \text{Ad}_{g^{-1}}\omega$ (equivariance)

### Covariant Derivative

For associated vector bundle, connection defines:
$$\nabla: \Gamma(E) \to \Gamma(E \otimes T^*M)$$

Satisfies Leibniz rule: $\nabla(fs) = df \otimes s + f\nabla s$.

## Curvature of Connection

### Curvature 2-Form

**Curvature** of connection $\omega$:
$$\Omega = d\omega + \frac{1}{2}[\omega, \omega]$$

$\mathfrak{g}$-valued 2-form on total space.

Measures failure of horizontal distribution to be integrable!

### Structure Equation

Cartan's structure equation:
$$\Omega = d\omega + \omega \wedge \omega$$

In matrix notation for vector bundles.

### Bianchi Identity

$$D\Omega = 0$$

where $D$ is exterior covariant derivative.

Fundamental identity in gauge theory!

## Holonomy

### Parallel Transport

Connection defines parallel transport along curves.

For loop $\gamma$ based at $p$: Linear map $P_\gamma: E_p \to E_p$.

### Holonomy Group

**Holonomy group** at $p$:
$$\text{Hol}_p(\nabla) = \{P_\gamma : \gamma \text{ is loop based at } p\} \subseteq GL(E_p)$$

Subgroup of structure group.

### Ambrose-Singer Theorem

Lie algebra of holonomy group generated by curvature values:
$$\mathfrak{hol}_p = \text{span}\{\Omega_p(u,v) : u,v \in T_pM\}$$

Curvature determines holonomy!

## Characteristic Classes

### Motivation

Topological invariants of bundles constructed from curvature.

Independent of connection choice!

### Chern Classes

For complex vector bundle $E$ with connection:

**Chern character**:
$$\text{ch}(E) = \text{tr}\left(\exp\left(\frac{i\Omega}{2\pi}\right)\right)$$

Chern classes $c_k(E) \in H^{2k}(M;\mathbb{Z})$.

### Pontryagin Classes

For real vector bundle:

**Pontryagin classes** $p_k(E) \in H^{4k}(M;\mathbb{Z})$.

Defined via complexification.

### Euler Class

For oriented even-rank real bundle:

**Euler class** $e(E) \in H^n(M;\mathbb{Z})$.

Generalizes Euler characteristic!

## Chern-Weil Theory

### Main Idea

Construct cohomology classes from curvature using invariant polynomials.

For invariant polynomial $P$ on $\mathfrak{g}$:
$$P(\Omega) \text{ is closed form}$$

Cohomology class independent of connection!

### Examples

- $\text{tr}(\Omega)$: First Chern class
- $\text{tr}(\Omega \wedge \Omega)$: Second Chern class / first Pontryagin class
- $\text{Pf}(\Omega)$: Euler class (for oriented bundles)

### Chern-Gauss-Bonnet Theorem

For compact oriented $2n$-manifold:
$$\int_M e(TM) = \chi(M)$$

Euler characteristic equals integral of Euler class!

## Fiber Bundles and Homotopy

### Classification

Principal $G$-bundles over $M$ classified by homotopy classes:
$$[M, BG]$$

where $BG$ is classifying space.

### Universal Bundle

Exists universal principal $G$-bundle $EG \to BG$.

Every bundle is pullback of universal bundle!

### Obstruction Theory

Existence of sections obstructed by characteristic classes.

Example: Sphere $S^n$ has nowhere-zero vector field iff $n$ odd (related to Euler class).

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Connection and Curvature
> Connection is 1-form (depends on choice). Curvature is 2-form (geometric invariant).

> [!warning] Forgetting That Characteristic Classes Are Topological
> Independent of metric and connection. Pure topology!

> [!warning] Assuming All Bundles Are Trivial
> Many interesting bundles are non-trivial (Möbius strip, tangent bundle of sphere).
