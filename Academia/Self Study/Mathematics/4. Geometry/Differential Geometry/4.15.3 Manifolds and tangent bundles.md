## Abstract Manifolds

### Definition

**Smooth manifold** $M$ of dimension $n$: Topological space with:

1. **Atlas**: Collection of charts $\{(U_\alpha, \phi_\alpha)\}$ where:
   - $U_\alpha \subseteq M$ open, covers $M$
   - $\phi_\alpha: U_\alpha \to \mathbb{R}^n$ homeomorphism to open set

2. **Smooth compatibility**: Transition maps smooth:
   $$\phi_\beta \circ \phi_\alpha^{-1}: \phi_\alpha(U_\alpha \cap U_\beta) \to \phi_\beta(U_\alpha \cap U_\beta)$$

### Examples

- $\mathbb{R}^n$: Single chart
- Sphere $S^n$: Stereographic projection (2 charts)
- Torus $T^2 = S^1 \times S^1$: Product structure
- Real projective space $\mathbb{RP}^n$: Lines through origin

## Tangent Spaces

### Motivation

Need intrinsic notion of "direction" at point, independent of embedding.

### Definitions

**Tangent space** $T_pM$ at $p \in M$:

Multiple equivalent definitions:
1. Derivations at $p$ (linear maps satisfying Leibniz rule)
2. Equivalence classes of curves through $p$
3. Partial derivatives in local coordinates

Dimension: $\dim(T_pM) = \dim(M) = n$

### Basis

In coordinates $(x^1, \ldots, x^n)$:
$$\left\{\frac{\partial}{\partial x^1}\bigg|_p, \ldots, \frac{\partial}{\partial x^n}\bigg|_p\right\}$$

forms basis for $T_pM$.

---

## Tangent Bundle

### Definition

**Tangent bundle**:
$$TM = \bigsqcup_{p \in M} T_pM$$

Disjoint union of all tangent spaces.

Natural projection: $\pi: TM \to M$, $\pi(v) = p$ if $v \in T_pM$.

### Structure

$TM$ is manifold of dimension $2n$.

Local coordinates: $(x^1, \ldots, x^n, v^1, \ldots, v^n)$ where $v = v^i \frac{\partial}{\partial x^i}$.

### Vector Fields

**Vector field**: Smooth section of $TM$.

Map $X: M \to TM$ with $\pi \circ X = \text{id}_M$.

In coordinates: $X = X^i(x) \frac{\partial}{\partial x^i}$.

## Cotangent Bundle and Differential Forms

### Cotangent Space

**Cotangent space** $T_p^*M = (T_pM)^*$ (dual space).

Elements are **covectors** or **1-forms**.

Basis dual to $\{\frac{\partial}{\partial x^i}\}$: $\{dx^1, \ldots, dx^n\}$.

### Cotangent Bundle

$$T^*M = \bigsqcup_{p \in M} T_p^*M$$

Also dimension $2n$.

### Differential Forms

**$k$-form**: Alternating multilinear map on $k$ tangent vectors.

Space of $k$-forms: $\Omega^k(M)$.

In coordinates:
$$\omega = \sum_{i_1 < \cdots < i_k} \omega_{i_1\ldots i_k} \, dx^{i_1} \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^{i_k}$$

---

## Exterior Derivative

### Definition

**Exterior derivative** $d: \Omega^k(M) \to \Omega^{k+1}(M)$:

Properties:
1. $d(f) = df$ for functions (usual differential)
2. $d(\omega \wedge \eta) = d\omega \wedge \eta + (-1)^k \omega \wedge d\eta$ ($\omega$ is $k$-form)
3. $d^2 = 0$

### In Coordinates

For $k$-form $\omega = \sum_I \omega_I \, dx^I$:
$$d\omega = \sum_I d\omega_I \wedge dx^I = \sum_I \sum_j \frac{\partial \omega_I}{\partial x^j} dx^j \wedge dx^I$$

### Examples

- $df = \frac{\partial f}{\partial x^i} dx^i$ (gradient)
- For 1-form $\omega = \omega_i dx^i$:
  $$d\omega = \sum_{i<j} \left(\frac{\partial \omega_j}{\partial x^i} - \frac{\partial \omega_i}{\partial x^j}\right) dx^i \wedge dx^j$$
  (curl in 3D)

## de Rham Cohomology

### Closed and Exact Forms

- **Closed form**: $d\omega = 0$
- **Exact form**: $\omega = d\eta$ for some $\eta$

Every exact form is closed ($d^2 = 0$), but converse may fail!

### de Rham Cohomology Groups

$$H^k_{dR}(M) = \frac{\{\text{closed } k\text{-forms}\}}{\{\text{exact } k\text{-forms}\}} = \frac{\ker(d: \Omega^k \to \Omega^{k+1})}{\text{im}(d: \Omega^{k-1} \to \Omega^k)}$$

Topological invariants!

### Examples

- $H^0_{dR}(M) \cong \mathbb{R}^c$ where $c$ = number of connected components
- $H^1_{dR}(S^1) \cong \mathbb{R}$ (angle form not exact)
- $H^k_{dR}(\mathbb{R}^n) = 0$ for $k > 0$ (Poincaré lemma)

## Pullback and Pushforward

### Differential of Map

For smooth map $f: M \to N$, **differential** at $p$:
$$df_p: T_pM \to T_{f(p)}N$$

Also called **pushforward** $f_*$.

In coordinates: Jacobian matrix!

### Pullback of Forms

**Pullback** $f^*: \Omega^k(N) \to \Omega^k(M)$:

For $\omega \in \Omega^k(N)$ and $v_1, \ldots, v_k \in T_pM$:
$$(f^*\omega)_p(v_1, \ldots, v_k) = \omega_{f(p)}(df_p(v_1), \ldots, df_p(v_k))$$

### Properties

- $f^*(\omega \wedge \eta) = f^*\omega \wedge f^*\eta$
- $f^*(d\omega) = d(f^*\omega)$ (commutes with $d$!)
- $(g \circ f)^* = f^* \circ g^*$

## Integration on Manifolds

### Orientation

**Orientable manifold**: Consistent choice of "positive" basis for each $T_pM$.

Equivalently: Exists nowhere-vanishing volume form.

### Volume Form

On oriented $n$-manifold: **Volume form** $\text{vol} \in \Omega^n(M)$.

In coordinates: $\text{vol} = \sqrt{|g|} \, dx^1 \wedge \cdots \wedge dx^n$ (for Riemannian metric).

### Integration

For compactly supported $n$-form $\omega$:
$$\int_M \omega$$

Defined via partition of unity and coordinate charts.

### Stokes' Theorem

For oriented $n$-manifold with boundary:
$$\int_M d\omega = \int_{\partial M} \omega$$

Generalizes fundamental theorem of calculus, Green's theorem, divergence theorem!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Tangent and Cotangent Spaces
> Vectors vs covectors transform differently under coordinate changes!

> [!warning] Assuming All Manifolds Are Orientable
> Möbius strip, Klein bottle, $\mathbb{RP}^{2n}$ are non-orientable.

> [!warning] Forgetting That de Rham Cohomology Is Topological
> Depends only on smooth structure, not metric or embedding.
