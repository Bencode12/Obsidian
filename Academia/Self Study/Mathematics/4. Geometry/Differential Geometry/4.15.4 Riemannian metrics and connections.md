## Riemannian Metrics

### Definition

**Riemannian metric** on manifold $M$: Smooth assignment of inner product to each tangent space.

$$g_p: T_pM \times T_pM \to \mathbb{R}$$

varying smoothly with $p$.

In coordinates:
$$g = g_{ij} \, dx^i \otimes dx^j$$

where $(g_{ij})$ is symmetric positive-definite matrix.

### Examples

- **Euclidean metric**: $g_{ij} = \delta_{ij}$ (identity matrix)
- **Sphere**: Induced metric from $\mathbb{R}^3$
- **Hyperbolic plane**: $ds^2 = \frac{dx^2 + dy^2}{y^2}$ (PoincarĂ© half-plane)

## Length and Distance

### Arc Length

For curve $\gamma: [a,b] \to M$:
$$L(\gamma) = \int_a^b \sqrt{g_{\gamma(t)}(\gamma'(t), \gamma'(t))} \, dt$$

In coordinates:
$$L(\gamma) = \int_a^b \sqrt{g_{ij} \frac{dx^i}{dt} \frac{dx^j}{dt}} \, dt$$

### Riemannian Distance

$$d(p,q) = \inf\{L(\gamma) : \gamma \text{ connects } p \text{ to } q\}$$

Makes $M$ into metric space!

## Levi-Civita Connection

### Motivation

Need way to differentiate vector fields along curves.

Problem: Tangent spaces at different points are different vector spaces!

### Covariant Derivative

**Connection** $\nabla$: Assigns to vector fields $X,Y$ a vector field $\nabla_X Y$.

Properties:
1. $\nabla_{fX+gY} Z = f\nabla_X Z + g\nabla_Y Z$ ($C^\infty$-linear in first argument)
2. $\nabla_X(Y+Z) = \nabla_X Y + \nabla_X Z$ (linear in second)
3. $\nabla_X(fY) = X(f)Y + f\nabla_X Y$ (Leibniz rule)

### Levi-Civita Connection

Unique connection satisfying:
1. **Metric compatibility**: $X\langle Y,Z\rangle = \langle \nabla_X Y, Z\rangle + \langle Y, \nabla_X Z\rangle$
2. **Torsion-free**: $\nabla_X Y - \nabla_Y X = [X,Y]$ (Lie bracket)

Fundamental theorem of Riemannian geometry!

## Christoffel Symbols

### Definition

In local coordinates, write:
$$\nabla_{\frac{\partial}{\partial x^i}} \frac{\partial}{\partial x^j} = \Gamma_{ij}^k \frac{\partial}{\partial x^k}$$

Coefficients $\Gamma_{ij}^k$ are **Christoffel symbols**.

### Formula

$$\Gamma_{ij}^k = \frac{1}{2}g^{kl}\left(\frac{\partial g_{jl}}{\partial x^i} + \frac{\partial g_{il}}{\partial x^j} - \frac{\partial g_{ij}}{\partial x^l}\right)$$

where $(g^{kl})$ is inverse matrix to $(g_{ij})$.

Not tensors! Depend on coordinate choice.

## Parallel Transport

### Definition

Vector field $V$ along curve $\gamma$ is **parallel** if:
$$\nabla_{\gamma'} V = 0$$

In coordinates:
$$\frac{dV^k}{dt} + \Gamma_{ij}^k \frac{dx^i}{dt} V^j = 0$$

Linear ODE system!

### Properties

- Preserves inner products: $\langle V(t), W(t)\rangle$ constant if both parallel
- Defines linear isomorphism $T_{\gamma(a)}M \to T_{\gamma(b)}M$
- Path-dependent in general (curvature!)

```desmos-graph
x = cos(t)
y = sin(t)
t = [0, 2*pi]
```

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Parallel transport around closed loop on sphere rotates vector.

## Geodesics

### Definition

**Geodesic**: Curve with zero acceleration.

$$\nabla_{\gamma'} \gamma' = 0$$

Generalizes straight lines to curved spaces!

### Geodesic Equations

In coordinates:
$$\frac{d^2x^k}{dt^2} + \Gamma_{ij}^k \frac{dx^i}{dt} \frac{dx^j}{dt} = 0$$

Second-order nonlinear ODE system.

### Properties

- Locally length-minimizing
- Constant speed: $\|\gamma'(t)\|$ constant
- Existence and uniqueness for given initial position and velocity

### Examples

- Euclidean space: Straight lines
- Sphere: Great circles
- Hyperbolic plane: Semicircles perpendicular to boundary

## Exponential Map

### Definition

**Exponential map** at $p$:
$$\exp_p: T_pM \to M$$

For $v \in T_pM$: $\exp_p(v) = \gamma_v(1)$ where $\gamma_v$ is geodesic with $\gamma_v(0) = p$, $\gamma_v'(0) = v$.

### Properties

- Diffeomorphism from neighborhood of $0 \in T_pM$ to neighborhood of $p \in M$
- $\exp_p(tv)$ traces geodesic
- Radial geodesics minimize distance near $p$

### Normal Coordinates

Coordinates centered at $p$ via $\exp_p^{-1}$.

In normal coordinates: $g_{ij}(p) = \delta_{ij}$ and $\Gamma_{ij}^k(p) = 0$.

Simplifies calculations at point!

## Curvature Tensor

### Riemann Curvature Tensor

Measures failure of second covariant derivatives to commute:

$$R(X,Y)Z = 
abla_X 
abla_Y Z - 
abla_Y 
abla_X Z - 
abla_{[X,Y]} Z$$

In coordinates:
$$R_{ijk}^l = \frac{\partial \Gamma_{jk}^l}{\partial x^i} - \frac{\partial \Gamma_{ik}^l}{\partial x^j} + \Gamma_{im}^l \Gamma_{jk}^m - \Gamma_{jm}^l \Gamma_{ik}^m$$

### Symmetries

- $R(X,Y)Z = -R(Y,X)Z$ (antisymmetric in first two)
- First Bianchi identity: $R(X,Y)Z + R(Y,Z)X + R(Z,X)Y = 0$
- Metric compatibility gives more symmetries

### Sectional Curvature

For 2-plane $\sigma \subseteq T_pM$ spanned by orthonormal $u,v$:
$$K(\sigma) = \langle R(u,v)v, u\rangle$$

Generalizes Gaussian curvature to higher dimensions!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Confusing Coordinate and Intrinsic Quantities
> Christoffel symbols depend on coordinates. Curvature is intrinsic.

> [!warning] Assuming Geodesics Are Globally Minimizing
> Only locally minimizing. May have conjugate points.

> [!warning] Forgetting That Parallel Transport Is Path-Dependent
> Different paths give different results if curvature nonzero.
