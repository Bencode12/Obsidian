## What is a Complex Number?

$$z = a + bi$$

|Part|Name|Symbol|
|---|---|---|
|$a$|Real part|$\text{Re}(z)$|
|$b$|Imaginary part|$\text{Im}(z)$|
|$i$|Imaginary unit|$i^2 = -1$|

|Type|Condition|Example|
|---|---|---|
|**Purely real**|$b = 0$|$z = 5$|
|**Purely imaginary**|$a = 0$|$z = 3i$|
|**General complex**|$a \neq 0,\ b \neq 0$|$z = 3 + 4i$|

---

## Conjugate and Modulus

**Conjugate** — flip the sign of the imaginary part:

$$\bar{z} = a - bi \qquad z\bar{z} = a^2 + b^2 = |z|^2$$

**Modulus** — distance from the origin in the complex plane:

$$|z| = \sqrt{a^2 + b^2}$$

---

## Polar and Euler Form

**Argument** — angle with the positive real axis:

$$\theta = \tan^{-1}!\left(\frac{b}{a}\right)$$

**Polar form:**

$$z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta) \qquad r = |z|$$

**Euler form:**

$$z = re^{i\theta} \qquad \text{where } e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$$

## Operations

### Addition and Subtraction

$$(a + bi) \pm (c + di) = (a \pm c) + (b \pm d)i$$

### Multiplication

$$(a + bi)(c + di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i$$

### Division

$$\frac{a + bi}{c + di} = \frac{(a + bi)(c - di)}{c^2 + d^2}$$

> [!tip] Multiply by the conjugate to divide The denominator becomes purely real: $c^2 + d^2$.

### Polar multiplication and division

$$z_1 z_2 = r_1 r_2 \left[\cos(\theta_1 + \theta_2) + i\sin(\theta_1 + \theta_2)\right]$$

$$\frac{z_1}{z_2} = \frac{r_1}{r_2} \left[\cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2) + i\sin(\theta_1 - \theta_2)\right]$$
