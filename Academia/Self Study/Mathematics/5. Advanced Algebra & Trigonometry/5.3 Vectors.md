### **5.3.1 What is a vector?**

A **vector** is a **quantity with both magnitude (length) and direction**.

- Examples: displacement, velocity, force
- **Notation:** v⃗\vec{v}v or **AB** with an arrow

**Contrast with scalar:**

- Scalar → magnitude only (e.g., speed, mass)
- Vector → magnitude + direction

---

### **5.3.2 Representation**

- Geometrically: arrow in 2D/3D space
- Algebraically: coordinates

v⃗=⟨vx,vy⟩(2D)\vec{v} = \langle v_x, v_y \rangle \quad \text{(2D)}v=⟨vx​,vy​⟩(2D) v⃗=⟨vx,vy,vz⟩(3D)\vec{v} = \langle v_x, v_y, v_z \rangle \quad \text{(3D)}v=⟨vx​,vy​,vz​⟩(3D)

- Magnitude of a vector:

### **5.3.3 Vector operations**

**1. Addition** (tip-to-tail or component-wise)

u⃗+v⃗=⟨ux+vx,uy+vy⟩\vec{u} + \vec{v} = \langle u_x + v_x, u_y + v_y \rangleu+v=⟨ux​+vx​,uy​+vy​⟩

**2. Subtraction**

u⃗−v⃗=⟨ux−vx,uy−vy⟩\vec{u} - \vec{v} = \langle u_x - v_x, u_y - v_y \rangleu−v=⟨ux​−vx​,uy​−vy​⟩

**3. Scalar multiplication**

kv⃗=⟨kvx,kvy⟩k \vec{v} = \langle k v_x, k v_y \ranglekv=⟨kvx​,kvy​⟩

**4. Dot product (scalar product)**

u⃗⋅v⃗=uxvx+uyvy\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = u_x v_x + u_y v_yu⋅v=ux​vx​+uy​vy​

- Relation to angle θ:

u⃗⋅v⃗=∣u⃗∣∣v⃗∣cos⁡θ\vec{u} \cdot \vec{v} = |\vec{u}| |\vec{v}| \cos θu⋅v=∣u∣∣v∣cosθ

**5. Cross product (3D only)**

u⃗×v⃗=vector perpendicular to both u and v\vec{u} \times \vec{v} = \text{vector perpendicular to both u and v} u×v=vector perpendicular to both u and v

- Magnitude: ∣u⃗×v⃗∣=∣u⃗∣∣v⃗∣sin⁡θ|\vec{u} \times \vec{v}| = |\vec{u}| |\vec{v}| \sin θ∣u×v∣=∣u∣∣v∣sinθ
