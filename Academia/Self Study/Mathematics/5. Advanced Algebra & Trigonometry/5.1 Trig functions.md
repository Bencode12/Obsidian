## The Six Functions — Ratios and Reciprocals

|Function|Ratio|Reciprocal identity|
|---|---|---|
|$\sin\theta$|$\text{opp}/\text{hyp}$|$\csc\theta = \dfrac{1}{\sin\theta}$|
|$\cos\theta$|$\text{adj}/\text{hyp}$|$\sec\theta = \dfrac{1}{\cos\theta}$|
|$\tan\theta$|$\text{opp}/\text{adj}$|$\cot\theta = \dfrac{1}{\tan\theta}$|

$$\tan\theta = \frac{\sin\theta}{\cos\theta} \qquad \cot\theta = \frac{\cos\theta}{\sin\theta}$$

## Domain and Range

|Function|Domain|Range|
|---|---|---|
|$\sin\theta$, $\cos\theta$|$\mathbb{R}$|$[-1,\ 1]$|
|$\tan\theta$|$\theta \neq \dfrac{\pi}{2} + n\pi$|$\mathbb{R}$|
|$\cot\theta$|$\theta \neq n\pi$|$\mathbb{R}$|
|$\sec\theta$|$\theta \neq \dfrac{\pi}{2} + n\pi$|$(-\infty,-1] \cup [1,\infty)$|
|$\csc\theta$|$\theta \neq n\pi$|$(-\infty,-1] \cup [1,\infty)$|

> [!tip] Why $\sec$ and $\csc$ skip $(-1, 1)$ $\sec\theta = 1/\cos\theta$ and $|\cos\theta| \leq 1$, so $|\sec\theta| \geq 1$. Same logic for $\csc\theta$.

## Pythagorean Identities

$$\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$$

Divide by $\cos^2\theta$:

$$\tan^2\theta + 1 = \sec^2\theta$$

Divide by $\sin^2\theta$:

$$1 + \cot^2\theta = \csc^2\theta$$

## Exact Values

|$\theta$ (°)|$\theta$ (rad)|$\sin\theta$|$\cos\theta$|$\tan\theta$|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|$0°$|$0$|$0$|$1$|$0$|
|$30°$|$\pi/6$|$1/2$|$\sqrt{3}/2$|$\sqrt{3}/3$|
|$45°$|$\pi/4$|$\sqrt{2}/2$|$\sqrt{2}/2$|$1$|
|$60°$|$\pi/3$|$\sqrt{3}/2$|$1/2$|$\sqrt{3}$|
|$90°$|$\pi/2$|$1$|$0$|undefined|

$\csc$, $\sec$, $\cot$ at any angle = reciprocal of $\sin$, $\cos$, $\tan$ respectively.

## Practice Problems

1. If $\sin\theta = 3/5$, find $\cos\theta$, $\tan\theta$, $\sec\theta$, $\csc\theta$, $\cot\theta$.
2. Convert $135°$ to radians.
3. Find $\sec(60°)$ and $\csc(30°)$ using exact values.
4. Prove: $\tan^2\theta + 1 = \sec^2\theta$ starting from $\sin^2\theta + \cos^2\theta = 1$.
5. Find all $\theta \in [0°, 360°]$ where $\cos\theta = -\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$.
