### **5.1.1 Definition**

A **trigonometric function** relates an angle of a triangle to **ratios of its sides**.

For an angle θ in a **right-angled triangle**:

|Function|Ratio|
|---|---|
|Sine (sin θ)|opposite / hypotenuse|
|Cosine (cos θ)|adjacent / hypotenuse|
|Tangent (tan θ)|opposite / adjacent|
|Cotangent (cot θ)|adjacent / opposite|
|Secant (sec θ)|hypotenuse / adjacent|
|Cosecant (csc θ)|hypotenuse / opposite|

### **5.1.2 Function domain and range**

|Function|Domain|Range|
|---|---|---|
|sin θ, cos θ|all real numbers|[−1, 1]|
|tan θ|θ ≠ 90° + n·180°|all real numbers|
|cot θ|θ ≠ n·180°|all real numbers|
|sec θ|θ ≠ 90° + n·180°|(−∞, −1] ∪ [1, ∞)|
|csc θ|θ ≠ n·180°|(−∞, −1] ∪ [1, ∞)|

### **5.1.3 Reciprocal relationships**

- csc θ = 1 / sin θ
- sec θ = 1 / cos θ
- cot θ = 1 / tan θ

### **5.1.4 Pythagorean identities**

1. sin² θ + cos² θ = 1
2. 1 + tan² θ = sec² θ
3. 1 + cot² θ = csc² θ

### **5.1.5 Common angles and values**

|θ (°)|θ (rad)|sin θ|cos θ|tan θ|
|---|---|---|---|---|
|0|0|0|1|0|
|30|π/6|1/2|√3/2|1/√3|
|45|π/4|√2/2|√2/2|1|
|60|π/3|√3/2|1/2|√3|
|90|π/2|1|0|∞|

- **Other functions** (csc, sec, cot) can be calculated as reciprocals.
