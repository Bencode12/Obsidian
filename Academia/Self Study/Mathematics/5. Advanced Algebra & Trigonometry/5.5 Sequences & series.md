## Sequences

An ordered list of numbers $a_1, a_2, a_3, \ldots, a_n, \ldots$ where $a_n$ is the $n$-th term. See [[Sequences]] for convergence.

---

## Arithmetic Sequence (AP)

Constant difference $d = a_n - a_{n-1}$.

$$a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d$$

**Sum of first $n$ terms:**

$$S_n = \frac{n}{2}\left[2a_1 + (n-1)d\right] = \frac{n}{2}(a_1 + a_n)$$

---

## Geometric Sequence (GP)

Constant ratio $r = a_n / a_{n-1}$.

$$a_n = a_1 r^{n-1}$$

**Sum of first $n$ terms** $(r \neq 1)$:

$$S_n = a_1 \cdot \frac{1 - r^n}{1 - r}$$

**Infinite sum** $(|r| < 1)$:

$$S_\infty = \frac{a_1}{1 - r}$$

> [!tip] Infinite GP converges only when $|r| < 1$ If $|r| \geq 1$ the series diverges — the sum grows without bound.

---

## Means

|Mean|Formula|
|---|---|
|**Arithmetic mean** of $a$ and $b$|$\dfrac{a + b}{2}$|
|**Geometric mean** of $a$ and $b$|$\sqrt{ab}$|

## Practice Problems

1. Find $S_{10}$ of the AP: $2, 5, 8, 11, \ldots$
2. Find $S_6$ of the GP: $3, 6, 12, 24, \ldots$
3. Find $S_\infty$ of the GP with $a_1 = 8$ and $r = \frac{1}{4}$.
4. An AP has $S_5 = 40$ and $a_1 = 4$. Find $d$.
5. Insert two geometric means between $4$ and $108$.
