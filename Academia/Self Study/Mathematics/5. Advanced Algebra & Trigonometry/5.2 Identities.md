### **5.2.1 What is an identity?**

A **trigonometric identity** is an equation that is **true for all values of θ** for which both sides are defined.  
You **prove** identities; you do not “solve” them.

---

### **5.2.2 Fundamental identities**

#### **1. Reciprocal identities**

sin⁡θ=1csc⁡θ,cos⁡θ=1sec⁡θ,tan⁡θ=1cot⁡θ\sin θ = \frac{1}{\csc θ}, \quad \cos θ = \frac{1}{\sec θ}, \quad \tan θ = \frac{1}{\cot θ}sinθ=cscθ1​,cosθ=secθ1​,tanθ=cotθ1​ csc⁡θ=1sin⁡θ,sec⁡θ=1cos⁡θ,cot⁡θ=1tan⁡θ\csc θ = \frac{1}{\sin θ}, \quad \sec θ = \frac{1}{\cos θ}, \quad \cot θ = \frac{1}{\tan θ}cscθ=sinθ1​,secθ=cosθ1​,cotθ=tanθ1​

---

#### **2. Quotient identities**

tan⁡θ=sin⁡θcos⁡θ,cot⁡θ=cos⁡θsin⁡θ\tan θ = \frac{\sin θ}{\cos θ}, \quad \cot θ = \frac{\cos θ}{\sin θ}tanθ=cosθsinθ​,cotθ=sinθcosθ​

---

#### **3. Pythagorean identities**

sin⁡2θ+cos⁡2θ=1\sin^2 θ + \cos^2 θ = 1sin2θ+cos2θ=1 1+tan⁡2θ=sec⁡2θ1 + \tan^2 θ = \sec^2 θ1+tan2θ=sec2θ 1+cot⁡2θ=csc⁡2θ1 + \cot^2 θ = \csc^2 θ1+cot2θ=csc2θ

These are the **most important identities** in trigonometry.

---

### **5.2.3 Even–odd identities**

- sin(−θ) = −sin θ
- cos(−θ) = cos θ
- tan(−θ) = −tan θ

---
