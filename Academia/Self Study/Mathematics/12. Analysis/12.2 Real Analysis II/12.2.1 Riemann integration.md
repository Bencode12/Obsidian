## Definition

A bounded function $f: [a,b] \to \mathbb{R}$ is **Riemann integrable** if the upper and lower Darboux integrals are equal.

> [!info] Tao Reference
> See *Analysis II* (4th ed.), Chapter 11: The Riemann Integral, Sections 11.1-11.5 (pp. 295-320)

---

## Darboux Sums

### Partitions

A **partition** $P$ of $[a,b]$ is a finite set:
$$P = \{x_0, x_1, ..., x_n\} \quad \text{where } a = x_0 < x_1 < ... < x_n = b$$

The **mesh** of $P$ is: $\|P\| = \max_{i}(x_i - x_{i-1})$

### Upper and Lower Sums

For each subinterval $[x_{i-1}, x_i]$, define:
- $M_i = \sup\{f(x) : x \in [x_{i-1}, x_i]\}$
- $m_i = \inf\{f(x) : x \in [x_{i-1}, x_i]\}$

**Upper Darboux sum**:
$$U(f,P) = \sum_{i=1}^n M_i(x_i - x_{i-1})$$

**Lower Darboux sum**:
$$L(f,P) = \sum_{i=1}^n m_i(x_i - x_{i-1})$$

> [!example] Visual Interpretation
> Upper sums use rectangles that overestimate the area; lower sums underestimate. As partitions get finer, they converge to the integral.

## Darboux Integrals

### Definitions

**Upper integral**:
$$\overline{\int_a^b} f = \inf_P U(f,P)$$

**Lower integral**:
$$\underline{\int_a^b} f = \sup_P L(f,P)$$

### Integrability Criterion

$f$ is **Riemann integrable** on $[a,b]$ if:
$$\overline{\int_a^b} f = \underline{\int_a^b} f$$

We denote this common value as:
$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx$$

---

## Riemann's Condition

**Theorem**: $f$ is Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$ if and only if:

$$\forall \epsilon > 0, \exists P \text{ such that } U(f,P) - L(f,P) < \epsilon$$

This is often easier to check than computing suprema and infima directly!

## Classes of Integrable Functions

### Theorem

The following functions are Riemann integrable on $[a,b]$:

|Class|Reason|
|---|---|
|Continuous functions|Uniform continuity on compact sets|
|Monotone functions|Bounded variation|
|Functions with finitely many discontinuities|Discontinuities have measure zero|

### Counterexample

The **Dirichlet function** is NOT Riemann integrable:
$$f(x) = \begin{cases} 1 & x \in \mathbb{Q} \\ 0 & x \notin \mathbb{Q} \end{cases}$$

For any partition, $U(f,P) = b-a$ and $L(f,P) = 0$.

## Properties of the Integral

### Linearity

If $f$ and $g$ are integrable on $[a,b]$:

$$\int_a^b (af + bg) = a\int_a^b f + b\int_a^b g$$

### Additivity

$$\int_a^c f = \int_a^b f + \int_b^c f$$

### Monotonicity

If $f(x) \leq g(x)$ for all $x \in [a,b]$:
$$\int_a^b f \leq \int_a^b g$$

### Triangle Inequality

$$\left|\int_a^b f\right| \leq \int_a^b |f|$$

## Fundamental Theorem of Calculus (Rigorous)

### Part 1

**Theorem**: If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and $F(x) = \int_a^x f(t)\,dt$, then $F$ is differentiable on $(a,b)$ and:

$$F'(x) = f(x)$$

> [!info] Tao Reference
> See *Analysis II* (4th ed.), Chapter 11, Section 11.9 (pp. 315-318)

### Part 2

**Theorem**: If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and $F$ is any antiderivative of $f$, then:

$$\int_a^b f(x)\,dx = F(b) - F(a)$$

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Assuming All Bounded Functions Are Integrable
> Bounded is necessary but not sufficient! The Dirichlet function is bounded but not integrable.

> [!warning] Confusing Darboux and Riemann Sums
> Darboux uses sup/inf; Riemann uses sample points. They're equivalent but conceptually different.

> [!warning] Forgetting Boundedness
> Unbounded functions cannot be Riemann integrable (though they might be improperly integrable).

## Practice Problems

Test integrability:

- Show that $f(x) = x^2$ is Riemann integrable on $[0,1]$
- Prove that a step function is Riemann integrable
- Is $f(x) = 1/x$ Riemann integrable on $[0,1]$? Why or why not?

Compute integrals:

- Use Darboux sums to compute $\int_0^1 x\,dx$
- Verify FTC for $f(x) = \sin(x)$ on $[0,\pi]$
- Show that $\int_{-1}^1 x^3\,dx = 0$ using symmetry

Conceptual:

- Why does continuity imply integrability?
- Explain the relationship between upper/lower sums and the integral
- What's the difference between Riemann and Lebesgue integration?

Challenge:

- Prove that monotone functions are Riemann integrable
- Show that if $f$ is integrable, then $|f|$ is integrable
- Research: What is Lebesgue's criterion for Riemann integrability?
