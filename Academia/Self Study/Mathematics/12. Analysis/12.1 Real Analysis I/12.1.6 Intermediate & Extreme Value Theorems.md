## Intermediate Value Theorem (IVT)

### Statement

**Theorem**: If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$ and $y$ is any value between $f(a)$ and $f(b)$, then there exists $c \in (a,b)$ such that $f(c) = y$.

> [!info] Tao Reference
> See *Analysis I* (4th ed.), Chapter 9: Continuous Functions on Intervals, Section 9.5 (pp. 248-251)

```desmos-graph
y = x^3 - x
y = 0.5
x = [-2, 2]
```

### Key Points

- Requires **continuity** on a **closed interval**
- Guarantees existence but NOT uniqueness
- Doesn't tell us HOW to find $c$, just that it exists

### Applications

**Root Finding**: If $f(a)$ and $f(b)$ have opposite signs, there's a root in $(a,b)$.

**Example**: Show $x^3 - x - 1 = 0$ has a solution in $(1,2)$.
- $f(1) = -1 < 0$
- $f(2) = 5 > 0$
- By IVT, $\exists c \in (1,2)$ with $f(c) = 0$

## Extreme Value Theorem (EVT)

### Statement

**Theorem**: If $f$ is continuous on a closed, bounded interval $[a,b]$, then $f$ attains both a maximum and minimum value on $[a,b]$.

That is, $\exists c,d \in [a,b]$ such that:
$$f(c) \leq f(x) \leq f(d) \quad \forall x \in [a,b]$$

> [!info] Tao Reference
> See *Analysis I* (4th ed.), Chapter 9: Continuous Functions on Intervals, Section 9.6 (pp. 251-254)

### Why Hypotheses Matter

|Condition|What Happens if Missing?|
|---|---|
|Continuity|$f(x) = 1/x$ on $[-1,1]$ (undefined at 0)|
|Closed interval|$f(x) = x$ on $(0,1)$ has no max/min|
|Bounded interval|$f(x) = x$ on $[0,\infty)$ has no maximum|

### Examples

**Example 1**: $f(x) = x^2$ on $[-1,2]$
- Minimum: $f(0) = 0$
- Maximum: $f(2) = 4$

**Example 2**: $f(x) = \sin(x)$ on $[0, 2\pi]$
- Minimum: $f(3\pi/2) = -1$
- Maximum: $f(\pi/2) = 1$

## Boundedness Theorem

### Statement

**Corollary of EVT**: If $f$ is continuous on $[a,b]$, then $f$ is bounded on $[a,b]$.

That is, $\exists M > 0$ such that $|f(x)| \leq M$ for all $x \in [a,b]$.

### Proof

By EVT, $f$ attains maximum $M$ and minimum $m$ on $[a,b]$. Let $K = \max\{|M|, |m|\}$. Then $|f(x)| \leq K$ for all $x \in [a,b]$. ∎

## Comparison of Theorems

|Theorem|Hypothesis|Conclusion|
|---|---|---|
|**IVT**|Continuous on $[a,b]$|Takes all intermediate values|
|**EVT**|Continuous on $[a,b]$|Attains max and min|
|**Boundedness**|Continuous on $[a,b]$|Function is bounded|

All three require the same hypotheses!

## Common Mistakes

> [!warning] Forgetting Closed Interval
> IVT and EVT require CLOSED intervals $[a,b]$, not open intervals $(a,b)$!

> [!warning] Assuming Uniqueness
> IVT guarantees EXISTENCE of $c$, not UNIQUENESS. There could be many such points!

> [!warning] Misapplying to Discontinuous Functions
> These theorems require CONTINUITY. Don't apply them to functions with jumps or holes!

> [!warning] Confusing Local and Global Extrema
> EVT gives GLOBAL extrema on $[a,b]$. Critical points give LOCAL extrema.

## Practice Problems

Apply IVT:

- Show that $x^5 + x - 1 = 0$ has exactly one real root
- Prove that $\cos(x) = x$ has a solution in $(0, 1)$
- Does $x^2 = \sin(x)$ have a solution in $(0, \pi/2)$?

Apply EVT:

- Find the maximum and minimum of $f(x) = x^3 - 3x$ on $[-2, 2]$
- Does $f(x) = \tan(x)$ attain a maximum on $(-\pi/2, \pi/2)$? Why or why not?
- Show that $f(x) = 1/x$ on $(0,1]$ violates EVT

Conceptual:

- Why do we need closed intervals for these theorems?
- Can a discontinuous function satisfy the conclusion of IVT?
- Explain why EVT implies the Boundedness Theorem

Challenge:

- Prove IVT using the completeness of $\mathbb{R}$ (supremum property)
- Show that if $f$ is continuous and injective on $[a,b]$, then $f$ is strictly monotone
- Research: What is the Darboux property and how does it relate to IVT?
