## Complex Numbers

A **complex number** has the form:

$$z = x + iy$$

where $x, y \in \mathbb{R}$ and $i^2 = -1$.

### Components

- **Real part**: $\text{Re}(z) = x$
- **Imaginary part**: $\text{Im}(z) = y$
- **Complex conjugate**: $\bar{z} = x - iy$
- **Modulus (absolute value)**: $|z| = \sqrt{x^2 + y^2}$

---

## Geometric Representation

### Complex Plane

Complex numbers can be visualized as points in the **Argand plane**:
- Horizontal axis: real part
- Vertical axis: imaginary part
- Distance from origin: modulus $|z|$

```graph
z = x + i*y
```

### Polar Form

Any complex number can be written as:

$$z = r(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta) = re^{i\theta}$$

where:
- $r = |z|$ (modulus)
- $\theta = \arg(z)$ (argument/angle)
- $\tan\theta = \frac{y}{x}$

> [!tip] Euler's Formula
> 
> $e^{i\theta} = \cos\theta + i\sin\theta$ connects exponential, trigonometric, and complex functions.

---

## Arithmetic Operations

### Addition and Subtraction

$$(a + bi) \pm (c + di) = (a \pm c) + (b \pm d)i$$

Geometrically: vector addition/subtraction in the plane.

### Multiplication

$$(a + bi)(c + di) = (ac - bd) + (ad + bc)i$$

In polar form: $r_1 e^{i\theta_1} \cdot r_2 e^{i\theta_2} = r_1 r_2 e^{i(\theta_1 + \theta_2)}$
- Moduli multiply
- Arguments add

### Division

$$\frac{a + bi}{c + di} = \frac{(a + bi)(c - di)}{c^2 + d^2} = \frac{ac + bd}{c^2 + d^2} + i\frac{bc - ad}{c^2 + d^2}$$

In polar form: $\frac{r_1 e^{i\theta_1}}{r_2 e^{i\theta_2}} = \frac{r_1}{r_2} e^{i(\theta_1 - \theta_2)}$

## Properties

### Conjugate Properties

- $z\bar{z} = |z|^2$
- $\overline{z + w} = \bar{z} + \bar{w}$
- $\overline{zw} = \bar{z}\bar{w}$
- $\text{Re}(z) = \frac{z + \bar{z}}{2}$, $\text{Im}(z) = \frac{z - \bar{z}}{2i}$

### Triangle Inequality

$$|z + w| \leq |z| + |w|$$

Equality holds when $z$ and $w$ have the same argument.

### De Moivre's Theorem

$$(\cos\theta + i\sin\theta)^n = \cos(n\theta) + i\sin(n\theta)$$

Useful for computing powers and roots.

---

## Roots of Complex Numbers

The **$n$th roots** of $z = re^{i\theta}$ are:

$$w_k = \sqrt[n]{r} \, e^{i(\theta + 2\pi k)/n}, \quad k = 0, 1, \ldots, n-1$$

- There are exactly $n$ distinct $n$th roots
- They form vertices of a regular $n$-gon centered at origin

### Example: Cube Roots of Unity

Solutions to $z^3 = 1$:
- $z_0 = 1$
- $z_1 = e^{2\pi i/3} = -\frac{1}{2} + i\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$
- $z_2 = e^{4\pi i/3} = -\frac{1}{2} - i\frac{\sqrt{3}}{2}$

## Practice Problems

1. Write $z = 3 + 4i$ in polar form.
2. Find all cube roots of $8i$.
3. Prove that $|zw| = |z||w|$ for any complex numbers $z, w$.
4. Simplify $(1 + i)^{10}$ using De Moivre's theorem.
5. Solve the equation $z^2 + 2z + 5 = 0$.
6. Show that the sum of the $n$th roots of unity is zero for $n > 1$.
7. Find the modulus and argument of $z = \frac{1+i}{1-i}$.
8. Verify the triangle inequality for $z = 3+4i$ and $w = 12-5i$.
9. Express $\cos(3\theta)$ in terms of $\cos\theta$ using De Moivre's theorem.
10. Prove that if $z$ is a root of a polynomial with real coefficients, then $\bar{z}$ is also a root.
