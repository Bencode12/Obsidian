## Cauchy's Theorem

**Cauchy's Theorem**: If $f$ is analytic on a simply connected domain $D$, then for any closed contour $C$ in $D$:

$$\oint_C f(z) \, dz = 0$$

### Simply Connected Domain

A domain is **simply connected** if every closed curve can be continuously shrunk to a point without leaving the domain (no holes).

> [!tip] Physical Interpretation
> 
> If $f$ is analytic everywhere inside a closed loop, the total "circulation" around the loop is zero.

---

## Cauchy's Integral Formula

If $f$ is analytic on and inside a simple closed contour $C$, and $z_0$ is any point inside $C$, then:

$$f(z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_C \frac{f(z)}{z - z_0} \, dz$$

### Generalized Form

For derivatives:

$$f^{(n)}(z_0) = \frac{n!}{2\pi i} \oint_C \frac{f(z)}{(z - z_0)^{n+1}} \, dz$$

---

## Key Consequences

### 1. Analytic Functions are Infinitely Differentiable

If $f$ is analytic at $z_0$, then $f^{(n)}(z_0)$ exists for all $n$.

### 2. Mean Value Property

For analytic $f$ on disk $|z - z_0| \leq R$:

$$f(z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} f(z_0 + Re^{i\theta}) \, d\theta$$

The value at the center equals the average on the boundary.

### 3. Maximum Modulus Principle

If $f$ is analytic on a bounded domain $D$ and continuous on $\bar{D}$, then $|f|$ attains its maximum on the boundary $\partial D$ (unless $f$ is constant).

> [!warning] Important Implication
> 
> Non-constant analytic functions cannot have local maxima of their modulus in the interior!

### 4. Liouville's Theorem

Every bounded entire function is constant.

**Proof**: Use Cauchy's estimate on $f'$ with arbitrarily large circles.

## Examples

### Example 1: Basic Application

Evaluate $\oint_C \frac{e^z}{z-1} \, dz$ where $C$ is the circle $|z|=2$:

1. $f(z) = e^z$ is entire
2. Singularity at $z=1$ is inside $C$
3. By Cauchy's formula: $\oint_C \frac{e^z}{z-1} \, dz = 2\pi i \cdot e^1 = 2\pi ie$

### Example 2: Higher Derivatives

Find $\oint_C \frac{\sin z}{z^3} \, dz$ where $C$ is $|z|=1$:

1. Rewrite as $\oint_C \frac{\sin z}{(z-0)^3} \, dz$
2. Use formula with $n=2$: $\frac{2!}{2\pi i} \oint_C \frac{\sin z}{z^3} \, dz = (\sin z)''|_{z=0}$
3. $(\sin z)'' = -\sin z$, so at $z=0$: value is $0$
4. Result: $\oint_C \frac{\sin z}{z^3} \, dz = 0$

### Example 3: Multiple Singularities

Evaluate $\oint_C \frac{1}{z^2-1} \, dz$ where $C$ is $|z|=2$:

1. Partial fractions: $\frac{1}{z^2-1} = \frac{1/2}{z-1} - \frac{1/2}{z+1}$
2. Both singularities ($z=\pm 1$) are inside $C$
3. Apply formula to each: $2\pi i(\frac{1}{2} - \frac{1}{2}) = 0$

## Cauchy's Estimate

If $|f(z)| \leq M$ on circle $|z-z_0| = R$, then:

$$|f^{(n)}(z_0)| \leq \frac{n! M}{R^n}$$

Used to prove Liouville's theorem and other results.

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\oint_C \frac{z^2}{z-3} \, dz$ where $C$ is $|z|=4$.
2. Use Cauchy's integral formula to find $f''(0)$ for $f(z) = e^{z^2}$.
3. Prove that if $f$ is entire and bounded, then $f' = 0$ everywhere.
4. Evaluate $\oint_{|z|=1} \frac{\cos z}{z^2} \, dz$.
5. Show that $\oint_C \frac{1}{z} \, dz = 2\pi i$ using Cauchy's integral formula.
6. Find $\oint_{|z|=2} \frac{e^z}{(z-1)^2} \, dz$.
7. Prove the mean value property for harmonic functions using Cauchy's formula.
8. Use the maximum modulus principle to show that if $|f(z)| \leq 1$ on $|z|=1$ and $f(0)=1$, then $f$ is constant.
9. Evaluate $\oint_C \frac{z}{(z-1)(z-2)} \, dz$ where $C$ is $|z|=1.5$.
10. Prove that an entire function satisfying $|f(z)| \leq A|z|^n$ for large $|z|$ must be a polynomial of degree at most $n$.
