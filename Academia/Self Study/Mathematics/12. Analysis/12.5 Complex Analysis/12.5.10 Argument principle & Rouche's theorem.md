## Argument Principle

The **Argument Principle** relates the number of zeros and poles of a meromorphic function to a contour integral.

### Statement

Let $f$ be meromorphic inside and on a simple closed contour $C$, with no zeros or poles on $C$. Then:

$$\frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_C \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)} \, dz = N - P$$

where:
- $N$ = number of zeros inside $C$ (counting multiplicity)
- $P$ = number of poles inside $C$ (counting order)

### Interpretation

The integral counts the **change in argument** of $f(z)$ as $z$ traverses $C$:

$$\frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_C \frac{f'(z)}{f(z)} \, dz = \frac{1}{2\pi} \Delta_C \arg f(z)$$

This is the winding number of the curve $f(C)$ around the origin.

> [!tip] Geometric Meaning
> 
> As you walk around contour $C$, count how many times $f(z)$ winds around the origin. This equals zeros minus poles inside.

---

## Proof Sketch

Near a zero of order $k$ at $z_0$: $f(z) = (z-z_0)^k g(z)$ where $g(z_0) \neq 0$

$$\frac{f'(z)}{f(z)} = \frac{k}{z-z_0} + \frac{g'(z)}{g(z)}$$

The residue at $z_0$ is $k$ (the multiplicity). Similarly, near a pole of order $m$, the residue is $-m$.

By residue theorem, the integral equals $2\pi i(N - P)$.

## Rouché's Theorem

**Rouché's Theorem**: Let $f$ and $g$ be analytic inside and on a simple closed contour $C$. If $|g(z)| < |f(z)|$ for all $z$ on $C$, then $f$ and $f+g$ have the same number of zeros inside $C$.

### Proof Idea

Consider $h_t(z) = f(z) + tg(z)$ for $t \in [0,1]$. The number of zeros varies continuously but must be an integer, so it's constant.

### Applications

#### Locating Zeros of Polynomials

Example: Show that $p(z) = z^4 + z + 1$ has exactly one zero in each quadrant.

1. On large circle $|z|=R$: dominant term is $z^4$
2. By Rouché's theorem, $p(z)$ has 4 zeros (same as $z^4$)
3. Use symmetry and additional contours to locate them

#### Stability Analysis

In control theory, determine if system has unstable poles using Nyquist criterion (based on argument principle).

## Examples

### Example 1: Counting Zeros

How many zeros does $f(z) = z^3 - 2z + 2$ have in $|z| < 2$?

1. On $|z|=2$: $|z^3| = 8$ and $|-2z+2| \leq 6$
2. Since $6 < 8$, by Rouché's theorem, $f$ has same zeros as $z^3$
3. Answer: 3 zeros (counting multiplicity)

### Example 2: Fundamental Theorem of Algebra

Every polynomial of degree $n$ has exactly $n$ roots in $\mathbb{C}$.

**Proof**: For $p(z) = a_n z^n + \cdots + a_0$ with $a_n \neq 0$:
- On large circle $|z|=R$: $|a_n z^n|$ dominates other terms
- By Rouché's theorem, $p(z)$ has $n$ zeros (same as $a_n z^n$)

### Example 3: Using Argument Principle

Find number of zeros of $f(z) = e^z - z$ in $|z| < 2$.

1. No poles (entire function)
2. Compute $\frac{1}{2\pi i} \oint_{|z|=2} \frac{e^z - 1}{e^z - z} \, dz$ numerically or estimate
3. Result: 1 zero in the disk

## Hurwitz's Theorem

If sequence of analytic functions $f_n$ converges uniformly to $f$ on compact sets, and each $f_n$ has no zeros in domain $D$, then either:
- $f$ has no zeros in $D$, or
- $f$ is identically zero

### 1. Control Theory (Nyquist Criterion)

Study distribution of zeros of zeta function and L-functions.
