## Analytic Functions

A function $f: D \to \mathbb{C}$ is **analytic** (or **holomorphic**) at $z_0$ if it is complex differentiable in a neighborhood of $z_0$.

### Complex Derivative

$$f'(z_0) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(z_0 + h) - f(z_0)}{h}$$

where $h \in \mathbb{C}$. The limit must exist and be the same regardless of how $h$ approaches 0.

> [!warning] Stronger than Real Differentiability
> 
> Complex differentiability is much stronger than real differentiability. If $f$ is analytic, it's infinitely differentiable and equal to its Taylor series!

---

## Basic Properties

### Linearity

If $f$ and $g$ are analytic, then:
- $(af + bg)' = af' + bg'$ for constants $a, b \in \mathbb{C}$
- $(fg)' = f'g + fg'$ (product rule)
- $(f/g)' = \frac{f'g - fg'}{g^2}$ (quotient rule, when $g \neq 0$)

### Chain Rule

If $f$ is analytic at $z_0$ and $g$ is analytic at $f(z_0)$, then:

$$(g \circ f)'(z_0) = g'(f(z_0)) \cdot f'(z_0)$$

## Elementary Analytic Functions

### Polynomials

$$P(z) = a_n z^n + a_{n-1} z^{n-1} + \cdots + a_1 z + a_0$$

- Analytic everywhere (entire functions)
- Derivative: $P'(z) = na_n z^{n-1} + \cdots + a_1$

### Exponential Function

$$e^z = e^x(\cos y + i\sin y)$$

Properties:
- $e^{z+w} = e^z e^w$
- $(e^z)' = e^z$
- Periodic with period $2\pi i$
- Never zero

### Trigonometric Functions

$$\sin z = \frac{e^{iz} - e^{-iz}}{2i}, \quad \cos z = \frac{e^{iz} + e^{-iz}}{2}$$

- $(\sin z)' = \cos z$, $(\cos z)' = -\sin z$
- $\sin^2 z + \cos^2 z = 1$
- Unbounded on $\mathbb{C}$ (unlike real case!)

### Logarithm

$$\log z = \ln|z| + i\arg z$$

- Multi-valued function
- Principal branch: $\text{Arg}(z) \in (-\pi, \pi]$
- $(\log z)' = \frac{1}{z}$ (on chosen branch)
- Branch cut required (typically negative real axis)

## Power Series

### Taylor Series

If $f$ is analytic at $z_0$, then:

$$f(z) = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{f^{(n)}(z_0)}{n!}(z-z_0)^n$$

converges in some disk $|z - z_0| < R$.

### Radius of Convergence

For power series $\sum a_n(z-z_0)^n$:

$$R = \frac{1}{\limsup_{n\to\infty} |a_n|^{1/n}}$$

- Series converges absolutely for $|z - z_0| < R$
- Series diverges for $|z - z_0| > R$
- Behavior on boundary $|z - z_0| = R$ varies

> [!example] Important Series
> 
> - $e^z = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{z^n}{n!}$ (entire, $R = \infty$)
> - $\frac{1}{1-z} = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} z^n$ for $|z| < 1$
> - $\sin z = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} \frac{(-1)^n z^{2n+1}}{(2n+1)!}$

---

## Entire Functions

A function is **entire** if it's analytic on all of $\mathbb{C}$.

Examples:
- Polynomials
- $e^z$, $\sin z$, $\cos z$
- Sums, products, and compositions of entire functions

### Liouville's Theorem

Every bounded entire function is constant.

> [!tip] Fundamental Theorem of Algebra
> 
> Liouville's theorem implies that every non-constant polynomial has at least one root in $\mathbb{C}$.

## Practice Problems

1. Show that $f(z) = \bar{z}$ is nowhere analytic.
2. Find the derivative of $f(z) = z^3 + 2iz^2 - 3z + 4i$.
3. Compute the Taylor series of $e^{2z}$ around $z = 0$.
4. Determine the radius of convergence of $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} \frac{z^n}{n^2}$.
5. Prove that $\frac{d}{dz}\sin z = \cos z$ using the definition.
6. Find all values of $\log(-1)$.
7. Show that $f(z) = |z|^2$ is differentiable only at $z = 0$.
8. Use Liouville's theorem to prove the Fundamental Theorem of Algebra.
9. Find the Taylor series of $\frac{1}{1+z^2}$ around $z = 0$ and determine its radius of convergence.
10. Prove that if $f$ is entire and $\text{Re}(f)$ is bounded, then $f$ is constant.
