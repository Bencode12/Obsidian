## Contour Integration

A **contour** is a piecewise-smooth curve in the complex plane. A **contour integral** integrates a complex function along such a curve.

### Parameterization

Let $C$ be a contour parameterized by $z(t) = x(t) + iy(t)$ for $a \leq t \leq b$. The contour integral is:

$$\int_C f(z) \, dz = \int_a^b f(z(t)) z'(t) \, dt$$

---

## Basic Properties

### Linearity

$$\int_C (af + bg) \, dz = a\int_C f \, dz + b\int_C g \, dz$$

### Additivity

If $C = C_1 + C_2$ (concatenation of contours):

$$\int_C f \, dz = \int_{C_1} f \, dz + \int_{C_2} f \, dz$$

### Reversal

If $-C$ is $C$ traversed in opposite direction:

$$\int_{-C} f \, dz = -\int_C f \, dz$$

---

## Estimation Lemma (ML Inequality)

If $|f(z)| \leq M$ for all $z$ on contour $C$, and $L$ is the length of $C$, then:

$$\left|\int_C f(z) \, dz\right| \leq ML$$

> [!tip] Use Case
> 
> Essential for proving convergence of integrals and bounding error terms.

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Integral Along Line Segment

Evaluate $\int_C z^2 \, dz$ where $C$ is the line segment from $0$ to $1+i$:

1. Parameterize: $z(t) = t(1+i)$, $0 \leq t \leq 1$
2. $z'(t) = 1+i$
3. Integral: $\int_0^1 [t(1+i)]^2 (1+i) \, dt = (1+i)^3 \int_0^1 t^2 \, dt$
4. Result: $\frac{(1+i)^3}{3} = \frac{-2+2i}{3}$

### Example 2: Integral Around Circle

Evaluate $\oint_C \frac{1}{z} \, dz$ where $C$ is the unit circle $|z|=1$ counterclockwise:

1. Parameterize: $z(t) = e^{it}$, $0 \leq t \leq 2\pi$
2. $z'(t) = ie^{it}$
3. Integral: $\int_0^{2\pi} \frac{1}{e^{it}} \cdot ie^{it} \, dt = \int_0^{2\pi} i \, dt = 2\pi i$

> [!example] Key Result
> 
> $\oint_C \frac{1}{z} \, dz = 2\pi i$ for any closed curve encircling the origin once counterclockwise.

### Example 3: Path Independence

For $f(z) = z^2$, integrate from $0$ to $1+i$ along two paths:
- Path 1: Straight line
- Path 2: Along real axis to $1$, then vertical to $1+i$

Both give the same result $\frac{(1+i)^3}{3}$ because $z^2$ has an antiderivative.

---

## Fundamental Theorem for Contour Integrals

If $f$ is continuous on domain $D$ and has an antiderivative $F$ (i.e., $F' = f$) on $D$, then for any contour $C$ from $z_1$ to $z_2$:

$$\int_C f(z) \, dz = F(z_2) - F(z_1)$$

### Corollary

If $f$ has an antiderivative on $D$, then $\oint_C f(z) \, dz = 0$ for any closed contour in $D$.

## Common Contour Integrals

|Integral|Result|Condition|
|---|---|---|
|$\oint_C z^n \, dz$|$0$|$n \neq -1$, $C$ closed|
|$\oint_C \frac{1}{z} \, dz$|$2\pi i$|$C$ encircles origin once|
|$\oint_C \frac{1}{z-a} \, dz$|$2\pi i$|$C$ encircles $a$ once|
|$\oint_C z^n \, dz$|$0$|$n \geq 0$, any closed $C$|

## Practice Problems

1. Evaluate $\int_C \bar{z} \, dz$ where $C$ is the unit circle traversed counterclockwise.
2. Compute $\int_C e^z \, dz$ along any path from $0$ to $\pi i$.
3. Use the ML inequality to bound $\left|\int_C \frac{1}{z^2+1} \, dz\right|$ where $C$ is the circle $|z|=2$.
4. Evaluate $\oint_C \frac{1}{z^2} \, dz$ where $C$ is the unit circle.
5. Show that $\int_C z \, dz$ depends only on endpoints, not the path.
6. Parameterize the ellipse $\frac{x^2}{4} + y^2 = 1$ and compute $\oint_C \bar{z} \, dz$.
7. Evaluate $\int_C \text{Re}(z) \, dz$ where $C$ goes from $0$ to $1$ to $1+i$.
8. Prove that $\oint_C \frac{1}{(z-a)^n} \, dz = 0$ for $n \geq 2$ and closed $C$ not passing through $a$.
9. Calculate $\int_C |z|^2 \, dz$ where $C$ is the semicircle $|z|=1$, $\text{Im}(z) \geq 0$.
10. Show that if $\oint_C f(z) \, dz = 0$ for all closed contours in $D$, then $f$ has an antiderivative in $D$.
