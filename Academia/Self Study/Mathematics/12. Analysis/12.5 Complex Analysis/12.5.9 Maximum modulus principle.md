## Maximum Modulus Principle

**Maximum Modulus Principle**: If $f$ is analytic on a bounded domain $D$ and continuous on $\bar{D}$, then $|f(z)|$ attains its maximum value on the boundary $\partial D$, unless $f$ is constant.

### Formal Statement

If $f$ is non-constant and analytic on domain $D$, then $|f(z)|$ has no local maximum in $D$.

> [!warning] Key Insight
> 
> Analytic functions cannot have "peaks" in their modulus inside the domain - maxima only occur at boundaries!

---

## Proof Strategy

Uses the **mean value property**: For analytic $f$ on disk $|z-z_0| \leq r$:

$$f(z_0) = \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} f(z_0 + re^{i\theta}) \, d\theta$$

Taking absolute values and using triangle inequality:

$$|f(z_0)| \leq \frac{1}{2\pi} \int_0^{2\pi} |f(z_0 + re^{i\theta})| \, d\theta$$

If $|f(z_0)|$ were a strict local maximum, this would be impossible unless $|f|$ is constant on the circle.

## Minimum Modulus Principle

If $f$ is analytic on domain $D$ and never zero, then $|f(z)|$ attains its minimum on the boundary $\partial D$ (unless $f$ is constant).

**Proof**: Apply maximum modulus principle to $1/f$.

---

## Applications

### 1. Bounding Analytic Functions

If you know $|f(z)| \leq M$ on boundary of region, then $|f(z)| \leq M$ everywhere inside.

### Example

Find maximum of $|f(z)| = |z^2 + 1|$ on unit disk $|z| \leq 1$:

1. Check boundary $|z| = 1$: $|z^2 + 1| \leq |z^2| + |1| = 2$
2. Maximum occurs at $z = \pm 1$: $|1 + 1| = 2$
3. By maximum modulus principle, this is the global maximum

### 2. Proving Identities

**Schwarz Lemma**: If $f$ is analytic on unit disk, $f(0) = 0$, and $|f(z)| \leq 1$, then:
- $|f(z)| \leq |z|$ for all $z$
- $|f'(0)| \leq 1$

**Proof**: Apply maximum modulus to $g(z) = f(z)/z$.

### 3. Uniqueness Theorems

If two analytic functions agree on a set with accumulation point, they're identical.

---

## Phragmén-Lindelöf Principle

Extension of maximum modulus to unbounded domains with growth conditions.

If $f$ is analytic in sector and doesn't grow too fast at infinity, maximum still occurs on boundary.

---

## Examples

### Example 1: Simple Application

Show that if $f$ is entire and $|f(z)| \leq 5$ for all $z$, then $f$ is constant.

**Solution**: By Liouville's theorem (consequence of maximum modulus), bounded entire functions are constant.

### Example 2: Finding Bounds

Let $f(z) = e^z$ on rectangle $0 \leq \text{Re}(z) \leq 1$, $0 \leq \text{Im}(z) \leq \pi$.

Maximum of $|e^z| = e^{\text{Re}(z)}$ occurs where $\text{Re}(z)$ is largest:
- On right edge: $|e^{1+iy}| = e$
- Maximum value: $e$ at $z = 1$

### Example 3: Zero Location

Prove that polynomial $p(z) = z^5 + z + 1$ has no zeros in $|z| > 2$.

For $|z| > 2$: $|z^5| = |z|^5 > 32$ while $|z+1| \leq |z|+1 < 3$
So $|p(z)| \geq |z^5| - |z+1| > 29 > 0$ → no zeros outside disk.

## Related Results

### Hadamard Three-Circles Theorem

For analytic $f$ on annulus, $\log M(r)$ is convex in $\log r$, where $M(r) = \max_{|z|=r} |f(z)|$.

### Borel-Carathéodory Theorem

Relates maximum of $|f|$ to maximum of $\text{Re}(f)$.

1. Use maximum modulus principle to prove Liouville's theorem.
2. Find the maximum of $|z^3 - 2z + 1|$ on the unit disk.
3. Prove that if $|f(z)| = 1$ on boundary of unit disk and $f$ has no zeros inside, then $f$ is constant.
4. Show that an entire function satisfying $|f(z)| \leq A + B|z|^n$ is a polynomial of degree at most $n$.
5. Apply Schwarz lemma to show that if $f$ maps unit disk to itself and $f(0) = 0$, then $|f'(0)| \leq 1$.
6. Prove the minimum modulus principle using the maximum modulus principle.
7. Find bounds for $|\sin z|$ on the rectangle $0 \leq x \leq \pi$, $0 \leq y \leq 1$.
8. Show that if $f$ is analytic on upper half-plane, continuous on closure, and bounded, then $\sup_{\text{Im}(z)>0} |f(z)| = \sup_{x \in \mathbb{R}} |f(x)|$.
9. Use maximum modulus to prove that if $f$ and $g$ are analytic on $D$ and $|f| = |g|$ on $\partial D$, then $f = cg$ for some constant $c$ with $|c|=1$.
10. Prove that a non-constant analytic function maps open sets to open sets (open mapping theorem) using maximum modulus principle.
