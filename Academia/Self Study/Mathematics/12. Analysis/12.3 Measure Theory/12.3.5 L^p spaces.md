## Definition

For $1 \leq p < \infty$, the **$L^p$ space** consists of all measurable functions $f$ such that:

$$\|f\|_p = \left(\int |f|^p \, d\mu\right)^{1/p} < \infty$$

For $p = \infty$:
$$\|f\|_\infty = \text{ess sup } |f| = \inf\{M : \mu(\{x : |f(x)| > M\}) = 0\}$$

Functions equal almost everywhere are identified (we work with equivalence classes).

---

## Key Properties

### Banach Space Structure

$(L^p, \|\cdot\|_p)$ is a **Banach space** (complete normed vector space) for all $1 \leq p \leq \infty$.

### Special Cases

|Space|Norm|Description|
|---|---|---|
|$L^1$|$\|f\|_1 = \int |f|$|Integrable functions|
|$L^2$|$\|f\|_2 = \sqrt{\int |f|^2}$|Square-integrable (Hilbert space)|
|$L^\infty$|$\|f\|_\infty = \text{ess sup } |f|$|Essentially bounded functions|

---

## Important Inequalities

### Hölder's Inequality

If $1 \leq p,q \leq \infty$ with $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} = 1$, and $f \in L^p$, $g \in L^q$, then:

$$\int |fg| \leq \|f\|_p \|g\|_q$$

Special case ($p=q=2$): Cauchy-Schwarz inequality
$$\int |fg| \leq \|f\|_2 \|g\|_2$$

### Minkowski's Inequality (Triangle Inequality)

For $f,g \in L^p$:
$$\|f + g\|_p \leq \|f\|_p + \|g\|_p$$

This proves that $\|\cdot\|_p$ is indeed a norm.

---

## Duality

### The Dual Space $(L^p)^*$

For $1 \leq p < \infty$ with conjugate exponent $q$ (where $\frac{1}{p} + \frac{1}{q} = 1$):

$$(L^p)^* \cong L^q$$

Every continuous linear functional $\phi$ on $L^p$ has the form:
$$\phi(f) = \int fg \, d\mu \quad \text{for some } g \in L^q$$

### Special Case: $L^2$ is Self-Dual

Since $\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{2} = 1$, we have $(L^2)^* \cong L^2$. This makes $L^2$ a **Hilbert space** with inner product:

$$\langle f, g \rangle = \int f\overline{g} \, d\mu$$

---

## Density Results

### Simple Functions are Dense

For $1 \leq p < \infty$, simple functions are dense in $L^p$. This means any $f \in L^p$ can be approximated arbitrarily well by simple functions.

### Continuous Functions are Dense

On $\mathbb{R}^n$ with Lebesgue measure, $C_c^\infty$ (smooth compactly supported functions) is dense in $L^p$ for $1 \leq p < \infty$.

> [!tip] Why Density Matters
> 
> To prove something for all $L^p$ functions, it often suffices to prove it for simple or smooth functions, then extend by density.

---

## Convergence in $L^p$

### Definition

$f_n \to f$ in $L^p$ means $\|f_n - f\|_p \to 0$.

### Relationships

|Convergence Type|Implication|
|---|---|
|$L^p$ convergence|Implies convergence in measure|
|$L^p$ convergence|Does NOT imply pointwise convergence|
|Pointwise + dominated|Implies $L^p$ convergence (by DCT)|
|Uniform convergence on finite measure|Implies $L^p$ convergence|

## Examples

### Example 1: $L^2[0,1]$

The space of square-integrable functions on $[0,1]$ with inner product:
$$\langle f, g \rangle = \int_0^1 f(x)g(x) \, dx$$

Orthonormal basis: $\{e^{2\pi i nx}\}_{n \in \mathbb{Z}}$ (Fourier series)

### Example 2: Sequence Spaces $\ell^p$

For counting measure on $\mathbb{N}$:
$$\ell^p = \left\{(x_n) : \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} |x_n|^p < \infty\right\}$$

With norm: $\|(x_n)\|_p = \left(\sum |x_n|^p\right)^{1/p}$

## Practice Problems

1. Prove Hölder's inequality using Young's inequality: $ab \leq \frac{a^p}{p} + \frac{b^q}{q}$.
2. Show that $L^2[0,1]$ is complete (hint: use Riesz-Fischer theorem).
3. If $f \in L^p \cap L^q$ with $p < q$, prove $f \in L^r$ for all $p \leq r \leq q$.
4. Prove that $\|f\|_p \to \|f\|_\infty$ as $p \to \infty$ for bounded $f$ on a finite measure space.
5. Show that $L^p$ is separable for $1 \leq p < \infty$ but $L^\infty$ is not.
6. Find the dual of $\ell^1$ and $\ell^\infty$.
7. If $f_n \to f$ in $L^p$ and $f_n \to g$ pointwise a.e., show $f = g$ a.e.
8. Prove Minkowski's inequality for $p = 2$ using Cauchy-Schwarz.
9. Show that the map $f \mapsto \|f\|_p$ is continuous on $L^p$.
10. Construct a sequence converging in $L^1$ but not pointwise anywhere.
