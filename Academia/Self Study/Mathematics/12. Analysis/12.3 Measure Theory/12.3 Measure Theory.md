## Overview

**Measure Theory** provides the rigorous foundation for integration, probability, and modern analysis. It generalizes the concept of "size" or "volume" to abstract sets and enables the Lebesgue integral, which overcomes limitations of the Riemann integral.

### Key Concepts

- **Measures**: Functions that assign "size" to sets (length, area, volume, probability)
- **Sigma-algebras**: Collections of sets closed under countable operations
- **Lebesgue Integration**: More powerful than Riemann integration, handles discontinuous functions
- **Convergence Theorems**: Monotone Convergence, Dominated Convergence, Fatou's Lemma
- **L^p Spaces**: Function spaces central to functional analysis and PDEs

### Why Measure Theory Matters

|Application|Role of Measure Theory|
|---|---|
|**Probability**|Probability is a measure; random variables are measurable functions|
|**Fourier Analysis**|L² spaces and convergence in mean|
|**Partial Differential Equations**|Weak solutions and Sobolev spaces|
|**Ergodic Theory**|Invariant measures and dynamical systems|
|**Geometric Measure Theory**|Fractals, Hausdorff dimension, rectifiability|

### Historical Context

Developed primarily by **Henri Lebesgue** (1902) to address limitations of Riemann integration. Later generalized by **Émile Borel**, **Constantin Carathéodory**, and others into the abstract framework used today.

## Study Tips

> [!tip] Focus on Intuition First
> 
> Before diving into technical proofs, understand what measures represent: they generalize "volume" to weird sets. Think of length for intervals, area for regions, probability for events.

> [!warning] Common Pitfall
> 
> Don't confuse "measurable" with "nice." Many pathological sets are measurable but counterintuitive (e.g., Vitali sets, Cantor set). Embrace the abstraction.

> [!example] Connection to Probability
> 
> If you know probability theory, you already know measure theory! Probability spaces are measure spaces with total measure 1. Random variables are measurable functions. Expectation is integration.
