## Rational Expressions

Related: [[3.8_Polynomials]] · [[Fractions]] · [[3.7_Quadratics]]

## What is a Rational Expression?

A rational expression is a **fraction where the numerator and/or denominator is a polynomial**.

$$\frac{x + 2}{x^2 - 4}$$

> [!warning] The denominator can never equal zero Always find the values of $x$ that make the denominator zero — these are **excluded** from the domain.

---

## Simplifying Rational Expressions

Factor the numerator and denominator, then **cancel common factors**.

$$\frac{x^2 - 4}{x + 2} = \frac{(x-2)(x+2)}{x+2} = x - 2 \quad (x \neq -2)$$

> [!tip] State the restriction After cancelling, always write the excluded value — $x \neq -2$ — because the original expression was undefined there.

---

## Multiplying and Dividing

### Multiplication

Multiply numerators together and denominators together, then simplify.

$$\frac{x}{x+1} \times \frac{x+1}{x+2} = \frac{x(x+1)}{(x+1)(x+2)} = \frac{x}{x+2}$$

### Division

Multiply by the **reciprocal** of the second fraction.

$$\frac{x}{x+3} \div \frac{2x}{x+3} = \frac{x}{x+3} \times \frac{x+3}{2x} = \frac{1}{2} \quad (x \neq -3,\ x \neq 0)$$

---

## Adding and Subtracting

Find a **common denominator**, then combine numerators.

**Same denominator:**

$$\frac{1}{x} + \frac{2}{x} = \frac{3}{x}$$

**Different denominators:**

$$\frac{1}{x} + \frac{1}{x+1} = \frac{x+1}{x(x+1)} + \frac{x}{x(x+1)} = \frac{2x+1}{x(x+1)}$$

## Practice Problems

1. Simplify $\dfrac{x^2 - 9}{x + 3}$. State any restrictions.
2. Multiply: $\dfrac{x+2}{x-1} \times \dfrac{x-1}{x+3}$.
3. Divide: $\dfrac{x^2}{x+4} \div \dfrac{x}{x+4}$.
4. Add: $\dfrac{3}{x} + \dfrac{2}{x+1}$.
5. Subtract: $\dfrac{5}{x+2} - \dfrac{3}{x-2}$.
