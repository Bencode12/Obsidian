## Systems of Equations

Related: [[3.3 Linear equations]] · [[3.6 Functions]] · [[Graphing_Basics]]

---

## What is a System of Equations?

A system of equations is a set of **two or more equations** using the same variables. The goal is to find values that satisfy **all equations simultaneously**.

$$\begin{cases} x + y = 10 \ 2x - y = 3 \end{cases}$$

---

## Methods of Solving

|Method|Process|Best used when|
|---|---|---|
|**Substitution**|Solve one equation for a variable, substitute into the other|One variable is already isolated|
|**Elimination**|Add or subtract equations to cancel a variable|Variables are aligned with opposite coefficients|
|**Graphical**|Plot both lines — solution is the intersection point|You need to visualise the solution or check for no solution|

---

## Substitution Method

**Example** — solve $\begin{cases} x + y = 10 \ 2x - y = 3 \end{cases}$

**Step 1 — Isolate** one variable from the first equation: $$y = 10 - x$$

**Step 2 — Substitute** into the second equation: $$2x - (10 - x) = 3$$

**Step 3 — Solve:** $$3x - 10 = 3 \implies 3x = 13 \implies x = \frac{13}{3}$$

**Step 4 — Find the second variable:** $$y = 10 - \frac{13}{3} = \frac{17}{3}$$

---

## Elimination Method

**Same example** — stack the equations:

$$\begin{cases} x + y = 10 \ 2x - y = 3 \end{cases}$$

**Add** both equations — the $+y$ and $-y$ cancel:

$$(x + y) + (2x - y) = 10 + 3$$ $$3x = 13 \implies x = \frac{13}{3}$$

> [!tip] When to multiply first If no variable cancels immediately, multiply one or both equations by a constant to create opposite coefficients, then add.

---

## Checking Your Solution

Always substitute the values back into **both** original equations. If both hold, the solution is correct.

$$x = \frac{13}{3},\quad y = \frac{17}{3}$$ $$x + y = \frac{13}{3} + \frac{17}{3} = \frac{30}{3} = 10 \ \checkmark$$ $$2x - y = \frac{26}{3} - \frac{17}{3} = \frac{9}{3} = 3 \ \checkmark$$

---

## Number of Solutions

|Lines on graph|Solutions|
|---|---|
|Intersect at one point|**One solution**|
|Parallel — never intersect|**No solution**|
|Same line (coincident)|**Infinite solutions**|
