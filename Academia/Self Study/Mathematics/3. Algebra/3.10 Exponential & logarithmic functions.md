## Exponential and Logarithmic Functions

Related: [[3.6 Functions]] · [[2.4 Exponents & powers]] · [[3.7 Quadratics]]

---

## Exponential Functions

An exponential function has the variable in the **exponent**:

$$f(x) = a^x \quad (a > 0,\ a \neq 1)$$

|$x$|$f(x) = 2^x$|
|---|---|
|1|2|
|2|4|
|3|8|

Exponential functions **grow or decay rapidly** — small changes in $x$ produce large changes in $f(x)$.

---

## Laws of Exponents

These apply directly to exponential functions — see [[Exponents]] for full detail.

|Law|Rule|
|---|---|
|Multiply|$a^m \times a^n = a^{m+n}$|
|Divide|$a^m \div a^n = a^{m-n}$|
|Power of a power|$(a^m)^n = a^{m \times n}$|
|Zero power|$a^0 = 1$|

---

## Logarithmic Functions

A logarithm is the **inverse** of an exponential function:

$$y = \log_a(x) \iff a^y = x$$

The logarithm answers: _"To what power must $a$ be raised to get $x$?"_

$$\log_2(8) = 3 \quad \text{because} \quad 2^3 = 8$$

---

## Converting Between Forms

$$a^y = x \quad \Longleftrightarrow \quad \log_a(x) = y$$

|Exponential|Logarithmic|
|---|---|
|$2^3 = 8$|$\log_2(8) = 3$|
|$10^2 = 100$|$\log_{10}(100) = 2$|
|$a^0 = 1$|$\log_a(1) = 0$|

---

## Laws of Logarithms

|Law|Rule|
|---|---|
|Product|$\log_a(xy) = \log_a x + \log_a y$|
|Quotient|$\log_a!\left(\dfrac{x}{y}\right) = \log_a x - \log_a y$|
|Power|$\log_a(x^n) = n\log_a x$|
|Identity|$\log_a a = 1$|
|Zero|$\log_a 1 = 0$|

> [!tip] Log laws mirror exponent laws Product → add exponents. Quotient → subtract. Power → multiply. The log laws are the exponent laws written in log form.
