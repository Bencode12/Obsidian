## What is an Expression?

An expression is a combination of **numbers, variables, and operations** — with **no equals sign**.

$$2x + 5 \qquad 3a^2 - 7 \qquad \frac{x+1}{2}$$

> [!tip] Expression vs Equation An expression has no solution — it can only be **evaluated**. An equation has an equals sign and can be **solved**.

---

## Parts of an Expression

|Part|Example in $3x^2 - 5x + 7$|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|**Term**|$3x^2$, $-5x$, $7$|Each part separated by $+$ or $-$|
|**Coefficient**|$3$, $-5$|The number multiplying the variable|
|**Variable**|$x$|The unknown symbol|
|**Constant**|$7$|A fixed number with no variable|
|**Exponent**|$2$|The power on the variable|

---

## Like and Unlike Terms

**Like terms** share the same variable and exponent — they can be combined.

$$3x + 5x = 8x \qquad 4a^2 + 2a^2 = 6a^2$$

**Unlike terms** cannot be combined.

$$3x + 5y \qquad 2x^2 + 3x$$

---

## Evaluating an Expression

Replace the variable with a number and calculate.

$$3x + 2 \quad \text{at} \quad x = 4$$ $$= 3 \times 4 + 2 = 12 + 2 = 14$$

## Practice Problems

1. Identify all terms, coefficients, and constants in $4x^2 - 2x + 9$.
2. Evaluate $5x - 3$ at $x = 6$.
3. Evaluate $2a^2 + a$ at $a = 3$.
4. Simplify $3x + 7x - 2x$.
5. Simplify $4a + 3b - a + 2b$.
6. Write an expression for "three more than twice a number $n$".
