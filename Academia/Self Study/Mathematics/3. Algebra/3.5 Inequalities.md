## What is an Inequality?

An inequality shows that two expressions are **not necessarily equal**. Instead of $=$, we use:

|Symbol|Meaning|Example|
|---|---|---|
|$>$|Greater than|$x > 5$ — any number above 5|
|$<$|Less than|$x < 2$ — any number below 2|
|$\geq$|Greater than or equal to|$y \geq 10$ — 10 or above|
|$\leq$|Less than or equal to|$y \leq 10$ — 10 or below|

---

## Solving Inequalities

Solving works exactly like a linear equation — with **one critical exception**.

> [!warning] The Negative Rule If you multiply or divide both sides by a **negative number**, you must **reverse the inequality sign**.

**Example — Solve $-2x > 6$**

$$-2x > 6$$ $$x < -3 \quad (\div -2 \text{ — flip the sign})$$

---

## Representing Solutions

### Number Line

|Symbol|Circle type|Meaning|
|---|---|---|
|$>$ or $<$|Open circle $\circ$|Value **not** included|
|$\geq$ or $\leq$|Closed circle $\bullet$|Value **included**|

Shade the region that satisfies the inequality.

### Interval Notation

|Inequality|Interval notation|
|---|---|
|$x > 5$|$(5,\ \infty)$|
|$x \leq 10$|$(-\infty,\ 10]$|
|$3 < x \leq 8$|$(3,\ 8]$|

> [!tip] Brackets vs parentheses $[\ ]$ — value is **included** (closed). $(\ )$ — value is **excluded** (open). $\infty$ always uses $(\ )$ — infinity is never reached.

## Compound Inequalities

|Type|Condition|Example|Meaning|
|---|---|---|---|
|**AND**|Must satisfy **both**|$x > 3$ and $x < 8$|$3 < x < 8$ — the overlap|
|**OR**|Must satisfy **at least one**|$x < 2$ or $x > 5$|Two separate regions|

4. Is $x = 5$ a solution to $3 < x < 8$?
