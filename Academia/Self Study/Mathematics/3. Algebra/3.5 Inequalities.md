### **3.5.1 What is an inequality?**

An **inequality** shows that two expressions are **not equal**, using symbols:

- >>> → greater than
- <<< → less than
- ≥\geq≥ → greater than or equal to
- ≤\leq≤ → less than or equal to

Example:

- x > 5 → x is any number greater than 5
- y ≤ 10 → y is any number less than or equal to 10

### **3.5.2 Solving inequalities**

Solve similarly to equations, with **one important rule**:

- **If you multiply or divide both sides by a negative number, reverse the inequality sign.**

Example:

- −2x > 6 → divide by −2 → x < −3

### **3.5.3 Representing solutions**

- **Number line:** shade all numbers that satisfy the inequality
- **Interval notation:** x > 5 → (5, ∞)

### **3.5.4 Compound inequalities**

Combine inequalities using **and/or**:

- x > 3 and x < 8 → 3 < x < 8
- x < 2 or x > 5 → x < 2 or x > 5
