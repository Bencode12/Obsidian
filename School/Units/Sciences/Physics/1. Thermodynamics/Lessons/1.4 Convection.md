# 1.4 Heat Convection

- [ ] Define convection as heat transfer through fluid movement

---

## 🔬 What is Convection?

**Convection** is the transfer of heat through the movement of fluids (liquids or gases).

### How Convection Works

```
    HOT FLUID RISES
         ↑
         │
    ┌────┴────┐
    │  HEAT   │
    │ SOURCE  │
    └────┬────┘
         │
         ↓
    COLD FLUID SINKS
```

1. Fluid near heat source warms up
2. Warm fluid expands → becomes less dense
3. Less dense fluid rises
4. Cooler, denser fluid sinks to replace it
5. Creates a **convection current**

---

## 📊 Types of Convection

### Natural Convection
- Caused by density differences
- No external force needed

**Examples:**
- Hot air rising
- Sea breeze
- Radiator heating a room
- Boiling water

### Forced Convection
- Fluid moved by external force
- Faster heat transfer

**Examples:**
- Fan cooling
- Pump in central heating
- Car radiator with fan
- Air conditioning

---

## 🌍 Real-World Examples

### 1. Room Heating
- Radiator warms nearby air
- Warm air rises to ceiling
- Cool air sinks toward radiator
- Creates circulation pattern

### 2. Sea Breeze
**Day:**
- Land heats faster than sea
- Warm air over land rises
- Cool air from sea moves in
- Creates sea breeze

**Night:**
- Land cools faster than sea
- Air flow reverses
- Creates land breeze

### 3. Boiling Water
- Bottom water heats first
- Rises to top
- Cooler water sinks
- Continuous circulation

### 4. Weather Systems
- Warm air rises at equator
- Moves toward poles
- Cools and sinks
- Creates global wind patterns

---

##  Applications

### Home Heating
- Radiators placed low (warm air rises)
- Air conditioning units placed high (cool air sinks)

### Cooking
- Convection ovens use fans
- More even heat distribution
- Faster cooking

### Car Engines
- Water pump circulates coolant
- Radiator removes heat
- Fan assists airflow

### Refrigerators
- Coolant circulates through pipes
- Absorbs heat from inside
- Releases heat outside

## ✏️ Practice Questions

### Question 1
**Explain** why hot water tanks are designed with the heater at the bottom.

**Answer:** Hot water rises, so heating from the bottom creates convection currents that heat all the water efficiently.

### Question 2
**Describe** how a convection current forms in a room with a radiator.

**Answer:**
1. Air near radiator warms up
2. Warm air becomes less dense and rises
3. Cool air from ceiling sinks
4. Creates circular current

### Question 3
**Compare** natural and forced convection. Give one example of each.

| Natural | Forced |
|---------|--------|
| Density-driven | Fan/pump-driven |
| Slower | Faster |
| Sea breeze | Car radiator fan |

### Question 4
**True or False:**
- Convection can occur in solids. (___)
- Warm fluids are less dense than cold fluids. (___)
- Forced convection is always faster than natural convection. (___)

---

## 📝 Summary

- Convection transfers heat through fluid movement
- Warm fluids rise, cool fluids sink
- Creates convection currents
- Natural: density-driven; Forced: fan/pump-driven
- Important in weather, heating, cooling systems
