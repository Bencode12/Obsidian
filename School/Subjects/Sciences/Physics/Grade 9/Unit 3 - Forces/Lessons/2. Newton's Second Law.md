# 2. Newton's Second Law of Motion

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] State Newton's Second Law

---

## 📜 Newton's Second Law

### Statement

**The acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.**

### Mathematical Form

```
F_net = ma
```

Where:
- **F_net** = Net force (Newtons, N)
- **m** = Mass (kilograms, kg)
- **a** = Acceleration (m/s²)

---

## 🔬 Understanding the Relationship

### Force and Acceleration

**Direct relationship:** More force → More acceleration

```
If mass is constant:
F = ma

Double the force → Double the acceleration
Triple the force → Triple the acceleration
```

### Mass and Acceleration

**Inverse relationship:** More mass → Less acceleration

```
If force is constant:
a = F/m

Double the mass → Half the acceleration
Triple the mass → 1/3 the acceleration
```

---

## 📊 The Force-Mass-Acceleration Triangle

```
    F
   / \
  m   a
```

**To find:**
- **F** = m × a
- **m** = F / a
- **a** = F / m

---

## 🧮 Worked Examples

### Example 1: Basic Calculation

**Problem:** A 5 kg object is pushed with a net force of 20 N. Calculate acceleration.

**Solution:**
```
F = ma
20 = 5 × a
a = 20/5 = 4 m/s²
```

### Example 2: Finding Force

**Problem:** What force is needed to accelerate a 1000 kg car at 3 m/s²?

**Solution:**
```
F = ma
F = 1000 × 3
F = 3000 N
```

### Example 3: Finding Mass

**Problem:** A force of 50 N causes an acceleration of 2 m/s². What is the mass?

**Solution:**
```
F = ma
50 = m × 2
m = 50/2 = 25 kg
```

## ⚖️ Weight vs Mass

### Mass
- Amount of matter in object
- Measured in kg
- **Same everywhere** in universe

### Weight
- Force of gravity on object
- Measured in Newtons (N)
- **Changes** with gravity

### Weight Formula

```
W = mg
```

Where:
- **W** = Weight (N)
- **m** = Mass (kg)
- **g** = Gravitational field strength (N/kg or m/s²)

On Earth: **g ≈ 9.8 N/kg ≈ 9.8 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²** (approximate)

### Example: Weight on Different Planets

| Location | g (m/s²) | Mass (kg) | Weight (N) |
|----------|----------|-----------|------------|
| Earth | 9.8 | 60 | 588 |
| Moon | 1.6 | 60 | 96 |
| Jupiter | 24.8 | 60 | 1488 |
| Space (far from planets) | 0 | 60 | 0 |

## 🔥 Friction Force

### What is Friction?

**Friction** is a force that opposes motion between surfaces in contact.

### Characteristics

- Acts **opposite** to direction of motion
- Depends on:
  1. Type of surfaces (rough/smooth)
  2. Force pressing surfaces together

### Friction in F = ma

When friction is present:
```
F_net = F_applied - F_friction = ma
```

### Example with Friction

**Problem:** A 10 kg box is pushed with 50 N. Friction is 20 N. Find acceleration.

**Solution:**
```
F_net = F_applied - F_friction
F_net = 50 - 20 = 30 N

F_net = ma
30 = 10 × a
a = 3 m/s²
```

---

## 🌍 Applications

### 1. Car Acceleration
- Engine provides force
- More powerful engine → more force → faster acceleration
- Lighter car → faster acceleration (same force)

### 2. Rocket Launch
- Huge thrust force needed
- Massive rocket → needs enormous force
- As fuel burns, mass decreases → acceleration increases

### 3. Sports
- Harder kick → ball accelerates more
- Heavier ball → needs more force for same acceleration

### 4. Elevator
- Going up: Normal force > Weight (you feel heavier)
- Going down: Normal force < Weight (you feel lighter)
- Constant speed: Normal force = Weight

## ✏️ Practice Problems

### Problem 1
Calculate the force needed to accelerate a 2 kg object at 5 m/s².

**Answer:** 10 N

### Problem 2
A 1500 kg car accelerates at 2 m/s². What is the net force?

**Answer:** 3000 N

### Problem 3
What is the weight of a 70 kg person on Earth? (g = 9.8 N/kg)

**Answer:** 686 N

### Problem 4
A 500 kg motorcycle accelerates from 0 to 20 m/s in 5 seconds.
a) Calculate acceleration
b) Calculate net force

**Solution:**
```
a) a = (v-u)/t = (20-0)/5 = 4 m/s²
b) F = ma = 500 × 4 = 2000 N
```

### Problem 5
**Explain** why it's easier to push an empty shopping cart than a full one.

**Answer:** Full cart has more mass, so requires more force for the same acceleration (F = ma).

---

## 📝 Summary

- F_net = ma (Newton's 2nd Law)
- Acceleration ∝ Force (direct)
- Acceleration ∝ 1/Mass (inverse)
- Weight: W = mg
- Mass (kg) ≠ Weight (N)
- Include friction: F_net = F_applied - F_friction
