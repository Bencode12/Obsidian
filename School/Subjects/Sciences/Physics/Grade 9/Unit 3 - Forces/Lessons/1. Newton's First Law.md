# 1. Newton's First Law of Motion (Law of Inertia)

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] State Newton's First Law

- [ ] Apply the law to real-world situations

---

## 📜 Newton's First Law

### Statement

**An object at rest stays at rest, and an object in motion stays in motion with constant velocity, unless acted upon by a net external force.**

### In Simple Terms

- Objects keep doing what they're doing
- Unless a force makes them change
- **Resistance to change = Inertia**

---

## 🔬 Key Concepts

### Inertia

**Inertia** is the tendency of an object to resist changes in its state of motion.

**Examples:**
- Book on table stays until you push it
- Passenger lurches forward when car brakes
- Tablecloth trick

### Mass and Inertia

- **Mass** is a measure of inertia
- More mass → More inertia → Harder to move/stop
- Units: kilograms (kg)

| Object | Mass | Inertia |
|--------|------|---------|
| Tennis ball | Small | Low |
| Bowling ball | Large | High |
| Car | Very large | Very high |

---

## 💪 Net Force

### Definition

**Net Force (F_net)** is the vector sum of all forces acting on an object.

### Balanced vs Unbalanced Forces

**Balanced Forces (F_net = 0):**
- No change in motion
- Object at rest → stays at rest
- Object moving → continues at constant velocity

**Unbalanced Forces (F_net ≠ 0):**
- Change in motion (acceleration)
- Object speeds up, slows down, or changes direction

### Examples

```
Box pushed from both sides:

← 5N    [BOX]    5N →

F_net = 5N - 5N = 0
Result: No motion (or constant velocity)
```

```
Box pushed unevenly:

← 3N    [BOX]    8N →

F_net = 8N - 3N = 5N to right
Result: Accelerates to right
```

## 🌍 Real-World Applications

### 1. Seat Belts

**Why wear seat belts?**
- Car stops suddenly (brakes/collision)
- Your body continues forward (inertia)
- Seat belt provides force to stop you
- Prevents hitting dashboard/windshield

### 2. Headrests

**Purpose:**
- Car hit from behind → accelerates forward
- Head tends to stay at rest (inertia)
- Neck can be injured
- Headrest pushes head forward with body

### 3. Shaking Ketchup Bottle

**How it works:**
- Shake bottle downward
- Stop bottle suddenly
- Ketchup continues moving (inertia)
- Ketchup comes out

### 4. Tightening Hammer Head

**Method:**
- Hit handle end on ground
- Handle stops
- Heavy head continues downward
- Wedges tighter on handle

## ⚖️ Equilibrium

### Types of Equilibrium

**Static Equilibrium:**
- Object at rest
- F_net = 0
- **Example:** Book on table

**Dynamic Equilibrium:**
- Object moving at constant velocity
- F_net = 0
- **Example:** Car on cruise control

### Conditions for Equilibrium

```
ΣF = 0 (sum of all forces = 0)
```

## ✏️ Practice Questions

### Question 1
**State** Newton's First Law in your own words.

### Question 2
**Explain** why you lurch forward when a bus suddenly brakes.

**Answer:** Your body has inertia - it tends to continue moving forward at the original speed even when the bus slows down.

### Question 3
Two people push a box. One pushes right with 10N, another pushes left with 6N.
a) What is the net force?
b) Will the box accelerate?

**Answers:**
a) F_net = 10N - 6N = 4N to the right
b) Yes, it will accelerate to the right

### Question 4
**True or False:**
- Inertia is a force. (___)
- More massive objects have more inertia. (___)
- An object in motion will eventually stop on its own. (___)
- If F_net = 0, the object must be at rest. (___)

**Answers:** False, True, False, False (could be moving at constant velocity)

---

## 📝 Summary

- Newton's 1st Law: Objects resist changes in motion
- Inertia = resistance to change in motion
- Mass measures inertia
- Net force = sum of all forces
- F_net = 0 → no acceleration (equilibrium)
- F_net ≠ 0 → object accelerates
