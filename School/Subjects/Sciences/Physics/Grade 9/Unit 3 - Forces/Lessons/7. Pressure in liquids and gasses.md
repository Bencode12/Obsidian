
Pascals law:
- Liquids and gasses exert pressure in all directions equally.
- **Pascal's law states that when there is an increase in pressure at any point in a confined fluid, there is an equal increase at every other point in the container.**

Hydraulic lift

Pressure at different depth

$\text{Fomula:}  p = \rho \cdot g \cdot h$ 
- $p - pressure [pa]$
- $\rho - \text{density of fluid} [\frac{kg}{m^3}] \text{or} [kg/m^3]$
- $g - \text{freefall acceleration} [\frac{m}{s^2}] \text{or} [m/s^2]$
- $h - \text{height of liquid} [m]$

$P = \frac{F}{S} \Rightarrow F = m\cdot g \Rightarrow P = \frac{m}{S} \cdot g \Rightarrow m = \rho \cdot V \Rightarrow P = \rho \cdot \frac{V}{S} \cdot g \Rightarrow V = S \cdot h \Rightarrow P = \rho \cdot \frac{S\cdot h}{S}\cdot g \text{S and S cross out} \Rightarrow P = \rho \cdot h \cdot g$
