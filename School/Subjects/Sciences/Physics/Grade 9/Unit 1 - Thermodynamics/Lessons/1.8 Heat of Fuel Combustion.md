# 1.8 Heat of Fuel Combustion

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Define calorific value of fuels
- [ ] Calculate energy released during combustion
- [ ] Compare different fuels by energy content
- [ ] Understand efficiency of heat engines

---

## 🔬 Calorific Value

### Definition

**Calorific Value** is the energy released per unit mass (or volume) of fuel when completely burned.

### Units
- **J/kg** or **MJ/kg** (for solids/liquids)
- **J/m³** or **MJ/m³** (for gases)

### Calorific Values of Common Fuels

| Fuel | Calorific Value (MJ/kg) |
|------|------------------------|
| Hydrogen | 142 |
| Petrol/Gasoline | 46 |
| Diesel | 45 |
| Coal | 24-30 |
| Wood (dry) | 15-18 |
| Natural Gas | 54 (MJ/m³) |
| Propane | 50 |
| Charcoal | 33 |

---

## 🧮 Energy from Combustion

### Formula

```
Q = m × CV
```

Where:
- **Q** = Heat energy released (J)
- **m** = Mass of fuel burned (kg)
- **CV** = Calorific value (J/kg)

---

## 📊 Worked Examples

### Example 1: Energy from Petrol

**Problem:** Calculate energy released when 5 kg of petrol burns completely. (CV = 46 MJ/kg)

**Solution:**
```
m = 5 kg
CV = 46 MJ/kg = 46,000,000 J/kg

Q = m × CV
Q = 5 × 46,000,000
Q = 230,000,000 J = 230 MJ
```

**Answer:** 230 MJ of energy released

### Example 2: Comparing Fuels

**Problem:** Which releases more energy: 2 kg of coal (CV = 28 MJ/kg) or 1 kg of diesel (CV = 45 MJ/kg)?

**Solution:**
```
Coal: Q = 2 × 28 = 56 MJ
Diesel: Q = 1 × 45 = 45 MJ
```

**Answer:** Coal releases more energy (56 MJ > 45 MJ)

## 🔥 Heat Engines

### What is a Heat Engine?

A device that converts heat energy into mechanical work.

### Examples:
- Car engines (petrol/diesel)
- Steam engines
- Jet engines
- Power station turbines

### Efficiency

```
Efficiency = (Useful Energy Output / Total Energy Input) × 100%
```

**Typical Efficiencies:**
- Petrol car engine: 20-30%
- Diesel engine: 35-40%
- Steam turbine: 40-45%
- **Most energy is lost as waste heat!**

---

## 🌍 Energy Losses in Engines

### Where Does Energy Go?

1. **Exhaust gases** (30-40%)
2. **Cooling system** (20-30%)
3. **Friction** (5-10%)
4. **Useful work** (20-40%)

### Improving Efficiency

- Better engine design
- Turbocharging
- Hybrid systems (recover braking energy)
- Electric vehicles (80-90% efficient)

---

## ⚡ Power from Fuel

### Power Formula

```
Power = Energy / Time
P = Q / t
```

**Units:** Watts (W) or kilowatts (kW)

### Example: Car Power

**Problem:** A car burns 0.5 kg of petrol per minute. Calculate power output. (CV = 46 MJ/kg)

**Solution:**
```
Energy per minute: Q = 0.5 × 46 = 23 MJ/min
Power: P = 23,000,000 J / 60 s = 383,333 W = 383 kW
```

**Note:** Actual useful power is less due to efficiency (~25% = 96 kW)

---

## 🌱 Environmental Considerations

### Combustion Products

**Complete Combustion:**
```
Fuel + O₂ → CO₂ + H₂O + Energy
```

**Incomplete Combustion:**
```
Fuel + O₂ → CO + C (soot) + H₂O + Less Energy
```

### Environmental Impacts

1. **CO₂** - Greenhouse gas → Climate change
2. **CO** - Poisonous gas
3. **NOx** - Acid rain, smog
4. **Particulates** - Respiratory problems

### Alternative Fuels

- **Biofuels** - Carbon neutral (plants absorb CO₂)
- **Hydrogen** - Only produces water
- **Electric** - Zero emissions at point of use

## ✏️ Practice Problems

### Problem 1
Calculate energy from burning 3 kg of wood. (CV = 16 MJ/kg)

**Answer:** 48 MJ

### Problem 2
A heater uses 0.2 kg of propane per hour. (CV = 50 MJ/kg)
a) Calculate energy released per hour
b) Calculate power in kW

**Answers:**
a) 10 MJ
b) 2.78 kW

### Problem 3
**Explain** why hydrogen is considered a cleaner fuel than coal.

**Answer:** Hydrogen produces only water when burned, while coal produces CO₂, SO₂, and particulates.

---

## 📝 Summary

- Calorific value = energy per kg of fuel
- Q = m × CV calculates energy from combustion
- Heat engines convert heat to work (20-40% efficient)
- Most energy is lost as waste heat
- Environmental impacts: CO₂, pollution
- Alternative fuels: hydrogen, biofuels, electric
