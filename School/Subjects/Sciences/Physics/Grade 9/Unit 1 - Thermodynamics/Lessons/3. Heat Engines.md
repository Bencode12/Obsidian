## 🔬 What is a Heat Engine?

### Definition

A **Heat Engine** is a device that converts heat energy (from fuel combustion) into mechanical work (motion).

### Basic Principle

```
Heat Source (Hot) → Engine → Work Output
                        ↓
                  Heat Sink (Cold)
```

### Examples of Heat Engines

| Engine Type | Fuel | Use |
|-------------|------|-----|
| Petrol Engine | Gasoline | Cars, motorcycles |
| Diesel Engine | Diesel | Trucks, ships |
| Steam Turbine | Coal/Gas | Power stations |
| Jet Engine | Aviation fuel | Aircraft |
| Steam Engine | Coal/Wood | Trains (historical) |

---

## ⚙️ How Heat Engines Work

### Four-Stroke Petrol Engine

**1. Intake Stroke**
- Piston moves down
- Air-fuel mixture enters cylinder

**2. Compression Stroke**
- Piston moves up
- Mixture compressed

**3. Power Stroke**
- Spark plug ignites mixture
- Explosion pushes piston down
- **This produces work!**

**4. Exhaust Stroke**
- Piston moves up
- Burnt gases expelled

### Energy Transformation

```
Chemical Energy (fuel) 
    ↓
Heat Energy (combustion) 
    ↓
Mechanical Energy (piston motion)
```

---

## 📊 Efficiency of Heat Engines

### Definition

**Efficiency** is the ratio of useful work output to total energy input.

### Formula

```
Efficiency = (Useful Energy Output / Total Energy Input) × 100%

or

Efficiency = (Work Done / Heat Supplied) × 100%
```

### Typical Efficiencies

| Engine Type | Efficiency |
|-------------|------------|
| Petrol Car Engine | 20-30% |
| Diesel Engine | 35-40% |
| Steam Turbine | 40-45% |
| Jet Engine | 30-35% |
| Electric Motor | 80-90% |

**Note:** Most energy is LOST as waste heat!

---

## 🔥 Energy Losses in Car Engines

### Where Does Energy Go?

```
100% Energy from Fuel
│
├─→ 25% Useful Work (moves car)
├─→ 35% Exhaust Gases (hot gases leave)
├─→ 30% Cooling System (radiator loses heat)
└─→ 10% Friction (engine parts rubbing)
```

### Why So Inefficient?

1. **Second Law of Thermodynamics**: Cannot convert all heat to work
2. **Friction**: Moving parts lose energy
3. **Heat Loss**: Engine gets hot, loses energy to surroundings
4. **Exhaust**: Hot gases carry away energy

---

## 🧮 Efficiency Calculations

### Example 1: Basic Efficiency

**Problem:** A car engine uses 1000 kJ of energy from petrol and produces 250 kJ of useful work. Calculate efficiency.

**Solution:**
```
Efficiency = (Useful Output / Total Input) × 100%
Efficiency = (250 / 1000) × 100%
Efficiency = 25%
```

### Example 2: Power Output

**Problem:** An engine has 30% efficiency and burns fuel at a rate that releases 50 kW of power. What is the useful power output?

**Solution:**
```
Efficiency = Useful Output / Total Input
0.30 = Useful Output / 50 kW
Useful Output = 0.30 × 50 = 15 kW
```

---

## 🌍 Improving Engine Efficiency

### Methods

1. **Turbocharging**
   - Uses exhaust gases to compress intake air
   - More power from same fuel

2. **Hybrid Systems**
   - Recover energy during braking
   - Electric motor assists engine

3. **Better Materials**
   - Lighter engines
   - Less friction

4. **Electric Vehicles**
   - 80-90% efficient
   - No waste heat from combustion

### Future Technologies

- **Hydrogen fuel cells** (50-60% efficient)
- **Improved batteries** for electric cars
- **Synthetic fuels** (carbon neutral)

## ️ Comparing Engine Types

### Petrol vs Diesel

| Petrol | Diesel |
|--------|--------|
| Spark ignition | Compression ignition |
| Lower efficiency (25%) | Higher efficiency (35%) |
| Quieter | More torque |
| Cheaper fuel | More CO₂ but less CO |

### Internal Combustion vs Electric

| Petrol Car | Electric Car |
|------------|--------------|
| 25% efficient | 85% efficient |
| CO₂ emissions | Zero direct emissions |
| Complex engine | Simple motor |
| Quick refuel | Longer charging |

## ✏️ Practice Problems

### Problem 1
A diesel engine produces 40 kJ of work from 100 kJ of fuel energy. Calculate efficiency.

**Answer:** 40%

### Problem 2
A power station turbine has 42% efficiency. If it burns coal releasing 1000 MW of heat, what is the electrical power output?

**Answer:** 420 MW

### Problem 3
**Explain** why electric cars are more efficient than petrol cars.

**Answer:** Electric motors convert electrical energy directly to motion (85%+ efficiency), while petrol engines must first burn fuel and convert heat to work (limited to ~25% by thermodynamics).

### Problem 4
**List** three ways energy is lost in a car engine.

**Answer:** Exhaust gases, cooling system, friction

---

## 📝 Summary

- Heat engines convert heat to mechanical work
- Four-stroke engine: Intake, Compression, Power, Exhaust
- Efficiency = (Useful Output / Total Input) × 100%
- Typical car engine: 20-30% efficient
- Most energy lost as: exhaust heat, cooling, friction
- Electric motors much more efficient (80-90%)
