# 2.4 Evaporation, Condensation and Boiling

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Distinguish between evaporation and boiling
- [ ] Explain condensation
- [ ] Define latent heat of vaporization
- [ ] Describe factors affecting evaporation rate
- [ ] Apply Q = mL to vaporization problems

---

## 💨 Evaporation

### Definition

**Evaporation** is the change from liquid to gas that occurs at the surface, at ANY temperature.

### How Evaporation Works

1. Fast-moving particles near surface gain enough energy
2. They escape from the liquid
3. Become gas/vapor molecules
4. Average energy of remaining particles decreases
5. **Liquid cools down** (evaporative cooling)

### Factors Affecting Evaporation Rate

| Factor | Effect | Example |
|--------|--------|-------|
| **Temperature** | Higher temp → Faster | Clothes dry faster in summer |
| **Surface Area** | Larger area → Faster | Spilled water dries faster |
| **Air Movement** | More wind → Faster | Wind dries clothes quickly |
| **Humidity** | Lower humidity → Faster | Desert air evaporates quickly |

---

## 🔥 Boiling

### Definition

**Boiling** is the rapid change from liquid to gas that occurs throughout the liquid at a specific temperature (boiling point).

### Boiling Point

- Temperature at which vapor pressure = atmospheric pressure
- Bubbles of gas form throughout the liquid
- Temperature stays constant during boiling

### Boiling Points of Common Substances

| Substance | Boiling Point (°C) |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Water | 100 |
| Ethanol | 78 |
| Oxygen | -183 |
| Nitrogen | -196 |
| Iron | 2862 |
| Mercury | 357 |

### Effect of Pressure

- **Higher pressure** → Higher boiling point
- **Lower pressure** → Lower boiling point

**Example:** Water boils at <100°C on mountains (lower atmospheric pressure)

---

## 💧 Condensation

### Definition

**Condensation** is the change from gas to liquid.

### How Condensation Occurs

1. Gas/vapor particles lose energy
2. Move more slowly
3. Come closer together
4. Form liquid droplets

### Examples of Condensation

- **Dew on grass** (water vapor condenses on cool surfaces)
- **Clouds** (water vapor condenses on dust particles)
- **Fogged windows** (warm moist air hits cold glass)
- **Distillation** (vapor condensed back to liquid)

---

## 🔥 Latent Heat of Vaporization

### Definition

**Specific Latent Heat of Vaporization (Lᵥ)** is the energy needed to change 1 kg of liquid to gas at constant temperature.

### Formula

```
Q = mLᵥ
```

Where:
- **Q** = Heat energy (J)
- **m** = Mass (kg)
- **Lᵥ** = Specific latent heat of vaporization (J/kg)

### Values for Common Substances

| Substance | Lᵥ (kJ/kg) |
|-----------|-----------|
| Water | 2260 |
| Ethanol | 855 |
| Oxygen | 213 |
| Mercury | 295 |

**Note:** Lᵥ is much larger than L (fusion) for the same substance!

---

## 📊 Comparing Evaporation and Boiling

| Evaporation | Boiling |
|-------------|---------|
| Surface only | Throughout liquid |
| Any temperature | At boiling point |
| Slow process | Rapid process |
| Causes cooling | Temperature constant |
| No bubbles | Bubbles form |

---

## 🧮 Energy Calculations

### Example 1: Boiling Water

**Problem:** Calculate energy to boil 2 kg of water at 100°C. (Lᵥ = 2260 kJ/kg)

**Solution:**
```
Q = mLᵥ
Q = 2 × 2,260,000
Q = 4,520,000 J = 4520 kJ
```

### Example 2: Complete Process

**Problem:** Calculate total energy to convert 1 kg of ice at 0°C to steam at 100°C.

**Given:**
- L (fusion) = 334 kJ/kg
- c (water) = 4200 J/kg·°C
- Lᵥ (vaporization) = 2260 kJ/kg

**Solution:**
```
Step 1: Melt ice: Q₁ = mL = 1 × 334,000 = 334,000 J
Step 2: Heat water: Q₂ = mcΔT = 1 × 4200 × 100 = 420,000 J
Step 3: Boil water: Q₃ = mLᵥ = 1 × 2,260,000 = 2,260,000 J

Total: Q = 334,000 + 420,000 + 2,260,000 = 3,014,000 J = 3014 kJ
```

## 🌍 Applications

### 1. Sweating (Body Cooling)
- Sweat evaporates from skin
- Absorbs latent heat from body
- Cools you down

### 2. Refrigerators
- Refrigerant evaporates (absorbs heat)
- Compressed and condensed (releases heat)
- Continuous cycle

### 3. Distillation
- Boil liquid mixture
- Condense vapor
- Separate components

### 4. Power Stations
- Water boiled to make steam
- Steam drives turbines
- Steam condensed back to water
- Cycle repeats

### 5. Cooking
- Pressure cookers: higher pressure → higher boiling point → faster cooking
- Steam burns worse than boiling water (releases latent heat)

## ✏️ Practice Problems

### Problem 1
Calculate energy to boil 0.5 kg of ethanol. (Lᵥ = 855 kJ/kg)

**Answer:** 427.5 kJ

### Problem 2
**Explain** why you feel cold when you come out of a swimming pool.

**Answer:** Water evaporates from your skin, absorbing latent heat from your body, causing cooling.

### Problem 3
**Compare** the energy needed to:
a) Melt 1 kg of ice at 0°C
b) Boil 1 kg of water at 100°C

**Answer:**
a) Q = 334 kJ
b) Q = 2260 kJ
Boiling requires much more energy!

### Problem 4

- Boiling occurs throughout the liquid. (___)
- Condensation releases energy. (___)
- Higher humidity increases evaporation rate. (___)

## 📝 Summary

- **Evaporation**: liquid → gas at surface, any temperature, causes cooling
- **Boiling**: liquid → gas throughout, at boiling point, temperature constant
- **Condensation**: gas → liquid, releases energy
- Latent heat of vaporization: Q = mLᵥ
- Water: Lᵥ = 2260 kJ/kg (much larger than fusion)
- Many practical applications in daily life
