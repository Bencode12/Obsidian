## Part A: Specific Heat Capacity (15 marks)

**Formula: Q = mcΔT**

### Question 1 (4 marks)
Calculate the energy needed to heat 3 kg of water from 15°C to 65°C.
(c_water = 4200 J/kg·°C)

### Question 2 (4 marks)
A 2 kg aluminum block (c = 900 J/kg·°C) absorbs 180,000 J of energy.
a) Calculate the temperature increase.
b) If the initial temperature was 20°C, what is the final temperature?

### Question 3 (4 marks)
0.5 kg of copper (c = 385 J/kg·°C) cools from 150°C to 50°C.
Calculate the energy released.

### Question 4 (3 marks)
**Explain** why water is used in central heating systems instead of other liquids.

## Part B: Latent Heat (15 marks)

**Formulas: Q = mL (fusion), Q = mLᵥ (vaporization)**

### Question 5 (4 marks)
Calculate the energy needed to melt 4 kg of ice at 0°C.
(L_fusion = 334 kJ/kg)

### Question 6 (4 marks)
How much energy is required to boil 0.5 kg of water at 100°C?
(L_vaporization = 2260 kJ/kg)

### Question 7 (7 marks)
Calculate the TOTAL energy to convert 2 kg of ice at 0°C to steam at 100°C.

Given:
- L_fusion = 334 kJ/kg
- c_water = 4200 J/kg·°C
- L_vaporization = 2260 kJ/kg

## 📝 Formula Sheet

```
Q = mcΔT (heating/cooling)
Q = mL (phase change)
ΔT = T_final - T_initial
```

**Constants:**
- c_water = 4200 J/kg·°C
- c_ice = 2100 J/kg·°C
- c_aluminum = 900 J/kg·°C
- c_copper = 385 J/kg·°C
- L_fusion (water) = 334 kJ/kg
- L_vaporization (water) = 2260 kJ/kg
