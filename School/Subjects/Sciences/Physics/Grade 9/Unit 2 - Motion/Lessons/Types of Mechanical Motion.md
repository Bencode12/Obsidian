# Types of Mechanical Motion

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Identify different types of motion
- [ ] Describe uniform and non-uniform motion
- [ ] Understand linear, circular, and oscillatory motion
- [ ] Recognize motion types in real-world examples

---

## 🔬 Classification of Motion

### 1. Based on Path

#### Linear (Rectilinear) Motion
- Motion along a straight line
- **Examples:** Car on straight road, falling object

#### Circular Motion
- Motion along a circular path
- **Examples:** Clock hands, car turning, planets orbiting

#### Oscillatory (Vibrational) Motion
- Back-and-forth motion about a fixed point
- **Examples:** Pendulum, guitar string, swing

#### Random Motion
- Irregular, unpredictable path
- **Examples:** Gas molecules, butterfly flight

---

### 2. Based on Speed

#### Uniform Motion
- Equal distances in equal time intervals
- **Constant speed**
- **Acceleration = 0**

```
Time:   0s    1s    2s    3s    4s
        │────│────│────│────│
Pos:    0m    5m   10m   15m   20m
(5m every second)
```

#### Non-Uniform Motion
- Unequal distances in equal time intervals
- **Changing speed**
- **Acceleration ≠ 0**

```
Time:   0s    1s    2s    3s    4s
        │──│───│────│─────│
Pos:    0m   2m   6m   12m   20m
(Increasing distance each second - accelerating)
```

---

### 3. Based on Dimension

#### One-Dimensional Motion
- Motion along a single axis
- **Example:** Car on straight road

#### Two-Dimensional Motion
- Motion in a plane
- **Example:** Projectile motion, circular motion

#### Three-Dimensional Motion
- Motion in space
- **Example:** Bird flying, airplane

---

##  Circular Motion

### Key Concepts

**Circumference:** Distance around circle
```
C = 2πr
```

**Period (T):** Time for one complete revolution
- Units: seconds (s)

**Frequency (f):** Number of revolutions per second
```
f = 1/T
```
- Units: Hertz (Hz)

**Speed in Circular Motion:**
```
v = 2πr / T = 2πrf
```

### Examples

| Object | Radius | Period | Speed |
|--------|--------|--------|-------|
| Clock second hand | 0.1m | 60s | 0.01 m/s |
| Car on curve | 50m | 20s | 15.7 m/s |
| Earth orbiting Sun | 1.5×10¹¹m | 365 days | 30,000 m/s |

---

## 🔄 Periodic Motion

### Definition
Motion that repeats at regular intervals.

### Examples

1. **Pendulum**
   - Swings back and forth
   - Period depends on length

2. **Mass on Spring**
   - Bounces up and down
   - Period depends on mass and spring constant

3. **Planetary Motion**
   - Planets orbit Sun
   - Period depends on distance from Sun

4. **Heart Beat**
   - Rhythmic contraction
   - Normal: ~1 Hz (60 beats/min)

## 📊 Comparing Motion Types

| Type | Path | Speed | Example |
|------|------|-------|---------|
| **Uniform Linear** | Straight | Constant | Car on cruise control |
| **Non-uniform Linear** | Straight | Changing | Car accelerating |
| **Uniform Circular** | Circle | Constant | Clock hand |
| **Oscillatory** | Back-forth | Changing | Pendulum |
| **Projectile** | Curve | Changing | Thrown ball |

## ✏️ Practice Questions

### Question 1
**Classify** the following motions:
a) Car braking at traffic light
b) Hands of a clock
c) Guitar string when plucked
d) Train on straight track at constant speed

**Answers:**
a) Non-uniform linear
b) Uniform circular
c) Oscillatory
d) Uniform linear

### Question 2
A car travels around a circular track of radius 100m in 40 seconds.
a) Calculate the circumference.
b) Calculate the speed.

**Solution:**
```
C = 2πr = 2 × π × 100 = 628m
v = C/T = 628/40 = 15.7 m/s
```

### Question 3
**Explain** the difference between uniform and non-uniform motion with examples.

## 📝 Summary

- **Linear**: Straight line path
- **Circular**: Circular path, period and frequency
- **Oscillatory**: Back-and-forth motion
- **Uniform**: Constant speed
- **Non-uniform**: Changing speed (acceleration)
- Motion can be classified by path, speed, or dimension
