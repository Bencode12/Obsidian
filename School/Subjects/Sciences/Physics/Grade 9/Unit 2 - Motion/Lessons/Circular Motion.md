# Circular Motion

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Describe circular motion and its characteristics
- [ ] Calculate speed and acceleration in circular motion
- [ ] Understand centripetal force
- [ ] Apply circular motion concepts to real situations

---

## 🔬 Characteristics of Circular Motion

### Key Quantities

**Radius (r):** Distance from center to object

**Period (T):** Time for one complete revolution
- Units: seconds (s)

**Frequency (f):** Number of revolutions per second
```
f = 1/T
```
- Units: Hertz (Hz) = revolutions/second

**Angular Velocity (ω):** Angle covered per unit time
```
ω = 2π/T = 2πf
```
- Units: rad/s

---

## ⚡ Speed in Circular Motion

### Linear Speed

Even though speed may be constant, **velocity is changing** (direction changes)!

```
v = Circumference / Period
v = 2πr / T
```

### Example

**Problem:** A car travels around a circular track (r = 50m) in 20 seconds. Find speed.

**Solution:**
```
v = 2πr / T
v = 2 × π × 50 / 20
v = 314 / 20 = 15.7 m/s
```

---

## 🔄 Centripetal Acceleration

### Why is there Acceleration?

- Velocity is a **vector** (speed + direction)
- In circular motion, **direction constantly changes**
- Therefore, **velocity changes**
- Change in velocity = **acceleration**

### Centripetal Acceleration

**Direction:** Always toward the center of circle

**Formula:**
```
a_c = v²/r
```

Or in terms of period:
```
a_c = 4π²r / T²
```

Where:
- a_c = centripetal acceleration (m/s²)
- v = linear speed (m/s)
- r = radius (m)

---

## 💪 Centripetal Force

### What Causes Circular Motion?

**Centripetal Force (F_c):** The net force directed toward the center that keeps an object moving in a circle.

```
F_c = ma_c = mv²/r
```

### Sources of Centripetal Force

| Situation | Centripetal Force Provided By |
|-----------|-------------------------------|
| Car turning | Friction between tires and road |
| Planet orbiting Sun | Gravitational force |
| Ball on string | Tension in string |
| Roller coaster loop | Normal force + gravity |
| Electron around nucleus | Electric force |

---

## ⚠️ Centrifugal "Force"

### Common Misconception

**Centrifugal force** is NOT a real force!

- It's the sensation of being pushed outward
- Actually your **inertia** trying to go straight
- In reality, centripetal force pulls you inward

**Example:** In a turning car:
- You feel pushed outward (centrifugal "force")
- Actually, the car door pushes you inward (centripetal force)
- Your body wants to continue straight (Newton's 1st Law)

## 🌍 Applications

### 1. Vehicles on Curves

**Flat Road:**
- Friction provides centripetal force
- Too fast → friction insufficient → skid

**Banked Road:**
- Road tilted inward
- Normal force helps provide centripetal force
- Less reliance on friction

### 2. Roller Coasters

**Loop-the-Loop:**
- At top: Gravity + Normal force provide centripetal force
- Minimum speed needed to stay on track:
```
v_min = √(gr)
```

### 3. Washing Machine Spin Cycle

- Clothes pushed against drum wall
- Water escapes through holes
- Centripetal force from drum wall

### 4. Centrifuges

- Separate substances by density
- Blood separation (plasma, cells)
- Uranium enrichment

## 🧮 Worked Examples

### Example 1: Car on Curve

**Problem:** A 1000 kg car travels at 20 m/s around a curve of radius 50m. Calculate:
a) Centripetal acceleration
b) Centripetal force
c) Minimum friction coefficient needed

**Solution:**
```
a) a_c = v²/r = 20²/50 = 400/50 = 8 m/s²

b) F_c = ma_c = 1000 × 8 = 8000 N

c) F_friction = μmg = F_c
   μ = F_c/(mg) = 8000/(1000×9.8) = 0.82
```

### Example 2: Ball on String

**Problem:** A 0.5 kg ball is whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1m at 2 revolutions per second. Find tension in string.

**Solution:**
```
f = 2 Hz
T = 1/f = 0.5 s
v = 2πr/T = 2π(1)/0.5 = 12.57 m/s

F_c = mv²/r = 0.5 × 12.57²/1 = 79 N
```

## ✏️ Practice Problems

### Problem 1
A child sits 2m from the center of a merry-go-round that completes one rotation every 5 seconds.
a) Calculate speed
b) Calculate centripetal acceleration

**Answers:** a) 2.51 m/s, b) 3.15 m/s²

### Problem 2
**Explain** why a motorcyclist leans inward when turning.

**Answer:** Leaning allows the normal force to have a horizontal component that provides centripetal force.

### Problem 3
What provides centripetal force for:
a) Earth orbiting Sun?
b) Car turning on flat road?
c) Ball on string?

**Answers:** a) Gravity, b) Friction, c) Tension

---

## 📝 Summary

- Circular motion requires centripetal force toward center
- Speed: v = 2πr/T
- Centripetal acceleration: a_c = v²/r
- Centripetal force: F_c = mv²/r
- Centrifugal force is not real - it's inertia
- Applications: vehicles, roller coasters, centrifuges
