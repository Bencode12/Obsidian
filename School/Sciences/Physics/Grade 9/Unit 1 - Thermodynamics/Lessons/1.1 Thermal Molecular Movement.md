# 1.1 Thermal Molecular Movement & Internal Energy

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Understand that all matter is made of constantly moving particles
- [ ] Explain the relationship between temperature and molecular motion
- [ ] Define internal energy as the total energy of all particles in a substance
- [ ] Distinguish between heat and temperature

---

## 🔬 Key Concepts

### Thermal Molecular Movement

**All matter is composed of particles (atoms and molecules) that are in constant motion.**

- **Solids**: Particles vibrate in fixed positions
- **Liquids**: Particles move more freely, sliding past each other
- **Gases**: Particles move rapidly in all directions

### Temperature and Motion

| Temperature | Particle Motion |
|-------------|-----------------|
| Higher | Faster movement |
| Lower | Slower movement |
| Absolute Zero (-273.15°C) | Minimum possible motion |

### Internal Energy

**Internal Energy (U)** = Total kinetic energy + Total potential energy of all particles

```
U = KE(total) + PE(total)
```

**Factors affecting internal energy:**
1. Temperature (higher temp → higher KE)
2. Mass (more particles → more energy)
3. State of matter (affects PE)
4. Type of substance

---

## 📊 Heat vs Temperature

| Heat | Temperature |
|------|-------------|
| Energy transfer between objects | Measure of average kinetic energy |
| Measured in Joules (J) | Measured in °C or K |
| Depends on mass | Independent of mass |
| **Q** | **T** |

---

## 🧪 Examples

### Example 1: Heating Water
When you heat water:
- Water molecules move faster
- Temperature increases
- Internal energy increases

### Example 2: Ice Melting
- Heat is absorbed from surroundings
- Molecules gain energy to break bonds
- Temperature stays constant during phase change

## ✏️ Practice Questions

1. **Explain** why a cup of hot tea has more internal energy than a cup of cold water.

2. **Compare** the molecular motion in ice, liquid water, and steam.

3. **Calculate**: If you have 2 kg of water at 50°C and 1 kg of water at 50°C, which has more internal energy? Why?

4. **True or False**: 
   - Temperature depends on the amount of substance. (___)
   - Heat flows from hot to cold objects. (___)
   - At absolute zero, all molecular motion stops. (___)

---

## 📝 Summary Notes

- Particles in all matter are constantly moving
- Temperature measures average kinetic energy of particles
- Internal energy is the total energy of all particles
- Heat is energy transfer, measured in Joules
- Temperature is measured in °C or Kelvin
