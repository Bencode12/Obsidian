# 1.5 Heat Radiation

## 📋 Learning Objectives

- [ ] Define radiation as heat transfer through electromagnetic waves
- [ ] Understand that radiation doesn't require a medium
- [ ] Identify factors affecting radiation absorption and emission
- [ ] Apply knowledge of radiation in practical contexts

---

## 🔬 What is Radiation?

**Radiation** is the transfer of heat through electromagnetic waves (infrared radiation).

### Key Properties

- **No medium required** - can travel through vacuum
- **Speed of light** - travels at 3 × 10⁸ m/s
- **All objects emit radiation** - if above absolute zero
- **Hotter objects emit more** radiation

```
SUN → → → → → EARTH
(Through vacuum of space)
```

---

## 📊 Absorption and Emission

### Surface Properties

| Surface | Absorption | Emission | Reflection |
|---------|------------|----------|------------|
| **Matte Black** | Excellent | Excellent | Poor |
| **Shiny White** | Poor | Poor | Excellent |
| **Dull Dark** | Good | Good | Fair |
| **Shiny Metal** | Poor | Poor | Excellent |

### Key Principles

1. **Good absorbers are good emitters**
2. **Poor absorbers are poor emitters (good reflectors)**
3. **Dark, matte surfaces** - best for absorption/emission
4. **Light, shiny surfaces** - best for reflection

---

## 🌍 Factors Affecting Radiation

### 1. Temperature
- Higher temperature → More radiation emitted
- Doubling temperature (K) increases radiation by 16× (Stefan-Boltzmann Law)

### 2. Surface Area
- Larger area → More radiation emitted/absorbed

### 3. Surface Color/Texture
- Dark, matte = better absorber/emitter
- Light, shiny = better reflector

### 4. Temperature Difference
- Greater difference → Faster net heat transfer

---

## 🧪 Real-World Applications

### 1. Solar Panels
- Black surface maximizes absorption
- Converts solar radiation to electricity/heat

### 2. Thermos Flask
- Shiny silvered surfaces
- Reflects radiation back
- Keeps drinks hot/cold

### 3. House Painting
- **Hot climates**: White walls reflect heat
- **Cold climates**: Dark colors absorb heat

### 4. Radiators
- Despite name, mostly heat by convection
- Painted white/silver (not black) for appearance
- Still effective due to temperature

### 5. Emergency Blankets
- Shiny metalized surface
- Reflects body heat back
- Prevents hypothermia

### 6. Car Radiators
- Painted black for better emission
- Helps dissipate engine heat

---

## ☀️ The Greenhouse Effect

### How it Works

1. Sun's radiation (visible light) passes through atmosphere
2. Earth absorbs radiation and warms up
3. Earth emits infrared radiation
4. Greenhouse gases (CO₂, water vapor) absorb infrared
5. Atmosphere warms up

### Importance
- Natural greenhouse effect keeps Earth habitable (~15°C average)
- Enhanced greenhouse effect → Global warming

## ✏️ Practice Questions

### Question 1
**Explain** why houses in hot countries are often painted white.

**Answer:** White surfaces reflect most solar radiation, keeping the house cooler by absorbing less heat.

### Question 2
**Compare** how heat from the Sun reaches Earth vs. how heat travels through a metal rod.

| Sun to Earth | Metal Rod |
|--------------|-----------|
| Radiation | Conduction |
| Through vacuum | Through material |
| Electromagnetic waves | Particle collisions |

### Question 3
A solar water heater has black pipes. **Explain** why.

**Answer:** Black surfaces are excellent absorbers of radiation, so black pipes absorb maximum solar energy to heat the water.

### Question 4
**True or False:**
- Radiation requires air or water to travel. (___)
- Shiny surfaces are good emitters of radiation. (___)
- All objects above absolute zero emit radiation. (___)
- Radiation travels at the speed of light. (___)

## 📝 Summary

- Radiation transfers heat via electromagnetic waves
- Doesn't require a medium (travels through vacuum)
- Dark, matte surfaces = good absorbers/emitters
- Light, shiny surfaces = good reflectors
- Important in solar energy, climate, insulation
